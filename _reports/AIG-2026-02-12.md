## Executive Summary

American International Group (AIG) is a global insurance company that has undergone a multi-year transformation, and recent quarterly results suggest the turnaround is gaining significant momentum. In Q4 2025, AIG reported adjusted EPS of $1.96 — a 51% year-over-year surge — beating consensus estimates by 3.5%. General Insurance underwriting income rose 48% YoY to $670 million, reflecting improved risk selection, pricing discipline, and operational efficiency.

The stock currently trades at approximately $78.92, representing a trailing P/E of ~13.1x and a dividend yield of ~2.3%. With a market capitalization of $42.3 billion and a relatively low beta of 0.61, AIG presents as a value-oriented, defensive holding within the insurance sector. The company has been a beneficiary of favorable property/casualty pricing conditions and has demonstrated disciplined underwriting improvement.

While the company's strategic simplification — including the separation of its life and retirement business — has sharpened its focus on commercial and personal insurance, AIG still faces risks from catastrophe losses, potential pricing cycle softening, and macroeconomic headwinds. Nonetheless, the improving earnings trajectory and reasonable valuation make AIG an attractive consideration for value-oriented investors.

## Growth Analysis

### Underwriting Momentum

The 48% increase in General Insurance underwriting income to $670 million in Q4 2025 is a powerful indicator of AIG's improved operating fundamentals. This growth is likely driven by:

1. **Pricing discipline** — The commercial P&C insurance market has experienced favorable pricing conditions, particularly in specialty lines, excess casualty, and property
2. **Improved loss ratios** — AIG's underwriting transformation has focused on portfolio optimization, exiting unprofitable lines, and tightening risk selection
3. **Operational efficiency** — Cost reduction initiatives and technology investments have improved the expense ratio

### Earnings Growth Trajectory

The 51% YoY surge in adjusted EPS demonstrates that AIG is in a rapid earnings improvement phase. While the pace of growth may moderate as comparisons become more challenging, the structural improvements in underwriting suggest sustainable profitability gains.

### Strategic Focus Areas

- **Commercial Insurance:** AIG remains a major player in commercial lines globally, with growth opportunities in specialty, financial lines, and cyber insurance
- **International Expansion:** AIG's global footprint provides diversification and access to growing markets
- **Capital Management:** Continued share repurchases could provide incremental EPS growth even if top-line growth moderates

- The P&C pricing cycle may begin to soften in certain lines as competition increases
- Catastrophe losses remain unpredictable and can significantly impact quarterly results
- The separation from Corebridge Financial means AIG no longer benefits from life/retirement earnings diversification

## Risk Assessment

### Key Risks

1. **Catastrophe Exposure:** As a major P&C insurer, AIG faces significant exposure to natural catastrophe losses (hurricanes, earthquakes, wildfires). A severe catastrophe season could materially impact earnings.

2. **Pricing Cycle Risk:** The favorable P&C pricing environment that has benefited AIG's underwriting results may moderate if new capital enters the market or competition intensifies.

3. **Investment Portfolio Risk:** Insurance companies hold large investment portfolios; rising interest rates benefit investment income but can create unrealized losses on fixed-income holdings.

4. **Regulatory Risk:** AIG operates in heavily regulated markets globally, subject to capital requirements, rate approval processes, and evolving regulatory frameworks.

5. **GAAP vs. Adjusted Earnings Divergence:** The gap between GAAP net income ($1.35/share) and adjusted EPS ($1.96/share) in Q4 2025 warrants scrutiny — investors should understand the nature and sustainability of the adjustments.

6. **Macro Sensitivity:** Economic downturns can reduce insurance demand (particularly in commercial lines) and increase claims frequency in certain categories.

### Mitigating Factors

- Low beta (0.61) suggests the stock has historically been less volatile than the broader market
- Dividend yield of 2.3% provides a cushion for total returns
- Diversified geographic and product mix reduces concentration risk

## Conclusion

**Overall Rating: 6.7/10 — Moderate Buy / Hold**

AIG represents an improving value opportunity within the insurance sector. The company's Q4 2025 results demonstrate that the multi-year transformation is bearing fruit, with adjusted EPS up 51% and GI underwriting income up 48% year-over-year. At a trailing P/E of 13.1x and dividend yield of 2.3%, the stock offers reasonable value with defensive characteristics (beta of 0.61).

However, several factors temper the enthusiasm: the divergence between GAAP and adjusted earnings, limited visibility into detailed financial statements, competitive gaps versus top-tier peers, and inherent catastrophe risk in the insurance business. The stock's position at roughly the midpoint of its 52-week range ($71.25 to $88.07) reflects a market that is cautiously optimistic but not yet fully pricing in the turnaround.

**Recommendation:** AIG is suitable for value-oriented investors with moderate risk tolerance seeking exposure to the insurance sector. A position at current levels ($78.92) offers a reasonable risk/reward profile, with potential upside toward the 52-week high and beyond if the earnings improvement continues. Investors should monitor the Q1 2026 results for evidence of sustained underwriting improvement and watch for any signs of pricing cycle deterioration.
