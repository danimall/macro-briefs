## Company Overview

Hawkins Inc (NASDAQ: HWKN) is a Minneapolis-based specialty chemical company operating across three primary segments: Industrial, Water Treatment, and Health & Nutrition. The company distributes, blends, and manufactures chemicals for water treatment, food processing, pharmaceutical, and industrial applications. With approximately 20.9 million shares outstanding and a market cap of ~$2.8 billion, it occupies a niche position in the specialty chemicals distribution chain.

The company has filed annual 10-K reports consistently through SEC EDGAR, with the most recent 10-K filed May 14, 2025 (for FY ending March 2025) and the most recent 10-Q filed January 28, 2026.

## Competitive Landscape

Hawkins operates in a fragmented specialty chemical distribution market. Key competitive dynamics:

- **Regional density advantages:** Chemical distribution is logistics-intensive. Proximity to customers matters because shipping hazardous chemicals is expensive. Hawkins's Midwest concentration creates a defensible regional position.
- **Water treatment secular tailwind:** Aging US water infrastructure, tightening EPA regulations on PFAS and other contaminants, and municipal capex expansion provide long-duration demand growth for water treatment chemicals.
- **Acquisition roll-up model:** The company has historically acquired smaller regional distributors to expand geographic coverage and product breadth. This is a proven playbook in distribution but creates execution risk and potential goodwill impairment if overpaid.
- **Switching costs:** Moderate. Chemical specifications and regulatory compliance create some customer stickiness, but the underlying products are largely commodity chemicals.

The competitive moat is narrow — primarily regional scale and customer relationships rather than proprietary technology or brand differentiation. A 6/10 competitive position reflects real but limited defensibility.

**Earnings uncertainty (HIGH):** The January 2026 selloff (18% in 4 days on the Q3 10-Q filing date) signals a potential growth deceleration or margin miss. Without the actual financial data, the magnitude and permanence of any earnings deterioration cannot be assessed.
