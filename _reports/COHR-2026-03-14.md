## Executive Summary

Coherent Corp is a photonics and optical connectivity company that has become one of the primary beneficiaries of AI infrastructure buildout. The stock surged ~325% over the past year on the back of accelerating transceiver demand for hyperscale data centers, culminating in a $2B strategic investment from NVIDIA announced March 2, 2026, and S&P 500 index inclusion effective March 23, 2026. Revenue growth of approximately 25-30% in recent quarters, driven by the Networking segment (optical transceivers, coherent optics), has validated the company's positioning at the intersection of AI compute scaling and high-bandwidth interconnect requirements.

The problem is price. At ~$243 per share, COHR trades at approximately 155x trailing earnings and an estimated 922x forward P/E based on available consensus data. The stock has appreciated 13.6x from its 52-week low. Director Skaggs sold $857K on March 9, CTO Eng sold $1.19M across two transactions in early March, EVP Digirolomo sold $945K on March 6, and CFO Luther sold $871K in February. Four senior insiders disposing a combined ~$3.9M in a two-week window is a clear signal that people with the most information about the company's trajectory consider current prices a reasonable exit point.

S&P 500 inclusion will generate mechanical buying from index funds over the coming days, providing a short-term technical catalyst. But index inclusion is a one-time flow event that historically produces a price bump followed by mean reversion. The secular demand thesis for optical interconnect in AI data centers is real and supported by multiple independent data points. The valuation already reflects this thesis and then some. Current risk-reward favors patience over urgency.

## Company Overview

Coherent Corp (formerly II-VI Incorporated) is a vertically integrated photonics company headquartered in Saxonburg, Pennsylvania. The company operates across three segments: Networking (optical transceivers, coherent optics for data centers and telecom), Materials (engineered materials including silicon carbide, zinc selenide), and Lasers (industrial and scientific laser systems). The Networking segment has become the dominant growth driver as AI workloads require exponentially higher bandwidth between GPUs in data center clusters.

The company's transformation accelerated with the 2022 acquisition of Coherent (formerly Finisar), which created a combined entity with ~$5B in annual revenue and positioned it as one of three major suppliers (alongside Lumentum/LITE and Broadcom/AVGO) of 800G and next-generation 1.6T optical transceivers essential for AI training clusters. NVIDIA's $2B investment announced March 2 deepens the supply relationship and funds R&D expansion and US-based manufacturing capacity.

## Growth Analysis

**Secular demand drivers are validated by multiple independent data points:**

1. NVIDIA's $2B strategic investment (March 2, 2026) represents the strongest possible customer-level validation. NVIDIA is investing to secure supply of optical interconnect for its next-generation data center architectures, implying multi-year demand visibility.

2. AI training cluster bandwidth requirements are growing faster than compute capacity. Each GPU rack requires increasing optical interconnect density as model sizes scale. The transition from 800G to 1.6T transceivers (and eventually 3.2T) provides a multi-year product upgrade cycle.

3. Hyperscaler capex budgets (Microsoft, Google, Meta, Amazon) continue to expand, with optical networking representing a growing share of total AI infrastructure spend.

4. S&P 500 inclusion (effective March 23) creates institutional ownership broadening that can support a higher valuation floor over time.

**Growth risks:**

- Customer concentration: A significant portion of Networking revenue likely comes from 3-5 hyperscaler customers. Loss of a single design win or share shift to competitors (Lumentum, Broadcom) would have outsized impact.
- Cyclicality: Optical networking has historically been boom-bust. The 2000-2001 telecom bust and 2018-2019 inventory correction in transceivers both caused 50%+ revenue declines in optical companies. AI demand may prove more durable, but the pattern demands respect.
- Execution risk on 1.6T transition: Technology transitions create opportunities for share shifts. Coherent must execute on yield ramp and qualification timelines.

## Valuation Assessment

This is the central problem with COHR at current levels. The stock trades at multiples that require everything to go right for several years:

**At ~155x trailing earnings**, the market is pricing in earnings growth of approximately 5-7x over the next 3-4 years to bring the P/E down to a reasonable 25-30x range. This implies earnings per share growing from roughly $1.55 currently to $8-10 within 3-4 years.

**Comparable valuations:** Lumentum (LITE) trades at 176x trailing / ~1,715x forward P/E at $622 — even more expensive. Broadcom (AVGO) trades at ~29x forward. The optical pureplay peers are priced as hyper-growth companies, while the diversified semiconductor peer trades at a fraction of the multiple. The relevant question is whether COHR's growth rate justifies a 5-6x premium to AVGO.

**Historical context:** The stock traded at $57 approximately one year ago. At $243, it has appreciated 325%. For the stock to deliver a positive return from here, investors need to believe that the next 325% of fundamental improvement is still ahead — after the NVIDIA deal has been announced, after S&P 500 inclusion, after the AI data center thesis has become consensus.

**S&P 500 inclusion mechanics:** Index inclusion typically generates 2-5% of float in passive buying over a compressed timeframe. This creates a short-term price boost that frequently reverses within 30-60 days as the mechanical flow completes. The stock has already run up on the announcement; much of the inclusion premium may be priced.

## Competitive Landscape

Coherent occupies a strong position in optical transceivers for AI data centers, alongside Lumentum (LITE) and Broadcom's optical division. The NVIDIA $2B investment creates a preferential supply relationship that competitors cannot easily replicate. Vertical integration (Coherent manufactures its own indium phosphide laser chips, modulators, and package assemblies) provides cost and supply chain advantages.

The competitive moat has real substance: optical transceiver qualification cycles are 6-12 months, manufacturing yields on 800G/1.6T transceivers are hard-won, and customer switching costs are meaningful once a supplier is designed into a data center architecture. Coherent's breadth across materials, lasers, and networking also provides diversification that pure-play optical companies lack.

Risks to competitive position include: (1) Broadcom's scale and existing ASIC relationships with hyperscalers give it leverage to bundle optical solutions, (2) Chinese competitors (Innolight, Eoptolink) are aggressively pricing 800G modules and may eventually qualify at Western hyperscalers, (3) co-packaged optics (CPO) could eventually shift value from discrete transceiver modules to silicon photonics integrated on chip, potentially favoring semiconductor companies over optical specialists.

## Risk Assessment

**Valuation risk (HIGH):** At 155x trailing earnings, any deceleration in growth expectations or margin expansion will produce outsized downward moves. High-beta (1.95) compounds this — in a broad market selloff, COHR will decline approximately 2x the index.

**Macro risk (HIGH):** The world model identifies a stagflationary environment with recession probability at 38-42%. AI capex cycles have historically correlated with broader capex cycles. If hyperscalers cut or defer optical networking spending due to economic slowdown, COHR's growth trajectory breaks.

**Insider selling (MODERATE-HIGH):** Four C-suite/director sales totaling $3.9M in two weeks is a meaningful pattern. Insiders are not adding to positions at these levels. While individual sales can reflect personal financial planning, the breadth and timing (coinciding with S&P 500 inclusion announcement) suggest informed comfort with selling.

**Concentration risk (MODERATE):** Revenue concentration in a small number of hyperscaler customers. Loss of a major design win would be visible in quarterly results 2-3 quarters before the financial impact, but by then the stock would likely have corrected significantly.

**Geopolitical risk (MODERATE):** The world model identifies helium scarcity from Qatar disruption as a potential semiconductor supply constraint (20-25% probability of affecting wafer starts). Coherent's materials division uses specialized gases and chemicals that could face supply disruption.

**Integration/execution risk (MODERATE):** The II-VI/Coherent merger created significant debt. While the NVIDIA $2B helps, the company is still managing a complex integration while trying to ramp next-generation products.

The NVIDIA strategic investment, validated demand from hyperscaler capex expansion, and vertical integration create a defensible competitive position with genuine multi-year growth visibility. The secular thesis is real.

The price already reflects this. At 155x trailing earnings after a 325% one-year run, with four insiders selling a combined $3.9M in two weeks, and a 1.95 beta in a market environment characterized by confirmed stagflation and 38-42% recession probability, the risk-reward is unfavorable for new positions. **Recommendation: HOLD.** Existing holders should maintain positions given secular tailwinds but should not add at current levels. The post-S&P-inclusion period (late March through April) may provide better entry points as mechanical buying completes and the stock reverts to fundamental-driven trading.
