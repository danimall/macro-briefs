## Executive Summary

Coherent Corp occupies a structurally advantaged position in the AI infrastructure buildout as a leading supplier of optical transceivers and photonic components for hyperscale data centers. The stock has surged ~325% over the past year, driven by accelerating transceiver demand from AI training and inference clusters that require high-bandwidth optical connectivity. S&P 500 inclusion effective March 23, 2026 creates a mechanical near-term buying catalyst from index-tracking funds.

The fundamental problem is valuation. At $242.76 per share, COHR trades at 155x trailing earnings and 922x forward earnings, implying the market has priced in years of margin expansion and revenue growth that must materialize without interruption. The 1.95 beta means this stock amplifies equity market moves by nearly 2x in a macro environment where the probability of further equity correction before a tradeable bottom is 40-50%. Director Skaggs sold $856K in stock on March 9. The secular thesis is correct; the price already reflects it.

## Company Overview

Coherent Corp (formerly II-VI Incorporated) is a global leader in engineered materials, optoelectronic components, and optical systems. The company serves data communications, telecommunications, industrial lasers, aerospace & defense, and life sciences markets. Following the 2022 merger with Coherent Inc. (formerly Finisar), the combined entity became one of the largest producers of optical transceivers — the components that connect servers within AI data center clusters using light rather than electrical signals. The company is headquartered in Saxonburg, Pennsylvania.

Key product lines include 800G and 1.6T optical transceivers, vertical-cavity surface-emitting lasers (VCSELs), silicon carbide (SiC) substrates for power electronics, and industrial laser components. The AI data center networking segment has become the dominant growth driver.

## Growth Analysis

**Secular Demand Driver:** AI data center optical interconnect demand is the primary growth engine. As AI clusters scale from thousands to tens of thousands of GPUs, the bandwidth requirements between compute nodes grow exponentially. Optical transceivers operating at 800G and transitioning to 1.6T are essential components. Hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, Meta, Amazon) are all expanding AI infrastructure capital spending, directly benefiting Coherent's transceiver business.

**Product Cycle Positioning:** The transition from 800G to 1.6T transceivers represents a multi-year upgrade cycle. Coherent is among the leaders (alongside Lumentum and Broadcom) in qualifying 1.6T products with hyperscale customers. Each generation transition carries higher ASPs and initially higher margins before competition compresses pricing.

**Earnings Estimate Trajectory:** Nasdaq coverage from February 2026 notes "remarkably improving earnings outlook" with analysts raising estimates, suggesting consensus revenue and EPS expectations have been revised upward. This is consistent with the stock's 325% appreciation.

**Adjacent Markets:** Silicon carbide substrates for EVs and power electronics provide diversification, though this segment has faced cyclical weakness as EV demand moderated. Industrial laser and aerospace/defense markets add stability but are not primary growth drivers.

**S&P 500 Inclusion (March 23):** Index funds tracking the S&P 500 must purchase COHR shares by the inclusion date. This creates a quantifiable mechanical demand catalyst. Historical data suggests S&P 500 additions see 3-7% excess returns in the weeks surrounding inclusion, though much of this may already be priced given the announcement was public since early March.

## Valuation Assessment

This is where the investment case faces its most significant challenge.

**155x trailing P/E** is expensive by any historical standard for a hardware/components company. Optical networking peers like Lumentum (LITE) and Ciena (CIEN) trade at lower multiples, though COHR's AI transceiver exposure arguably justifies a premium. The question is how large that premium should be.

**922x forward P/E** is difficult to reconcile with a growth story unless forward estimates are temporarily depressed by one-time items. If this figure is accurate at face value, the stock is priced as though current earnings are irrelevant and only a distant future earnings trajectory matters — a profile more consistent with pre-revenue biotech than a $5B+ revenue hardware company.

**Price-to-Sales:** With a $45.5B market cap and estimated ~$5-6B revenue, COHR trades at roughly 7.5-9x sales. This is elevated for a components company but within the range for high-growth AI infrastructure plays.

**Comparison to AI infrastructure peers:** AVGO trades at ~25-30x forward earnings with a far more diversified revenue base. If COHR's normalized forward earnings power is $2-3 per share (implying forward P/E of 80-120x at current prices), the valuation would still be rich but within the range of high-growth AI hardware names. The 922x forward figure suggests either data quality issues or genuinely concerning earnings weakness ahead.

**Margin of Safety:** Effectively zero. At $242.76, any execution stumble, demand slowdown, or macro-driven multiple compression would result in significant downside. The 52-week low of $45.58 demonstrates how violently this stock can reprice.

## Competitive Landscape

Coherent operates in a competitive but structurally favorable market:

**Direct Competitors in Optical Transceivers:**
- **Lumentum (LITE):** Also added to S&P 500 alongside COHR; primary peer in optical transceivers and photonic components
- **Broadcom (AVGO):** Larger, more diversified; competes in optical components as part of a broader semiconductor portfolio
- **InnoLight Technology:** Chinese competitor gaining share in 800G transceivers; represents the primary competitive threat on pricing

**Competitive Advantages:**
- Vertical integration from materials (substrates, wafers) through packaged components gives cost and supply chain advantages
- Long-standing customer relationships with hyperscalers
- Broadest photonics portfolio in the industry (transceivers, VCSELs, lasers, SiC)
- Scale from the Coherent/Finisar merger

**Competitive Risks:**
- Chinese competitors (InnoLight) are aggressively pricing 800G transceivers, compressing margins
- Technology transitions (from pluggable to co-packaged optics) could disrupt current product architectures
- Customer concentration: hyperscale customers have significant bargaining power

## Risk Assessment

**Valuation Risk (High):** At 155x trailing earnings, any deceleration in AI capex spending triggers severe multiple compression. The stock fell from $300 to $242 (19%) in recent weeks, demonstrating this sensitivity.

**Beta/Macro Risk (High):** Beta of 1.95 in a stagflationary environment with rising recession probability (35-40%) and potential further equity correction (40-50% probability per world model) creates substantial drawdown exposure. A 15% broad market decline would imply ~30% COHR drawdown.

**Supply Chain Risk (Moderate-High):** Per the world model, helium scarcity from Qatar LNG disruption affects semiconductor manufacturing, and petrochemical cost pressures affect substrate/wafer production. Coherent's SiC and optical component manufacturing could face input cost increases. The sulphur disruption channel adds further risk to semiconductor-adjacent supply chains.

**Insider Selling:** Director Skaggs sold $856K in stock on March 9, 2026. Multiple Form 4 filings throughout February and March 2026 suggest additional insider selling activity, though specific details on other transactions are unavailable. This is a mild negative signal — insiders sell for many reasons, but selling into a parabolic advance is noteworthy.

**Customer Concentration:** Hyperscale customers (likely top 5 = 40-60% of revenue) create revenue concentration risk. Any single customer's AI capex pause would be material.

**Integration Risk (Declining):** The Coherent/Finisar merger is now ~4 years old, and integration execution has generally been viewed positively. Remaining debt from the acquisition continues to weigh on the balance sheet, though deleveraging progress has been a positive catalyst.

**Cyclicality:** Optical components have historically been deeply cyclical (2001 telecom bust, 2019 trade war downturn). AI demand provides a secular overlay, but the industry's cyclical character has not been eliminated.

## Conclusion

Coherent Corp has the right product at the right time. Optical transceivers for AI data centers represent a genuine, multi-year secular growth opportunity, and COHR's vertical integration and scale position it as a primary beneficiary. The S&P 500 inclusion on March 23 adds a near-term mechanical catalyst.

The valuation makes a BUY recommendation untenable. At 155x trailing earnings, the stock prices in flawless execution across multiple years of accelerating growth. The 922x forward P/E figure, if accurate, raises additional concerns about near-term earnings trajectory. A 1.95 beta in a macro environment characterized by $100 oil, rising recession probability, and potential further equity correction creates asymmetric downside risk.

For investors with existing positions, holding through the S&P 500 inclusion makes sense as a tactical decision. For new capital, the risk/reward profile requires a lower entry point. A pullback to the $180-200 range (roughly 25-30% lower) would reduce the trailing P/E to ~100-110x and provide a more defensible entry point, particularly if accompanied by continued earnings estimate revisions upward. The stock remains on the optical interconnects BUY watchlist from the world model but requires price discipline at these levels.

**Recommendation: HOLD** — Strong secular thesis, insufficient valuation support for new money at $242.76.
