## Executive Summary

Acuity Inc. (formerly Acuity Brands) is a North American commercial and industrial lighting and building management solutions provider trading at $260.83, down 31% from its 52-week high of $380.17. The company beat Q1 FY2026 earnings expectations ($4.69 reported vs. $4.52 consensus), and the board authorized a 17% dividend increase to $0.20/quarter in January 2026, signaling management confidence in cash generation. An institutional buyer (140 Summer Partners) initiated a position in early March.

The core tension: the stock has re-rated meaningfully lower from peak levels despite solid fundamental execution. The decline correlates with the broader market selloff driven by stagflation confirmation (Q4 GDP 0.7%, core PCE 3.1%, oil >$100) and Middle East geopolitical disruption. At ~20x forward P/E, AYI is reasonably valued for a mid-cap industrial with moderate growth, but 1.45 beta means continued macro deterioration will disproportionately hit this name. CFO Karen Holcom sold ~$1.5M in stock on January 28 at ~$309 — 19% above the current price — and SVP/GC Barry Goldman sold aggressively at $362-370 in October 2025. These sales occurred at materially higher prices and represent a pattern of insider distribution.

The absence of detailed financial statements in the dataset constrains the depth of this analysis. Recommendation is HOLD pending Q2 FY2026 results (April 2, 2026) and improved macro visibility.

## Company Overview

Acuity Inc. (ticker AYI) specializes in intelligent lighting and building management solutions, serving commercial and industrial clients primarily across North America. The company has been transitioning from a traditional lighting manufacturer toward a technology-enabled building solutions platform, incorporating IoT capabilities and space management software alongside its core LED lighting products.

Market capitalization stands at approximately $8.0 billion. The company operates on a fiscal year ending August 31, with the most recent quarterly report (Q1 FY2026) filed January 8, 2026.

## Growth Analysis

Acuity's growth vectors include: (1) the ongoing renovation and retrofit cycle in commercial lighting (LED conversion still incomplete in older building stock), (2) building management and IoT software recurring revenue attached to lighting infrastructure, and (3) data-center and industrial construction demand driven by AI infrastructure buildout.

Zacks categorized AYI as a "strong growth stock" in two separate articles during February-March 2026. The Q1 FY2026 earnings beat provides one data point of above-consensus execution.

**Constraints on growth:** Commercial construction activity is sensitive to credit conditions and GDP growth. With Q4 2025 GDP at 0.7% and recession probability at 38-42% for Q1-Q2 2026 per my macro framework, the near-term demand environment for commercial lighting products is at risk of softening. Higher oil prices ($98-108 central range) increase input costs for plastics, metals, and transportation, compressing margins unless passed through via pricing.

Growth score is moderate: the company is executing well operationally but faces significant cyclical headwinds.

## Competitive Landscape

Acuity competes with Signify (formerly Philips Lighting), Hubbell, Eaton's lighting division, and smaller regional players. Its competitive advantages include: (1) scale in North American commercial/industrial lighting, (2) a growing portfolio of connected building solutions and IoT capabilities, and (3) long-standing relationships with electrical distributors and contractors.

The shift toward intelligent building management creates potential for recurring software revenue and higher switching costs versus commodity LED fixtures. The degree to which AYI has successfully transitioned to this model will be better evaluated when Q2 FY2026 results are released April 2.

## Risk Assessment

**Macro sensitivity (HIGH):** Beta of 1.45 means AYI amplifies market moves by roughly 45%. In the current stagflationary environment with recession probability at 38-42%, this is a material headwind. The March 3 news article explicitly noted AYI fell 4.4% on geopolitical tension/oil price fears.

**Input cost inflation:** Rising oil prices increase costs for raw materials (metals, plastics) and transportation. Acuity's ability to pass through these costs depends on demand elasticity in commercial lighting — in a weakening economy, pass-through pricing power diminishes.

**Commercial construction cycle:** New commercial construction starts and renovation activity are leading indicators for AYI demand. Credit tightening (credit cascade probability 55-60%) and rising borrowing costs reduce new project feasibility, particularly for speculative office and retail construction.

**Insider selling:** The CFO sold $1.5M of stock at ~$309 on January 28 (the same day as the earnings release window). The General Counsel sold ~$1.6M across multiple transactions at $362-370 in October 2025. Both sales are at prices well above current levels and represent meaningful personal wealth extraction. While these may be pre-planned 10b5-1 dispositions, the pattern of two senior executives selling significant blocks within three months warrants attention. The CFO retains 21,523 shares (~$5.6M at current prices), so her remaining stake provides alignment, but the signal is net-negative.

**Tariff exposure:** Section 301 probes targeting 60 economies create uncertainty for companies sourcing components internationally. Acuity's North American manufacturing base may partially insulate it, but supply chains for electronic components (drivers, sensors, microcontrollers) likely have Asian exposure.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- Q1 FY2026 earnings beat and 17% dividend increase indicate improving operational performance and management confidence in forward cash generation.
- Stock is 31% below 52-week highs, providing a more attractive entry than recent months.
- Long-term secular tailwinds from LED conversion, building automation, and energy efficiency mandates remain intact.
- IoT/building management software layer creates potential for recurring revenue and improved margin mix over time.
- Institutional buying (140 Summer Partners) suggests value-oriented money is entering at these levels.

### Bear Case
- 1.45 beta in a stagflationary macro with recession probability 38-42% means the stock is likely to underperform defensive alternatives if the economy weakens further.
- Commercial construction spending is one of the most rate-sensitive and GDP-sensitive categories; a 6.25%+ mortgage/commercial lending rate environment suppresses new project starts.
- Two senior insiders (CFO and GC) sold meaningful stock in the last five months at prices 16-29% above current levels.
- Oil above $100 compresses margins through input costs and transportation expenses.
- The company's transition from commodity lighting to technology-enabled building solutions is ongoing; the pace and margin contribution of this shift remain unclear without detailed segment data.

## Conclusion

AYI is a well-run mid-cap industrial that beat earnings, raised its dividend, and attracted institutional buying. At ~20x forward earnings and 31% off its high, the stock is not expensive on an absolute basis. However, the macro environment is hostile to cyclical industrials with above-market beta: rising oil prices, confirmed stagflation across six dimensions, and 38-42% recession probability all argue against adding cyclical exposure here. The insider selling pattern adds caution.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The Q2 FY2026 earnings release on April 2 will provide critical data on whether commercial demand is holding up or deteriorating. Investors should wait for that report and improved macro clarity before initiating or adding. A pullback toward the $220-230 range (closer to the 52-week low and ~17x forward earnings) would present a more attractive risk-reward entry. Conversely, a rebound above $310 with confirmed demand resilience would justify re-evaluation toward BUY.

The lack of detailed financial statements in the available data prevents higher-conviction scoring. Key unknowns include: leverage levels, free cash flow trends, segment revenue mix between legacy lighting and technology solutions, and gross margin trajectory under current input cost pressures.
