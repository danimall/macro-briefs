## Executive Summary

IAC Inc is a holding company trading at $38.19 with a market cap of ~$2.7B, negative trailing EPS of -$1.49, and a forward P/E of -25.6x. The company is in active portfolio rationalization mode, having announced a $320M divestiture of Care.com to Pacific Avenue Capital Partners on March 2, 2026. This transaction, combined with a large open-market purchase of ~1.1M shares by the company itself in December 2025 (~$39.9M) and Aristeia Capital's ~$100M stake build, suggests insiders and sophisticated investors see value in the remaining portfolio. However, the complete absence of financial statement data in our dataset, persistent negative earnings, and a hedge fund liquidation (Incline Global sold its entire $15.4M position) create an unresolvable information asymmetry that prevents a BUY recommendation. In the current stagflationary macro regime with oil at $119 and the Fed discussing rate hikes, IAC's consumer-facing internet businesses face headwinds from weakening advertising spend and consumer discretionary pullback.

## Company Overview

IAC is a diversified holding company with a history of building and spinning off internet businesses. Its most notable prior spin-offs include Match Group (MTCH), Vimeo, and Angi. The current portfolio includes Dotdash Meredith (digital and print media), a significant stake in MGM Resorts, and various smaller internet properties. IAC's corporate strategy centers on acquiring, operating, and eventually spinning off or divesting digital businesses once they reach scale.

The company has ~71.1M shares outstanding and trades on NASDAQ. No sector or industry classification was provided in the data, but IAC functions as an internet-focused conglomerate/holding company.

## Financial Analysis

**Critical data gap:** The financial statements (income statement, balance sheet, cash flow) returned empty for both annual and quarterly periods. This severely limits quantitative analysis. What follows is based on the available profile data and observable transactions.

**Profitability:** Trailing EPS of -$1.49 and forward P/E of -25.6x indicate the company is operating at a loss. On ~71.1M shares, this implies trailing net losses of approximately $106M. There is no dividend.

**Market Cap vs. Transaction Value:** The $320M Care.com sale represents roughly 12% of IAC's current $2.7B market cap. This is meaningful value extraction from what IAC characterized as a "non-core" asset. The key question is where those proceeds go — debt reduction, buybacks, or reinvestment.

**Share Repurchase Activity:** IAC Inc. (listed as 10% owner) purchased 1,098,748 shares at $36.30/share on December 5, 2025, totaling $39.9M. This is a material signal from the entity itself, representing roughly 1.5% of shares outstanding.

**Insider Compensation:** Recent insider transactions are dominated by RSU conversions and tax withholdings — routine compensation events rather than discretionary buying or selling. CFO/COO Halpin had significant RSU conversions (148,508 shares total across multiple tranches in February 2026), with tax withholdings at $35.65/share. No insiders made open-market purchases at current levels, and no insiders sold shares beyond tax withholding obligations.

## Growth Analysis

Without financial statements, growth assessment relies on qualitative signals:

**Portfolio simplification:** The Care.com divestiture continues a pattern of shedding non-core assets to focus capital. Whether this drives growth or merely delays value destruction depends on reinvestment quality.

**Dotdash Meredith integration risk:** The 2021 acquisition of Meredith for ~$2.7B was IAC's largest bet. Digital media faces secular headwinds from AI-generated content and shifting advertising patterns. Google's algorithm changes have historically impacted Dotdash's traffic-dependent model.

**MGM stake:** IAC holds a significant stake in MGM Resorts. MGM's value fluctuates with Las Vegas/Macau gaming volumes and broader consumer discretionary spending, which is contracting in the current environment (multiple retailers citing macro headwinds, NFP at -92K, gasoline +21%).

**Forward P/E of -25.6x** confirms consensus expects continued losses, providing no visibility on an earnings inflection.

**Sum-of-parts framework** is the appropriate valuation method for IAC, but without balance sheet data (total debt, cash positions, investment portfolio mark-to-market), a rigorous SOTP is impossible from available data.

Observable valuation anchors:

Higher rates exacerbate this discount because the optionality embedded in IAC's portfolio has a longer duration.

## Competitive Landscape

IAC operates across multiple segments, each with different competitive dynamics:

**Digital Media (Dotdash Meredith):** Competes with Condé Nast, BuzzFeed, Hearst, and increasingly AI-generated content. Advertising market is cyclically weak and structurally challenged by AI summarization reducing click-through traffic.

**Online Services:** Various properties compete in fragmented internet services markets.

**Holding Company Peers:** Compared to other internet-focused conglomerates, IAC's track record of spin-offs (Match, Vimeo) is distinctive. Barry Diller's involvement provides operational credibility. However, the Vimeo spin-off has underperformed significantly, and Match Group shares have lost substantial value since the spin.

IAC's competitive advantage resides primarily in its management team's dealmaking expertise and willingness to pursue unconventional transactions. This is difficult to quantify and depends heavily on execution.

## Risk Assessment

**1. Information Opacity (HIGH):** The absence of financial statement data in this analysis mirrors a broader challenge with IAC — the holding company structure makes consolidated financials less informative than segment-level economics, which require deep 10-K analysis.

**2. Macro Sensitivity (HIGH):** IAC's portfolio is heavily exposed to advertising spend (Dotdash Meredith) and consumer discretionary (MGM stake). In a stagflationary environment with recession probability at 40-45%, both revenue streams face pressure. Oil at $119 compresses consumer spending, reducing both advertising budgets and gaming/leisure expenditure.

**3. Concentration Risk (MODERATE):** Dotdash Meredith likely represents the largest single component of IAC's remaining portfolio after Care.com divestiture. Digital media faces structural headwinds from AI content generation.

**4. Holding Company Discount (MODERATE):** Conglomerate discounts tend to widen during periods of market stress and rising rates. IAC's discount could expand from typical 15-25% to 25-35% if macro conditions deteriorate further.

**5. Hedge Fund Positioning (MIXED):** Incline Global's complete liquidation of its $15.4M stake is bearish. Aristeia Capital's ~$100M stake build is bullish. Net signal is ambiguous.

**6. Key Person Risk (MODERATE):** Barry Diller's involvement is central to IAC's strategic identity. Succession planning for the 84-year-old chairman remains a long-term concern.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- Care.com divestiture at $320M demonstrates ability to monetize assets above perceived value, with proceeds available for buybacks or debt reduction
- Aristeia Capital's ~$100M stake build represents informed money seeing a sum-of-parts discount
- Company's own $39.9M open-market purchase at $36.30 signals management's view of undervaluation
- Dotdash Meredith integration could generate substantial value if digital transformation succeeds
- Historical track record of creating shareholder value through spin-offs (Match Group from ~$0 to $50B+ peak market cap)
- Trading 24% below 52-week high provides potential entry point if macro stabilizes

### Bear Case
- Negative EPS of -$1.49 with no visible path to profitability
- Stagflationary macro environment compresses advertising revenue and consumer spending, both critical to IAC's portfolio
- AI content generation structurally threatens Dotdash Meredith's traffic-dependent business model
- Rate hike discussion widens holding company discount rates
- MGM stake value exposed to consumer discretionary downturn
- Incline Global's complete liquidation suggests institutional skepticism
- Vimeo and post-spin Match Group underperformance challenges the narrative that IAC's spin-off machine reliably creates value

## Conclusion

IAC presents a classic holding company puzzle: potential sum-of-parts value that may or may not be realizable, wrapped in a negative-earnings entity during a hostile macro environment. The Care.com divestiture and company share repurchase are constructive signals, but they are insufficient to overcome the combination of (1) negative earnings, (2) absent financial statement detail, (3) stagflationary headwinds to advertising and consumer discretionary businesses, and (4) widening holding company discounts in a rising-rate environment.

The stock is appropriately priced at $38.19 for the uncertainty it carries. Without visibility into consolidated financials, segment-level profitability, and debt structure, a directional call requires more information than is available. Investors considering IAC should wait for either (a) a clear earnings inflection in Dotdash Meredith, (b) announcement of a value-crystallizing transaction (spin-off, additional divestiture), or (c) a pullback to the $29-32 range where the downside/upside ratio improves materially.

**Recommendation: HOLD**
