## Executive Summary

Annaly Capital Management is the largest publicly traded mortgage REIT by market cap (~$15.8B), primarily investing in agency mortgage-backed securities (MBS) funded through short-term repo financing. Q4 CY2025 was strong: revenue of $921.8M (+74.8% YoY), GAAP EPS of $1.40 (66.7% above consensus), and adjusted EPS of $0.74 (beating the $0.72 estimate). The stock trades at $21.86 with a forward P/E of ~7.8x and a dividend yield of ~9.4%.

The fundamental problem is that NLY's business model — borrowing short to lend long with 6-8x leverage — is maximally exposed to the current macro regime. Stagflation (0.7% GDP, 3.1% core PCE, -92K NFP) creates a particularly toxic environment: the Fed cannot cut rates due to inflation, meaning funding costs stay elevated, while economic deterioration increases prepayment uncertainty and spread volatility. Oil at $98-108 with ceasefire rejected adds persistent inflationary pressure that delays any rate normalization.

Three members of the C-suite (CEO Finkelstein, COO Campbell, CFO Wolfe) all executed open-market sales in February 2026 — $50K shares by the CEO at $23.13, $28K shares by the COO at $23.12, and $16.5K shares by the CFO at $23.14 — on top of routine grant vesting and tax withholding. This coordinated selling at prices only ~5% above current levels reduces confidence in management's near-term outlook.

## Company Overview

Annaly operates through three investment segments: **Agency** (agency RMBS backed by GSE guarantees, the core portfolio), **Residential Credit** (non-agency residential mortgage assets), and **Mortgage Servicing Rights** (MSRs). The agency portfolio generates net interest income from the spread between MBS yields and repo funding costs. MSRs provide a natural hedge because their value rises with interest rates, partially offsetting duration risk in the agency book.

The company had ~$90B+ in total assets as of recent filings (detailed balance sheet data unavailable in the provided dataset). As a REIT, Annaly distributes substantially all taxable income as dividends.

## Financial Analysis

**Q4 CY2025 Results:**
- Revenue: $921.8M, +74.8% YoY — the surge reflects wider net interest margins as the yield curve steepened and portfolio repositioning in prior quarters.
- GAAP EPS: $1.40, beating consensus by 66.7%. This includes mark-to-market gains on the portfolio.
- Adjusted EPS: $0.74, beating the $0.72 consensus by $0.02. This is the more relevant metric, excluding unrealized gains.
- Trailing P/E: 7.8x based on the $21.86 share price.

**Dividend:** The ~9.4% yield implies an annualized dividend of approximately $2.06/share. At $0.74 adjusted quarterly earnings, annualized earnings power is ~$2.96, giving a payout ratio around 70%. This provides moderate coverage, but the payout ratio is sensitive to NIM compression. Historically, Annaly has cut dividends during adverse rate environments — the dividend was reduced multiple times between 2020-2023.

**Balance Sheet:** Detailed data is unavailable, but mortgage REITs of Annaly's scale typically operate at 6-8x leverage on equity. With a $15.8B market cap, the implied equity base (including preferred) is roughly $13-15B, suggesting a total asset portfolio of $80-100B+. The company finances primarily through overnight and short-term repo, creating significant refinancing risk.

**Preferred Stock:** Annaly has multiple preferred series outstanding (Series F at 6.95% referenced in recent filings). These preferred dividends reduce common equity earnings and represent fixed obligations senior to common distributions.

## Growth Analysis

Growth for mortgage REITs is a function of three variables: (1) net interest margin (NIM) between asset yields and funding costs, (2) portfolio size (leverage × equity), and (3) book value trajectory.

The 74.8% YoY revenue growth in Q4 reflects NIM expansion as the yield curve normalized from its deeply inverted state in 2023-2024. This growth rate is non-recurring — it represents a one-time regime shift from inverted to positively-sloped curve. Forward growth depends on:

- **Yield curve shape:** Currently modestly positive, which supports NIM. But FOMC ambiguity (March 18-19 meeting) means the front end of the curve is uncertain. If the Fed holds at current rates while the long end drifts lower on recession fears, the curve could flatten again, compressing NIM.
- **Portfolio deployment:** With agency MBS spreads currently ~140-160bps above Treasuries (wider than historical averages), there is opportunity to deploy capital at attractive spreads. However, spread widening often signals rising risk, not opportunity.
- **Book value:** Mortgage REIT "growth" is largely about preserving book value per share. Annaly's book value has declined persistently over the past decade through a combination of realized losses, dilution, and dividend distributions exceeding total return. A stock that traded at $17+ per share in 2013 (pre-reverse split adjusted) has seen chronic book value erosion.

Growth prospects score: 5.0. The recent earnings beat reflects favorable conditions that are unlikely to persist in the current macro environment.

## Valuation Assessment

At $21.86 with a forward P/E of ~7.8x and a dividend yield of ~9.4%, NLY appears cheap on standard metrics. However, mortgage REIT valuation requires context:

**Price-to-Book:** The critical metric for mREITs. Without exact book value data, we can estimate: if Annaly's equity is ~$13-14B across ~725M shares outstanding (implied by $15.8B market cap / $21.86 price), book value per share is approximately $18-19. This would place NLY at ~1.15x book value — a premium to book, which suggests the market is pricing in some forward earnings power. Historically, NLY trades between 0.85-1.15x book.

**Dividend Yield vs. Peers:** AGNC Investment yields ~12-13%, making NLY's ~9.4% yield less compelling on a pure income basis. However, NLY's lower yield partly reflects lower leverage and better dividend coverage.

**BTIG's $25 Price Target:** BTIG upgraded NLY to Buy in January with a $25 target, representing ~14% upside. This target appears achievable only in a scenario where the yield curve steepens further and credit spreads tighten — conditions inconsistent with the current stagflationary macro.

Valuation score: 6.5. Cheap on earnings, reasonable on price-to-book, but the low multiple is justified by the risk profile and cyclical nature of earnings.

## Competitive Landscape

Annaly is the largest agency-focused mortgage REIT, which provides several advantages:
- **Scale in repo financing:** Larger balance sheets get marginally better repo terms from counterparties.
- **Hedging sophistication:** The company's hedging desk and MSR portfolio provide tools to manage duration risk that smaller competitors lack.
- **Diversification across Agency, Residential Credit, and MSR segments:** Most competitors focus narrowly on agency MBS.

Primary competitors include AGNC Investment ($8B market cap), Starwood Property Trust, and Two Harbors Investment. NLY's scale advantage is real but diminishing — agency MBS is a commoditized asset class where alpha is primarily generated through leverage management and hedging, not security selection.

Competitive position score: 6.5. Scale leader in a commoditized sector with meaningful but limited structural advantages.

## Risk Assessment

**Interest Rate Risk (HIGH):** NLY's earnings are a direct function of the spread between long-term MBS yields and short-term repo costs. The current FOMC information matrix is maximally uncertain: 3.1% core PCE argues against cuts, 0.7% GDP argues for them. Any surprise in either direction moves NLY's portfolio value and NIM simultaneously. With the March 18-19 FOMC meeting days away, near-term volatility is elevated.

**Leverage Risk (HIGH):** At 6-8x leverage, a 2% adverse move in portfolio value wipes out 12-16% of equity. Agency MBS carry no credit risk (GSE-guaranteed), but they carry significant duration and convexity risk. In the 2022-2023 rate hiking cycle, Annaly's book value declined substantially.

**Macro Regime Risk (ELEVATED):** Stagflation is among the worst environments for levered mortgage REITs. Rising inflation prevents rate cuts that would benefit the portfolio, while weakening economic growth doesn't help either — agency MBS spreads don't tighten in recessions the way they do when the Fed eases. The 38-42% recession probability and 20-25% probability of no cuts in 2026 both weigh against NLY.

**Insider Selling (MODERATE NEGATIVE):** CEO Finkelstein sold 50,000 shares at $23.13 on Feb 18, and another 50,000 at $21.16 in November 2025. COO Campbell sold 26,491 shares at $22.83 on Feb 25 and 28,225 at $23.12 on Feb 18. CFO Wolfe sold 16,536 shares at $23.14 on Feb 18. All three C-suite members selling within the same window, beyond tax withholding, is a concrete negative signal. Yahoo Finance UK specifically flagged this as potentially recasting management confidence in the mortgage strategy.

**Oil / Inflation Risk:** Oil at $98-108 (central range) with ceasefire rejected feeds directly into CPI, delaying any Fed pivot. Each month of delayed easing costs NLY ~$0.01-0.02/share in elevated funding costs, which cumulates meaningfully over quarters.

**Credit Cascade Risk (INDIRECT):** If the 55-60% probability credit cascade materializes (Ares/KKR redemption restrictions, Amazon crowding out), repo funding markets could tighten as counterparties reduce exposure to levered entities. This is a fat tail risk that could force portfolio deleveraging at unfavorable prices.

Risk factors score: 4.0. Multiple independent risk channels are simultaneously elevated.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- Q4 results demonstrate strong earnings power in the current rate environment; adjusted EPS of $0.74/quarter annualizes to ~$2.96, well covering the ~$2.06 dividend.
- If the yield curve steepens further (Fed eventually cuts while long rates hold), NIM expands and book value appreciates. BTIG's $25 target implies ~14% price upside plus the 9.4% yield.
- MSR portfolio provides a natural hedge against rising rates, partially insulating the company from adverse rate moves.
- At ~1.15x book value, the stock is not expensive for a well-managed mREIT with a diversified portfolio.
- Largest mREIT with institutional-quality management and scale advantages in repo and hedging.

### Bear Case
- Stagflation is the worst macro regime for levered mortgage REITs: inflation keeps funding costs high, economic weakness creates spread volatility, and FOMC policy uncertainty prevents clear positioning.
- Three C-suite members selling in the same February 2026 window — beyond tax withholding — suggests management is taking chips off the table near the recent highs ($23+, near the 52-week high of $24.52).
- Annaly's long-term total return track record is poor. The stock has destroyed book value per share over most 5-10 year holding periods. The high dividend yield compensates for this, but total returns after dividend reinvestment have historically underperformed simpler fixed income alternatives.
- A credit cascade (55-60% probability per our macro assessment) could disrupt repo markets. Annaly's overnight repo dependency makes it a forced seller in any funding market stress event.
- No cuts in 2026 (20-25% probability) would mean funding costs remain elevated for the entire year, compressing NIM and potentially threatening dividend coverage.
- Oil at $100+ for sustained periods pushes CPI toward 3.5-4.5%, making rate cuts even less likely, extending the period of elevated short-term rates.

## Conclusion

NLY delivered strong Q4 results and trades at a reasonable valuation for its asset class. The ~9.4% dividend yield is well-covered at current earnings run rates. However, the company sits at the intersection of multiple macro risk factors that are all trending adversely: stagflationary data, FOMC paralysis, oil-driven inflation persistence, and potential credit market stress. Coordinated C-suite selling in February 2026 adds a concrete microeconomic negative signal.

The stock is priced for a continuation of favorable conditions (stable/steepening curve, accessible repo, no credit stress). The current macro regime makes that continuation uncertain at best. For investors who already own NLY, the earnings coverage provides some protection and the dividend provides reason to hold. For new capital, the risk-reward is unattractive given the macro headwinds. The 7.8x P/E is not cheap enough to compensate for the possibility of dividend cuts, book value deterioration, or repo market disruption in a stagflationary recession.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** Existing positions should be maintained for income but not added to. The stock becomes more interesting on a pullback to 0.90-0.95x book value (roughly $17-18), or if the macro regime shifts meaningfully toward rate normalization. Neither condition is currently met.
