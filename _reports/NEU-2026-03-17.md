## Executive Summary

Newmarket Corporation reported FY2025 net income of $419M ($44.44 EPS) on the back of $520M petroleum additives operating profit and $47M specialty materials operating profit. The stock dropped ~15% on February 12, 2026, following a Q4 earnings miss — Q4 net income of $81.3M ($8.65/share) represented a sharp sequential decline from the implied ~$12/share quarterly run-rate in the first three quarters. At the current price of ~$606-621, NEU trades at approximately 13.6x trailing earnings with a 2.05% dividend yield ($12.00/year).

The company occupies a defensible niche as one of only four global manufacturers of petroleum lubricant additives (through subsidiary Afton Chemical), but the business faces structural headwinds: low single-digit volume growth, commodity input sensitivity, and long-term EV adoption risk. The 30% decline from the October 2025 high of $876 has compressed the valuation to historically reasonable levels, though the Q4 weakness and bearish commentary from value investors (per the March 2026 Insider Monkey bear case article) suggest fundamental deterioration may not yet be fully priced in.

Given limited growth prospects, macro sensitivity to the current stagflationary environment (oil at $98-108, diesel at $5/gallon affecting both input costs and downstream demand patterns), and the absence of analyst coverage to provide forward estimates, this is a HOLD. The stock merits watching for a more attractive entry point in the $520-560 range, where the yield and earnings multiple would provide adequate margin of safety.

## Company Overview

Newmarket Corporation is a Richmond, Virginia-based holding company operating primarily through its subsidiary Afton Chemical Corporation, one of the world's largest producers of petroleum additives — specialty chemicals added to lubricants, fuels, and other petroleum products to enhance performance. The company also operates a smaller Specialty Materials segment, which contributed $47M in operating profit in FY2025.

Key characteristics:
- **Market cap:** ~$5.8B
- **Shares outstanding:** ~9.4M (micro-float by large-cap standards)
- **Founder-family controlled:** CEO Thomas E. Gottwald holds ~118K+ shares personally plus family trusts totaling over 360K shares (Bruce C. Gottwald). The Gottwald family effectively controls the company.
- **Exchange:** NYSE
- **Last earnings:** February 11, 2026 (Q4/FY2025)

The petroleum additives market is an oligopoly. Newmarket (Afton Chemical), Lubrizol (Berkshire Hathaway), Infineum (ExxonMobil/Shell JV), and Chevron Oronite account for the vast majority of global supply. This structure provides pricing power but also limits addressable market expansion.

## Growth Analysis

Newmarket's growth profile is constrained by the nature of its end markets:

1. **Volume growth** in petroleum additives tracks global lubricant demand, which grows at ~1-2% annually in developed markets and slightly faster in emerging markets. The company does not disclose detailed volume data publicly.

2. **Pricing power** exists due to oligopoly structure but is modulated by raw material costs (base oils, petrochemical feedstocks) that fluctuate with oil prices. In the current $98-108 oil environment, input costs are elevated, and the Q4 weakness suggests Newmarket may have been unable to fully pass through cost increases.

3. **Specialty Materials** ($47M operating profit) is growing from a small base but remains a minor contributor. No disclosed expansion plans in the available data.

4. **EV transition risk** is a long-term structural headwind. Battery EVs require no engine lubricant additives, though transmission fluids and thermal management fluids partially offset. The timeline is measured in decades, not years, but the direction is unambiguous.

5. **No R&D investment data** is available in the provided financials. Historical 10-K filings show Newmarket spends on formulation R&D (new additive packages for next-generation engine specifications), which is necessary to maintain OEM approvals.

Growth prospects score at 5.0: stable but unexciting, with low single-digit organic growth offset by long-term secular headwinds.

## Competitive Landscape

Newmarket's competitive position is its strongest attribute:

1. **Oligopoly structure:** Four producers control the global petroleum additives market. Barriers to entry are extremely high — years of formulation testing, OEM approval processes, and capital-intensive blending infrastructure.

2. **Customer switching costs:** OEMs and lubricant blenders qualify additive packages over multi-year testing cycles. Switching suppliers requires re-qualification, which is costly and time-consuming. This creates annuity-like revenue streams.

3. **Competitive dynamics:** Lubrizol (Berkshire subsidiary) is the largest player. Infineum (Exxon/Shell JV) is #2. Newmarket/Afton Chemical is #3 globally. Chevron Oronite rounds out the oligopoly. Price discipline is generally maintained, though periodic competitive pressures emerge.

4. **Geographic diversification:** Afton Chemical operates globally, with significant revenue from North America, Europe, and Asia-Pacific. This provides some buffer against regional economic weakness.

## Risk Assessment

### Near-Term Risks (Elevated)

1. **Stagflation passthrough:** Oil at $98-108 raises base oil and petrochemical feedstock costs. If Newmarket cannot pass these through with a lag (evidence from Q4 suggests difficulty), margins compress. Diesel at $5/gallon also raises freight costs for distribution.

2. **Demand sensitivity:** In a recessionary environment (38-42% probability per macro model), industrial lubricant demand declines as manufacturing activity slows. Automotive lubricant demand is more resilient (vehicles still need oil changes) but not immune.

3. **Small float volatility:** With only 9.4M shares outstanding, NEU is susceptible to outsized price moves on relatively small volume. The October 9, 2025 one-day drop from $873 to $779 (10.8%) and the February 12, 2026 drop from $697 to $597 (14.4%) illustrate this.

4. **Bear case circulating:** The March 2026 Insider Monkey article references a bearish thesis on Value Investors Club. When sophisticated value investors publish short theses on previously underfollowed names, it can create sustained selling pressure.

### Long-Term Risks

5. **EV transition:** Structural, slow-moving, but directionally negative for core petroleum additives demand over 15-20 years.

6. **Concentration:** The company is essentially a single-product business (petroleum additives = ~92% of operating profit). Specialty Materials provides some diversification but is too small to change the profile.

7. **Family control:** The Gottwald family's large ownership provides alignment but also governance risk — limited external oversight, related-party transaction risk, and succession uncertainty.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case

- **Oligopoly pricing power reasserts:** Q4 weakness represents a temporary lag in cost passthrough. FY2026 pricing actions restore margins, and EPS recovers to $48-50+. At 14-15x, the stock reaches $670-750.
- **Buyback support:** The tiny float means even modest buyback activity provides meaningful EPS accretion and price support.
- **Defensive characteristics:** Lubricant additives are consumables with recurring demand. Even in recession, vehicles and industrial equipment require lubrication. Revenue drawdowns are typically 10-15%, not catastrophic.
- **Dividend provides 2% carry while waiting.**

### Bear Case

- **Q4 was a leading indicator, not an anomaly.** Elevated oil prices persist, compressing margins. Global industrial slowdown reduces volume. FY2026 EPS drops to $38-42, and the stock de-rates to 12x → $456-504.
- **EV transition accelerates.** Regulatory mandates and falling battery costs pull forward the decline in ICE vehicle production, reducing additive demand earlier than expected.
- **Bearish attention.** The Value Investors Club short thesis attracts additional short interest on a stock with limited natural buying constituency (no index weight, minimal analyst coverage, family-controlled).
- **Macro headwinds compound.** Hormuz disruption raises feedstock costs while simultaneously reducing downstream economic activity — the classic stagflationary squeeze on specialty chemicals.

## Conclusion

Newmarket Corporation is a high-quality niche business — oligopoly positioning, capital-light model, consistent free cash flow, and disciplined capital allocation through buybacks and dividends. At 13.6x trailing earnings with a 2.05% yield, the valuation is reasonable but not compellingly cheap given the Q4 weakness and macro headwinds.

The Q4 sequential decline (EPS from ~$12 quarterly run-rate to $8.65) needs to be contextualized in future quarters. If it represents a one-quarter cost passthrough lag, the stock is attractively priced. If it represents the beginning of sustained margin pressure from $100+ oil, current levels may be a trap.

**Why not BUY:** The Q4 miss was material and unexplained in granular detail (no detailed financials available). The current macro environment — stagflation with $98-108 oil and recession probability at 38-42% — creates unfavorable conditions for specialty chemicals input cost management. A bearish thesis is circulating among sophisticated investors. The stock needs at least one quarter of recovery to confirm the bull case, and a more attractive entry point (~$520-560, representing 11-12.5x on $44 EPS) would provide adequate margin of safety.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** Monitor Q1 2026 results (expected April/May 2026) for evidence of margin recovery. Consider upgrading to BUY on a pullback to $520-560 or on evidence that cost passthrough has normalized.
