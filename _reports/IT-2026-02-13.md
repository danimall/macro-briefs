## Executive Summary

Gartner Inc (IT), the world's leading research and advisory company, has experienced a dramatic decline of approximately 47% in its stock price over the course of 2025, marking its worst annual performance in over two decades. The stock currently trades at approximately $153.63, representing a forward P/E of ~15.9x — a valuation that appears historically compressed for a company with Gartner's franchise quality. Q4 2025 revenue came in at $1.75 billion, up 2.2% year-over-year, while full-year 2025 revenue reached approximately $6.5 billion with adjusted EBITDA of roughly $1 billion-plus.

The company beat Q4 2025 EPS expectations, reporting $3.94 versus the $3.50 consensus, demonstrating continued profitability discipline even as top-line growth stalled. However, contract value (CV) growth decelerated to just 1% year-over-year in Q4, with the US federal government segment acting as a significant drag — excluding US federal, CV grew 4%. Management has outlined a 2026 revenue target of $6.455 billion and is pursuing an AI-driven transformation strategy centered on its "AskGartner" product.

While Gartner's subscription-based business model, high contribution margins (~67%), and dominant competitive position provide a solid foundation, the near-term environment is challenging. Enterprise customers have pulled back on research and consultancy spending amid macroeconomic pressures, and the path to reacceleration remains uncertain. The current valuation prices in significant pessimism, potentially creating an opportunity for patient investors if growth re-emerges.

## Company Overview

Gartner Inc is the world's leading research and advisory company, serving C-suite executives and senior enterprise leaders across virtually every function and industry. The company operates primarily through three segments:

- **Research** — The core subscription-based segment providing proprietary research, peer networking, and expert advisory
- **Conferences** — In-person and virtual events bringing together business leaders
- **Consulting** — Custom consulting engagements leveraging Gartner's research capabilities

Headquartered in Stamford, Connecticut, Gartner serves approximately 15,000+ enterprise clients globally. The company's subscription-heavy revenue model (Research typically represents ~80% of total revenue) provides significant revenue visibility and recurring cash flows. Gartner's market capitalization currently stands at approximately $11.1 billion.

Contract value is the key leading indicator for Gartner's research business, and the deceleration to 1% overall is concerning, though the 4% ex-federal figure suggests the core commercial business retains some momentum.

## Growth Analysis

### Near-Term Headwinds
Gartner's 2025 was characterized as a "lost year" with the stock down 47% YTD through year-end 2025. Enterprise customers pulled back on research and consultancy spending due to:
- Macroeconomic uncertainty and cost-cutting cycles
- US federal government spending reviews and DOGE-related budget pressures
- Extended sales cycles and deferred purchasing decisions

### Growth Catalysts
1. **AI-Driven Transformation:** Gartner is investing heavily in AI capabilities, particularly its "AskGartner" product that leverages AI to enhance the user experience and value proposition for subscribers. This could improve retention and upsell potential.
2. **Total Addressable Market:** The global market for IT research and advisory services remains large and underpenetrated. Gartner estimates its TAM at tens of billions of dollars.
3. **International Expansion:** CV growth excluding the US federal segment at 4% suggests international and commercial markets still have growth potential.
4. **Conferences Recovery:** Post-pandemic conference revenues have been recovering, providing an incremental growth lever.

### 2026 Outlook
Management's $6.455 billion revenue target for 2026 suggests modest growth expectations. The key question is whether CV growth can reaccelerate from the 1% Q4 level as the US federal headwind stabilizes and AI-driven products gain traction.

## Competitive Landscape

Gartner holds a dominant competitive position in the IT research and advisory market:

**Strengths:**
- **Market Leader:** Gartner is the undisputed #1 player in technology research, with the Magic Quadrant framework being an industry standard
- **Brand and Trust:** Decades of research credibility make Gartner the go-to source for enterprise technology decisions
- **Subscription Model:** ~80% recurring revenue provides high visibility and customer stickiness
- **Network Effects:** The breadth and depth of Gartner's client base creates proprietary data and peer benchmarking advantages that are difficult to replicate
- **High Switching Costs:** Enterprises deeply embed Gartner research into procurement and strategic planning processes

**Key Competitors:**
- **Forrester Research (FORR):** Significantly smaller, focused on customer experience and digital transformation
- **IDC (International Data Corporation):** Strong in market sizing and forecasting, but less advisory-focused
- **McKinsey, BCG, Bain:** Compete in strategic consulting but not in the subscription research model
- **Emerging AI-native research tools:** Potential long-term disruptors

Gartner's moat remains wide, but the risk from AI-driven disruption — where enterprises can potentially access synthesized insights without a Gartner subscription — is worth monitoring.

## Risk Assessment

### Key Risks

1. **Macroeconomic Sensitivity (High):** Enterprise IT spending is cyclical, and Gartner's revenue is correlated with corporate willingness to invest in research and advisory services. The 2025 downturn demonstrates this vulnerability clearly.

2. **US Federal Government Exposure (High):** The federal segment is dragging overall CV growth. Ongoing government efficiency reviews (DOGE-related) and potential budget cuts could continue to pressure this segment throughout 2026.

3. **AI Disruption Risk (Medium-High):** Large language models and AI tools could democratize access to technology insights, potentially reducing demand for traditional research subscriptions. Gartner is investing in AI (AskGartner), but execution risk remains.

4. **Revenue Concentration (Medium):** Heavy reliance on subscription research revenue means any structural shift away from traditional research formats could be impactful.

5. **Valuation Whiplash (Medium):** The stock has experienced extreme volatility — from $529 to $139 in roughly a year. This suggests the market is uncertain about the business's intrinsic value and future trajectory.

6. **Currency Risk (Low-Medium):** As a global business, FX fluctuations can impact reported results, though the company typically provides FX-neutral metrics.

### Mitigating Factors
- Subscription model provides revenue floor and visibility
- 67% contribution margins provide buffer for earnings even in downturns
- AI investments could strengthen rather than erode the value proposition
- Long-term track record of consistent value creation

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- **Trough Valuation:** At ~16x earnings, the market is pricing Gartner like a low-growth, commoditized business rather than a dominant franchise with 67% margins. If growth normalizes to even low-to-mid single digits, significant multiple expansion is possible.
- **AI as Catalyst, Not Threat:** Gartner's AI transformation (AskGartner) could enhance rather than cannibalize the business, improving user engagement, retention, and willingness-to-pay. The company's proprietary data sets are a competitive advantage in training AI models.
- **Federal Headwind Fades:** Government spending reviews may stabilize, allowing the 4% ex-federal CV growth rate to become the company-wide run rate.
- **Capital Returns:** Gartner has historically been an aggressive share repurchaser. At depressed prices, buybacks could be highly accretive.
- **Price Target:** $200-$250 over 12-18 months (Forbes outlines a $200 scenario).

### Bear Case
- **Secular Disruption:** AI tools fundamentally reduce enterprise demand for traditional research subscriptions, leading to structural CV erosion. Gartner's Magic Quadrant loses relevance as procurement decisions become more data-driven and automated.
- **Prolonged Enterprise Spending Downturn:** If macroeconomic conditions deteriorate further, CV growth could turn negative, earnings could compress, and the stock could test new lows.
- **Federal Exposure Worsens:** Continued US government spending cuts could accelerate, creating a multi-year headwind that offsets commercial growth.
- **Multiple Compression Persists:** The market may refuse to re-rate the stock until there is clear evidence of CV reacceleration, leading to an extended period of dead money.
- **Downside Risk:** $120-$130 if growth deteriorates further.

## Conclusion

**Rating: Cautiously Constructive / Moderate Buy**

Gartner represents a high-quality franchise trading at a historically depressed valuation after a brutal 2025. The ~16x forward P/E for a company with 67% contribution margins, a subscription-heavy model, and a dominant competitive position in technology research appears to offer a meaningful margin of safety. The Q4 2025 earnings beat ($3.94 vs. $3.50 expected) demonstrates that even in a challenging environment, the company can deliver profitability.

However, risks are non-trivial. The deceleration in contract value growth to just 1% overall, heavy US federal government headwinds, and the emerging threat of AI-driven disruption to traditional research models all demand caution. The 2026 revenue target of $6.455 billion does not signal a return to robust growth.

For investors with a 12-24 month time horizon and tolerance for volatility, Gartner at current levels represents a compelling risk-reward opportunity. The downside appears limited given the trough valuation and durable business model, while the upside — if CV growth reaccelerates and the multiple normalizes — could be substantial. We would recommend building a position gradually, with particular attention to quarterly CV growth metrics as the key leading indicator for the bull thesis to play out.
