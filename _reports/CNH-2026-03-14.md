## Executive Summary

CNH Industrial is a global manufacturer of agricultural and construction equipment, operating in the trough of a severe ag equipment downcycle. Q4 2025 results beat depressed expectations (EPS $0.19 vs. $0.11 consensus) and ag net sales grew 5% Y/Y, but the stock fell ~5.5% on weak 2026 guidance: ag EBIT margins of 4.5-5.5%, reflecting continued soft demand, tariff headwinds, and unfavorable product mix. Full-year 2025 revenues declined 9% on lower industry equipment demand.

At $10.65, CNH trades at ~26x trailing and forward earnings with a $13.2B market cap. The current macro environment compounds the cyclical weakness: $100+ oil raises input costs for farmers, tariff uncertainty from Section 301 probes covering 60 economies clouds international trade flows, and fertilizer price inflation (sulphur disruption + spring planting dynamics) squeezes farm margins further. Three board-level insiders purchased shares in November 2025 at $9.50-10.30, providing a modest confidence signal, but the fundamental recovery catalyst remains absent. The average analyst price target of $14.13 implies 33% upside, though achieving that requires an ag cycle turn that macro conditions currently argue against.

## Company Overview

CNH Industrial manufactures and sells agricultural equipment (tractors, combines, planters, sprayers under Case IH and New Holland brands) and construction equipment (excavators, loaders, dozers under CASE Construction). The company was spun out of Fiat Industrial and listed on NYSE. Domiciled in the UK, with substantial operations across North America, Europe, and Latin America. The agricultural segment is the dominant revenue and profit driver.

The company competes directly with Deere & Company (DE) and AGCO Corporation (AGCO) in agriculture, and with Caterpillar (CAT) and Komatsu in construction. CNH typically trades at a discount to Deere on brand strength, dealer network quality, and precision agriculture technology positioning.

## Growth Analysis

**Near-term growth is constrained by multiple converging headwinds:**

1. **Farm income compression:** US net farm income has been declining as crop prices (corn, soybeans, wheat) remain below 2022 peaks. Lower farm income directly reduces farmer willingness to purchase new equipment, particularly large tractors and combines with $300K-$500K+ price tags.

2. **Dealer inventory destocking:** During downturns, dealers carry excess used and new inventory. Until channel inventory normalizes, new production orders remain suppressed even if end-demand stabilizes. Management noted "continuing challenges in the markets" in the Q4 call.

3. **Tariff headwinds:** CNH explicitly cited tariff impacts in the 2026 margin guide. The Section 301 probes targeting 60 economies create ongoing uncertainty for a company with global manufacturing and sales footprints. Tariffs raise input costs (steel, components) and potentially trigger retaliatory measures against US-manufactured equipment exports.

4. **Oil at $100+:** Higher energy costs simultaneously raise farmer input costs (diesel, fertilizer) and increase CNH's manufacturing/logistics costs. The fertilizer price inflation documented in the world model further squeezes farmer profitability.

5. **Construction segment:** Construction equipment demand is sensitive to infrastructure spending and housing starts. With mortgage rates above 6.25% and housing starts under pressure, this segment faces its own headwinds.

**Positive offsets are limited:** Q4 showed 5% ag revenue growth driven by pricing and FX, indicating some ability to hold/raise prices. Cost savings programs are partially offsetting tariff impacts. But the 2026 EBIT margin guide of 4.5-5.5% implies these offsets are insufficient to drive earnings growth.

**Upside scenario:** If US farm policy (e.g., Farm Bill renewal, biofuel mandates, trade deal with China) boosts crop prices and farm income, the ag equipment cycle could turn in late 2026 or 2027. CNH would have significant operating leverage on recovery — margins expanding from 5% back toward 10%+ would roughly double ag segment profitability on flat revenue.

## Valuation Assessment

CNH at 25.9x forward earnings appears expensive in absolute terms for a machinery company. Context matters:

- **Cyclical trough valuation:** Cyclical companies often trade at high P/E multiples at the bottom because earnings are compressed. Investors who wait for "cheap" P/E ratios on cyclicals typically buy near the top when earnings are peaking. The relevant comparison is price-to-normalized-earnings.
- **If mid-cycle EPS is roughly 2x current levels (~$0.80-1.00/share annualized, implying mid-cycle P/E of ~11-13x),** the valuation is reasonable for patient investors with a 2-3 year horizon.
- **Deere comparison:** DE trades at ~20x forward earnings with stronger brand, better precision ag positioning, and higher margins. CNH's discount to Deere is warranted.
- **Dividend yield of 2.3%** provides modest income while waiting for cycle turn.

The average analyst target of $14.13 (revised up 10% on Feb 25) implies 33% upside from $10.65. This target likely embeds expectations of some margin recovery through 2026-2027.

**Risk to valuation:** If the stagflation scenario intensifies (recession probability 38-42% per world model), trough earnings could extend or deepen. A recession would push farm equipment replacement cycles out further and pressure the financial services portfolio with dealer and farmer defaults.

## Competitive Landscape

CNH holds the #2 position in global agricultural equipment behind Deere, with meaningful share in North America, Europe, and Latin America. Key competitive dynamics:

- **Precision agriculture gap:** Deere has invested aggressively in autonomous driving, machine learning, and precision planting technology (See & Spray, ExactEmerge). CNH has responded with its Raven acquisition and precision ag initiatives, but generally trails Deere by 2-3 years in technology adoption and dealer integration.
- **Dealer network:** CNH's Case IH and New Holland dealer networks are less consolidated and less financially robust than Deere's, which creates a competitive disadvantage in service quality and parts availability.
- **Construction:** CNH's CASE Construction brand competes against Caterpillar, Komatsu, and Volvo CE with a smaller dealer network and narrower product range.
- **Brand loyalty:** In ag equipment, brand switching costs are moderate (farmers invest in brand-specific implements, develop relationships with local dealers). CNH has meaningful brand loyalty, particularly in row-crop regions (Case IH) and in Europe (New Holland).

CNH's competitive position is defensible but structurally secondary to Deere in most markets.

## Risk Assessment

**Macro risks (elevated):**
- **Oil above $100:** Directly compresses farm margins through diesel and fertilizer costs. The world model's central oil range of $98-108 with ceiling of $115-130 creates sustained headwind.
- **Tariff uncertainty:** Section 301 probes + potential duties add cost pressure and dampen trade-dependent ag exports (US grain/oilseed exports are a key driver of farmer confidence and equipment purchases).
- **Recession (38-42% probability):** Would extend the ag downcycle and pressure the financial services portfolio. CNH's 1.33 beta means the stock amplifies broader market moves.
- **Fertilizer inflation:** Spring planting with elevated fertilizer costs reduces farmer free cash flow available for equipment purchases.

**Company-specific risks:**
- **Financial services credit quality:** Captive finance portfolios at ag equipment companies carry concentration risk. Extended downcycles historically produce elevated delinquencies.
- **Inventory management:** Dealer inventory levels need to normalize before new orders recover. Management commentary suggests this process is incomplete.
- **FX exposure:** Substantial revenue from Europe and Latin America creates currency translation risk.

**Mitigants:**
- Three insider purchases in November 2025 (Heywood at $9.52, Sorensen at $9.62, Howard Buffett at $9.68-10.29) totaling ~$658K suggest board-level confidence in a floor near $9-10.
- Cost savings programs are partially offsetting margin headwinds.
- Equipment replacement cycles are deferrable but not permanently avoidable — aging fleet creates pent-up demand that eventually converts.

## Conclusion

CNH is a cyclically depressed agricultural equipment manufacturer trading at trough earnings with legitimate long-term recovery potential. The core analytical question is timing: when does the ag cycle turn, and can you tolerate the volatility and macro risk until it does?

Current macro conditions argue against an imminent recovery. Oil above $100, tariff uncertainty from 60-economy Section 301 probes, fertilizer inflation, and 38-42% recession probability all extend the timeline for farm income recovery and equipment purchase resumption. The 2026 guidance of 4.5-5.5% ag EBIT margins confirms management doesn't expect material improvement this year.

The insider buying at $9.50-10.30 and current price of $10.65 suggest limited additional downside absent recession, but the upside catalysts are 12-24 months away at minimum. For a portfolio already positioned defensively per the current macro regime, CNH adds cyclical exposure and macro sensitivity that conflict with the dominant risk profile.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The stock is appropriately priced for its current risk-reward profile. Accumulation below $9.50 (near insider purchase levels) would improve the risk-reward for patient investors willing to hold through the cycle. Initiating at $10.65 with 26x trailing earnings during stagflation and an extending ag downcycle does not meet the elevated bar for cyclical BUY calls required by current macro conditions.
