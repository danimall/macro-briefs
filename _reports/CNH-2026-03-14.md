## Executive Summary

CNH Industrial is a global manufacturer of agricultural and construction equipment, operating through brands including Case IH, New Holland, and STEYR. The company reported Q4 2025 results that beat depressed expectations (EPS $0.19 vs. $0.11 consensus) but guided to weak 2026 margins (ag EBIT 4.5-5.5%), reflecting persistent softness in global ag equipment demand. Full-year 2025 revenues declined 9% on lower industry equipment volumes, though Q4 showed a 6% sequential improvement.

At $10.65 per share and ~$13.2B market cap, CNH trades at approximately 26x trailing and forward earnings — elevated for a cyclical manufacturer in a downturn. The stock sits 25% below its 52-week high of $14.27 and 18% above its 52-week low of $9.00. The analyst consensus target of $14.13 implies ~33% upside, but this target assumes a recovery in ag equipment demand that faces material headwinds from $100+ oil prices (raising input costs for farmers), tariff uncertainty from Section 301 probes across 60 economies, and compressed farm margins globally.

The investment case hinges on timing the cyclical trough correctly. Board-level insider buying near the lows ($9.50-$10) provides some floor confidence, but the macro regime — stagflationary with declining consumer spending and rising input costs — argues against premature positioning in capital-intensive cyclicals with high beta (1.33).

## Company Overview

CNH Industrial manufactures and sells agricultural equipment (tractors, combines, planters, sprayers) and construction equipment (excavators, loaders, dozers) globally. The company was spun off from Fiat Industrial and CNH Global in 2013 and is domiciled in the UK (previously Netherlands). Key brands include Case IH, New Holland Agriculture, STEYR, Case Construction, and New Holland Construction. CNH also operates a financial services segment providing retail financing to equipment purchasers.

The agricultural segment is the primary revenue driver, and its performance is tied to global crop prices, farm income, and equipment replacement cycles. Construction equipment is smaller and more correlated to infrastructure spending and housing activity.

## Growth Analysis

**Near-Term (2026):** The outlook is weak. Management guided to ag EBIT margins of 4.5-5.5%, down from already depressed levels. Several headwinds converge:

1. **Farm income compression:** $100+ oil raises diesel, fertilizer, and transportation costs for farmers, squeezing margins and reducing equipment purchasing power. CF Industries' sulphur scarcity thesis from the world model further elevates input costs.
2. **Tariff uncertainty:** Section 301 probes across 60 economies create purchasing hesitation among farmers with international exposure. CNH's global manufacturing footprint means both supply chain cost increases and demand-side uncertainty.
3. **Inventory destocking:** Dealer inventories remain elevated from the 2023-2024 ordering cycle; the pipeline must clear before new orders accelerate.
4. **Interest rates:** With FOMC holding rates elevated (core PCE 3.1%), equipment financing costs remain high, suppressing demand for large capital goods.

**Medium-Term (2027-2028):** The agricultural equipment cycle is fundamentally mean-reverting. The current downcycle has been running for approximately 18-24 months. If crop prices stabilize and farm income recovers, pent-up demand for equipment replacement should drive a recovery. CNH's technology investments (precision agriculture, autonomous equipment) could support market share gains. However, the current macro regime suggests the trough may extend longer than typical cycles.

**Structural Growth:** Precision agriculture adoption, autonomous equipment, and connected solutions represent genuine long-term opportunities. CNH has been investing through its technology stack, as evidenced by the CTO's equity compensation. Global food security concerns and labor scarcity in agriculture support secular demand growth for productivity-enhancing equipment.

## Valuation Assessment

At $10.65 and ~26x earnings, CNH is expensive on current earnings but potentially cheap on normalized earnings. The key question is what "normalized" means in the current environment.

**Trough earnings analysis:** If 2025 EPS is approximately $0.41 (implied by the trailing P/E of 25.9x at $10.65), and mid-cycle EPS for CNH has historically been $1.00-$1.50, the stock trades at roughly 7-11x mid-cycle earnings. That range is attractive relative to Deere (DE) which typically trades at 14-18x mid-cycle.

**The problem:** There is no evidence the trough has been reached. 2026 guidance implies continued earnings pressure. If 2026 EPS comes in at $0.30-$0.50 (consistent with guided margin compression), the stock could trade lower before the cycle turns.

**Analyst target:** The consensus $14.13 target represents a 33% premium to the current price. This target likely embeds assumptions about demand recovery in H2 2026 or 2027 that the macro environment may not support.

**Dividend support:** The 2.33% yield provides modest income while waiting for the cycle to turn, assuming the dividend is maintained through the trough.

## Competitive Landscape

CNH competes primarily with Deere & Company (DE) and AGCO Corporation (AGCO) in agricultural equipment, and with Caterpillar (CAT) and Volvo CE in construction equipment.

**Market position:** CNH holds the #2 position globally in agricultural equipment behind Deere. Its dual-brand strategy (Case IH and New Holland) provides broader market coverage across price points and geographies. Strong presence in North America, Europe, and Latin America.

**Competitive advantages:**
- Global dealer network with captive financing capability
- Broad product portfolio spanning the full range of ag and construction equipment
- Precision agriculture technology investments
- Brand heritage (Case IH, New Holland) with loyal customer bases

**Competitive disadvantages:**
- Deere's technology lead in precision agriculture (autonomous driving, See & Spray) is widening
- Lower margins than Deere across the cycle, suggesting weaker pricing power
- Construction equipment segment is sub-scale relative to Caterpillar
- Less vertically integrated than Deere (which manufactures more components in-house)

The 4.5-5.5% ag EBIT margin guidance for 2026 compares unfavorably to Deere's typically higher margins, confirming CNH's #2 positioning translates to structurally lower profitability.

## Risk Assessment

**Cyclical Risk (HIGH):** Agricultural equipment demand is highly cyclical. The current downcycle is coinciding with a stagflationary macro regime — the worst possible combination for capital goods manufacturers. Beta of 1.33 means CNH amplifies broader market moves, and the world model's 38-42% recession probability would further depress demand.

**Input Cost Risk (HIGH):** $100+ oil directly increases steel, rubber, and transportation costs for CNH's manufacturing operations. The tariff environment creates additional uncertainty on component sourcing costs. Management has flagged tariffs as a specific headwind in the 2026 outlook.

**Credit Risk (MODERATE-HIGH):** CNH's financial services arm extends credit to farmers purchasing equipment. If farm income deteriorates further (plausible given rising input costs and potential crop price volatility), delinquency rates on the equipment finance portfolio could rise, requiring increased provisioning.

**Currency Risk (MODERATE):** Global operations with significant European and Latin American exposure create FX translation risk. Q4 2025 benefited from favorable FX, but this can reverse quickly.

**Demand Timing Risk (MODERATE):** The primary investment thesis requires accurately timing the cyclical trough. If the trough extends into 2027 (consistent with the macro regime described in the world model), the stock could trade sideways or lower for an extended period despite appearing "cheap" on normalized earnings.

**Tariff Risk (MODERATE):** Section 301 probes across 60 economies create direct cost increases on imported components and indirect demand effects through farmer uncertainty about trade policy.

## Conclusion

CNH Industrial is a fundamentally sound franchise caught in a cyclical downturn that the current macro environment may extend. The stock's apparent cheapness on mid-cycle earnings (7-11x) is offset by the absence of evidence that the trough has been reached, a weak 2026 outlook, and a macro regime that compounds rather than alleviates the agricultural equipment downcycle. At 1.33 beta in a market environment where further correction probability is 40-50%, the risk-reward is unattractive for near-term positioning.

The board insider buying near $9.50-$10 provides a useful data point: informed insiders believe the stock is undervalued at those levels. The current price of $10.65 is only 10-12% above those purchases, suggesting limited additional downside from here absent a broader market selloff or recession confirmation. However, upside requires a demand recovery that the macro environment does not currently support.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** Appropriate for existing holders willing to collect the 2.33% dividend through the trough. Accumulation between $9.00-$9.50 (near the 52-week low and insider purchase levels) would be more compelling. Initiating a new position at $10.65 requires confidence in a cyclical inflection that the data does not yet support. The calibration framework's directive against Consumer Discretionary-adjacent cyclical BUY calls at elevated P/E multiples during deteriorating macro conditions applies here.
