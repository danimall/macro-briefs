## Executive Summary

Cavco Industries is a mid-cap manufacturer and retailer of factory-built housing with a market capitalization of $3.8B, currently trading at $506 after a sharp 29% decline from its January 2026 high of $713. The stock dropped ~20% in a single session around January 30, 2026 earnings, and has been range-bound between $400-$580 since. At ~22x forward earnings with EPS of $20.71, CVCO is neither cheap enough to warrant aggressive accumulation nor expensive enough to avoid outright, given the quality of the franchise. The business benefits from secular tailwinds around housing affordability and manufactured housing adoption, and the September 2025 acquisition of American Homestar adds both manufacturing and retail capacity.

The critical tension is between company-specific quality and macro headwinds. CEO Bill Boor purchased $495K of stock at $495 on February 4, 2026, shortly after the earnings collapse. Director David Greenblatt bought ~$235K and Director Steven Moster bought ~$137K in the same window. This coordinated insider buying at the post-selloff lows is a meaningful positive signal. However, the macro environment — mortgage rates at 7-month highs, 19% refinance demand collapse, and stagflationary consumer pressures from $5/gallon diesel passing through to all goods — directly compresses demand for manufactured housing. The company's financial services segment (mortgage origination) faces particular headwinds from the rate environment.

## Company Overview

Cavco Industries manufactures factory-built homes, modular homes, park model RVs, vacation cabins, and commercial structures under multiple brand names. The company operates through two segments: (1) Factory-Built Housing, which designs, produces, and retails manufactured and modular homes, and (2) Financial Services, which provides mortgage lending and insurance services to manufactured home buyers. Headquartered in Phoenix, Arizona, CVCO completed the acquisition of American Homestar in September 2025, adding manufacturing and retail operations.

The manufactured housing industry serves as an affordable alternative to site-built homes, with average selling prices approximately 50-60% below conventional construction. This positions CVCO at the intersection of the housing affordability crisis — a structural demand driver — and cyclical interest rate sensitivity — a near-term headwind.

## Competitive Landscape

Cavco is the third-largest manufactured housing producer in the US behind Clayton Homes (Berkshire Hathaway subsidiary, private) and Skyline Champion (SKY). The manufactured housing industry is moderately concentrated, with the top three producers controlling roughly 50% of production. Key competitive dynamics:

1. **Clayton Homes' dominance:** As a Berkshire subsidiary, Clayton has virtually unlimited capital and operates a vertically integrated model (manufacturing, retail, lending, insurance). CVCO competes directly against this well-capitalized incumbent.

2. **Skyline Champion as public comp:** SKY trades at similar multiples and serves as the closest public comparable. CVCO's American Homestar acquisition mirrors SKY's own acquisition strategy.

3. **Barriers to entry:** Moderate. Manufacturing facilities require significant capital investment, and dealer networks take years to build. Regulatory compliance (HUD standards for manufactured housing) adds complexity. CVCO's integrated model (factory + retail + lending) creates stickier customer relationships than pure manufacturers.

4. **Policy tailwind potential:** The housing affordability bill that CEO Boor discussed on CNBC could be material. If federal or state governments expand zoning acceptance for manufactured housing or provide buyer incentives, this directly expands CVCO's addressable market.

## Risk Assessment

**Macro Risks (High Severity):**
- Mortgage rates at 7-month highs with 19% refinance demand collapse. Manufactured home buyers are disproportionately rate-sensitive given lower income levels.
- Stagflationary environment compresses consumer purchasing power. CPI tracking toward 3.2-5.2% erodes real wages.
- Diesel at $5/gallon directly increases transportation costs for factory-built homes shipped to retail locations. CVCO cannot fully pass through transportation costs without reducing demand.

**Earnings Revision Risk (Moderate-High):**
The January 30 earnings selloff (stock dropped from $617 to $492, a 20% single-day decline on 5x normal volume) suggests the company either missed estimates, guided lower, or both. The flat forward-to-trailing P/E ratio (both 22x) implies consensus has already reset expectations, but further downward revision risk exists if the macro environment deteriorates further.

**Financial Services Segment Risk:**
CVCO's lending arm faces dual headwinds: (1) lower origination volume from reduced buyer activity, and (2) potential credit quality deterioration among manufactured home borrowers, who tend to have lower credit scores and less financial cushion against economic stress.

**Acquisition Integration Risk:**
American Homestar integration is ongoing. While the September 2025 completion suggests operational progress, achieving targeted synergies during a weakening housing market is more difficult than during expansion.

**Concentration Risk:**
Small float (7.76M shares) creates liquidity risk and amplifies price volatility in both directions. The January selloff demonstrated this: a relatively small amount of selling pressure produced a 20% single-day decline.

**Mitigating Factors:**
- Coordinated insider buying at $456-$500 suggests management views current prices as undervalued
- Housing affordability legislation could expand the addressable market
- Manufactured housing is counter-cyclical in the sense that higher site-built costs push buyers toward factory-built alternatives

**Bear Case:**

Diesel cost passthrough compresses margins. Financial Services segment experiences credit deterioration. Earnings decline 15-20% from current levels. Stock revisits 52-week low of $393, representing 22% downside.

**Why HOLD (not BUY):**
The insider buying signal is strong but insufficient to override the macro headwinds. Per the world model, homebuilders are a structural AVOID due to 19% refinance demand collapse, and while manufactured housing is distinct from homebuilding, the demand transmission mechanism is similar: higher rates → higher monthly payments → fewer qualified buyers → lower unit volumes. The 22x P/E provides no margin of safety if earnings compress. The January selloff magnitude (20% single-day decline) signals fundamental deterioration that insider buying alone cannot cure.

**Why HOLD (not AVOID):**
CVCO is qualitatively different from the homebuilders in the AVOID bucket. Manufactured housing serves a lower price point and benefits from affordability-driven demand substitution. The company has a vertically integrated model with financial services providing recurring revenue. The insider buying cluster is large and coordinated enough to establish a credible near-term floor. CEO advocacy on housing affordability legislation provides a positive optionality that homebuilders lack. At $506, the stock is already 29% below its high.

## Conclusion

CVCO is a quality franchise in a structurally attractive niche, facing cyclical headwinds that could persist for multiple quarters. The coordinated insider buying at $456-$500 establishes a near-term floor and signals management conviction, but the macro environment — rising mortgage rates, stagflationary consumer pressures, diesel cost passthrough — creates earnings downside risk that the 22x P/E multiple does not adequately compensate for. The American Homestar acquisition adds strategic value but faces integration risk in a weakening demand environment. **HOLD** at current levels, with potential upgrade to BUY if: (1) mortgage rates decline below 6.5%, (2) housing affordability legislation advances, or (3) the stock revisits the $400-420 range where the risk/reward improves materially. Entry below $420 (approximately 20x on current EPS, aligning with the insider buying zone) would warrant reconsideration.
