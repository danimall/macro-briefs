## Executive Summary

O'Reilly Automotive is one of the premier aftermarket automotive parts retailers in North America, operating over 6,000 stores across the United States and Mexico with more than $16 billion in annual sales. The company serves both the do-it-yourself (DIY, ~50% of sales) and professional installer (~47% of sales) segments, creating a dual-market approach that provides diversification and scale advantages. O'Reilly closed 2025 with a strong finish, posting 5.6% comparable store sales growth in Q4 and 4.7% for the full year, with a 13% increase in Q4 diluted EPS year-over-year.

Despite the strong top-line performance, Q4 2025 adjusted EPS of $0.71 narrowly missed the consensus estimate of $0.72, largely due to cost pressures from self-insurance and healthcare expenses. Analysts responded by trimming price targets following the earnings release, and the stock currently trades around $95.21 per share. The company has guided for 2026 full-year EPS of $3.10–$3.20 and revenue of $18.7–$19.0 billion, with comparable store sales growth targeted at 3%–5%.

While O'Reilly's competitive position remains formidable—supported by industry-leading distribution capabilities, strong brand loyalty, and a defensive end market—the stock commands a premium valuation at approximately 31.7x forward earnings. Investors must weigh the company's reliable execution and recession-resistant business model against the current valuation, which already bakes in much of the positive thesis. We view ORLY as a high-quality hold with a modest risk-reward skew at current prices.

## Company Overview

O'Reilly Automotive Inc, headquartered in Springfield, Missouri, is the third-largest aftermarket auto parts retailer in the United States by store count and among the top two by revenue. The company was founded in 1957 and has grown organically and through acquisitions (most notably the 2008 CSK Auto acquisition) into a dominant national player.

**Key Business Characteristics:**
- **Store Count:** Over 6,000 domestic stores with growing presence in Mexico
- **Annual Revenue:** Over $16 billion (2025), with 2026 guidance of $18.7–$19.0 billion
- **Revenue Mix:** ~50% DIY / ~47% Professional / ~3% Other
- **Market Cap:** ~$80.4 billion
- **Beta:** 0.60 (significantly below market, reflecting defensive characteristics)
- **Dividend:** None (capital returned primarily through share repurchases)

The aftermarket auto parts industry benefits from several secular tailwinds: an aging vehicle fleet (average U.S. vehicle age exceeds 12 years), increasing vehicle complexity requiring more parts, and miles driven trends that correlate with maintenance needs. The business is largely non-discretionary, as consumers and repair shops require parts regardless of economic conditions.

## Financial Analysis

### Revenue & Growth
O'Reilly demonstrated consistent top-line strength throughout 2025:
- **Q4 2025 comparable store sales growth:** 5.6%
- **Full-year 2025 comparable store sales growth:** 4.7%
- **2026 revenue guidance:** $18.7 billion – $19.0 billion
- **2026 comparable store sales growth target:** 3%–5%

The professional segment has been a particularly strong contributor, with investment partnerships highlighting it as a key stock driver in Q3 2025.

### Earnings
- **Q4 2025 adjusted EPS:** $0.71, up from $0.66 in Q4 2024 (a 7.6% increase)
- **Q4 2025 diluted EPS growth:** 13% year-over-year
- **Full-year 2026 EPS guidance:** $3.10–$3.20
- **Trailing P/E:** 31.67x | **Forward P/E:** 31.67x

The Q4 EPS miss versus consensus ($0.71 vs. $0.72) was marginal but notable given the typically consistent beat pattern for this company. The miss was driven by elevated SG&A costs, specifically self-insurance claims and healthcare expenses.

### Profitability & Margins
While detailed financial statements were not available in the data feed, the company's historical operating margins have consistently exceeded 19–20%, among the best in the auto parts retail space. Management acknowledged "meaningful cost pressure" heading into 2026, which may create near-term margin headwinds.

### Capital Allocation
O'Reilly does not pay a dividend, instead prioritizing aggressive share buybacks. This has been a hallmark of management's capital allocation strategy, consistently reducing the share count and amplifying EPS growth beyond what organic earnings growth would deliver. The 13% Q4 diluted EPS growth versus underlying revenue growth reflects the contribution of buybacks.

### Balance Sheet
O'Reilly has historically operated with a leveraged balance sheet (typical for the auto parts retail sector), using debt to fund share repurchases. While specific debt figures were unavailable from the data, the company's consistent free cash flow generation has historically supported this leverage strategy. The low beta of 0.60 suggests the market perceives limited financial risk despite the leverage.

## Growth Analysis

### Organic Growth Drivers
1. **Comparable Store Sales:** Consistent mid-single-digit comp growth (4.7% FY2025) driven by ticket growth and transaction count
2. **Professional Segment Momentum:** The pro segment (~47% of sales) continues to gain share as independent repair shops increasingly rely on O'Reilly's same-day/next-day delivery capabilities
3. **Aging Vehicle Fleet:** The U.S. average vehicle age continues to rise, expanding the addressable market for aftermarket parts

### Expansion Plans
- O'Reilly outlined **ambitious expansion plans** during the Q4 2025 earnings call, including accelerated North American store openings
- Mexico expansion provides a long runway for unit growth in an underpenetrated market
- The company has been investing in distribution infrastructure to support geographic expansion

### 2026 Guidance
- **Revenue:** $18.7B–$19.0B
- **Comparable store sales growth:** 3%–5%
- **EPS:** $3.10–$3.20

The guidance implies revenue growth of approximately 7–10% year-over-year (assuming ~$17.5B in FY2025 revenues) and continued healthy EPS growth, though the range suggests management sees some uncertainty around cost trends.

### Risks to Growth
- Deceleration in comp sales from 4.7% (2025) to 3%–5% guidance midpoint suggests potential moderation
- Rising SG&A costs could compress earnings growth relative to revenue growth
- Electric vehicle adoption, while still a very long-term threat, could eventually reduce aftermarket demand for traditional parts

- Highly predictable, non-cyclical revenue streams
- Consistent execution and market share gains

However, the premium is elevated even for ORLY's historical standards. Based on 2026 EPS guidance of $3.10–$3.20, the stock is trading at approximately 29.8x–30.7x the high end of guidance, which leaves limited room for multiple expansion.

### Peer Comparison

## Competitive Landscape

### Market Position
O'Reilly operates in a consolidated but still fragmented aftermarket auto parts industry. The "Big Three" (AutoZone, O'Reilly, and Advance Auto Parts) dominate the organized retail channel, but independent stores, warehouse distributors, and online players also compete.

**Key Competitive Advantages:**
1. **Dual-Market Model:** O'Reilly's balanced exposure to both DIY and professional segments provides diversification that pure-play competitors lack
2. **Distribution Network:** Industry-leading hub-and-spoke distribution system enabling same-day delivery to professional customers — a critical competitive differentiator
3. **Parts Availability:** Broad SKU assortment across hard parts, maintenance items, and accessories
4. **Culture & Execution:** Strong operational culture focused on customer service, which drives repeat business and professional loyalty
5. **Scale:** Over 6,000 stores create purchasing power and logistical efficiency

### Competitive Threats
- **AutoZone (AZO):** Closest peer; also well-managed with strong buyback program; more DIY-focused
- **Advance Auto Parts (AAP):** Underperforming peer; recent operational turnaround effort; potential to become more competitive under new leadership
- **Amazon/Online:** Limited threat in the professional segment due to urgency of repair needs; moderate threat in DIY for routine maintenance items
- **Electric Vehicles:** Very long-term threat as EVs have fewer traditional maintenance parts; however, the aging ICE fleet ensures decades of demand

### Industry Dynamics
The aftermarket auto parts industry is fundamentally attractive:
- Non-discretionary demand (vehicles need maintenance regardless of economy)
- Aging fleet provides secular tailwind
- Fragmented independent shop channel creates opportunity for share gains
- High barriers to entry due to distribution infrastructure requirements

## Risk Assessment

### Near-Term Risks
1. **Cost Pressures:** Self-insurance and healthcare costs are rising, as highlighted in the Q4 call, which could compress margins in 2026
2. **Analyst Sentiment:** Post-Q4 earnings, analysts slashed price targets, indicating potential near-term pressure on the stock
3. **Macro Sensitivity:** While auto parts demand is defensive, a sharp recession could delay some non-essential maintenance spending

### Medium-Term Risks
4. **Tariff/Trade Risk:** Import tariffs on auto parts from China and Mexico could increase COGS, though ORLY has historically passed through costs effectively
5. **Labor Market:** Tight labor markets and wage inflation could further pressure SG&A
6. **Mexico Expansion Risk:** International expansion introduces currency, regulatory, and operational risks

### Long-Term Risks
7. **EV Disruption:** Electric vehicles require significantly less maintenance (no oil changes, fewer brake replacements, no transmission service); as EV penetration grows, addressable market could shrink
8. **Valuation Risk:** At 31.7x earnings, any execution stumble or multiple compression could result in meaningful downside

### Mitigating Factors
- **Low Beta (0.60):** Indicates stock is less volatile than the market, providing downside protection
- **Non-cyclical demand:** Auto parts consumption is relatively stable through economic cycles
- **Pricing power:** Essential nature of products allows cost pass-through
- **Management track record:** Decades of consistent execution reduces operational risk

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- **Consistent Compounder:** ORLY's combination of mid-single-digit comp growth, new store openings (including Mexico), and aggressive share buybacks can compound EPS at 10–15% annually
- **Defensive Qualities:** Low beta (0.60) and non-discretionary demand make ORLY an attractive holding in uncertain macro environments
- **Professional Segment Gains:** Continued market share capture in the professional channel provides a multi-year growth runway
- **Accelerated Expansion:** North American expansion plans, particularly in Mexico, offer a long runway for unit growth
- **Aging Fleet Tailwind:** Average vehicle age continues to rise, ensuring robust aftermarket demand
- **Target Price (Bull):** If ORLY achieves the high end of 2026 guidance ($3.20 EPS) and earns a 34x multiple, the stock could reach ~$109, representing ~14% upside

### Bear Case
- **Valuation Stretch:** At 31.7x forward earnings, the premium valuation leaves little margin of safety; any disappointment could trigger multiple contraction
- **Cost Headwinds:** Rising self-insurance, healthcare, and wage costs could erode margins and suppress EPS growth below expectations
- **Comp Deceleration:** 2026 guidance of 3%–5% comps represents potential moderation from 2025's 4.7%
- **EV Transition:** While decades away from being material, the market may begin to price in reduced long-term TAM as EV adoption accelerates
- **Analyst Downgrades:** Post-Q4 price target reductions suggest the near-term setup is challenging
- **Target Price (Bear):** If cost pressures intensify and the multiple compresses to 27x on $3.10 EPS, the stock could decline to ~$84, representing ~12% downside

## Conclusion

O'Reilly Automotive remains one of the highest-quality businesses in U.S. retail, characterized by a defensive end market, exceptional execution, and a proven capital allocation strategy. The company's Q4 2025 results confirmed the strength of the business model, with 5.6% comp growth and 13% EPS growth demonstrating continued momentum.

However, at ~31.7x forward earnings, the stock is priced for continued perfection. The slight Q4 EPS miss and analyst price target cuts suggest the market is becoming more discerning about the risk-reward at current levels. Cost pressures from self-insurance and healthcare expenses represent a tangible near-term headwind that could weigh on margin expansion.

**Overall Recommendation: HOLD**

We rate ORLY as a **Hold** for current shareholders and a **Watch/Accumulate on Weakness** for prospective buyers. The ideal entry point would be closer to the 52-week low (~$85–$88), where the risk-reward becomes more attractive. For long-term investors already holding the stock, the defensive characteristics, consistent compounding, and strong competitive position justify maintaining the position. The weighted overall score of **7.15/10** reflects a high-quality business at a fair-to-full valuation.
