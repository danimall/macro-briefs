## Executive Summary

Sempra is a San Diego-based energy infrastructure holding company operating through three key segments: Sempra California (SDG&E and SoCalGas), Sempra Texas Utilities (Oncor), and Sempra Infrastructure (LNG and renewable energy projects). With a market capitalization of approximately $60.6 billion and a beta of 0.72, the company offers a defensive positioning within the Utilities sector while maintaining exposure to high-growth secular themes including AI-driven electricity demand and LNG export capacity.

The company recently narrowed its FY2025 adjusted EPS guidance to the high end of its $4.30–$4.70 range (approximately $4.70), beating consensus estimates of $4.54, and reaffirmed its 2026 guidance. However, a significant headwind emerged in the form of a $471 million after-tax earnings impact from a Track 2 proposed decision affecting SDG&E in California. This regulatory setback underscores the ongoing risk of operating in the heavily regulated California utility environment, even as the company's Texas and infrastructure segments continue to deliver strong results.

From a valuation perspective, at $92.79 per share, Sempra trades at a trailing P/E of ~28x and a forward P/E of ~21.2x, which is on the higher end for regulated utilities. Analyst consensus targets average $102.62, implying roughly 10.6% upside from current levels. The stock's 52-week range of $61.90–$95.72 suggests it is trading near the upper end of its recent range, limiting near-term upside potential unless growth catalysts materialize more forcefully.

## Company Overview

Sempra is one of the largest utility holding companies in North America, providing essential energy services through three operating segments:

- **Sempra California**: Includes San Diego Gas & Electric (SDG&E) and Southern California Gas Company (SoCalGas), serving millions of customers in Southern California with electric and natural gas distribution.
- **Sempra Texas Utilities**: Primarily its ownership stake in Oncor, the largest electric transmission and distribution utility in Texas, which serves over 10 million customers.
- **Sempra Infrastructure**: Encompasses the company's LNG export facilities, renewable energy assets, and energy storage operations, including the Cameron LNG facility and development-stage projects like Port Arthur LNG.

The company is headquartered in San Diego, California, and has been publicly traded since its formation in 1998. Its diversified portfolio across regulated utilities and energy infrastructure provides a blend of stable cash flows and growth optionality.

## Growth Analysis

### Near-Term Growth Drivers
1. **Oncor Rate Base Growth**: Texas continues to experience rapid population and economic growth, driving strong electricity demand. Oncor's regulated rate base is expected to grow at a high-single-digit pace.
2. **AI-Driven Power Demand**: Multiple analysts have highlighted Sempra as a defensive way to benefit from the surge in electricity demand from data centers and AI workloads, particularly through its Texas and California utility operations.
3. **LNG Infrastructure**: Port Arthur LNG and Cameron LNG expansion provide significant growth optionality as global LNG demand increases.

### Long-Term Growth Potential
- Sempra has guided for a long-term EPS growth rate of 6–8%, which is above the utility sector average of 4–6%
- The transition from trailing P/E of 28x to forward P/E of 21.2x implies the market expects approximately $4.38 in forward EPS, consistent with the company's guidance
- Capital deployment into high-return infrastructure projects (transmission, LNG, renewables) supports sustained rate base and earnings growth

### Risks to Growth
- California regulatory environment remains unpredictable and could dampen returns in the Sempra California segment
- LNG project execution risks, including cost overruns and permitting delays
- Rising interest rates increase the cost of financing large capital programs

## Competitive Landscape

Sempra operates in a competitive landscape that includes other large diversified utility holding companies:

- **NextEra Energy (NEE)**: The largest U.S. utility by market cap, with a stronger renewables portfolio but a higher valuation premium
- **Duke Energy (DUK)**: Similar regulated utility profile but with less LNG/infrastructure exposure
- **Southern Company (SO)**: Comparable regulated utility operations, predominantly in the Southeast
- **Dominion Energy (D)**: Restructured portfolio focused on regulated utilities

**Competitive Advantages**:
1. **Geographic Diversification**: Unlike many peers concentrated in one region, Sempra operates across California, Texas, and internationally
2. **LNG Optionality**: Few regulated utility peers have meaningful exposure to LNG export infrastructure
3. **Texas Growth**: Oncor's position in the fastest-growing large state provides a structural growth advantage
4. **Scale**: ~$60.6 billion market cap provides access to capital markets and diversification benefits

**Competitive Disadvantages**:
1. **California Regulatory Risk**: The CPUC's adversarial stance toward utilities creates earnings volatility
2. **Lower Dividend Yield**: At ~2.9%, Sempra's yield is below the sector average, potentially less attractive to income-focused investors

## Risk Assessment

### Regulatory Risk (High)
- The $471 million after-tax impact from the Track 2 proposed decision highlights the material regulatory risk in California
- SDG&E is exploring regulatory remedies, but outcomes are uncertain
- California wildfire liability remains an ongoing concern, despite legislative reforms

### Interest Rate Sensitivity (Moderate)
- As a capital-intensive utility, Sempra is sensitive to rising interest rates that increase financing costs
- The beta of 0.72 indicates below-average market sensitivity but above the typical pure regulated utility

### Execution Risk (Moderate)
- Large-scale infrastructure projects (Port Arthur LNG, Oncor transmission) carry execution and cost overrun risks
- The $10 billion infrastructure stake sale, while enhancing flexibility, dilutes future upside from those assets

### ESG and Climate Risk (Moderate)
- California wildfire exposure through SDG&E
- LNG operations face increasing scrutiny from environmental regulators and advocacy groups
- Transition to clean energy presents both opportunities and challenges

### Concentration Risk (Low-Moderate)
- While diversified across three segments, California still represents a significant portion of earnings
- LNG revenues are subject to global commodity price fluctuations

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- **AI and Data Center Demand**: Sempra is uniquely positioned among utilities to capture the surge in electricity demand from AI/data centers, particularly through Oncor in Texas
- **LNG Growth Catalyst**: Successful execution of Port Arthur LNG and Cameron LNG expansion could drive significant incremental earnings and cash flow
- **Above-Average Growth**: 6–8% EPS growth guidance exceeds utility sector norms, justifying a premium valuation
- **$10B Infrastructure Sale**: Enhances balance sheet flexibility and de-risks the capital spending program
- **Earnings Beat Track Record**: Consistent history of meeting or exceeding estimates, with FY2025 guidance raised to the high end

### Bear Case
- **California Regulatory Headwinds**: The $471 million Track 2 impact is a significant drag, and future rate cases could further pressure the California segment
- **Valuation Premium**: Trading at 21x forward earnings vs. sector average of 17–19x leaves limited upside if growth expectations are not met
- **Rising Rate Environment**: Higher interest rates increase the cost of capital for Sempra's multi-billion-dollar capital program
- **Below-Average Dividend Yield**: Income investors may find better yields elsewhere in the utility sector
- **LNG Execution Risk**: Port Arthur LNG faces permitting, construction, and commercial risks

## Conclusion

Sempra represents a **moderate-conviction Hold** at current levels. The company's diversified utility and infrastructure portfolio, strong Texas growth story, and LNG optionality provide a compelling long-term narrative. However, the stock's premium valuation (21.2x forward P/E), recent California regulatory setbacks ($471M earnings impact), and proximity to its 52-week high ($95.72) limit near-term upside potential.

For new investors, a more attractive entry point would be in the $80–$85 range, where the risk/reward becomes more favorable. For existing holders, the stock's defensive characteristics, consistent dividend, and above-average growth profile support a continued hold. Investors seeking higher income may want to consider covered call strategies (as noted by options analysis suggesting yield enhancement from 2.9% to 9.3%) to supplement the below-average dividend yield.

**Overall Score: 6.5/10** — A quality utility company with genuine growth catalysts, but current valuation and regulatory risks warrant caution.
