## Executive Summary

United Therapeutics reported Q4 2025 EPS of $7.70, beating consensus of $6.78 by 14%, with earnings up 24% YoY. The stock has appreciated 32% over the trailing six months and ~100% from its 52-week low of $267. Management announced a $2B share repurchase authorization and priced a $1.5B accelerated share repurchase (ASR), signaling confidence in intrinsic value and willingness to return capital aggressively at current levels. H.C. Wainwright raised its target to $600, maintaining a Buy rating.

The company's core franchise remains prostacyclin analogs for pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH), led by Tyvaso (inhaled treprostinil), with Phase III TETON-2 data representing a potential expansion into idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis (IPF), a significantly larger addressable market. The main competitive threat is Liquidia's Yutrepia, which has captured 16% market share since its June 2025 launch. At 17.7x forward earnings with a 0.78 beta and no dividend, UTHR offers a relatively defensive biotech profile with genuine growth catalysts, appropriate for the current macro regime where healthcare equities provide insulation from stagflation dynamics.

## Company Overview

United Therapeutics is a Silver Spring, Maryland-based biotechnology company focused on pulmonary, oncologic, and organ manufacturing therapies. The company markets four commercial products centered on the prostacyclin pathway for PAH: Tyvaso (inhaled and nebulized treprostinil), Remodulin (subcutaneous/IV treprostinil), Orenitram (oral treprostinil), and Unituxin (dinutuximab for neuroblastoma). The company's longer-term pipeline includes xenotransplantation (genetically modified pig organs) and the TETON program exploring treprostinil in IPF.

Market capitalization stands at approximately $23.5B, implying roughly 43.8M diluted shares at the current price of $536.12. The company pays no dividend but is returning capital through buybacks.

## Growth Analysis

**Tyvaso Franchise:** The primary near-term growth driver. Tyvaso DPI (dry powder inhaler) simplifies administration relative to nebulized Tyvaso, which should support share-of-new-starts and switching. Revenue growth has been strong enough to drive a 32% stock appreciation over six months and 24% EPS growth.

**TETON-2 (IPF Expansion):** Phase III data in IPF represents the most significant pipeline catalyst. IPF affects approximately 100,000 patients in the US, with limited treatment options (pirfenidone and nintedanib are the only approved therapies). Positive TETON-2 data would roughly double UTHR's addressable market. The news characterizes this as opening "a potential new market in IPF."

**Xenotransplantation:** UTHR's long-duration bet on genetically modified pig organs for transplant remains pre-commercial but represents a genuinely large optionality — the organ transplant market is constrained by supply, creating enormous potential if the technology proves safe and efficacious. This is a 5-10 year horizon asset with binary risk/reward.

**Liquidia Competition:** Yutrepia's 16% market share capture since June 2025 is the primary offset to growth. A generic treprostinil competitor eroding share from the prostacyclin franchise creates pricing pressure and share loss risk that partially offsets volume growth from new indications.

## Valuation Assessment

At $536.12 and ~17.7x forward earnings, UTHR trades at a discount to both the broad biotech sector (~25-30x) and the S&P 500 (~20-22x forward). The discount likely reflects product concentration risk and the Yutrepia competitive threat.

Annualized EPS of ~$30 against a $536 share price yields a 5.6% earnings yield. Adding the ~6-7% annualized buyback yield (if the full $2B is executed over 12 months), the total shareholder yield approaches 12%, which is attractive for a company with 24% earnings growth and pipeline optionality.

H.C. Wainwright's $600 target implies ~12% upside from current levels. Without detailed analyst consensus data, I estimate a reasonable target range of $400-$600, with the low end reflecting a scenario where Yutrepia competition accelerates and TETON-2 fails, and the high end reflecting continued Tyvaso growth plus positive TETON-2 readout.

A simple DCF framework: if EPS grows at 10-15% annually for the next 3-5 years (conservative given 24% current growth, offset by competition), a terminal 15-18x P/E yields a fair value range of $520-$650. The current price sits in the lower half of this range.

## Competitive Landscape

**PAH Market:** UTHR dominates the prostacyclin segment but faces intensifying competition. Liquidia's Yutrepia at 16% share in under a year is a meaningful competitive development. Yutrepia is an inhaled treprostinil product approved under the 505(b)(2) pathway, meaning it directly competes with Tyvaso on mechanism. The key question is whether Yutrepia share gains stabilize or continue to accelerate.

**Broader PAH Competition:** Johnson & Johnson's Opsumit (macitentan) and other endothelin receptor antagonists compete in the broader PAH market but target a different pathway. UTHR's prostacyclin products are typically used in more severe disease or as add-on therapy, providing some insulation from non-prostacyclin competition.

**IPF:** If TETON-2 succeeds, UTHR would compete with Roche (pirfenidone) and Boehringer Ingelheim (nintedanib). Both existing therapies slow disease progression but have meaningful side effect profiles, leaving room for a treprostinil-based alternative if efficacy and tolerability data are favorable.

**Xenotransplantation:** No direct commercial competitors at comparable stage. eGenesis is the closest competitor in xenotransplantation, but the field remains broadly pre-commercial.

## Risk Assessment

**Product Concentration:** UTHR derives the majority of revenue from treprostinil-based products. Tyvaso alone likely accounts for >50% of revenue. Any safety signal, manufacturing disruption, or accelerated competitive erosion (particularly from Yutrepia) would disproportionately impact the business.

**Liquidia/Yutrepia:** 16% share capture in 8 months is rapid. If Yutrepia reaches 25-30% share over the next 12-18 months, Tyvaso's growth trajectory flattens materially. Pricing pressure may follow.

**TETON-2 Binary Risk:** A Phase III failure in IPF would remove the largest pipeline catalyst and likely result in a 15-20% correction, as some IPF optionality is embedded in the current price.

**Macro Insulation:** UTHR's products treat life-threatening chronic conditions — demand is inelastic to economic cycles. The 0.78 beta confirms this. In a stagfllationary environment with consumer spending contracting, UTHR's revenue is insulated because PAH patients cannot defer treatment.

**Regulatory:** Specialty pharma faces ongoing drug pricing reform risk, including potential Medicare negotiation under the IRA. Tyvaso's pricing power could face government-driven compression over the medium term.

**Insider Activity:** Multiple Form 4 filings in February-March 2026 (20 filings in the last 3 weeks). Without detailed transaction types (buy vs. sell vs. option exercise), the volume of filings is notable but inconclusive. The cluster around the Q4 earnings date (Feb 25-26) likely reflects scheduled equity compensation transactions rather than discretionary trading.

- $2B buyback ($1.5B ASR priced immediately) provides direct EPS accretion of 6-7% and a floor under the stock

- TETON-2 Phase III fails, removing the largest pipeline catalyst and triggering a 15-20% derating

- Buyback at $530+ may prove poorly timed if competitive dynamics deteriorate (management buying high)
- Stock has already appreciated 100% from 52-week low; much of the near-term good news may be priced

## Conclusion

UTHR offers a combination of characteristics that is uncommon in the current market: growing earnings (24% YoY), below-market valuation (17.7x forward), aggressive capital return (8.5% of market cap in buybacks), meaningful pipeline optionality (IPF), and defensive beta (0.78). The Liquidia competitive threat and TETON-2 binary risk are the primary offsets, but at current valuation levels, UTHR is priced for the competitive risk without full credit for the pipeline optionality.

The stock scores well on the calibration framework's criteria for a BUY: it is in the Healthcare sector (historically strong BUY accuracy at +3.48% average), has a forward P/E below 20x (clearing the FCF yield threshold), and operates in a non-discretionary end market insulated from the macro deterioration. The $1.5B ASR provides a near-term mechanical catalyst for EPS accretion.

**Recommendation: BUY** with a fair value range of $560-$600 over the next 6-12 months, assuming stable competitive dynamics and no material negative TETON-2 readout. Position sizing should reflect the binary pipeline risk; this is a conviction position but not a maximum-conviction one.
