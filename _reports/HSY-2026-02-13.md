## Executive Summary

The Hershey Company is one of America's most iconic consumer staples businesses, commanding leading market share in U.S. chocolate and confectionery. The company has recently navigated one of the most challenging commodity cost environments in its history, with cocoa and sugar prices reaching multi-decade highs through 2024-2025, compressing margins and earnings despite pricing actions. The stock has recently rallied sharply from its 52-week low of $150.04, climbing near $226, as cocoa spot prices have begun to moderate—fueling expectations of a meaningful margin recovery in the second half of 2026 and beyond.

However, at a trailing P/E ratio of approximately 53x, the stock already appears to discount a significant recovery. Hershey's forward P/E of ~53x is well above its historical 5-year average of approximately 24-26x and far exceeds food sector peers trading at 18-22x. While the company's brand strength, pricing power, and loyal consumer base provide a durable competitive moat, investors must weigh whether the current valuation adequately compensates for risks including still-volatile commodity costs, potential volume elasticity from aggressive price hikes, and limited near-term earnings growth. On balance, we view HSY as a **Hold** at current levels—a quality franchise priced for a recovery that must still materialize.

The bull case rests on cocoa/sugar deflation driving outsized EPS growth in late 2026 and 2027, while the bear case centers on a sustained high-cost environment, consumer trade-down, and valuation compression if earnings disappoint.

## Company Overview

**The Hershey Company** (NYSE: HSY), founded in 1894 and headquartered in Hershey, Pennsylvania, is the largest chocolate manufacturer in North America and a leading snack company globally. The company operates through three reportable segments:

- **North America Confectionery** — The core segment, including iconic brands such as Hershey's, Reese's, Kit Kat (U.S. license), Kisses, Jolly Rancher, and Twizzlers.
- **North America Salty Snacks** — Includes SkinnyPop, Dot's Pretzels, and Pirate's Booty, acquired to diversify beyond chocolate.
- **International** — Operations in over 80 countries, though the U.S. remains the dominant market (>85% of revenue).

Hershey's market capitalization stands at approximately **$45.9 billion**. The company pays a dividend yielding approximately **2.5%** and has a notably low beta of **0.14**, reflecting its defensive, consumer staples profile.

- **Market Cap**: ~$45.9 billion
- **52-Week Range**: $150.04 – $234.87

The trailing P/E of ~53x implies that full-year 2025 EPS was approximately **$4.26** (calculated as $226.26 / 53.07), which represents a dramatic decline from Hershey's 2023 peak EPS of approximately $9.00+. This earnings compression is primarily attributable to soaring cocoa input costs that more than doubled from 2023 to 2025, alongside elevated sugar prices.

### Margin Pressure
While detailed financial statements were not available in the data feed, the significant earnings decline (EPS roughly halving from peak levels) and news reports confirm that gross margins contracted materially in 2024-2025. Hershey implemented multiple rounds of price increases to partially offset costs, but these were insufficient to fully protect profitability while also creating some volume/mix headwinds.

### Balance Sheet & Cash Flow
Hershey historically maintains a moderate leverage profile typical of consumer staples companies (net debt/EBITDA typically in the 2-3x range). The company has maintained its dividend through the downturn (current yield ~2.5%), which signals management's confidence in cash flow sustainability, though the payout ratio on depressed earnings is elevated. Hershey's consistent filing of 10-K and 10-Q reports on schedule suggests no material reporting or liquidity concerns.

### Dividend
Hershey has a long track record of dividend payments and increases, making it a staple in income-oriented portfolios. At the current price, the ~2.5% yield is moderate but well-supported by the company's cash-generative business model, even during periods of margin stress.

## Growth Analysis

### Near-Term Outlook (2026)
Hershey reported Q4 and full-year 2025 results on February 5, 2026, and provided a 2026 outlook. The stock's subsequent rally to a 52-week high suggests the guidance was received positively, likely reflecting:

1. **Cocoa/sugar deflation**: Spot cocoa prices have begun declining from their 2024-2025 peaks. While Hershey hedges commodity costs, there is typically a 6-12 month lag before lower spot prices flow through to reported costs.
2. **Pricing carryover**: Prior price increases remain embedded in the revenue base, meaning even moderate volume recovery would drive positive operating leverage.
3. **Salty snacks growth**: The snacks portfolio provides a non-cocoa-exposed growth vector.

### Medium-Term Growth Drivers
- **Margin normalization**: If cocoa returns toward $5,000-$6,000/ton (from peaks above $10,000), gross margin expansion could drive double-digit EPS growth in 2027-2028.
- **International expansion**: Hershey remains underrepresented internationally compared to peers like Mondelez and Nestlé, providing a long runway.
- **Innovation & portfolio management**: Continued premiumization and snacking trends favor Hershey's portfolio.

### Growth Concerns
- **Volume elasticity**: Cumulative price increases of 15-25% over two years may permanently alter consumer behavior, with trade-down to private label or smaller pack sizes.
- **Limited top-line growth**: In a mature U.S. confectionery market, organic revenue growth is typically 3-5%, meaning EPS growth is heavily dependent on margin recovery rather than structural top-line acceleration.

| EV/EBITDA (est.) | ~25-30x | ~18-20x | ~14-17x |

The elevated P/E is partly an artifact of depressed 2025 earnings. If we assume normalized EPS recovers to $7.00-$8.00 by 2027 (still below the 2023 peak), the stock trades at approximately **28-32x forward normalized earnings**—still a premium but more reasonable for a high-quality consumer staples company.

However, the average analyst price target as of December 2025 was **$192.26**, with a range of $132-$231. At $226, the stock has already exceeded the average target and trades near the top of the range. This suggests limited upside from current levels unless estimates are significantly revised higher.

Morgan Stanley's upgrade to Overweight (December 2025) was a notable positive catalyst, but the stock's subsequent 25%+ rally may have already captured this thesis.

## Competitive Landscape

### Market Position — Score: 8/10
Hershey dominates U.S. chocolate with approximately **35-37% market share**, roughly double its nearest competitor (Mars). Key competitive advantages include:

- **Brand portfolio**: Reese's is the #1 confectionery brand in the U.S.; Hershey's, Kisses, and Kit Kat are household names.
- **Distribution network**: Decades of retail relationships and shelf-space dominance create high barriers to entry.
- **Seasonal dominance**: Hershey captures outsized share during Halloween, Valentine's Day, Easter, and Christmas—critical selling occasions.
- **Salty snacks diversification**: Acquisitions of SkinnyPop, Dot's, and Pirate's Booty provide exposure to the faster-growing snacking category.

### Competitive Threats
- **Mars/Wrigley**: The largest private competitor, with a global presence that dwarfs Hershey's international footprint.
- **Mondelez (MDLZ)**: Owner of Cadbury and Oreo, competing across chocolate and snacks globally.
- **Private label**: Rising in periods of inflation-driven price increases, particularly in non-chocolate categories.
- **Health & wellness trends**: Long-term headwind for traditional confectionery, though indulgence occasions remain resilient.

## Risk Assessment

### Commodity Risk (High)
Cocoa and sugar represent Hershey's largest input costs. While spot prices have begun moderating, cocoa remains at historically elevated levels. Any reversal in the deflationary trend—due to weather events, West African supply disruptions, or speculative trading—would delay margin recovery.

### Volume/Demand Risk (Moderate)
Cumulative price increases create risk of sustained volume declines. If consumers permanently shift purchasing habits (smaller sizes, fewer occasions, private label substitution), Hershey's revenue base may be structurally lower.

### Concentration Risk (Moderate)
Hershey derives ~85%+ of revenue from North America and is heavily dependent on chocolate/confectionery. This geographic and category concentration exposes the company to U.S. consumer spending trends and regulatory changes (e.g., sugar taxes, labeling requirements).

### Regulatory & ESG (Moderate)
- Increasing scrutiny on sustainable cocoa sourcing and child labor in supply chains.
- Potential for sugar/junk food regulation (taxes, advertising restrictions) in key markets.
- GLP-1 weight-loss drugs represent an emerging headwind, though the impact on confectionery consumption remains uncertain and likely modest in the near term.

### Valuation Risk (High)
At 53x trailing earnings, any disappointment in the recovery trajectory could trigger significant multiple compression. The stock has rallied ~50% from its 52-week low, and much of the bullish thesis appears priced in.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- **Cocoa deflation drives outsized margin recovery**: If cocoa prices continue declining, Hershey could see gross margin expansion of 300-500bps over the next 12-18 months, driving EPS toward $8.00+ by 2027.
- **Volume recovery**: As pricing stabilizes, consumer demand normalizes, and Hershey recaptures lost volume through innovation and promotions.
- **Defensive positioning**: In an uncertain macro environment, Hershey's low beta (0.14) and consistent dividend make it an attractive defensive holding.
- **Institutional accumulation**: Recent filings show institutions like TD Private Client Wealth and Allstate adding positions, suggesting smart money is positioning for recovery.
- **Snacks diversification**: Salty snacks portfolio provides a non-cocoa growth engine and improves portfolio resilience.

### Bear Case
- **Cocoa prices remain elevated or re-accelerate**: Structural supply constraints in West Africa could keep cocoa above $7,000/ton for longer, preventing meaningful margin recovery.
- **Valuation overshoot**: At 53x earnings, any earnings miss or guidance disappointment could trigger a sharp correction. The stock has already exceeded the average analyst target of $192.
- **GLP-1 impact**: Widespread adoption of weight-loss medications could structurally reduce snacking/confectionery consumption over a multi-year horizon.
- **Consumer trade-down**: Permanent volume loss from aggressive pricing could impair the franchise value of core brands.
- **Limited international growth**: Unlike peers, Hershey has struggled to build meaningful international scale, limiting long-term growth potential.

## Conclusion

**Rating: Hold**

The Hershey Company is undeniably a high-quality consumer staples franchise with iconic brands, dominant market share, and a proven business model. The emerging commodity cost deflation in cocoa and sugar creates a legitimate margin recovery catalyst that could drive meaningful EPS growth over the next 18-24 months.

However, at ~53x trailing earnings and having already rallied ~50% from its 52-week low, the stock's valuation appears to reflect much of this recovery potential. The average analyst price target of $192 as of December 2025 suggests the market has moved ahead of consensus expectations. For new investors, we would recommend waiting for either (1) a pullback toward $190-$200 to provide a more attractive entry point, or (2) concrete evidence from upcoming quarterly reports that margin recovery is materializing faster than expected.

For existing shareholders, the improving commodity backdrop and Hershey's defensive characteristics support maintaining positions, but adding at current levels carries elevated valuation risk. The 2.5% dividend yield provides modest income while waiting for the earnings recovery to validate the current stock price.

**Overall Score: 5.8/10** — A quality franchise at a full valuation, with recovery upside balanced by execution and commodity risks.
