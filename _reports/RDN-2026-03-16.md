## Executive Summary

Radian Group is a private mortgage insurance (MI) company trading at 7.5x forward earnings and 0.98x price-to-book, a substantial discount to the multi-line insurance industry average of 2.33x P/B. Q4 2025 adjusted operating EPS of $1.16 beat consensus by ~5%, and the MI business was characterized as "strong" by management. Full-year EPS of $4.39 on a share base of 136M implies roughly $598M in annual earnings power on a ~$4.5B market cap.

The valuation is cheap for a reason. Revenue grew only 0.8% YoY in Q4, housing turnover remains suppressed by mortgage rates at 7-month highs (and structurally elevated given bond safe-haven failure pushing yields higher), and the macro backdrop — confirmed stagflation with NFP at -92K and recession probability at 38-42% — directly threatens the credit quality of Radian's insured mortgage portfolio. CEO Thornberry sold 60,000 shares across three transactions from August to October 2025, and the Chief Digital Officer liquidated his entire position in December 2025. These are not signals of conviction from those with the best visibility.

The stock merits a HOLD at current levels. The 3.1% dividend yield and sub-8x earnings provide meaningful downside support, but the macro regime is hostile to mortgage insurance, and the absence of analyst coverage data limits external validation of the bull case.

## Company Overview

Radian Group Inc., headquartered in Wayne, Pennsylvania, is primarily a private mortgage insurer. Its core subsidiary, Radian Guaranty, provides mortgage insurance on residential first-lien loans, protecting lenders and investors from default-related losses. The company also has smaller businesses in real estate services and title insurance through its Radian Real Estate segment. Market cap is approximately $4.5B with ~136M shares outstanding, trading on the NYSE.

## Financial Analysis

**Earnings:** Q4 2025 net income was $154.8M, or $1.12 per share GAAP ($1.16 adjusted operating). The adjusted figure beat consensus of $1.10 by 5.5% and improved 2.7% YoY. Full-year trailing EPS is $4.39.

**Revenue:** Q4 2025 operating revenues of $302M increased 0.8% YoY — effectively flat. Revenue was actually down $1.37M from the prior year period per Benzinga's earnings summary. This near-zero revenue growth in a period when the insured portfolio was still benefiting from residual pandemic-era origination vintages suggests the organic growth engine is stalling.

**Dividend:** The board declared a $0.255/share quarterly dividend in February 2026, implying $1.02 annualized and a 3.1% yield at $32.86. This is well-covered at a ~23% payout ratio on $4.39 EPS.

**Balance Sheet:** The 0.98x forward P/B ratio indicates the stock is trading approximately at book value. For a mortgage insurer, book value is the critical metric because it represents the claims-paying capacity of the insurance subsidiary. Trading near book means the market is assigning approximately zero franchise premium to Radian's ongoing business.

**Share Count:** At 136M shares, down from historical levels, indicating ongoing buyback activity that reduces outstanding equity over time.

**Key Financial Limitation:** Detailed financial statements (income statement, balance sheet, cash flow) were not available in the data feed for granular analysis of loss reserves, risk-to-capital ratios, or investment portfolio composition. These are the most important metrics for evaluating MI credit risk and are absent from this analysis.

## Growth Analysis

Growth prospects for Radian are constrained by the current and projected housing market environment:

**Mortgage Origination Volume:** The primary driver of new insurance written (NIW) is purchase mortgage origination volume. With mortgage rates at 7-month highs and the bond safe-haven failure pushing yields structurally higher, the sub-6% mortgage rate window is closed for all of 2026. This mechanically suppresses both purchase originations (affordability) and refinance activity (rate disincentive). LEN reported -13% revenue and -53% EPS in Q1. TOL's CEO sold $11.5M in stock. The housing market is in structural contraction.

**Persistency vs. NIW:** Existing mortgage insurance policies remain in force longer when homeowners cannot refinance, which supports premium income on the existing book (high persistency). However, this benefit has a ceiling — the existing book only runs off more slowly; it does not grow. Net positive persistency effects are already largely priced in given RDN has been operating in a high-rate environment since 2022.

**Revenue Trajectory:** The 0.8% YoY revenue growth in Q4 2025 is the key data point. At essentially zero growth with no visible catalyst for acceleration (mortgage rates rising, not falling; housing starts declining; consumer spending contracting), forward revenue growth is likely flat to slightly negative.

**Radian Real Estate Services:** The title and real estate services segment provides modest diversification but is also tied to housing transaction volumes. It does not provide a counter-cyclical growth engine.

**Growth Score Rationale:** 5.5 reflects flat current growth with limited visibility on catalysts. The business is stable but not expanding.

## Valuation Assessment

RDN presents one of the more compelling pure valuation cases in the insurance sector:

- **Forward P/E:** 7.5x — significantly below S&P 500 (~18-20x) and below many financial sector peers
- **Trailing P/E:** 7.5x — same as forward, implying consensus expects flat earnings (consistent with the revenue story)
- **Price/Book:** 0.98x vs. industry average 2.33x — a 58% discount to peers
- **Dividend Yield:** 3.1% — above market average, well-covered
- **52-Week Range:** $29.32 - $38.84; current price $32.86 (15% below 52-week high, 12% above low)

The discount to book value is the most telling metric. When a financial company trades below book, the market is either (a) expecting future losses that will erode book value, (b) assigning negative franchise value to the business, or (c) the stock is genuinely undervalued. In a stagflationary environment with rising recession probability (38-42%), option (a) is plausible — the market may be anticipating elevated claims on the MI portfolio if unemployment rises materially above 4.4%.

However, at 7.5x earnings with a 3.1% yield, the bar for positive returns is low. Even with flat-to-slightly-declining earnings, the dividend alone generates meaningful return if the multiple holds.

## Competitive Landscape

The private MI industry is a concentrated oligopoly with six primary players: MGIC Investment (MTG), Radian (RDN), Essent Group (ESNT), NMI Holdings (NMIH), Enact Holdings (ACT), and Arch Capital's MI division. The competitive dynamics are relatively stable:

**Moat Assessment:** Moderate. MI is a regulated business with state licensing requirements, capital adequacy standards (PMIERs), and established lender relationships creating switching costs. However, the product is largely commoditized — lenders choose MI providers primarily on pricing, service quality, and credit availability, limiting pricing power.

**Market Position:** Radian is one of the two legacy MI players (with MGIC) and maintains a top-3 market share position. The MI business was described as "strong" in Q4 results, suggesting competitive position is stable.

**GSE Risk:** The MI business exists because Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac require it on loans with less than 20% down payment. Any change to GSE policy, housing finance reform, or FHA pricing adjustments could structurally alter MI demand. This is a permanent background risk.

## Risk Assessment

**Housing Market Deterioration (PRIMARY RISK):** Mortgage rates at 7-month highs and rising → reduced origination → lower NIW → stagnant-to-declining revenue. If recession materializes (38-42% probability), unemployment rising above 5% would trigger elevated MI claims. The stagflationary macro regime is the single most hostile environment for mortgage insurers: inflation keeps rates high (suppressing volumes) while economic weakness increases defaults (driving losses).

**Credit Quality:** Without access to detailed loss reserve data, the credit quality of the insured portfolio cannot be assessed granularly. However, the post-2010 mortgage vintages have historically exhibited strong credit performance due to tighter underwriting standards. The key question is whether the current macro shock — oil at $98-108, -92K payrolls, consumer spending contraction — triggers sufficient unemployment to breach the credit quality of 2020-2024 vintage loans.

**Insider Selling:** CEO Thornberry sold 60,000 shares across three transactions (August, September, October 2025) totaling ~$2.1M. The Chief Digital Officer sold his entire 13,957-share position in December 2025. General Counsel Hoffman sold 10,000 shares in September 2025. There are no insider purchases in the data. This pattern — consistent selling by multiple C-suite executives with no offsetting buys — is a yellow flag. It does not confirm a negative thesis, but it provides zero insider conviction signal.

**Rate Environment Sensitivity:** Radian benefits from higher rates on its investment portfolio income but suffers from lower MI origination volume. The net effect depends on the magnitude and persistence of rate changes. In the current regime, the volume headwind likely dominates.

**Regulatory Risk:** Changes to PMIERs capital requirements, GSE reform, or FHA pricing could alter the competitive landscape. The Section 301 trade probes creating broad economic uncertainty compound this regulatory overhang.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- 7.5x earnings and 0.98x book represent meaningful discount to intrinsic value for a stable, cash-generative business
- 3.1% dividend yield well-covered at 23% payout ratio provides income floor
- High persistency in existing MI book supports current revenue even as NIW slows
- Post-2010 mortgage vintages have strong credit quality, and loss experience may remain benign even in mild recession
- Any positive development in housing (rate cuts, GDP stabilization) would directly benefit volumes and sentiment
- Zacks "Strong Buy" rating (per news) suggests some analyst community support

### Bear Case
- Revenue essentially flat at +0.8% with no visible growth catalyst for 12+ months
- Mortgage rates structurally elevated due to bond safe-haven failure, closing the window for origination recovery
- Recession probability 38-42% → unemployment above 5% would trigger elevated MI claims, eroding book value and justifying the current sub-1x P/B
- CEO and multiple executives selling consistently with no insider buying
- Homebuilders (LEN -53% EPS, TOL CEO selling $11.5M) are the leading indicator; MI follows with a lag
- No analyst price targets available limits external validation
- Detailed financial statement data unavailable for thorough loss reserve and capital adequacy analysis

## Conclusion

Radian Group at 7.5x earnings and 0.98x book is cheap, and the Q4 2025 earnings beat confirms the business remains fundamentally sound in the current environment. However, the macro regime is the worst possible combination for a mortgage insurer: inflation keeping rates high (volume suppression) and growth deteriorating (credit risk). Revenue growth of 0.8% confirms the volume story is already playing out. Multiple insiders selling with zero buying reinforces the caution.

The stock warrants a **HOLD**. The valuation provides meaningful downside support — it's difficult to see sustained trading below 0.85-0.90x book absent actual loss deterioration — and the 3.1% dividend provides carry while waiting. A BUY upgrade would require either: (1) evidence of housing market stabilization (mortgage applications trending up, builder confidence recovering), (2) visibility on Fed rate cuts that would reduce mortgage rates, or (3) a price decline to ~$29-30 (approximately 0.88x current book) providing sufficient margin of safety against the recession scenario. At current prices, the risk-reward is balanced, not compelling.
