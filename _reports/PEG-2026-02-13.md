## Executive Summary

Public Service Enterprise Group (PEG) is a predominantly regulated energy company headquartered in New Jersey, operating through its regulated utility subsidiary PSE&G and its wholesale power generation arm PSEG Power, which owns interests in three nuclear plants and clean energy projects. The company currently trades at approximately $84.54 per share with a market capitalization of ~$42.2 billion, a trailing P/E of 20.5x, and a forward P/E of 24.0x. With a beta of only 0.60, PEG offers a defensive investment profile complemented by a roughly 3.0% dividend yield.

The investment thesis for PEG centers on its strategic positioning at the intersection of regulated utility stability and nuclear generation upside. The company has been consistently investing in infrastructure modernization and expanding its nuclear generation capacity — a theme that has gained significant momentum as data center demand and clean energy mandates grow. Major Wall Street firms including UBS (Buy) and JP Morgan (Overweight) maintain favorable coverage, with an average one-year price target of $92.85, implying approximately 10% upside from current levels.

However, the stock's valuation has expanded meaningfully, with the forward P/E of 24.0x sitting above historical utility averages. Investors must weigh the premium valuation against PEG's tangible growth catalysts, its defensive characteristics, and the broader interest rate environment. The limited availability of detailed financial statements in our dataset constrains the depth of quantitative analysis, though publicly available earnings data and analyst coverage provide a reasonable basis for assessment.

## Company Overview

Public Service Enterprise Group Inc (NYSE: PEG) is one of the few US utilities with a diversified business mix that includes:

- **PSE&G (Public Service Electric and Gas Company):** A regulated utility providing electricity and gas delivery services across New Jersey. This segment provides stable, predictable earnings driven by rate base growth and regulatory mechanisms.
- **PSEG Power:** Owns all or a share of three nuclear power plants and clean energy projects. This segment has attracted significant investor attention given the nuclear renaissance driven by AI/data center power demand and carbon-free energy mandates.

The company is headquartered in Newark, New Jersey, and operates in one of the most densely populated and economically significant service territories in the United States.

## Growth Analysis

### Infrastructure Modernization
PEG continues to invest consistently in infrastructure modernization to enhance service reliability for its customers. Regulated capital expenditure programs in New Jersey support rate base growth, which translates directly into regulated earnings growth. Utility CapEx programs in areas like grid modernization, gas infrastructure replacement, and clean energy investments provide multi-year earnings visibility.

### Nuclear Generation Expansion
Perhaps the most compelling growth narrative for PEG is its nuclear fleet. As one of the largest nuclear operators among regulated utility parent companies, PEG stands to benefit from:

- **Data Center Power Demand:** The explosion in AI and data center development has created unprecedented demand for reliable, baseload, carbon-free power — exactly what nuclear provides.
- **Clean Energy Mandates:** Federal and state policies increasingly favor carbon-free generation sources, positioning nuclear as a critical component of the energy mix.
- **Potential Nuclear Premium Contracts:** Nuclear operators have the potential to secure premium power purchase agreements with large technology companies seeking 24/7 clean energy.

### Earnings Growth Trajectory
The Q3 2025 earnings beat ($1.13 vs. $1.02 expected) suggests the company is on a solid earnings trajectory. Analyst targets imply confidence in continued earnings growth, though the gap between trailing P/E (20.5x) and forward P/E (24.0x) warrants monitoring for potential earnings headwinds or one-time items affecting comparability.

## Competitive Landscape

### Market Position
PEG occupies a relatively unique position among US utilities:

1. **Regulated Utility + Nuclear:** Few utilities combine a large regulated distribution utility with significant nuclear generation assets. This dual structure provides both earnings stability and growth optionality.
2. **Premium Service Territory:** New Jersey is a densely populated, affluent state with strong demand for electricity and gas services. The regulatory environment, while occasionally contentious, has generally been supportive of utility investment.
3. **Nuclear Fleet as Competitive Moat:** Nuclear power plants are essentially irreplaceable assets — no new plants are being constructed in the near term, creating scarcity value for existing operators.

### Peer Comparison
Key peers include Exelon (EXC), Dominion Energy (D), Duke Energy (DUK), and NextEra Energy (NEE). PEG differentiates itself through its concentrated nuclear exposure and its high-quality New Jersey service territory. Compared to pure regulated utilities, PEG offers more growth upside; compared to merchant generators, it offers more earnings stability.

## Risk Assessment

### Regulatory Risk (Moderate)
As a predominantly regulated utility, PEG is exposed to regulatory decisions regarding rate cases, allowed returns on equity, and capital expenditure approvals. New Jersey's regulatory environment has been generally constructive but can introduce uncertainty.

### Interest Rate Sensitivity (Moderate)
Utility stocks are sensitive to interest rate movements. With a beta of only 0.60, PEG is less volatile than the broader market but could face headwinds if long-term interest rates rise, as higher rates increase borrowing costs and make dividend yields relatively less attractive.

### Nuclear Operational Risk (Low-Moderate)
Nuclear plants require significant ongoing investment, are subject to NRC oversight, and face risks from unplanned outages. However, PEG's nuclear fleet has generally operated reliably.

### Valuation Risk (Moderate-High)
At 24x forward earnings, PEG is priced for growth that must be delivered. Any disappointment in nuclear monetization, regulatory outcomes, or broader earnings growth could lead to multiple compression.

### Concentration Risk (Moderate)
PEG's operations are heavily concentrated in New Jersey, exposing the company to state-specific economic, weather, and regulatory conditions.

### ESG Considerations (Positive)
PEG's nuclear fleet is a significant positive from an ESG perspective, as nuclear generation produces zero direct carbon emissions. This positions the company favorably for clean energy mandates and ESG-focused investment flows.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- **Nuclear Renaissance:** Growing data center demand and clean energy mandates create significant upside for nuclear generation assets. PEG could secure premium contracts for carbon-free baseload power, driving earnings growth well above regulated utility averages.
- **Rate Base Growth:** Continued infrastructure investment in New Jersey supports mid-to-high single-digit regulated earnings growth.
- **Earnings Momentum:** Q3 2025 earnings beat demonstrates execution capability. If the company continues to exceed expectations, multiple expansion could continue.
- **Defensive Characteristics:** Low beta (0.60), ~3% dividend yield, and regulated earnings provide downside protection in volatile markets.
- **Analyst Support:** Major banks maintain Buy/Overweight ratings with meaningful upside to price targets.

### Bear Case
- **Elevated Valuation:** Forward P/E of 24x is above historical utility ranges and leaves little room for error. Any growth disappointment could trigger a significant de-rating.
- **Nuclear Monetization Uncertainty:** While nuclear optionality is compelling, the timeline and magnitude of additional earnings from data center contracts or clean energy premiums remain uncertain.
- **Interest Rate Headwinds:** A sustained higher-for-longer interest rate environment could compress utility valuations and increase financing costs.
- **Regulatory Uncertainty:** Adverse regulatory decisions in New Jersey could constrain rate base growth and returns on equity.
- **Limited Diversification:** Heavy concentration in one state limits geographic diversification.

## Conclusion

**Overall Recommendation: HOLD / Moderate Buy**

Public Service Enterprise Group is a high-quality utility with a compelling strategic positioning at the intersection of regulated utility stability and nuclear generation growth. The company's Q3 2025 earnings beat, consistent infrastructure investment, and nuclear fleet optionality provide a solid foundation for continued value creation. Analyst sentiment is broadly positive, with an average price target of $92.85 implying ~10% upside.

However, at a forward P/E of 24x, much of the positive thesis appears to be priced in. The stock trades at a meaningful premium to the utility sector average, and investors should be mindful that the nuclear monetization narrative, while compelling, carries execution and timing risks.

For income-oriented investors seeking defensive utility exposure with upside optionality, PEG remains an attractive portfolio holding. For value-oriented investors, a pullback toward the low-$80s or mid-$70s range would offer a more compelling entry point. The ~3% dividend yield provides reasonable compensation while waiting for the growth thesis to fully materialize.

**Overall Score: 6.8/10** — A solid defensive holding with attractive growth catalysts, modestly constrained by premium valuation and limited financial data granularity.
