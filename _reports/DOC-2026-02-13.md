## Executive Summary

Healthpeak Properties Inc (DOC) is a Denver-based healthcare-focused REIT with a market capitalization of approximately $12.1 billion, currently trading at $16.28 per share. The company owns, operates, and develops real estate for healthcare discovery and delivery, encompassing life science, medical office, and senior housing assets. DOC recently reported Q4 2025 results that beat consensus EPS estimates by 114%, reporting $0.47 vs. $0.22 expected, while simultaneously announcing a transformative strategic pivot — the planned IPO spin-off of its 34-community, 10,422-unit senior housing portfolio into a newly-formed REIT called Janus Living.

The investment case for DOC is defined by a tug-of-war between its attractive 7.2% dividend yield with improving occupancy/cash flow fundamentals on one side, and significant execution risk from its strategic restructuring on the other. Goldman Sachs initiated coverage with a Neutral rating and a $17 price target, citing improving occupancy and cash flow visibility. However, Seeking Alpha's recent downgrade to HOLD characterized the strategic pivot as "a masterclass in value destruction," criticizing the dual-CEO structure and external management of the new entity.

With limited detailed financial statement data available for deep quantitative analysis, this report relies heavily on available market data, recent earnings disclosures, analyst commentary, and news flow to assess DOC's investment merits. The stock currently sits 23.5% below its 52-week high of $21.28, presenting either a value opportunity or a value trap depending on execution of strategic initiatives.

## Company Overview

Healthpeak Properties Inc is a fully integrated real estate investment trust (REIT) headquartered in Denver, Colorado. The company focuses on three primary segments:

- **Life Science** — Laboratory and research facilities primarily serving biotech and pharmaceutical tenants
- **Medical Office** — Outpatient medical facilities often located on or near hospital campuses
- **Senior Housing** — Continuing care retirement communities and assisted living facilities (planned for spin-off)

The company trades on the NYSE under the ticker DOC (formerly PEAK, the ticker changed following the 2024 merger with Physicians Realty Trust). Healthpeak positions itself as a leading owner, operator, and developer of real estate for "healthcare discovery and delivery."

**Key Corporate Events:**
- **Janus Living IPO** (announced January 2026): Healthpeak plans to contribute its 34-community, 10,422-unit senior housing portfolio to a newly-formed REIT called Janus Living, to be taken public via IPO. This represents a significant strategic refocusing toward life science and medical office assets.
- **Q4 2025 Earnings Beat**: Reported EPS of $0.47 vs. $0.22 estimated (114% beat), with revenue up $21.4M year-over-year.

## Growth Analysis

### Secular Tailwinds

Healthpeak benefits from several structural growth drivers:

1. **Aging Demographics**: The U.S. senior population (65+) is projected to grow significantly through 2040, driving demand for senior housing and medical office space.
2. **Life Science Demand**: Despite cyclical biotech funding fluctuations, long-term demand for lab space remains robust as pharmaceutical R&D spending increases.
3. **Healthcare Spending Growth**: U.S. healthcare spending as a percentage of GDP continues to expand, supporting demand across all DOC segments.
4. **Improving Occupancy**: Goldman Sachs highlighted improving occupancy rates and cash flow visibility, suggesting the post-pandemic recovery in healthcare real estate continues.

### Revenue Growth

Q4 2025 revenue was up $21.4 million year-over-year, indicating positive organic growth. The specific growth rate cannot be calculated without full historical revenue data, but the trajectory is directionally positive.

### Strategic Growth Initiatives

The Janus Living spin-off is a double-edged sword for growth:
- **Bull case**: Allows management to focus capital allocation on higher-growth life science and medical office assets, potentially improving overall portfolio quality and growth profile.
- **Bear case**: Separates a recovering senior housing portfolio at a potentially inopportune time, and the external management structure raises governance concerns.

### Growth Score Considerations

While healthcare real estate has strong secular tailwinds, the company is in the middle of a major strategic transformation that creates near-term uncertainty. Lab space demand has faced headwinds from reduced biotech funding, though this appears to be improving. Overall growth prospects are moderate.

## Risk Assessment

### High-Priority Risks

1. **Execution Risk — Janus Living Spin-Off**: The externally managed, dual-CEO structure has drawn significant criticism. External management often leads to conflicts of interest and fee leakage. This is the single most impactful risk factor.

2. **Capital Allocation Concerns**: Seeking Alpha's characterization of the strategic pivot as "value destruction" reflects a broader market concern about management's capital allocation track record.

3. **Interest Rate Sensitivity**: With a beta of 1.096, DOC is roughly market-sensitive. REITs broadly are vulnerable to rising interest rates, which increase borrowing costs and make dividend yields less competitive vs. risk-free rates.

4. **Life Science Market Cyclicality**: Biotech funding slowdowns can reduce demand for lab space, DOC's key remaining growth asset class post-spin.

### Moderate Risks

5. **Dividend Sustainability Post-Spin**: The redistribution of assets and cash flows following the Janus Living IPO could pressure the go-forward dividend.

6. **Concentration Risk Post-Spin**: Removing senior housing assets concentrates the remaining portfolio in life science and medical office, reducing diversification.

7. **Regulatory Risk**: Healthcare real estate is indirectly exposed to changes in Medicare/Medicaid reimbursement and healthcare policy.

### Lower-Priority Risks

8. **ESG/Governance**: External management of Janus Living raises governance concerns typical of externally-managed REITs.
9. **Macro Sensitivity**: General economic slowdowns can impact occupancy and rental rates.

## Conclusion

**Rating: HOLD / Neutral**

Healthpeak Properties presents a complex investment case defined by its attractive income characteristics offset by significant strategic execution risk. The ~7.2% dividend yield is compelling for income-oriented investors, and the Q4 2025 earnings beat demonstrates underlying operational strength. However, the planned Janus Living spin-off introduces meaningful uncertainty around capital allocation, governance, and portfolio composition.

For existing shareholders, the recommendation is to **hold** and monitor execution of the Janus Living IPO and its impact on the remaining DOC portfolio's financial profile. The Goldman Sachs $17 price target provides a reasonable near-term fair value anchor, suggesting modest upside of ~4.4%.

For prospective investors, the risk-reward is balanced but not compelling enough for a strong buy recommendation. The stock should be considered only by income-focused investors with above-average risk tolerance who believe management will execute the strategic transition effectively. Key catalysts to watch include: (1) Janus Living IPO pricing and structure details, (2) go-forward DOC dividend policy post-spin, (3) life science occupancy trends, and (4) management commentary on capital allocation priorities.

**Fair Value Estimate**: $17-$18 per share (roughly in line with Goldman Sachs' target and representing 4-10% upside from current levels, plus the 7.2% dividend yield for a potential total return of 11-17% over 12 months if execution proceeds smoothly).
