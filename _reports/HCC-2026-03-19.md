## Executive Summary

Warrior Met Coal is a pure-play US metallurgical (steelmaking) coal producer operating mines in Alabama's Blue Creek seam. The company reported Q4 2025 EPS of $0.44 (missing consensus of $0.60 by 27%) on trailing twelve-month EPS of just $1.08, producing a trailing P/E of ~81x at the current price of ~$88. This extreme multiple reflects a cyclical earnings trough, with met coal prices soft throughout 2025. The critical investment thesis centers on the Blue Creek mine, which commenced longwall mining in Q4 2025 and is expected to drive 30%+ sales volume growth in 2026, structurally lowering costs and expanding production capacity.

The stock has appreciated approximately 77% over the trailing twelve months, from ~$49 in mid-March 2025 to ~$88 currently, with a sharp surge in November 2025 (from ~$66 to $81 in a single session on Nov 6) likely coinciding with Blue Creek production milestones. The 52-week high of $105.34 was reached in mid-January 2026, followed by a pullback to the current $88 level. The market is pricing in meaningful earnings recovery from Blue Creek volume growth, but at $4.8B market cap, the stock requires met coal prices to stabilize or improve alongside flawless execution on the ramp-up. Given the macro backdrop of stagflation, potential recession, and uncertain Chinese steel demand, the risk/reward at current levels is balanced.

## Company Overview

Warrior Met Coal operates three underground mines in Alabama, producing high-quality hard coking coal (HCC) used in steelmaking. The company emerged from bankruptcy in 2016 and has since established itself as one of the lowest-cost met coal producers in the US, benefiting from high-quality reserves in the Blue Creek coal seam. The Blue Creek mine expansion represents the most significant growth catalyst in the company's history, adding a third longwall operation that management expects will increase production capacity by approximately 4 million short tons annually at full run rate.

The company sells primarily to international steel producers, with significant exposure to European, Asian, and South American markets. This export orientation makes HCC sensitive to global steel production trends, seaborne met coal pricing (typically benchmarked to Australian indices), and USD strength.

## Growth Analysis

**Blue Creek Mine — The Central Thesis:**
- Longwall mining commenced Q4 2025.
- Management guided for 30%+ sales volume growth in 2026.
- The mine is expected to structurally lower the company's cost base due to favorable geology and modern equipment.
- At full run rate (expected over 2-3 years), Blue Creek adds approximately 4M short tons of annual capacity, roughly doubling total production from pre-expansion levels of ~7-8M tons.

**Volume growth vs. price realization:** The 30%+ volume increase is meaningful, but the earnings impact depends critically on met coal prices. At current depressed prices (~$180-200/ton benchmark), additional volume at lower marginal cost improves profitability modestly. If met coal recovers toward $250-300/ton (still well below 2022 peaks), the earnings leverage is substantial — potentially $8-12/share in normalized EPS, which would compress the P/E to 7-11x.

**Risk to volume growth:** Ramp-up execution risk is real. Underground longwall operations can face geological surprises, equipment commissioning delays, and labor constraints. The company's existing labor relations have been strained historically (a multi-year UMWA strike concluded in 2023).

## Competitive Landscape

**Strengths:**
- Alabama Blue Creek seam produces among the highest-quality hard coking coal globally (low ash, high fluidity, high CSR).
- Proximity to the Port of Mobile provides cost-effective access to seaborne markets.
- After Blue Creek ramp, Warrior will be one of the largest US met coal producers with ~10M+ tons of annual capacity.
- Lower quartile cost position on the global seaborne met coal cost curve.

**Weaknesses:**
- Single-basin concentration in Alabama — no geographic diversification.
- Pure-play met coal exposure with zero thermal coal or other commodity diversification.
- Exposure to seaborne pricing means competition with Australian producers (BHP, Anglo American) who have scale advantages.

**Industry dynamics:** Global met coal supply is structurally constrained as major mining companies have underinvested in new capacity due to ESG pressures and uncertain long-term steel demand. This supply constraint supports met coal prices above historical averages over time, but near-term demand weakness from Chinese property sector deleveraging and European industrial slowdown creates headwinds.

## Risk Assessment

**1. Met coal price cyclicality (HIGH):** The single most important variable. Seaborne met coal prices can swing 50-100% within a year. Current prices near $180-200/ton are below the cost of production for marginal producers, suggesting a floor, but recovery timing is uncertain. The world model's stagflationary environment with recession probability at 40-45% is negative for steel demand and met coal pricing.

**2. Blue Creek execution risk (MODERATE):** Longwall ramp-ups in underground mining carry meaningful operational risk. Delays of 3-6 months are common. Geological conditions, ventilation issues, or equipment problems could push full production attainment beyond management guidance.

**3. Global steel demand (HIGH):** China accounts for ~55% of global steel production. Chinese property sector weakness directly reduces met coal demand. India's steel sector is growing but remains a smaller buyer. European steel production is declining amid energy cost pressures and weak manufacturing PMIs. The current macro regime (oil at $119, potential rate hikes, consumer spending declining) is unfavorable for steel-intensive industrial activity.

**4. Labor relations (MODERATE):** The 2021-2023 UMWA strike was resolved, but tensions remain. A new CBA negotiation could disrupt production at any point.

**5. Forex risk (MODERATE):** As a US-dollar-cost producer selling into global markets where contracts are partially denominated in foreign currencies or benchmarked to Australian indices (in AUD), currency moves affect realized pricing.

**6. ESG/regulatory (LOW-MODERATE):** Met coal faces less regulatory pressure than thermal coal (steel has no near-term substitute for coke-based blast furnace production), but investor ESG screens restrict the shareholder base, potentially depressing the multiple.

**7. Insider activity:** Recent insider transactions are exclusively grant/award vesting and mandatory tax withholding — no voluntary open-market purchases or sales. CEO Scheller holds 367,793 shares (~$32M at current prices), representing meaningful alignment. However, the absence of open-market buying during the recent pullback from $105 to $85 is notable.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- Blue Creek ramp proceeds on schedule, adding 4M tons at costs below current mine averages.
- Met coal prices recover to $250-280/ton as global supply remains constrained and steel demand stabilizes.
- Normalized EPS of $10-12 yields a P/E of 7-9x at current prices.
- Blue Creek positions HCC as a premier met coal producer globally, potentially attracting M&A interest from diversified miners.
- The current macro environment with oil at $119 and Middle East disruption could tighten coal supply chains and support pricing.

### Bear Case
- Global recession materializes (40-45% probability per world model), crushing steel demand and met coal prices to $150-170/ton.
- Blue Creek ramp encounters geological or operational delays, pushing volume growth below 30% guidance.
- At 81x trailing P/E, any negative surprise on pricing or volumes triggers a sharp de-rating — the stock traded at $38 just 11 months ago.
- Chinese steel production peaked in 2023 and is in structural decline as China pivots to EAF (electric arc furnace) steelmaking, which uses scrap rather than met coal.
- The $4.8B market cap implies the market already prices in a successful Blue Creek ramp, leaving limited upside and significant downside if execution falters.

## Conclusion

HCC presents a classic cyclical commodity dilemma: the company has a genuine structural growth catalyst in Blue Creek, but the stock has already rallied 77% over the past year to price in much of that upside. At 81x trailing earnings, the valuation requires substantial earnings normalization — and that normalization depends on both successful mine ramp-up (company-specific) and met coal price recovery (macro-dependent). The macro environment is hostile to the bull case: stagflation, potential rate hikes, recession probability at 40-45%, and weak global industrial activity all weigh on steel demand.

The stock is a **HOLD** at current levels. The risk/reward improves meaningfully below $70 (where normalized earnings of $6-7 would imply 10-11.5x, in line with peer multiples) or on evidence that met coal prices are recovering above $240/ton. Conversely, a retest of $100+ without corresponding earnings improvement would shift the thesis toward AVOID. Position sizing should reflect the extreme earnings volatility inherent in single-commodity producers — this is a stock that can move 50%+ in either direction within a quarter based on met coal benchmark settlement prices.
