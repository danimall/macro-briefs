## Executive Summary

Parsons Corporation reported FY2025 revenue of $6.4 billion, a 6% decline year-over-year (9% organic) driven primarily by a confidential contract wind-down in its Federal Solutions segment. Record net income of $241 million (+3%) and adjusted EBITDA of $609 million partially offset the top-line weakness, indicating margin improvement. However, Q4 2025 specifically missed consensus on both revenue and earnings, triggering a 15% single-day drop on February 11, 2026. A subsequent loss on a $12.5 billion contract bid drove further selling in March 2026, pushing shares to $54.18 — a 39% decline from the October 2025 high of $89.50.

FY2026 guidance of $6.5-6.8 billion in revenue and $615+ million in adjusted EBITDA suggests low-single-digit organic growth as the confidential contract headwind laps. The Critical Infrastructure segment continues to grow, and Parsons remains well-positioned in defense modernization and national security technology markets that align with the current elevated geopolitical threat environment. Analysts have turned cautious on the 2026 outlook per Jefferies commentary, and the absence of visible analyst price targets in our dataset limits conviction on near-term upside.

The stock's valuation at ~24x trailing P/E is reasonable for a defense/infrastructure services company, but the combination of organic revenue contraction, a major contract loss, and macro uncertainty (stagflationary environment, potential DOGE-related federal spending scrutiny) creates enough near-term risk to warrant a HOLD rather than a BUY at current levels. The stock would become more attractive in the $45-48 range (~20x earnings), where downside risk is better compensated.

## Company Overview

Parsons Corporation is a defense technology and infrastructure company providing solutions across two segments: Federal Solutions (national security, intelligence, defense) and Critical Infrastructure (transportation, environmental, cybersecurity). The company serves the U.S. Department of Defense, intelligence agencies, and various federal/state/local government entities. With ~107 million shares outstanding and a market cap of approximately $5.8 billion, it sits in the mid-cap space between larger prime contractors (LMT, RTX, NOC) and smaller specialized defense firms.

## Financial Analysis

**Revenue:** FY2025 revenue of $6.4 billion declined 6% YoY, with organic revenue declining 9%. Excluding the confidential contract, total revenue grew 12% (8% organic). This bifurcation is critical: the underlying business is growing at a healthy rate, but a single large program wind-down masks that performance.

**Profitability:** Record net income of $241 million (+3% YoY) on declining revenue indicates meaningful margin expansion. Adjusted EBITDA of $609 million implies a ~9.5% EBITDA margin, up from prior year levels. EPS of $2.20 (trailing) represents solid profitability for a government services firm.

**Q4 2025 Specifics:** The quarter missed Wall Street estimates on both top and bottom lines, with the stock falling ~15% on February 11 (from $70.21 to $60.25). Volume on the earnings day was 4.7 million shares — nearly 4x average — suggesting institutional repositioning.

**FY2026 Guidance:** Revenue of $6.5-6.8B implies 2-6% growth. Adjusted EBITDA of $615M+ suggests modest margin expansion. The midpoint of the revenue range ($6.65B) at $615M EBITDA implies ~9.2% margins, roughly flat with FY2025. This guidance was deemed soft by analysts.

**Balance Sheet:** Without detailed balance sheet data in our dataset, we rely on the company's publicly reported metrics. Government contractors typically carry moderate leverage. The 10-K filed February 11, 2026 would contain the full picture. No dividend is paid, which preserves cash for acquisitions and debt reduction.

**Cash Flow:** Not available in the provided dataset. Government contractors often have lumpy cash conversion due to contract timing, but Parsons has historically generated positive free cash flow.

## Valuation Assessment

At $54.18 and EPS of $2.20, PSN trades at ~24.6x trailing earnings. The forward P/E is listed at 23.9x, suggesting consensus expects modest earnings growth.

**Peer context:** Large defense primes (LMT at ~18x, RTX at ~20x, NOC at ~19x) trade at lower multiples but have more diversified revenue streams and larger backlogs. Defense IT/services peers (LDOS, BAH, SAIC) typically trade in the 15-22x range. PSN's current multiple is at the high end of the services peer set, which is difficult to justify given -6% revenue growth and a major contract loss.

**Valuation support:** The stock has declined 39% from its October high, which has compressed the multiple from what was likely a 30x+ P/E at peak. At $54, the stock is near its 52-week low of $52.37. Historical support is visible around the $52-54 level.

**Attractive entry:** At 20x FY2026E earnings (~$2.40-2.50 estimated), the stock would need to trade at $48-50 to represent compelling value. The current level offers modest downside protection but insufficient margin of safety given execution uncertainty.

## Competitive Landscape

Parsons competes in a fragmented defense/infrastructure services market against LDOS, BAH, SAIC, KBR, and the engineering arms of larger firms like Jacobs and AECOM. Its differentiation centers on technology integration (cyber, space, missile defense) and dual-segment exposure to both federal and critical infrastructure markets.

The $12.5 billion contract loss is a material competitive data point. Large contract wins and losses are inherent to the government services business, but this specific loss may signal pricing pressure or competitive disadvantage on mega-deals. Without knowing the winning bidder, we cannot assess whether this reflects structural or idiosyncratic factors.

Parsons' acquisition strategy has been active, contributing to the 12% total revenue growth ex-confidential contract. M&A execution in this space has historically been accretive given the relatively low valuation multiples paid for government services businesses.

## Risk Assessment

**Key risks (ordered by probability × impact):**

1. **Federal spending uncertainty (HIGH):** DOGE-related efficiency initiatives and potential budget scrutiny create headline risk for all government services firms. While defense/intelligence spending is structurally supported by the Iran conflict and broader geopolitical tensions, non-defense federal contracts (infrastructure, environment) could face pressure.

2. **Confidential contract continuation (MEDIUM-HIGH):** The wind-down of this program has been the primary driver of organic revenue decline. If additional programs face similar wind-downs, the growth trajectory would be further impaired. Concentration risk in a single classified program is a structural vulnerability.

3. **Contract loss momentum (MEDIUM):** The $12.5B contract loss could signal competitive disadvantage, pricing pressure, or simply bad luck. Multiple large contract losses in quick succession would meaningfully impair the growth outlook.

4. **Macro stagflation (MEDIUM):** Rising inflation and potential recession could pressure federal budgets, though defense spending is typically counter-cyclical.

5. **Valuation premium risk (MEDIUM):** At ~24x earnings, PSN carries a premium to defense services peers. Further multiple compression toward 18-20x is plausible if growth disappointments continue.

**Insider activity:** Recent transactions are exclusively tax withholdings on vesting RSUs (transaction code "F") and new grants ("A"). No open-market purchases or sales are visible. The CEO holds ~563K shares (~$30.5M at current prices), which represents meaningful alignment. The absence of open-market buying after a 39% decline is notable — not bearish per se (insiders may be in blackout windows), but the absence of a signal is itself a signal.

## Conclusion

PSN is a quality defense/infrastructure services company experiencing a difficult transition period. The confidential contract wind-down obscures genuine underlying growth, and the stock's 39% decline from highs has compressed the valuation to more reasonable levels. However, at ~24x trailing earnings, the stock remains expensive relative to peers given the current growth profile. The $12.5B contract loss and analyst caution on FY2026 create near-term headwinds.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The defense sector thesis aligns with the current geopolitical environment, and Parsons' dual-segment exposure provides diversification. But the combination of (1) organic revenue contraction, (2) a major contract loss, (3) premium-to-peers valuation, and (4) unclear near-term catalysts means the risk/reward is insufficiently skewed to the upside at $54. An attractive entry point would be $45-48 (~20x estimated FY2026 earnings), where the margin of safety better compensates for execution risk. Alternatively, a strong Q1 2026 earnings report demonstrating organic revenue inflection would justify upgrading to BUY at current levels.
