## Executive Summary

Merit Medical Systems reported a solid Q4 2025 with adjusted EPS of $1.04 (beating the $0.96 consensus by 8.3%) and revenue growth that topped estimates. The company guided FY2026 to $1.61-1.63B in revenue (+6-8% reported, +5-7% constant currency) and non-GAAP EPS of $4.01-$4.15 (+5-8% YoY). New product launches, including the recently announced Resilience Through-the-Scope Esophageal Stent, reinforce the company's innovation trajectory in interventional medical devices.

The stock has fallen approximately 30% from its April 2025 52-week high of $107.27 to ~$70. At $70, trailing P/E is ~33x GAAP EPS of $2.13, while forward P/E on the midpoint of non-GAAP guidance ($4.08) is approximately 17x. The gap between GAAP and non-GAAP earnings is meaningful and warrants scrutiny. Insider activity includes Executive Chairman Fred Lampropoulos selling 55,000 shares worth ~$4.8M in November 2025, and COO Neil Peterson exercising options and selling 5,000 shares at $90 in January 2026. These are modestly negative signals, though the February 2026 grant/awards to executives at ~$78 provide some offset as compensation-related alignment.

The macro environment presents real risks. Merit Medical has international operations exposed to currency headwinds (the company explicitly flagged constant currency vs. reported growth divergence of ~1-2 percentage points). As a medical device manufacturer, MMSI faces potential tariff exposure on components and finished goods. The stock's steep decline has compressed valuation toward historical averages, but insufficient financial statement granularity in the available data prevents a confident assessment of balance sheet leverage and margin sustainability.

## Company Overview

Merit Medical Systems is a global manufacturer and marketer of disposable medical devices used in interventional, diagnostic, and therapeutic procedures, particularly in cardiology, radiology, oncology, critical care, and endoscopy. The company is headquartered in South Jordan, Utah, and trades on NASDAQ under the ticker MMSI. Market capitalization stands at approximately $4.1B based on ~59.4M shares outstanding at $68.27 (profile price) or ~$4.17B at the $70.23 closing price on March 17, 2026.

The company's product portfolio spans catheter-based technologies, drainage products, biopsy devices, embolization products, and endoscopic stents. The recent U.S. commercial launch of the Resilience TTS Esophageal Stent (announced March 16, 2026) expands the endoscopy franchise, which has been a growth area.

## Growth Analysis

### Revenue Growth Trajectory

FY2026 guided revenue growth of 6-8% represents a continuation of Merit Medical's mid-to-high single-digit organic growth trajectory. The medical device sector broadly grows at 4-6% annually, so MMSI is growing above the industry average, supported by:

1. **New product launches:** The Resilience TTS Esophageal Stent is the latest example. Zacks highlighted a "strong product portfolio" and continued R&D spending as growth drivers.
2. **Geographic expansion:** International revenue contributes meaningfully, though currency creates volatility.
3. **Market share gains in interventional procedures:** Growing volumes of minimally invasive procedures (catheter-based interventions, embolization, biopsy) provide secular tailwinds.

### Margin Expansion

Q4 2025 results showed margin expansion per Nasdaq reporting ("margins expand"). The 12% adjusted EPS growth on what appears to be high-single-digit revenue growth implies operating leverage. If the company can sustain 100-200bps of annual margin improvement, the non-GAAP EPS growth rate should continue exceeding revenue growth.

### R&D Investment

Zacks specifically cited continued R&D spending as a positive. Medical device companies typically invest 5-8% of revenue in R&D; Merit Medical's pipeline activity (new stent launches, endoscopy expansion) suggests adequate reinvestment.

The valuation is neither compelling enough to warrant a BUY nor egregiously overpriced for an AVOID.

## Competitive Landscape

Merit Medical operates in the competitive medical device market alongside larger players including Boston Scientific, Medtronic, Cook Medical, Teleflex, and AngioDynamics. The company's competitive advantages include:

1. **Breadth of product portfolio:** Merit offers over 700 medical devices across multiple clinical specialties, creating cross-selling opportunities and sticky customer relationships.
2. **Focus on interventional/minimally invasive procedures:** This is a structurally growing end market as healthcare systems globally shift toward less invasive treatment modalities.
3. **Vertical integration:** Merit manufactures many of its own components, which provides some cost control and supply chain resilience.

Competitive risks include the significantly larger R&D budgets and sales forces of major players like Boston Scientific and Medtronic, who can compete aggressively in Merit's end markets. Merit's scale disadvantage means it must compete on product innovation and specialization rather than commercial breadth.

## Risk Assessment

### Macro/Stagflation Risk (Elevated)

The current stagflationary macro regime creates several headwinds:
- **Hospital capital budgets:** If recession probability materializes (38-42% per macro framework), hospital procurement departments may defer purchases of disposable medical devices or negotiate harder on pricing.
- **Currency headwinds:** The 1-2pp gap between reported and constant currency growth could widen if dollar strength persists or accelerates.
- **Tariff exposure:** Medical device manufacturers with international supply chains face Section 301 tariff risk. Merit Medical manufactures in the US, Mexico, Netherlands, and other locations. Cross-border component flows are vulnerable to tariff escalation.

### Insider Selling (Modestly Negative)

- Executive Chairman Fred Lampropoulos sold 55,000 shares for ~$4.8M in November 2025 at prices of $85-88. He retained ~1.02M shares, so the sales represent ~5% of his holdings.
- COO Neil Peterson exercised options at $54.40 and sold 5,000 shares at $90 in January 2026 for $450K, a routine exercise-and-sell.
- February 2026 insider activity was entirely compensation-related grants and tax withholding, which is neutral.

The Lampropoulos selling pattern is worth monitoring. Selling 5% of holdings at prices 20-25% above current levels is not alarming in isolation, but it reflects insiders comfortable taking liquidity above $85, and the stock is now well below those levels.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- Merit Medical is growing above-industry at 6-8% revenue growth with margin expansion and 5-8% EPS growth.
- The stock has corrected 30% from highs, compressing the forward non-GAAP P/E to ~17x, which is at the low end of medtech historical ranges.
- New product launches (Resilience TTS stent, continued endoscopy expansion) provide visible revenue catalysts.
- Interventional/minimally invasive procedures are a secular growth market with demographic tailwinds (aging population, rising procedure volumes globally).
- Q4 2025 beat of 8.3% on EPS demonstrates execution capability.

### Bear Case
- GAAP earnings tell a different story: trailing GAAP P/E of ~33x is not cheap. The GAAP-to-non-GAAP bridge needs scrutiny.
- The macro environment is hostile: stagflation pressures hospital budgets, currency headwinds narrow reported growth, and tariff escalation could impact costs.
- Executive Chairman selling $4.8M in November 2025 at prices now 20-25% above current levels.
- Without balance sheet visibility, leverage risk is unquantifiable.
- Smaller scale vs. Boston Scientific, Medtronic creates competitive vulnerability in periods of market stress when customers consolidate vendor relationships.
- The stock is near its 52-week low ($67.42) and has been in a sustained downtrend since April 2025, with lower highs and lower lows. Technical momentum is decidedly negative.

## Conclusion

Merit Medical is a fundamentally sound mid-cap medtech company with above-average growth, margin expansion, and a pipeline of new product launches. The Q4 2025 beat and FY2026 guidance raise were genuine positives. However, the stock's 30% decline reflects real headwinds: macro uncertainty, currency pressure, the GAAP/non-GAAP earnings gap, and insider selling at higher prices.

At ~17x forward non-GAAP earnings, the stock is fairly valued for a mid-single-digit grower in a difficult macro environment. The absence of detailed financial statement data prevents a higher-conviction assessment. A more compelling entry point would be below $65 (approximately 16x the low end of non-GAAP guidance), where the risk/reward shifts more favorably. Alternatively, evidence of accelerating growth or margin expansion in Q1 2026 results could justify paying current prices.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The company merits monitoring for a potential upgrade to BUY if: (1) the stock approaches $60-65 (15-16x forward non-GAAP), (2) Q1 2026 results confirm or raise guidance, or (3) macro headwinds abate. The sustained downtrend and insider selling pattern argue against buying into the decline without a clear catalyst.
