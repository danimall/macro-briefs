## Executive Summary

ABM Industries is a $2.2B market cap facilities services company trading at $37.46, down 29% from its 52-week high of $52.94 and sitting at its 52-week low. The selloff accelerated after the March 10 Q1 FY2026 earnings report, which beat revenue estimates ($2.244B, +6.1% YoY) but missed on EPS. Management reaffirmed FY2026 adjusted EPS guidance of $3.85-$4.15, implying the stock trades at 9.0-9.7x guided adjusted earnings — a material discount to its trailing P/E of ~14.5x on reported EPS of $2.59.

The divergence between trailing EPS ($2.59) and guided adjusted EPS ($3.85-$4.15) suggests significant non-recurring items or adjustments inflating the guided figure relative to GAAP, or that Q1's miss was timing-related with back-half-loaded earnings. The WGNSTAR acquisition positions ABM in semiconductor facility services, aligning with secular data center/chip spending. However, the COO sold ~$1.8M in open-market stock in January-February 2026, which is a negative signal given the subsequent price decline. In a stagflationary macro environment with oil at $119 and labor cost inflation, ABM's thin-margin, labor-intensive model faces headwinds that the current valuation may not fully discount.

## Company Overview

ABM Industries provides facility services including janitorial, engineering, parking, electrical & lighting, energy solutions, HVAC & mechanical, and landscape & turf services across the United States and internationally. The company operates through several segments: Business & Industry (B&I), Aviation, Education, Manufacturing & Distribution, and Technical Solutions.

Key recent development: ABM acquired WGNSTAR, strengthening its semiconductor facility services capability. This positions the company to capture spending from the ongoing AI infrastructure buildout and domestic chip manufacturing expansion (CHIPS Act tailwinds).

Revenue scale is ~$8.8-9.0B annualized based on the $2.244B Q1 figure. The company employs a large labor force typical of facilities management, making it sensitive to wage inflation and labor availability.

Segment-level organic growth: Aviation +10%, Technical Solutions +7%, Manufacturing & Distribution +7%, B&I +4%, Education +2%.

**Earnings:** Q1 adjusted EPS missed the $0.87 consensus estimate (exact reported figure not disclosed in provided data, but news confirms an earnings miss). Full-year adjusted EPS guidance of $3.85-$4.15 implies significant earnings improvement in subsequent quarters; this back-half weighting introduces execution risk.

**Trailing EPS vs. Guided:** Trailing GAAP EPS of $2.59 vs. guided adjusted EPS of $3.85-$4.15 represents a 49-60% gap. This spread reflects either heavy adjustments (acquisition costs, restructuring) or genuine earnings growth expectations. **Free Cash Flow:** Management reported ~$50M in Q1 free cash flow. Annualized that would be ~$200M, or roughly 9% FCF yield on the current $2.19B market cap. However, facilities services FCF is typically seasonal with Q1 being weaker, so the actual annual figure may be higher.

**Dividend:** The ~3.0% dividend yield at current prices provides income support. At trailing EPS of $2.59, the payout ratio appears manageable.

**Market Cap:** $2.19B on 58.5M shares outstanding at $37.46.

## Growth Analysis

**Organic Growth Trajectory:** 5.5% consolidated organic growth is healthy for a mature facilities services business. Aviation (+10%) is the standout segment, likely benefiting from airport traffic recovery and expanded service contracts.

**Acquisition Strategy:** The WGNSTAR acquisition targets semiconductor cleanroom services, a high-growth niche. Maxim's March 11 upgrade to Buy with a $50 price target specifically cited data center and chip exposure as the catalyst. This acquisition provides exposure to the $1T+ AI infrastructure pipeline without the valuation premium of pure-play tech names.

**Technical Solutions & Manufacturing:** Both grew 7% organically, suggesting ABM is capturing share in higher-value services. The shift toward technical services (energy management, electrical, HVAC) typically carries higher margins than janitorial/basic facilities work.

**Constraints:** ABM's growth is capped by the availability and cost of labor. With unemployment at 4.4% and wage inflation persistent, scaling headcount while maintaining margins is the core operational challenge. Oil at $119 raises transportation and supply costs for a business with geographically distributed operations.

## Competitive Landscape

ABM competes with Cushman & Wakefield, CBRE (facilities management division), Sodexo, Aramark, and smaller regional players. The facilities services industry is fragmented with low barriers to entry for basic services (janitorial) but higher barriers for technical/specialized services (energy solutions, semiconductor cleanrooms).

**Moat Assessment:** ABM has limited pricing power in commodity janitorial services. Its competitive advantage comes from: (1) scale — multi-service bundling across large portfolios, (2) customer switching costs in integrated facilities management contracts, and (3) increasingly, specialized capabilities in semiconductor and data center services via WGNSTAR. The semiconductor niche provides a modest moat due to cleanroom certification requirements and customer qualification processes.

The industry's fundamental economics are challenging: thin margins (typically 3-5% operating), labor-intensive operations, and limited pricing power relative to input cost inflation. ABM's shift toward Technical Solutions is strategically correct but remains a work in progress.

## Risk Assessment

**Labor Cost Inflation:** ABM's largest expense is labor. In a stagflationary environment with persistent wage pressure, margin compression is the primary risk. If ABM cannot pass through wage increases to customers on a timely basis, adjusted margins will miss targets.

**Energy Costs:** Oil at $119 increases fuel costs for ABM's fleet operations and raises indirect costs (cleaning supplies, equipment transportation). This was not a factor when FY2026 guidance was set.

**Macro Sensitivity:** Facilities services revenue correlates with commercial real estate occupancy, business activity, and corporate spending. A recession (40-45% probability per macro model) would reduce demand for ABM's services as clients cut discretionary facility spending and renegotiate contracts.

**Guidance Credibility:** The $3.85-$4.15 adjusted EPS guide requires significant earnings acceleration from Q1's $0.64 GAAP EPS. Even adjusting for seasonality, delivering ~$3.20-$3.50 in the remaining three quarters requires ~$1.07-$1.17 per quarter — nearly double Q1's GAAP result. Management's track record on guidance delivery is unclear from available data.

**Insider Selling:** COO Rene Jacobsen sold 31,034 shares on January 13 ($1.37M) and 9,339 shares on February 9 ($441K) in open-market sales — total ~$1.81M. This is a meaningful negative signal, particularly given the subsequent price decline. Other insider activity was limited to routine tax withholding on equity grants.

**Rate Environment:** Potential rate hikes (10-15% probability) would increase ABM's borrowing costs on any variable-rate debt and reduce the relative attractiveness of its 3% dividend yield.

## Conclusion

ABM is a HOLD at current levels. The valuation on guided adjusted EPS is compelling, and the data center/semiconductor thesis via WGNSTAR provides a credible growth vector. However, several factors prevent a BUY recommendation: (1) the Q1 earnings miss combined with aggressive full-year guidance creates execution risk, (2) the COO's $1.81M in open-market selling is a negative insider signal, (3) the stagflationary macro environment — oil at $119, potential rate hikes, and 40-45% recession probability — creates hostile operating conditions for a thin-margin, labor-intensive business, and (4) the absence of detailed financial statements prevents adequate balance sheet and cash flow analysis. The stock deserves monitoring for: (a) Q2 earnings delivery confirming the earnings ramp, (b) insider buying at these depressed levels, and (c) evidence of successful margin management despite energy and labor cost inflation. A confirmed earnings recovery with stable margins would warrant upgrading to BUY in the $35-38 range.
