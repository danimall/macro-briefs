## Executive Summary

The Cigna Group (CI) is a diversified global health services company that delivered a strong fiscal year 2025, reporting nearly $6 billion in net income on 11% year-over-year revenue growth. The company beat Q4 2025 EPS expectations, reporting $8.08 versus the consensus estimate of $7.88 — a 22% year-over-year increase — demonstrating operational discipline and the strength of its complementary portfolio of health businesses, including its Evernorth Health Services segment (which houses Express Scripts, its PBM).

Despite the strong results, the investment picture carries nuance. Cigna lowered its full-year 2026 earnings guidance even while projecting at least $8 billion in adjusted income from operations, signaling potential headwinds from rising medical costs and the ongoing transition to a rebate-free pharmacy benefits model. Meanwhile, the FTC settlement with Express Scripts over anticompetitive insulin pricing practices introduces both regulatory risk and an opportunity to rebuild public trust. The stock trades at approximately 12.9x trailing earnings and 23.0x forward earnings, suggesting the market is pricing in some earnings deceleration or uncertainty.

Overall, Cigna presents a compelling value proposition for investors seeking exposure to managed care with improving operating metrics, a reasonable dividend yield of ~2.1%, and a low-beta profile (0.24), though investors must weigh regulatory and competitive risks carefully.

## Company Overview

The Cigna Group is a US-based global health services company operating primarily through two major segments: **Evernorth Health Services** (pharmacy benefit management, specialty pharmacy, and care delivery) and **Cigna Healthcare** (medical insurance, behavioral health, dental, and other supplemental benefits). Following the 2018 acquisition of Express Scripts, the company has positioned itself as a vertically integrated health services platform.

## Growth Analysis

### Drivers of Growth
1. **Evernorth Health Services**: The pharmacy benefits and specialty pharmacy segment continues to drive volume growth. Higher specialty volumes were specifically cited as a key driver of Q4 outperformance.
2. **Revenue Momentum**: 11% top-line growth in 2025 is robust for a mature managed care company of this scale.
3. **Rebate-Free PBM Model**: Cigna's Express Scripts is transitioning to a rebate-free model in pharmacy benefits, which could attract new clients seeking pricing transparency and position the company favorably as the PBM industry faces regulatory scrutiny.
4. **2026 Outlook**: Projected adjusted income from operations of at least $8 billion represents meaningful growth from 2025 levels.

### Growth Headwinds
- **Rising Medical Costs**: The company acknowledged improving but still elevated medical cost trends, a sector-wide challenge.
- **Lowered 2026 EPS Guidance**: Despite the strong 2025 finish, the lowered forward guidance suggests management is cautious about near-term earnings trajectory.
- **PBM Transition Costs**: Moving to a rebate-free model may temporarily compress margins before long-term benefits materialize.

## Competitive Landscape

Cigna operates in the highly consolidated US managed care and pharmacy benefit management industries:

### Key Competitors
- **UnitedHealth Group (UNH)**: The industry leader with ~$550B+ market cap; vertically integrated through Optum
- **Elevance Health (ELV)**: ~$100B+ market cap; strong Blue Cross Blue Shield affiliations
- **Humana (HUM)**: ~$35B market cap; Medicare Advantage focused
- **CVS Health/Aetna (CVS)**: Vertically integrated with pharmacy retail and PBM (Caremark)

### Competitive Advantages
1. **Evernorth/Express Scripts Scale**: One of the three largest PBMs, providing significant pharmacy volume leverage
2. **Diversified Revenue Streams**: Balance between health insurance and health services reduces concentration risk
3. **Employer Market Strength**: Strong position in commercial/employer-sponsored health insurance
4. **Operational Efficiency**: SG&A ratio of 5.3% is best-in-class, reflecting superior cost management
5. **Global Presence**: International operations provide diversification not available to most peers

### Competitive Risks
- PBM industry facing unprecedented regulatory scrutiny
- UnitedHealth's Optum continues to gain share across care delivery
- CVS Health's integrated pharmacy-PBM-insurer model provides end-to-end competition

## Risk Assessment

### Regulatory Risk (High)
- The **FTC settlement with Express Scripts** over anticompetitive insulin pricing is a major development. While resolved, it signals ongoing regulatory focus on PBM practices.
- The PBM industry broadly faces potential federal legislation that could restructure rebate economics or require greater pricing transparency.
- Healthcare reform proposals could impact commercial insurance margins.

### Medical Cost Risk (Moderate-High)
- Rising healthcare utilization and cost inflation remain persistent challenges. Management noted "costs keep rising" even as 2025 profits approached $6 billion.
- The medical cost ratio (MLR) trajectory will be critical for 2026 margin performance.

### Business Model Transition Risk (Moderate)
- The move to a rebate-free PBM model is strategically sound but carries execution risk. Revenue mix and margins may shift during the transition period.

### Concentration Risk (Moderate)
- Significant revenue concentration in the US healthcare system
- Dependence on large employer clients for commercial insurance

### Macro Sensitivity (Low)
- Beta of 0.236 indicates the stock is highly defensive and relatively insulated from broad market volatility
- Healthcare demand is relatively recession-resistant

### Litigation/ESG (Moderate)
- PBM practices remain a litigation target
- ESG considerations around drug pricing and access are increasingly important to institutional investors

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- **Attractive Valuation**: At ~12.9x trailing earnings with a 2.1% dividend yield, CI trades at a significant discount to peers like UnitedHealth, offering meaningful upside if the valuation gap narrows.
- **Strong Execution**: 11% revenue growth, 22% Q4 EPS growth, and improving SG&A efficiency (5.3% vs. 6.0%) demonstrate management's ability to drive profitable growth.
- **PBM Transformation Upside**: The rebate-free model could become a competitive differentiator, attracting new clients and improving regulatory standing.
- **$8B+ 2026 Earnings Power**: At least $8 billion in adjusted operating income translates to significant EPS and free cash flow, supporting buybacks and dividend growth.
- **Defensive Characteristics**: Ultra-low beta (0.24) and consistent cash flows make CI an attractive holding in uncertain macro environments.

### Bear Case
- **Regulatory Overhang**: The FTC settlement is just the beginning — Congress and state regulators continue to target PBM practices, potentially disrupting the Express Scripts business model.
- **Forward P/E Expansion**: The jump from 12.9x trailing to 23.0x forward P/E suggests meaningful earnings headwinds that the market is already pricing in.
- **Rising Medical Costs**: Persistent healthcare cost inflation could compress margins in the Cigna Healthcare segment, particularly if pricing lags utilization trends.
- **Competitive Pressure**: UnitedHealth and CVS Health continue to invest aggressively in vertical integration and care delivery, potentially eroding Cigna's market position over time.
- **Model Transition Uncertainty**: The rebate-free PBM shift is unproven at scale and could temporarily hurt profitability or client retention.

## Conclusion

**Overall Rating: 7.1/10 — Moderate Buy**

The Cigna Group presents a fundamentally sound investment with attractive trailing valuation, improving operational metrics, and a differentiated strategic direction in its PBM business. The company's 2025 results were strong across the board — $6 billion in net income, 11% revenue growth, and best-in-class SG&A efficiency — and the 2026 outlook of $8 billion+ in adjusted operating income supports continued earnings growth.

However, investors should approach with measured expectations. The gap between trailing and forward P/E ratios signals anticipated earnings pressure, likely from the PBM transition and rising medical costs. Regulatory risk remains elevated given the FTC settlement and ongoing Congressional scrutiny of PBM practices. The stock's current position (~18% below its 52-week high) offers a reasonable entry point, but material upside likely depends on successful execution of the rebate-free model and stabilization of medical cost trends.

For investors seeking defensive healthcare exposure with income (2.1% yield) and value characteristics (sub-13x trailing P/E), CI merits a position in diversified portfolios. We recommend a **moderate buy** with a 12-18 month investment horizon, with particular attention to Q1/Q2 2026 results for evidence of the PBM transition's financial impact.
