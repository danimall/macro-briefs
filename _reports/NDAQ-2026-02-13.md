## Executive Summary

Nasdaq, Inc. (NDAQ) is a leading global financial infrastructure company that has successfully transformed itself from a pure-play exchange operator into a diversified technology and data provider serving capital markets worldwide. The company hit a record $5 billion in annual revenue for 2025, driven by 13% year-over-year quarterly revenue growth in Q4 2025 and strong contributions from its technology, index, and listing businesses. The Q4 2025 EPS of $0.96 beat consensus expectations of $0.91-$0.922, representing a 26% increase from $0.76 per share a year ago.

Despite these strong fundamentals, NDAQ stock has pulled back approximately 14% from its 52-week high of $101.79 to a recent price of $79.01, creating a potentially attractive entry point. The company's ongoing strategic pivot toward higher-margin, recurring revenue streams—including its FinTech solutions, anti-financial-crime technology (bolstered by the Adenza acquisition), and index licensing—positions it well for sustained growth. However, the stock still commands a premium valuation at approximately 25x trailing and forward earnings, and elevated debt levels from recent acquisitions remain a consideration.

The investment case for Nasdaq rests on its unique competitive moat as a critical financial market infrastructure provider, its strong brand, and its ability to cross-sell technology solutions to an expanding client base. Risks include regulatory changes, competition from alternative trading venues, and macroeconomic sensitivity of trading volumes and IPO activity.

## Company Overview

Nasdaq, Inc. operates as a global technology company serving capital markets and other industries. The company's business spans several key segments:

- **Market Services**: Operating equity, derivative, and fixed-income exchanges, including the Nasdaq Stock Market
- **Index**: Licensing Nasdaq-branded indices (e.g., Nasdaq-100) for ETFs, futures, and options
- **Financial Technology**: Providing market surveillance, trade management, regulatory technology, and anti-financial-crime solutions
- **Listings**: Generating fees from companies listing on the Nasdaq exchange

**Key Company Facts:**
- **Market Cap**: ~$45.1 billion
- **Sector**: Financial Services
- **Headquarters**: New York, NY, USA
- **Website**: nasdaq.com
- **Beta**: 1.05 (roughly in line with market risk)
- **52-Week Range**: $64.84 – $101.79
- **Dividend Yield**: ~1.34%

The company has undergone significant transformation in recent years, particularly with the acquisition of Adenza (a financial technology and risk management platform), shifting its revenue mix increasingly toward recurring, technology-driven revenue streams.

## Growth Analysis

### Revenue Growth Drivers

1. **Technology Solutions (~50%+ of revenue)**: The FinTech segment, bolstered by the Adenza acquisition, continues to grow as banks, brokers, and asset managers adopt Nasdaq's market surveillance, regulatory compliance, and anti-financial-crime technology. This segment generates recurring, subscription-like revenue.

2. **Index Business**: Nasdaq's index licensing revenue benefits from the secular growth in passive investing and ETF adoption. The Nasdaq-100 is one of the most widely tracked indices globally, with trillions in assets benchmarked to it. Rising equity markets directly boost AUM-linked licensing fees.

3. **Listings**: Nasdaq maintained over a decade of listings leadership in 2025, raising **$46.65 billion from new listings**—the strongest year for capital raises since 2021. Additionally, **22 companies representing $1.2 trillion in market capitalization** switched or listed on Nasdaq, demonstrating the exchange's attractiveness.

4. **Organic Growth**: The Q4 results reflect meaningful organic growth across divisions, not just acquisition-driven expansion.

### Growth Catalysts

- **AI and Data Analytics**: Nasdaq is investing in AI-powered surveillance and analytics tools, positioning itself to monetize the growing demand for intelligent market infrastructure
- **Global Expansion**: Cross-selling technology solutions to international exchanges and financial institutions
- **IPO Pipeline Recovery**: After a subdued 2022-2023, IPO activity recovered meaningfully in 2025, benefiting listing revenues
- **Passive Investing Tailwind**: Continued growth in ETFs tracking Nasdaq indices drives recurring index licensing revenue

### Historical Growth Trajectory

- Q4 2025 vs. Q4 2024: Revenue +13% YoY, EPS +26% YoY
- The company was the **best-performing financial exchange stock year-to-date** in 2025, up over 16.4% in the final two months of 2025 alone
- Annual revenue milestone of $5 billion in 2025 vs. historical levels suggests double-digit multi-year CAGR

## Valuation Assessment

### Current Valuation

At $79.01, Nasdaq trades at:
- **Trailing P/E**: 25.2x
- **Forward P/E**: 25.2x (essentially flat, suggesting the market expects steady-state earnings)
- **Dividend Yield**: 1.34%

### Peer Comparison Context

Exchange and financial data companies typically trade at premium multiples due to their recurring revenue profiles and competitive moats. Key peers include:
- **Intercontinental Exchange (ICE)**: Typically trades at 22-28x forward earnings
- **CME Group (CME)**: Typically trades at 22-26x forward earnings
- **S&P Global (SPGI)**: Typically trades at 28-35x forward earnings
- **London Stock Exchange Group (LSEG)**: Typically trades at 25-30x forward earnings

Nasdaq's ~25x P/E is broadly in line with exchange peers but at a discount to pure-play data/analytics companies like S&P Global. Given Nasdaq's increasing technology revenue mix, there is an argument for multiple expansion toward the higher end of the peer range over time.

### Valuation Assessment

The stock's 14% pullback from its 52-week high of $101.79 has made the valuation more attractive, though it is not cheap in absolute terms. The forward P/E of ~25x is reasonable given:
- 26% YoY EPS growth in the most recent quarter
- Record revenue and improving margins
- Increasing mix of recurring, technology-driven revenue

However, the near-parity between trailing and forward P/E suggests analysts may be pricing in growth deceleration, which limits near-term upside potential without positive catalysts.

## Competitive Landscape

### Competitive Strengths (Moat)

1. **Brand and Network Effects**: The Nasdaq name is synonymous with technology-driven capital markets. The brand attracts listings (particularly tech companies), which drives trading volumes, index licensing, and data revenue.

2. **Critical Infrastructure Status**: As a designated exchange, Nasdaq benefits from high barriers to entry and regulatory protections. Switching costs for listed companies are significant.

3. **Index Franchise**: The Nasdaq-100 and Nasdaq Composite are among the most recognized indices globally. This franchise generates high-margin, recurring revenue that scales with AUM growth.

4. **Technology Platform Scale**: Nasdaq's market technology powers over 130 marketplaces, clearinghouses, and regulators globally, creating deep integration and switching costs.

5. **Listings Leadership**: Over a decade of listings leadership, with the strongest capital raise year since 2021 ($46.65 billion in 2025), demonstrates sustained competitive advantage.

### Competitive Threats

- **Alternative Trading Systems (ATS)** and dark pools continue to capture trading volume
- **Competition from NYSE/ICE** for large-cap listings and trading market share
- **Emerging fintech competitors** in surveillance and compliance technology
- **Crypto exchanges** potentially diverting retail investor attention

## Risk Assessment

### Key Risks

1. **Acquisition Integration Risk**: The Adenza acquisition significantly increased Nasdaq's debt load. While the company is actively managing this through tender offers, integration execution remains critical.

2. **Market Sensitivity**: Despite diversification, Nasdaq's revenues remain partially correlated with equity market levels (affecting trading volumes, index AUM, and IPO activity). A sustained market downturn could pressure multiple revenue lines.

3. **Regulatory Risk**: As a regulated exchange, Nasdaq is subject to SEC oversight and potential changes in market structure rules (e.g., tick size reform, payment for order flow regulations).

4. **Debt Levels**: Post-Adenza, the company carries elevated leverage. While FCF generation is strong, higher-for-longer interest rates increase debt servicing costs.

5. **Competition in Technology**: The FinTech space is increasingly competitive, and Nasdaq must continually innovate to maintain its technology edge.

6. **Concentration Risk**: Heavy reliance on the Nasdaq-100 index franchise for licensing revenue means any regulatory changes to index fund structures could be impactful.

### Mitigating Factors

- Diversified revenue streams across exchange services, technology, indices, and listings
- High recurring revenue mix (~75%+ from solutions businesses)
- Active debt management and strong FCF generation
- Beta of 1.05 suggests moderate overall market sensitivity
- Consistent dividend (~1.34% yield) signals financial discipline

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case (Target: $95-105)

- **Revenue momentum continues**: Record $5 billion in 2025 revenue with 13% quarterly growth sets the stage for continued expansion. Technology and index revenues have structural tailwinds from digitalization and passive investing.
- **Margin expansion**: As Adenza integration matures and the technology revenue mix increases, operating margins should expand, driving EPS growth ahead of revenue growth.
- **Multiple re-rating**: As the market recognizes Nasdaq's transformation into a technology/data company (similar to S&P Global or MSCI), the P/E multiple could expand from ~25x to 28-30x.
- **IPO cycle recovery**: Continued normalization of IPO activity in 2026 would boost listing revenues and attract incremental trading volumes.
- **Deleveraging**: Strong FCF allows rapid debt reduction, improving the balance sheet and potentially enabling more aggressive shareholder returns.

### Bear Case (Target: $60-70)

- **Valuation compression**: If earnings growth decelerates to single digits, the ~25x P/E could contract to 20-22x, implying 10-20% downside.
- **Market downturn**: A significant equity market correction would simultaneously reduce trading volumes, index licensing fees, and IPO activity.
- **Integration challenges**: If Adenza synergies underwhelm or technology integration proves more costly than expected, margins could disappoint.
- **Rising competition**: Loss of listings market share to NYSE or erosion of technology market share to specialized fintech competitors could impair the growth narrative.
- **Regulatory headwinds**: SEC market structure reforms could negatively impact exchange economics.

## Conclusion

**Overall Rating: 7.1/10 — Moderate Buy**

Nasdaq, Inc. represents a high-quality financial infrastructure franchise that has successfully evolved from an exchange operator into a diversified technology and data company. The company's record $5 billion revenue, 26% EPS growth, and consistent earnings beats demonstrate strong execution. The 14% pullback from 52-week highs provides a more attractive entry point, though the stock is not deeply discounted at ~25x earnings.

The investment thesis is supported by structural tailwinds in passive investing, financial technology adoption, and IPO market recovery. The primary risks center on acquisition-related debt, market cyclicality, and premium valuation. For long-term investors seeking exposure to capital markets infrastructure with a growing technology component, Nasdaq offers a compelling risk-reward profile at current levels. The dividend yield of ~1.34% provides a modest income floor while the company executes on its growth strategy.

**Recommendation**: Accumulate on weakness below $78-80 for a 12-18 month target of $90-95, implying ~15-20% total return including dividends. Position size should be moderate given the premium valuation and macro sensitivity.
