## Executive Summary

Houlihan Lokey is a global investment bank with dominant market share in mid-market M&A advisory and financial restructuring. The stock has declined ~34% from its 52-week high of $211.78 (September 2025) to $139.51 as of March 16, 2026, driven by macro deterioration, a disappointing Q3 FY2026 earnings report on January 28 (which triggered a single-day drop from ~$180 to ~$169), and sustained selling pressure through March. At approximately 21x forward and trailing earnings with EPS of $5.82, valuation has compressed meaningfully but remains above historical trough levels for advisory-focused investment banks.

The current stagflationary macro environment creates a genuinely bifurcated outlook for HLI's business segments. Rising interest rates, geopolitical uncertainty, and a 38-42% recession probability should suppress M&A deal volumes — the firm's largest revenue source. Simultaneously, these same conditions should drive restructuring activity higher, as credit stress builds and the private credit cascade (55-60% probability per our framework) generates fee-generating distressed situations. The net effect depends on the relative magnitude and timing of these opposing forces. HLI's restructuring franchise is among the best in the industry, providing a partial natural hedge that most pure-play M&A advisors lack.

The data gaps are significant: financial statements were not available in the provided data, limiting our ability to assess balance sheet strength, margin trends, and cash flow quality with precision. This analytical constraint, combined with notable insider selling by senior executives, prevents a BUY recommendation despite the attractive price decline.

## Company Overview

Houlihan Lokey operates as a global investment bank across three primary segments: Corporate Finance (M&A advisory), Financial Restructuring, and Financial and Valuation Advisory (FVA). The firm has built its reputation in the mid-market, typically handling transactions in the $100M-$5B range, though it increasingly competes for larger mandates. Market capitalization stands at approximately $7.5B with 54.5M shares outstanding. The firm's fiscal year ends in March, meaning the most recent quarterly filing (10-Q filed February 3, 2026) covers the fiscal Q3 ending December 2025.

The company announced Q3 FY2026 results on January 28, 2026. The stock's price action tells the story: it closed at $180.53 on the announcement date, then fell to $168.81 the next day on 1.38M shares — more than 3x average volume. This suggests an earnings miss or guidance disappointment, though specific figures are not available in our dataset.

HLI has been expanding its European presence, with a February 2026 announcement of senior hires in specialty distribution coverage in Frankfurt, consistent with its long-term strategy of internationalizing a historically US-centric franchise.

## Growth Analysis

**Structural growth drivers:**
1. Mid-market M&A advisory market share gains — HLI has consistently taken share from both smaller boutiques and larger banks that underserve the mid-market.
2. European expansion — the February 2026 specialty distribution hires in Frankfurt indicate continued investment in geographic diversification.
3. Restructuring countercyclicality — as the credit cycle turns (22 data points supporting credit cascade per our framework), restructuring revenue should accelerate in FY2026-2027.
4. FVA segment provides recurring, less cyclical revenue from valuation opinions, fairness opinions, and portfolio valuations.

**Cyclical headwinds:**
1. M&A deal volumes are highly sensitive to CEO confidence, financing availability, and equity market stability. All three are under pressure in the current stagflationary environment.
2. Higher interest rates directly suppress leveraged buyout activity, a meaningful source of mid-market advisory fees.
3. Geopolitical uncertainty (Iran conflict, Section 301 probes against 60 economies) creates a "wait and see" posture among corporate boards, delaying deal announcements.
4. The 38-42% recession probability, if realized, would further compress transaction volumes before restructuring revenue could fully compensate.

**Net assessment:** Growth is likely to be uneven across segments. Restructuring could see 20-40% revenue growth over the next 12-18 months as credit stress materializes. Corporate Finance revenue likely declines 10-20% from cyclical peak levels. FVA provides stability. The blended outcome depends on segment mix, which we cannot precisely model without current financial statements.

## Competitive Landscape

HLI's competitive position is its strongest attribute. The firm holds the #1 market position in U.S. M&A transactions under $1B by deal count, according to Thomson Reuters and Refinitiv league tables. Its restructuring practice is consistently ranked top-3 globally.

**Moat characteristics:**
- Relationship-driven business with high switching costs — mid-market companies develop long-term relationships with their banker, and repeat business is common.
- Restructuring expertise is difficult to replicate — requires deep legal, financial, and operational knowledge that takes decades to build.
- Dual-class share structure (Class A and Class B) gives management alignment through significant ownership, though it also limits external governance pressure.
- Brand strength in the mid-market is arguably stronger than any single competitor.

**Competitive threats:**
- Larger banks (Goldman, JP Morgan) periodically push down-market when bulge bracket deal flow is weak, which tends to happen during exactly the macro conditions currently prevailing.
- Technology-enabled advisory platforms have not meaningfully disrupted the relationship-driven middle market, but AI-assisted deal sourcing and analysis tools could compress margins over a multi-year horizon.

## Risk Assessment

**Macro sensitivity (HIGH):** Investment banking revenue is among the most cyclically sensitive of any business model. The confirmed stagflationary regime, 38-42% recession probability, and rising credit stress create a challenging near-term environment for the Corporate Finance segment. The restructuring hedge partially offsets this, but the timing mismatch (M&A slows immediately; restructuring accelerates with a lag) creates a potential earnings valley.

**Insider selling (MODERATE):** $10.3M in aggregate insider sales over the past 6 months (Siegert $8.0M, Gold $1.4M, Schriesheim $854K, Crain $185K) with zero open-market purchases. Most of these involved Class B to Class A conversions followed by immediate sale, which is standard for partnership-style investment bank share structures. Still, the absence of any buying signal is notable at these price levels.

**Concentration risk (MODERATE):** Revenue concentration in advisory fees means HLI generates almost no recurring revenue in the traditional sense. Each quarter's revenue depends on deals closing, which can shift between quarters unpredictably.

**Credit cascade exposure (MODERATE-HIGH):** While restructuring revenue benefits from distress, HLI also has exposure to the private credit ecosystem through its Financial and Valuation Advisory segment, which provides portfolio company valuations for alternative asset managers. If the credit cascade (55-60% probability) materializes aggressively, there could be both increased restructuring mandates and potential reputational/legal risks from prior valuation work.

**Data gap risk:** The absence of financial statement data in this analysis is itself a risk factor — we cannot verify that the balance sheet has not deteriorated or that compensation ratios have not expanded.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- Restructuring revenue accelerates meaningfully in calendar 2026 as the credit cycle turns, potentially more than offsetting M&A declines given HLI's #1-3 restructuring ranking.
- The 34% decline from highs creates an attractive entry point for a franchise that has compounded revenue and earnings at mid-teens rates over the past decade.
- European expansion gains traction, diversifying revenue geographically and reducing dependence on U.S. deal activity.
- Pent-up M&A demand (delayed by uncertainty) eventually releases when geopolitical conditions stabilize, creating a revenue surge.
- At 21x forward earnings near 52-week lows, downside is limited if the firm executes on restructuring and maintains its mid-market dominance.

### Bear Case
- M&A volumes decline 20-30% as recession probability crystallizes, overwhelming restructuring revenue growth.
- Forward earnings estimates get cut, pushing the "true" forward P/E into the mid-to-high 20s — expensive for a cyclically declining business.
- Insider selling continues, signaling management's private assessment of near-term prospects.
- The stock is trading near its 52-week low for a reason — the January earnings miss may have revealed structural revenue pressure that the market is still digesting.
- Rising interest rates and bond safe-haven failure mean the discount rate applied to cyclical businesses continues to increase, compressing multiples further.
- In a severe credit cascade, advisory fees may be delayed as deals and restructurings become more complex and take longer to close.

## Conclusion

Houlihan Lokey is a high-quality franchise trading at a meaningful discount to its recent highs. The restructuring hedge provides genuine protection that most M&A-focused investment banks lack, making it better positioned than pure-play advisory peers for the current macro environment. However, the 21x forward P/E is not distressed, the macro headwinds for M&A activity are intensifying, insider selling is visible, and the critical absence of financial statement data prevents the level of analytical confidence required for a BUY recommendation.

The stock warrants monitoring for a potential upgrade if: (1) restructuring revenue acceleration is confirmed in subsequent quarterly results, (2) the price approaches 18x forward earnings (~$117 at current estimates), or (3) insider buying emerges. At current levels, the risk/reward is balanced. HOLD.
