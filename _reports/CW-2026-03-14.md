## Executive Summary

Curtiss-Wright reported Q4 2025 revenue of $947M (+14.9% YoY) and adjusted EPS of $3.79, beating consensus of $3.66 by 3.6%. Full-year 2026 guidance of ~$3.74B in revenue came in 1.2% above analyst estimates, with management projecting operating margin expansion and double-digit EPS growth. The company operates across three structurally advantaged end markets: naval defense (Columbia- and Virginia-class submarine programs), commercial nuclear power, and industrial process controls.

The fundamental problem is price. At ~50x trailing earnings and a $24.2B market cap, CW trades at a significant premium to both its own history and the broader A&D peer group (which typically trades 18-25x). The stock has risen from a 52-week low of $266.88 to $656, a 146% move, which has front-loaded years of the expected earnings growth into the current share price. CFO K. Christopher Farkas sold 3,105 shares worth $2.16M on February 18, 2026 — six days after the strong Q4 report — at an average price of $694.51.

The company's competitive positioning remains strong, with multi-decade defense programs providing revenue visibility uncommon in industrials. The FY2026 outlook is credible. But at current valuation levels, the stock requires sustained mid-teens revenue growth and continued margin expansion for several years to justify the multiple, and any execution miss or macro-driven defense spending slowdown would create meaningful downside.

## Company Overview

Curtiss-Wright is a diversified industrial company focused on highly engineered products and services for aerospace & defense, commercial nuclear power, and industrial process markets. The company traces its lineage to the Wright brothers and Glenn Curtiss. It operates through three segments: Defense Electronics, Naval & Power, and Aerospace/Industrial.

Key end-market exposures include:
- **Naval defense:** Reactor coolant pumps and instrumentation for Columbia-class ballistic missile submarines and Virginia-class attack submarines. These are multi-decade programs with long production cycles.
- **Commercial nuclear:** Equipment and instrumentation for power plants globally, benefiting from the nuclear renaissance driven by data center power demand.
- **Process controls:** Sensors, instrumentation, and control systems for oil & gas, chemical, and industrial applications.

The company is headquartered in Davidson, NC. Beta of 0.94 indicates near-market sensitivity, consistent with a defense-industrial hybrid rather than a pure play.

## Growth Analysis

CW benefits from three structural growth drivers, each with different duration profiles:

**1. Naval Defense Programs (10-20 year visibility)**
Columbia-class SSBN production runs through the late 2030s. Virginia-class SSN production continues in parallel. CW supplies critical reactor components for both programs. The US Navy's submarine industrial base is capacity-constrained, which protects pricing power and limits competitive entry. The current geopolitical environment (Iran conflict, elevated defense spending across nine independent confirmations in the world model) supports sustained or increased naval procurement budgets.

**2. Commercial Nuclear Power (5-10 year visibility)**
Data center electricity demand is driving a nuclear renaissance. CW provides instrumentation and reactor components. The combination of AI infrastructure buildout and carbon reduction commitments creates sustained demand for nuclear capacity additions and plant life extensions.

**3. Industrial/Process Controls (cyclical, 1-3 year visibility)**
Oil & gas, chemical, and industrial process markets are cyclical. Current elevated oil prices ($98-108 central range per world model) support capex spending by energy producers, which benefits CW's sensor and control systems businesses.

FY2026 revenue guidance of ~$3.74B implies ~8-10% growth from FY2025 levels (Q4 alone was $947M, implying FY2025 revenue around $3.4-3.5B). Management's double-digit EPS growth guide suggests margin expansion through operating leverage and mix shift toward higher-margin defense content.

## Valuation Assessment

This is where the thesis breaks down for a new buyer. At ~50x trailing earnings:

- CW trades at approximately 2x the typical A&D peer group multiple (LMT ~18x, RTX ~22x, NOC ~25x, GD ~20x).
- The premium is partially justified by CW's nuclear exposure (secular growth) and its position in the submarine industrial base (limited competition). But 2x the peer multiple requires sustained outperformance that leaves zero room for error.
- Even assuming FY2026 EPS of ~$14.50 (double-digit growth from ~$13/share implied FY2025), the stock trades at ~45x forward earnings. This is expensive for an industrial company, regardless of end-market quality.
- The stock is 10% below its 52-week high of $730.12 and 146% above its 52-week low. The magnitude of the move suggests much of the positive thesis is already reflected.

Morgan Stanley raised its price target to $760 on February 13 (from $660), maintaining Overweight. This implies ~16% upside from current levels but reflects the most bullish sell-side view. The $760 target requires ~52x what would be a best-case FY2026 EPS scenario.

For the valuation to be reasonable, CW would need to compound EPS at 15%+ annually for the next 3-4 years, bringing the P/E down to the mid-20s by 2029-2030. This is achievable given the program pipeline, but it's a demanding requirement that leaves no buffer for acquisition integration issues, program delays, or defense budget uncertainty.

## Competitive Landscape

CW's competitive moat varies by segment:

**Naval/Nuclear:** Strong moat. The submarine reactor component business has limited competition due to security clearances, specialized manufacturing capabilities, and long qualification timelines. Switching costs for the US Navy are measured in years and billions of dollars. This is CW's crown jewel.

**Defense Electronics:** Moderate moat. Competes with larger primes (RTX, L3Harris) and specialized players (Mercury Systems). Product-level differentiation exists but is less structural than the naval business.

**Process Controls:** Weak-to-moderate moat. Competes with Emerson Electric, Honeywell, and other diversified industrials. Pricing power is cyclical and tied to end-market conditions.

The blended moat is genuinely above-average for a mid-cap industrial. The question is whether the ~50x multiple appropriately compensates for the 30-40% of revenue that comes from cyclical, competitively exposed markets.

## Risk Assessment

**Valuation risk (HIGH):** At 50x earnings, any growth deceleration or margin miss would compress the multiple rapidly. A reversion to even 35x (still a premium to peers) would imply ~30% downside.

**Insider selling (MODERATE):** CFO Farkas sold $2.16M of stock on February 18, one week after the Q4 beat. This is a single data point — insiders sell for many reasons — but the timing and size are notable. No open-market purchases by insiders are visible in the recent transaction data.

**Defense budget concentration (MODERATE):** Submarine programs are politically durable but not immune to sequestration or continuing resolution delays. A debt ceiling crisis or defense budget cap would slow procurement.

**Acquisition integration (MODERATE):** CW has grown partly through acquisitions. Integration execution is critical to the margin expansion story. Missteps would be disproportionately punished at this valuation.

**Macro sensitivity (LOW-MODERATE):** Beta of 0.94 suggests CW moves with the broader market. In a stagflationary environment with recession probability at 38-42%, the cyclical industrial segments face demand headwinds even as defense segments benefit.

**Nuclear regulatory risk (LOW):** Commercial nuclear faces regulatory approval timelines that can delay revenue recognition. This is a known risk factor for the 5-10 year growth thesis.

## Conclusion

CW is a high-quality company at a premium price. The end-market positioning is genuinely differentiated: submarine reactor components, commercial nuclear, and process controls create a three-legged growth platform that few mid-cap industrials can match. Q4 2025 results and FY2026 guidance confirm operational execution remains strong.

The valuation, however, leaves no margin of safety. At ~50x earnings, the stock prices in several years of above-peer growth and margin expansion. The CFO's $2.16M sale shortly after the Q4 beat is a secondary concern that reinforces the valuation thesis. For existing holders, the position is worth maintaining given the structural tailwinds. For new capital, the risk-reward at $656 is unfavorable relative to other defense names (LMT at ~18x, RTX at ~22x) that offer comparable geopolitical exposure at substantially lower multiples. **HOLD** for existing positions; do not initiate at current levels.
