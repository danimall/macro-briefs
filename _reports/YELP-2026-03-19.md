## Executive Summary

Yelp is a $1.45B market cap local business review and advertising platform trading at $24.32, down 31% from its 52-week high of $41.22 and near the bottom of its range ($19.60 low). At ~10.9x trailing and forward EPS of $2.24, the stock screens as cheap on an earnings basis. Management guided 2026 revenue of $1.455-1.475B during the Q4 2025 earnings call on February 12, 2026, positioning the company for low-to-mid single-digit growth off what they described as "record net revenue" in 2025.

The post-earnings selloff was severe: YELP dropped from $23.21 on February 11 to $20.98 on February 13, a 10% decline in two sessions, suggesting the market found the 2026 outlook disappointing relative to expectations. The stock has since recovered partially to $24.33. Two C-suite officers sold shares in early March 2026 (CFO Schwarzbach sold 10,000 shares at $25.00; COO Nachman sold 2,499 shares at $24.19), which is a mildly negative signal given the stock's depressed level.

The core question is whether Yelp's AI product investments (55+ AI features launched in 2025) and services vertical focus can sustain growth and defend against competitive encroachment from Google's local search products and emerging AI assistants. At current valuation, much of the downside appears priced in, but the growth trajectory is insufficiently compelling and macro headwinds to local advertising spend are real.

## Company Overview

Yelp operates a platform connecting consumers with local businesses, generating revenue primarily through advertising sold to businesses (restaurants, home services, auto services, etc.). The business model depends on Yelp's user-generated review content driving consumer traffic, which is then monetized through CPC/CPM advertising and lead generation products. The company has increasingly pivoted toward the services vertical (home services, contractors), which represents its highest-growth segment.

Key characteristics:
- **Revenue scale:** ~$1.4B+ annual revenue (2025 was described as record)
- **Profitability:** EPS of $2.24, implying ~$133M net income on ~60M diluted shares
- **Capital return:** No dividend; share count of 59.5M suggests active buyback program (the company has historically repurchased shares)
- **Headcount:** Not disclosed in available data, but the company has undergone periodic restructuring

## Growth Analysis

**Revenue Trajectory:** The 2026 guidance of $1.455-1.475B implies 3-5% revenue growth. CEO Stoppelman highlighted services vertical expansion and 55+ AI-powered features launched in 2025 as growth drivers. The company presented at the Morgan Stanley TMT Conference on March 3, 2026, where CFO Schwarzbach presumably discussed growth initiatives.

**Growth Concerns:**
1. **Low-single-digit revenue growth** at this stage of the company's lifecycle (founded 2004, public since 2012) suggests Yelp is approaching maturity in its core markets.
2. **Services vertical dependency:** The home services category is directly exposed to consumer spending contraction and housing market weakness. In the current stagflationary environment with 19% refinance demand collapse and mortgage rates at 7-month highs, home services demand faces headwinds. Fewer housing transactions → fewer home improvement projects → lower services ad spend.
3. **AI features as growth driver:** While 55 AI features is a high cadence, the question is whether these features drive incremental revenue or merely maintain competitive parity. AI-powered search assistants (ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity) threaten to disintermediate Yelp's position as a discovery layer between consumers and local businesses.

**Addressable Market:** The US local advertising market is estimated at $150-170B annually. Yelp captures roughly 1% of this. The company's ability to grow share depends on whether its product improvements can outpace competitive alternatives. Google Maps/Search remains the dominant local discovery platform.

## Valuation Assessment

At 10.9x earnings and an estimated 9-10% FCF yield, YELP is cheap relative to both the broader market and its historical trading range. The stock has traded at 15-25x earnings during periods of higher growth expectations.

**Cheap-for-a-reason analysis:**
- The low multiple accurately reflects low growth (3-5%)
- It also prices in meaningful competitive risk from Google and AI
- A 10-11x P/E on a low-growth, structurally challenged platform is not obviously mispriced

**Upside scenario:** If services vertical growth accelerates or AI features drive meaningful engagement/conversion improvements, a re-rating to 14-16x earnings ($31-36 per share) is plausible. This represents 28-48% upside.

**Downside scenario:** If consumer spending contraction reduces local ad budgets and AI search tools erode Yelp's traffic, EPS could compress to $1.50-1.80, and the multiple could contract to 8-9x. This implies $12-16 per share, or 34-51% downside.

The risk/reward is roughly balanced at current prices, which is consistent with a HOLD rating.

## Competitive Landscape

**Google** remains Yelp's primary competitive threat. Google Maps, Google Business Profiles, and Google Local Service Ads directly compete for the same local advertising dollars. Google's distribution advantage (embedded in Android, Chrome, Search) is structurally superior. Yelp's antitrust complaints against Google have not resulted in meaningful remediation.

**AI Search Assistants:** ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google's AI Overviews are beginning to answer "best restaurant near me" or "plumber in my area" queries directly, potentially reducing clicks to Yelp. This is an early-stage but structurally significant threat. Yelp's 55 AI features in 2025 may partially offset this, but Yelp lacks the distribution scale to compete with platform-level AI integration.

**Angi/HomeAdvisor (ANGI):** Competes directly in home services lead generation. ANGI has struggled similarly with growth, suggesting the category may be more competitive than growing.

**Yelp's moat** rests on its review database (accumulated user-generated content over 20 years) and brand recognition among consumers. This moat is real but narrowing. Reviews are increasingly commoditized across platforms, and younger consumers may default to social media or AI for local recommendations.

## Risk Assessment

**1. AI Disintermediation (HIGH):** The most significant structural risk. If AI assistants capture a meaningful share of local discovery queries, Yelp's traffic and advertising revenue decline. This is a slow-moving but potentially existential threat over a 3-5 year horizon.

**2. Consumer Spending Contraction (MODERATE-HIGH):** In the current stagflationary macro regime, local businesses — Yelp's advertising customers — face revenue pressure. Small businesses typically reduce advertising spend early in a downturn. Home services, Yelp's highest-growth vertical, is directly tied to housing activity, which is weakening.

**3. Google Competitive Pressure (MODERATE):** Ongoing and structural. Google continues to invest in local search features. Yelp has limited ability to compete on distribution.

**4. Insider Selling (MODERATE):** CFO selling 10,000 shares at $25 — essentially at the current price level — suggests he does not view the stock as materially undervalued. This is a weak-to-moderate negative signal.

**5. Concentration Risk (MODERATE):** Yelp's revenue is heavily concentrated in the US market and dependent on the local advertising spending cycle.

**Mitigating Factors:**
- Net cash balance sheet limits financial distress risk
- ~10% FCF yield provides downside support
- Active buyback program provides technical support
- Management tenure (Stoppelman has been CEO since founding) provides continuity

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- At 10.9x earnings and ~10% FCF yield, the stock prices in significant decline that may not materialize
- Services vertical continues growing at above-company-average rates, eventually re-accelerating total revenue growth
- AI features (55+ launched in 2025) improve user engagement and advertiser ROI, driving higher ARPU
- Buyback program at depressed prices creates meaningful per-share value accretion
- Potential acquisition target for a larger platform seeking local advertising scale
- Fair value in bull case: $31-36 (14-16x current EPS)

### Bear Case
- 3-5% revenue growth reflects structural maturity, not a temporary slowdown
- AI search assistants progressively disintermediate Yelp's consumer traffic over 2-3 years
- Stagflationary macro reduces local business advertising budgets, compressing revenue
- Home services vertical particularly exposed to housing market weakness
- Google continues to absorb share of local search and advertising
- CFO selling shares at current levels suggests limited insider conviction
- Fair value in bear case: $12-16 (8-9x compressed EPS of $1.50-1.80)

## Conclusion

YELP is optically cheap at 10.9x earnings but faces structural competitive challenges (Google, AI search) and cyclical headwinds (consumer/SMB spending contraction, housing weakness affecting services vertical). The 2026 revenue guidance of $1.455-1.475B implies low-single-digit growth, which is insufficient to drive multiple expansion. CFO open-market selling at $25 weakens the contrarian bull case. The balance sheet is clean and the FCF yield provides a floor, but the growth profile and competitive positioning do not support a BUY recommendation in the current environment.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The stock requires either (a) evidence of revenue growth re-acceleration above 5%, (b) demonstrated AI feature monetization success, or (c) a further price decline to $18-20 (7-8x earnings) to provide sufficient margin of safety against structural risks. At current prices, the risk/reward is approximately balanced.

**Why not BUY:** Despite the low P/E, the growth rate is decelerating, the competitive position is weakening structurally against Google and AI assistants, the macro environment pressures Yelp's core SMB advertising customer base, and the CFO is selling stock at current prices. These factors collectively warrant patience.
