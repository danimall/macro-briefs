## Executive Summary

Glaukos is an ophthalmic medical technology and pharmaceutical company focused on glaucoma, corneal disorders, and retinal diseases. The company reported Q4 2025 revenue growth of 38.1% and raised its 2026 revenue outlook, signaling strong commercial adoption of its product portfolio including the iDose TR sustained-release glaucoma implant. Revenue acceleration is genuine and differentiated.

The fundamental problem is that Glaukos remains significantly unprofitable. Trailing EPS is -$3.28, and the company reported a GAAP loss of $133.7M in Q4 2025 alone — well beyond what revenue growth can close in the near term. At a $6B market cap, investors are paying for a profitability inflection that has yet to materialize. The forward P/E of -31.5x confirms there is no near-term earnings expectation. In a macro regime where the Fed is discussing rate hikes and risk-free rates are elevated, the discount rate applied to distant future earnings has risen materially.

The stock rallied from ~$77 to $130 between late October 2025 and late January 2026, driven by the Q4 earnings beat on revenue and raised guidance. It has since pulled back to ~$103, reflecting both broader market weakness and likely profit-taking after the sharp run. The current price embeds meaningful optimism about the iDose TR commercial ramp but offers limited margin of safety given persistent losses.

## Company Overview

Glaukos Corporation, headquartered in Aliso Viejo, California, develops and commercializes therapies for glaucoma, corneal disorders, and retinal diseases. Its core products include:

- **iStent / iStent inject W** — Micro-invasive glaucoma surgery (MIGS) devices implanted during cataract surgery
- **iDose TR** — A sustained-release travoprost implant for glaucoma, representing the company's most significant recent product launch
- **Corneal health franchise** — Including iLink corneal cross-linking for keratoconus
- **Pipeline** — Retinal disease programs and next-generation pharmaceutical approaches

The company trades on the NYSE with ~58M shares outstanding and a market cap of approximately $6.0B.

## Financial Analysis

### Revenue Trajectory
Q4 2025 revenue was reported at approximately $143M (based on news references to "quarterly sales of $143"), representing 38.1% year-over-year growth. This is a significant acceleration, likely driven by the iDose TR commercial ramp. The company raised its 2026 revenue outlook alongside the Q4 results, indicating confidence in sustaining this growth trajectory.

### Profitability
The picture here is materially negative:
- **Q4 2025 adjusted EPS:** -$0.28 (missed consensus of -$0.22)
- **Q4 2025 GAAP net loss:** $133.7M ($2.32 per share)
- **Trailing EPS:** -$3.28
- **Forward P/E:** -31.5x (no expectation of profitability in the forward period)

The gap between adjusted loss (-$0.28) and GAAP loss (-$2.32) is enormous — approximately $2.04 per share of adjustments in a single quarter. This delta warrants scrutiny; it likely includes stock-based compensation, amortization of intangibles, and potentially impairment charges. Investors relying on the adjusted figure are ignoring real economic costs.

### Balance Sheet
Detailed balance sheet data was not available in the financial statements feed. However, given the magnitude of losses ($133.7M in a single quarter on ~$143M revenue), cash burn is severe. The company's ability to fund operations depends on its existing cash position and potential future capital raises. At roughly $134M quarterly cash burn (GAAP basis, though actual cash burn would differ from GAAP loss), liquidity runway is a relevant concern without more detail.

### Insider Activity
Recent insider transactions are predominantly tax withholding dispositions (Form F), which are automatic and non-discretionary. More notable:
- **CEO Thomas Burns** exercised 166,000 stock options at $30.92 (strike price) on Feb 9, 2026, and gifted 67,353 shares. The exercise was at a strike far below market (~$113 at the time), capturing ~$13.6M in unrealized gain. He did not sell the shares on the open market, which is modestly constructive.
- **Aimee Weisner** (appears to be a director/insider) exercised 15,000 options at $24.69 and sold 15,000 shares at $115-119, netting approximately $1.4M in proceeds. This is a clean exercise-and-sell, reducing exposure.

No open-market purchases by any insider in the available data. The absence of insider buying at a company losing $3+/share annually is unsurprising but provides no positive signal.

## Growth Analysis

### Revenue Growth Drivers
The 38.1% Q4 growth rate is the strongest signal in the dataset. Key drivers:
1. **iDose TR commercial launch** — This sustained-release travoprost implant addresses glaucoma medication non-compliance, a significant clinical need. Early adoption trends appear strong based on the revenue acceleration.
2. **MIGS franchise expansion** — iStent inject W continues to benefit from growing MIGS adoption in cataract surgery settings.
3. **Corneal cross-linking** — iLink maintains a strong position in keratoconus treatment.

### Raised 2026 Guidance
Management raised 2026 revenue outlook post-Q4, which multiple analysts responded to by boosting their forecasts. This is a positive forward signal, though the magnitude of the guidance raise is not specified in available data.

### Pipeline
Glaukos has retinal disease programs in development. These represent potential long-term upside but also significant R&D spending that contributes to current losses. Binary clinical trial outcomes create substantial uncertainty.

### Growth Sustainability Concerns
- Revenue at ~$143M/quarter ($572M annualized run-rate) against a $6B market cap implies ~10.5x trailing revenue. This is expensive for a med-tech company that hasn't demonstrated operating leverage.
- The iDose TR launch is still early. Penetration curves for MIGS/sustained-release glaucoma devices could plateau as the most enthusiastic early-adopter surgeons are captured first.

## Competitive Landscape

### Strengths
- **First-mover advantage in MIGS:** Glaukos pioneered the micro-invasive glaucoma surgery category with the iStent platform.
- **iDose TR differentiation:** The sustained-release implant concept is differentiated versus daily eye drops, addressing a real clinical problem (non-compliance rates of 40-60% in glaucoma patients).
- **Surgeon relationships:** Established distribution and training infrastructure in ophthalmology.

### Competitive Threats
- **Alcon** and **Johnson & Johnson** have significantly larger ophthalmology franchises and can compete on price, distribution, and surgeon access.
- **AbbVie** (Allergan) dominates glaucoma pharmaceuticals with Lumigan/bimatoprost and has sustained-release programs.
- **Generic travoprost** is widely available; the iDose TR's value proposition depends on the convenience premium exceeding the cost differential.
- **New entrants** in MIGS and sustained-release drug delivery continue to emerge.

### Moat Assessment
Glaukos has a moderate moat based on regulatory approvals (FDA PMA for iDose TR), physician training/switching costs, and first-mover positioning. However, the moat is narrower than established device companies because Glaukos lacks the scale, product breadth, and profitability to weather competitive responses or pricing pressure.

## Risk Assessment

### Critical Risks
1. **Persistent unprofitability:** At -$3.28 EPS with $133.7M quarterly GAAP losses, the company is consuming capital rapidly. Any disruption to capital markets access (credit cascade, risk-off environment) could pressure the stock disproportionately.

2. **Dilution risk:** With ongoing losses and no dividend, equity financing remains a likely pathway. 58M shares outstanding could increase materially.

3. **iDose TR commercialization risk:** If adoption plateaus below expectations, or if payer reimbursement becomes restrictive, the revenue trajectory could disappoint.

4. **Macro sensitivity:** Growth stocks with negative earnings are among the most rate-sensitive equity assets. The Fed's discussion of rate hikes is directly negative for GKOS's implied valuation.

5. **Pipeline binary risk:** Clinical trial failures in retinal or next-generation programs could remove optionality that supports the current valuation.

6. **Single-product concentration:** The stock's recent performance is heavily dependent on iDose TR. Any safety signal, manufacturing issue, or competitive response to this product would have outsized impact.

### Macro Interaction
In the current stagflationary environment with oil at $119 and the Fed discussing rate hikes, GKOS faces multiple headwinds: higher discount rates compress the value of future earnings that don't yet exist; healthcare procedure volumes could soften if consumer spending weakens and elective procedures are deferred; and risk appetite for unprofitable growth companies typically contracts in this environment.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- iDose TR represents a paradigm shift in glaucoma treatment, capturing a large portion of the ~$7B global glaucoma market over the next 5-7 years
- 38% revenue growth accelerates further as iDose TR moves beyond early adopters
- Gross margin expansion occurs as the manufacturing base scales, driving toward operating profitability by 2027-2028
- Pipeline programs in retinal disease succeed, opening additional multi-billion-dollar markets
- A larger ophthalmology company acquires Glaukos at a premium (strategic value of the iDose TR platform)

### Bear Case
- Revenue growth decelerates as early-adopter enthusiasm fades and reimbursement hurdles emerge
- Losses persist or widen as R&D spending on pipeline programs continues, requiring dilutive capital raises
- Rising interest rates increase discount rates, compressing the multiple on unprofitable growth stocks
- Competitive responses from Alcon, AbbVie, or J&J erode iDose TR's market position
- Pipeline failures remove the optionality premium embedded in the stock
- Macro deterioration reduces elective procedure volumes and pressures hospital/ASC capital budgets

## Conclusion

Glaukos has a legitimate growth story anchored by the iDose TR commercial launch and a strong Q4 2025 revenue result. The 38.1% growth rate is impressive, and the raised 2026 guidance provides near-term visibility.

However, the stock trades at ~10.5x annualized revenue with deeply negative earnings (-$3.28 EPS), $133.7M in Q4 GAAP losses, and no clear timeline to profitability. In a macro environment where the Fed is discussing rate hikes and real yields are rising, the cost of holding unprofitable growth stocks has increased materially. The absence of any open-market insider buying reinforces the lack of a near-term catalyst to reprice the stock higher.

The recommendation is **HOLD**. The growth story is real but fully reflected in the current price. Entry at current levels (~$103) offers insufficient margin of safety given the persistent losses, macro headwinds, and binary pipeline risk. A more attractive entry point would emerge below $80-85 (where the stock traded in October 2025), or upon demonstration of a credible path to operating profitability.
