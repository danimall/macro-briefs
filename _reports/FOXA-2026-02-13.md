## Executive Summary

Fox Corporation is a diversified media company operating across cable news (Fox News), broadcast television (FOX), sports programming (FOX Sports), and ad-supported streaming (Tubi). The company currently trades at approximately $56.43 per share, representing a market capitalization of ~$22.8 billion. With a trailing P/E of ~12.1x and a forward P/E of ~10.1x, Fox trades at a notable discount to the broader market, reflecting both the value of its dominant news franchise and the structural challenges facing traditional media companies.

Recent quarterly results (Q2 FY2026, reported February 4, 2026) exceeded revenue expectations, buoyed by a seven-game World Series in October and continued strength at Fox News and Tubi. The stock has pulled back approximately 7.7% from recent levels (trading near $56.43 versus a 52-week high of $76.39), potentially creating an opportunity for value-oriented investors. The company's low beta of 0.52 suggests relatively defensive characteristics compared to the broader market.

Despite limited granular financial statement data in the dataset provided, the available profile metrics, news sentiment, and SEC filing history paint a picture of a stable, cash-generative media company navigating the transition from linear to digital distribution. The key thesis revolves around whether Tubi's growth can offset inevitable cord-cutting declines and whether Fox's sports rights portfolio provides sufficient differentiation.

## Company Overview

Fox Corporation was spun off from 21st Century Fox in March 2019 following the sale of entertainment assets to The Walt Disney Company. The company is headquartered in New York and operates through several key segments:

- **FOX News Media** — Includes Fox News Channel, Fox Business Network, and related digital properties. Fox News has maintained its position as the #1-rated cable news network for over two decades.
- **FOX Sports** — Holds rights to NFL, MLB (World Series), college football, NASCAR, FIFA World Cup (2026), and other marquee sports properties.
- **FOX Entertainment** — Operates the FOX broadcast network, producing and distributing entertainment programming.
- **FOX Television Stations** — Owns and operates 29 full-power broadcast stations across major U.S. markets.
- **Tubi** — A free, ad-supported streaming platform that has emerged as a significant growth driver, competing in the FAST (Free Ad-Supported Television) space.

The company operates a dual-class share structure, with the Murdoch family controlling voting power through Class B shares. FOXA represents the Class A shares with limited voting rights.

## Growth Analysis

### Revenue Growth Drivers

1. **Tubi** — The ad-supported streaming platform represents Fox's most compelling growth vector. Tubi has grown rapidly, benefiting from the broader trend of viewers shifting from paid subscription fatigue to free, ad-supported alternatives. The platform reportedly serves over 80 million monthly active users and is increasingly attracting premium advertising dollars.

2. **Sports Rights** — Fox's portfolio of premium sports rights (NFL, MLB, FIFA World Cup 2026) provides both advertising revenue upside and retransmission consent leverage. The 2026 FIFA World Cup, which Fox holds U.S. broadcast rights to, represents a significant one-time revenue opportunity.

3. **Political Advertising** — Fox's broadcast stations benefit from political advertising cycles. While Q2 FY2026 lacked political ad revenue (as noted in news coverage — "despite lack of political ads"), the next major political cycle in 2026 midterms could provide a tailwind.

4. **Affiliate Fee Growth** — Fox News commands premium affiliate fees from cable and satellite distributors, though this revenue stream faces long-term pressure from subscriber declines.

### Growth Headwinds

- **Cord-Cutting** — Linear TV subscriber declines continue to erode the traditional cable bundle, pressuring affiliate fee revenue.
- **Advertising Cyclicality** — Traditional TV advertising remains cyclical and is increasingly challenged by digital alternatives (Google, Meta, Amazon).
- **Sports Rights Inflation** — While sports rights drive viewership, they also come with escalating costs that can pressure margins.

The forward P/E compression from 12.1x (trailing) to 10.1x (forward) suggests the market expects meaningful earnings growth in the near term, likely driven by Tubi scaling, World Cup-related revenue, and operating leverage.

## Competitive Landscape

### Fox News — Dominant but Concentrated
Fox News Channel remains the undisputed leader in cable news ratings, consistently outperforming CNN and MSNBC in total day and primetime viewership. This dominance translates into:
- Premium advertising CPMs
- Strong affiliate fee negotiating leverage
- Cultural and political influence

However, the cable news audience skews older, raising long-term demographic concerns.

### FOX Sports — Differentiated by Premium Rights
Fox's sports portfolio is among the strongest in media:
- **NFL** — Thursday Night Football, NFC Sunday games, Super Bowl rotation
- **MLB** — World Series, League Championship Series
- **FIFA World Cup 2026** — Exclusive U.S. broadcast rights
- **College Football** — Big Ten, Big 12 conferences
- **NASCAR** — Multi-year rights

Sports remains the most valuable content in media for live viewership and advertiser demand.

### Tubi — Growing FAST Player
Tubi competes with Pluto TV (Paramount), Roku Channel, Freevee (Amazon), and Peacock's free tier. Tubi's advantages include Fox's programming library, promotional opportunities across FOX broadcast, and a pure AVOD model that avoids subscription fatigue.

### Competitive Threats
- **Streaming giants** (Netflix, Amazon, Apple) bidding up sports rights costs
- **Digital advertising platforms** (Google, Meta) capturing advertising market share
- **News alternatives** — Podcasts, YouTube, and social media fragmenting the news audience

## Risk Assessment

### Key Risks

1. **Governance / Dual-Class Structure (High)** — The Murdoch family's controlling interest through Class B shares means FOXA shareholders have limited voting rights. Recent Murdoch family trust disputes have added uncertainty about future strategic direction.

2. **Cord-Cutting Acceleration (High)** — Linear TV subscriber losses continue. If affiliate fee revenues decline faster than Tubi can scale, margins could compress.

3. **Litigation Risk (Medium)** — Fox News has faced significant legal challenges, including the $787.5 million Dominion Voting Systems settlement in 2023. Future litigation could impose material costs.

4. **Sports Rights Cost Inflation (Medium)** — Renewal of major sports rights packages could significantly increase programming costs, pressuring margins.

5. **Regulatory Risk (Medium)** — Changes in FCC broadcast ownership rules, retransmission consent regulations, or political/regulatory pressures could impact operations.

6. **Advertiser Sensitivity (Medium)** — Fox News's politically polarizing content occasionally leads to advertiser boycotts, though historically these have been temporary.

7. **Key Person Risk (Medium)** — The company's brand and strategic direction are closely tied to the Murdoch family's leadership and vision.

8. **Macro Sensitivity (Low-Medium)** — With a beta of 0.52, Fox is less sensitive to broad market movements, but advertising revenue is cyclically exposed.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- **Tubi becomes a material value driver:** If Tubi continues its rapid growth trajectory and achieves significant scale (100M+ MAUs with improving ARPU), it could be worth $5-10 billion on a standalone basis, representing a substantial portion of Fox's current market cap.
- **FIFA World Cup 2026 catalyst:** The U.S.-hosted World Cup represents a once-in-a-generation advertising and viewership event that could drive significant revenue in FY2026-2027.
- **Fox News moat is durable:** Fox News's audience loyalty and brand strength provide a steady cash flow engine that supports investment in growth areas.
- **Attractive valuation:** At ~10x forward earnings with a ~1% dividend yield and active buyback program, the stock offers compelling total return potential if secular fears prove overblown.
- **Sports rights as structural advantage:** Live sports remain the most valuable content in media, and Fox's portfolio provides differentiation and leverage.

### Bear Case
- **Cord-cutting reaches critical mass:** If cable/satellite subscribers decline faster than expected, affiliate fee revenues could deteriorate sharply, overwhelming Tubi's growth.
- **Sports rights become uneconomical:** Escalating rights costs could squeeze margins, particularly if advertising revenues don't keep pace.
- **Governance concerns:** The dual-class structure and Murdoch family dynamics create risks that the company may not be managed purely in Class A shareholders' interests.
- **Litigation overhang:** Future legal challenges related to news coverage could impose significant financial and reputational costs.
- **Tubi faces intense competition:** The FAST/AVOD space is becoming increasingly crowded, and Tubi may struggle to differentiate without massive content spending.
- **Demographic headwinds:** Fox News's older-skewing audience raises questions about long-term viewership sustainability.

## Conclusion

Fox Corporation presents a **moderate buy** opportunity for value-oriented investors with a medium-term horizon. The stock's forward P/E of ~10x is attractive relative to the quality of its assets (Fox News, premium sports rights, growing Tubi platform), and the upcoming FIFA World Cup 2026 provides a tangible near-term catalyst.

However, investors must weigh the secular decline of linear television, governance concerns related to the dual-class structure, and potential litigation risks. The company's low beta (0.52) provides some portfolio diversification benefit, and the modest dividend yield adds a small income component.

**Overall Score: 6.4/10** — Fox Corporation is a reasonably valued media company with strong competitive positions in news and sports, but faces meaningful structural headwinds and governance limitations. The stock is best suited for value-oriented investors comfortable with media industry dynamics and willing to accept limited shareholder governance rights in exchange for an attractive earnings multiple and exposure to growth via Tubi and premium sports content.

**Recommendation:** Hold / Moderate Buy on pullbacks below $55, with a 12-month price target range of $62-70.
