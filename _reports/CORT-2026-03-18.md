The class period spans October 31, 2024 through December 30, 2025, with the lead plaintiff deadline set for April 21, 2026. At least six law firms have filed or are investigating claims against the company and its top executives.

The stock currently trades at $33.51, down 71.4% from its 52-week high of $117.33 reached on March 31, 2025. The company had been on a strong growth trajectory through most of 2025, with the stock rising from ~$55 in mid-March 2025 to a peak of $117.33 on March 31, 2025, before a volatile but broadly upward trend through September 2025.

## Valuation Assessment

At $33.51 with a trailing P/E of 40.9x on $0.82 EPS, the stock is not cheap by conventional pharmaceutical standards. Specialty pharma companies with single-product concentration and active litigation typically trade at 8-15x earnings. The 40.9x multiple suggests either (a) the market expects significant earnings growth that would compress the forward multiple, or (b) the EPS figure is depressed and doesn't reflect normalized earnings power.

However, any valuation framework must incorporate the litigation discount. Securities fraud class actions in pharma can result in:
- Direct settlement costs (typically 2-5% of market cap for mid-cap pharma, but highly variable)
- Management distraction and legal costs
- Reputational damage affecting physician prescribing behavior
- Potential regulatory scrutiny triggered by fraud allegations

The stock is down 72% from its 52-week high. The question is whether this decline adequately prices in the litigation risk. Given the absence of detailed financials to assess balance sheet strength and the ongoing nature of the litigation, the current price does not offer sufficient margin of safety.

## Conclusion

Corcept Therapeutics presents a situation where the risks materially outweigh the potential rewards. The core problem is informational: the securities fraud lawsuits allege material misrepresentation during a 14-month class period, and the available data does not contain the detailed financial statements needed to independently assess whether the company's stated results are reliable. The 50.4% single-day collapse, continued downward drift to new lows, insider selling by the Chief Development Officer, and the sheer number of law firms pursuing claims all point toward a deteriorating trajectory rather than static weakness at a depressed price.

The February 2026 earnings release reportedly showed strong results, and the company did not collapse further on that disclosure, which provides some floor. But the stock's inability to hold any rally since January (the ~$46 bounce faded immediately) suggests institutional sellers are using strength to exit.

**Recommendation: AVOID.** The litigation timeline extends through at least April 21 (lead plaintiff deadline) and likely years beyond. The absence of verifiable financial data, the pattern of insider selling, and the continued downward price trajectory make this an uninvestable situation at current levels. Investors seeking pharma exposure should look to companies with clean legal profiles and verifiable financials. Re-evaluation would be warranted only after: (1) detailed financials become available and can be independently verified, (2) the securities fraud allegations are specifically disclosed and can be assessed for materiality, and (3) the stock demonstrates sustained price stabilization above $35.
