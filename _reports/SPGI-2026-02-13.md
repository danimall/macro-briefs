## Executive Summary

S&P Global Inc. is one of the most important infrastructure companies in global financial markets, operating dominant franchises in credit ratings, market indices (including the flagship S&P 500), data analytics, and commodity intelligence. The company's competitive position is among the strongest in the financial services industry, supported by regulatory moats, network effects, and deeply embedded data products that create high switching costs.

However, the company recently faced a sharp sell-off, with shares plunging approximately 16% following its Q4 2025 earnings release on February 10, 2026. The quarter delivered an EPS of $4.30, narrowly missing consensus expectations of $4.33, while revenues came in above estimates. More critically, the company's 2026 earnings guidance trailed analyst expectations, triggering the significant price correction. The stock now trades at approximately $397, well below its 52-week high of $579.05.

Despite the near-term disappointment, S&P Global's long-term investment thesis remains intact. The company benefits from secular trends in data demand, ESG analytics, private credit markets, and AI-driven product development (as highlighted by its strategic partnership with Google Cloud). The current pullback may represent an attractive entry point for long-term investors, though investors should monitor the trajectory of the 2026 guidance and whether the earnings shortfall reflects cyclical or structural issues.

## Company Overview

**S&P Global Inc.** (NYSE: SPGI) is a leading global provider of transparent and independent credit ratings, benchmarks, analytics, and data to the capital and commodity markets. The company operates through several key segments:

- **S&P Global Ratings** — The world's leading credit rating agency alongside Moody's
- **S&P Global Market Intelligence** — Financial data, analytics, and research
- **S&P Dow Jones Indices** — Benchmark indices including the S&P 500
- **S&P Global Commodity Insights** (formerly Platts) — Energy and commodity pricing
- **S&P Global Mobility** — Automotive industry data and analytics

Following the transformative $44 billion merger with IHS Markit in 2022, the company significantly expanded its data and analytics capabilities. More recently, in December 2025, S&P Global announced the $1.8 billion acquisition of With Intelligence, further bolstering its data offerings.

## Competitive Landscape

S&P Global possesses one of the widest economic moats in the financial services industry:

### Moat Sources

1. **Regulatory Moat (Ratings):** The credit ratings industry is effectively an oligopoly shared among S&P, Moody's, and Fitch. Regulatory barriers (NRSRO designation) make new entry extremely difficult. Issuers effectively need ratings from at least two of these agencies.

2. **Network Effects (Indices):** The S&P 500 and other indices are deeply embedded in global financial infrastructure. Trillions of dollars are benchmarked or directly indexed to SPGI's indices. Switching costs are extraordinarily high.

3. **Data Lock-In (Market Intelligence & Commodity Insights):** Customers deeply integrate SPGI data into their workflows, models, and systems. Switching costs are substantial.

4. **Brand Strength:** The S&P name is synonymous with market benchmarks and credit quality assessment globally.

### Key Competitors

- **Moody's Corporation (MCO)** — Primary competitor in ratings; also expanding in data/analytics
- **MSCI Inc.** — Competitor in indices and ESG data
- **Bloomberg LP** — Competitor in financial data terminals
- **LSEG (London Stock Exchange Group / Refinitiv)** — Competitor in data and analytics
- **ICE (Intercontinental Exchange)** — Competitor in data and indices

**Competitive Score: 9/10** — S&P Global's competitive position is exceptional. The ratings duopoly and index franchise are among the most durable moats in global business.

## Risk Assessment

### Key Risks

1. **Cyclical Earnings Sensitivity (Moderate-High):** The ratings business is directly tied to debt issuance volumes. Rising rates or economic slowdowns can significantly impact this segment. Beta of 1.23 confirms above-average market sensitivity.

2. **Regulatory Risk (Moderate):** Post-2008 financial crisis, credit rating agencies face ongoing regulatory scrutiny. Potential changes in how ratings are used in regulations could impact the business.

3. **Guidance Execution Risk (Near-Term):** The disappointing 2026 guidance has reset expectations. If the company fails to meet even reduced expectations, further downside is possible.

4. **Integration/Acquisition Risk (Moderate):** The IHS Markit merger was massive, and the With Intelligence acquisition adds complexity. Integration missteps could impair value.

5. **AI Disruption Risk (Low-Moderate):** While SPGI is investing in AI (Google Cloud partnership), there is a risk that AI could commoditize some data products or that competitors leverage AI more effectively.

6. **Concentration Risk (Low):** The company is well-diversified across segments and geographies, reducing concentration risk.

7. **Litigation Risk (Low-Moderate):** Rating agencies have historically faced litigation related to ratings quality, though SPGI has generally managed this well.

8. **Macro Sensitivity:** At a beta of 1.23, the stock will amplify broader market moves. A recession scenario could simultaneously reduce ratings volumes and compress the multiple.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case 🐂

- **Overreaction to Guidance:** The 16% single-day drop may be an overreaction to a modest EPS miss ($0.03) and conservative guidance. Management teams frequently set beatable guidance.
- **Franchise Value Intact:** The ratings oligopoly, S&P 500 index franchise, and data moat are unchanged. These are among the most valuable business assets in the world.
- **AI Upside Not Priced In:** The Google Cloud partnership and AI-enhanced products could drive meaningful revenue acceleration and margin expansion not captured in current guidance.
- **Valuation Compression Creates Opportunity:** At ~27x earnings, the stock is trading at a meaningful discount to its historical 30-35x range. Mean reversion alone could drive 15-20% upside.
- **Goldman Sachs $640 Target:** Even with some reduction, significant analyst upside exists.
- **Dividend Aristocrat Status:** Consistent dividend growth provides a floor and attracts income-oriented investors.

### Bear Case 🐻

- **Guidance Is Real Signal:** The below-consensus 2026 guidance may reflect genuine deceleration in core growth drivers, not conservatism.
- **Debt Issuance Headwinds:** If interest rates remain elevated, debt issuance volumes (and thus ratings revenues) could remain under pressure.
- **Multiple May Not Re-Expand:** In a higher-rate environment, premium multiples for financial data companies may be structurally lower than the past decade.
- **Acquisition Digestion:** The With Intelligence deal adds $1.8 billion in goodwill and integration costs at a time when the base business is showing stress.
- **Near 52-Week Low:** Trading just above the 52-week low of $390.73, technical support could break, triggering further selling.
- **Market Cap Destruction:** The loss of ~$40 billion in market cap in weeks suggests institutional repositioning that may take time to reverse.

## Conclusion

**Overall Score: 7.3/10 — Moderate Buy**

S&P Global remains one of the highest-quality businesses in the financial services sector, with a competitive moat that is virtually unassailable in its core ratings and indices businesses. The recent ~31% decline from 52-week highs, triggered by a modest Q4 2025 EPS miss and below-consensus 2026 guidance, has created a more attractive entry point at approximately 27x trailing earnings.

However, the investment is not without risk. The disappointing guidance may signal genuine near-term headwinds, particularly in the cyclically-sensitive ratings business. Investors should monitor Q1 2026 results closely for signs of whether management's guidance reflects conservatism or deteriorating fundamentals.

**Recommendation:** For long-term investors with a 3-5 year horizon, the current price of ~$397 represents a reasonable entry point into a world-class franchise at a below-average valuation. Dollar-cost averaging into a position is advisable given near-term uncertainty. Short-term investors should be cautious, as the stock is technically weak and sentiment is negative following the guidance disappointment.

**Target Entry Range:** $380-$410 (accumulation zone near 52-week lows)
**Risk Management:** Consider a stop-loss below $370 if the 52-week low breaks decisively.
