## Company Overview

Dream Finders Homes is a Jacksonville, Florida-based homebuilder that went public in 2021. The company operates an asset-light model, primarily using lot option contracts rather than owning land outright, which reduces capital intensity but creates exposure to lot option expiration risk during downturns. DFH builds primarily in the Southeast and Mid-Atlantic markets, targeting entry-level and first-time move-up buyers. The company completed the acquisition of Crescent Homes in 2022, expanding its geographic footprint and moving into higher price points.

With 34.1 million shares outstanding and a current price of $15.47, the market cap stands at approximately $535M. The company pays no dividend. EPS is reported at $2.14, implying trailing P/E of 7.2x, which equals the forward P/E — suggesting consensus expects flat earnings at best.

## Competitive Landscape

DFH competes against larger, better-capitalized homebuilders (LEN, DHI, PHM, NVR, TOL, MDC/TMHC) with significant disadvantages:

- **Scale:** $535M market cap vs. LEN at ~$35B. Smaller builders have less negotiating power with suppliers, subcontractors, and land sellers.
- **Geographic concentration:** DFH is concentrated in the Southeast and Mid-Atlantic. While Florida and Texas are structurally growing markets, they are also the most competitive for homebuilders due to permissive zoning and available land.
- **Asset-light model:** The lot option strategy is a competitive differentiator in normal times, reducing capital requirements and improving ROE. In downturns, it becomes a liability — lot option deposits are forfeited, and the company has less land inventory to carry through the cycle at reduced basis.
- **Mortgage rate sensitivity:** DFH targets entry-level and first-time move-up buyers, the most rate-sensitive segment of the market. These buyers have the smallest down payments and the highest debt-to-income ratios, making them the first to be priced out when rates rise.

The Bretton Fund letter expressing optimism about DFH's long-term positioning acknowledges competitive merits but provides no near-term catalyst.
