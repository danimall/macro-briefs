## Executive Summary

Chemed Corporation operates two dominant franchises — VITAS Healthcare, the largest US hospice provider, and Roto-Rooter, the largest US residential/commercial plumbing and drain cleaning company. The stock has declined 37% from its 52-week high of $623.61 (April 3, 2025) to ~$398, primarily driven by two negative earnings surprises: a mid-2025 miss that triggered a 15.6% single-day drop, and a Q4 2025 miss on February 25, 2026 that caused a further 14.7% decline. Q4 2025 adjusted EPS of $6.42 missed the Zacks consensus by 8.55%, with revenue flat YoY at $639.3M.

Management guided for 5.5-6.5% VITAS revenue growth in 2026 but warned that 55% of earnings will be back-half weighted, indicating near-term margin pressure as the company works through an unfavorable admissions mix shift (143bp negative acuity mix in Q4) and Roto-Rooter revenue recovery. The $300M share repurchase authorization announced February 13, 2026 represents ~5.6% of current market cap and provides downside support. However, CEO Kevin McNamara's open-market sale of 2,000 shares at $403.18 on March 12 — just two weeks after the earnings miss — is a modestly negative signal.

At 21.7x forward earnings, the stock is not expensive relative to its historical range, but the earnings base itself is under pressure with two consecutive misses. The healthcare services sector has shown strong BUY returns in calibration (+3.48%), but the specific execution risks at Chemed warrant patience until there is evidence that the admissions mix and Roto-Rooter recovery are materializing.

## Company Overview

Chemed Corporation (NYSE: CHE) is a Cincinnati-based holding company with two operating subsidiaries:

**VITAS Healthcare** — The nation's largest provider of end-of-life (hospice) care, operating across multiple states. Revenue is primarily Medicare-reimbursed, creating both stability (government payer) and regulatory risk (rate changes, Medicare Cap limitations). Q4 2025 saw a $2.4M Medicare Cap billing limitation accrued for programs outside Florida.

**Roto-Rooter** — The nation's largest provider of residential and commercial plumbing, drain cleaning, and water restoration services. This segment provides counter-cyclical diversification — plumbing emergencies are non-discretionary — but is sensitive to housing activity levels and consumer spending patterns.

Market cap: ~$5.4B. Shares outstanding: 13.77M (low float, which amplifies price moves on earnings surprises). Current price: ~$398.

## Financial Analysis

### Earnings Performance

Q4 2025 adjusted EPS: $6.42, down 6% YoY and 8.55% below consensus. GAAP EPS: $6.02, up 2% YoY. The gap between adjusted and GAAP reflects the direction of one-time items, but both figures point to margin compression.

Trailing EPS: $18.34 (per profile data). At $398, this implies a trailing P/E of 21.7x. Forward P/E is also listed at 21.7x, suggesting Street estimates have converged around flat-to-minimal growth from the trailing figure — consistent with management's back-half-weighted 2026 guidance.

Revenue: Q4 2025 at $639.3M, flat YoY. For a company that was previously growing revenue mid-to-high single digits, flat revenue combined with margin compression signals a material deceleration.

### Margin Pressure

The earnings call highlighted a 143 basis point negative acuity mix shift at VITAS. This is significant: hospice reimbursement varies by patient acuity level, and a shift toward higher-volume but lower-acuity hospital-based short-stay admissions mechanically reduces revenue per patient day and compresses margin. Management also cited a ~20bp drag from Medicare Cap and other contra-revenue items.

At Roto-Rooter, the CEO acknowledged results "fell short of expectations" and emphasized recovery initiatives — language consistent with revenue softness in the residential services segment, likely reflecting broader consumer spending pressure.

### Capital Allocation

The $300M share repurchase authorization (February 13, 2026) at current prices would retire ~753K shares, or 5.5% of shares outstanding. This is a substantial commitment and provides mechanical EPS support even if operating performance remains subdued. Chemed has historically been an aggressive and effective repurchaser — the share count has declined meaningfully over recent years.

Quarterly dividend of $0.60/share, implying ~$2.40 annualized, for a yield of ~0.60%. This is a token dividend; capital return is primarily via buyback.

### Balance Sheet

Detailed financial statements were not available in the data provided. However, the company's ability to authorize a $300M buyback suggests balance sheet capacity. Chemed has historically maintained moderate leverage (typically 1-2x net debt/EBITDA). The healthcare services business generates strong free cash flow given its asset-light model on the VITAS side.

## Growth Analysis

**VITAS (2026 guidance: 5.5-6.5% revenue growth):** This is a reasonable target given demographic tailwinds — the 65+ US population is growing ~3% annually, and hospice penetration of eligible patients remains below 50%. The constraint is not demand but rather the quality of admissions growth. The 143bp negative mix shift in Q4 demonstrates that growing admissions volume while maintaining margin requires careful referral source management and clinical program development.

**Roto-Rooter:** Management signaled "revenue recovery" as a 2026 priority. In the current macro environment (consumer spending contracting, gasoline prices elevated), discretionary plumbing services face headwinds. Emergency services remain non-discretionary, but water restoration and add-on services are more cyclically sensitive.

**Earnings cadence:** 55% of 2026 earnings back-half weighted implies H1 2026 EPS will be meaningfully below H2. This creates risk of further negative sentiment if Q1 or Q2 results look weak in isolation, even if the full-year target remains achievable.

## Valuation Assessment

At $398 and $18.34 trailing EPS, CHE trades at 21.7x trailing earnings. The forward P/E is also 21.7x, implying consensus expects roughly flat EPS in fiscal 2026.

**Historical context:** CHE has typically traded in a 22-28x P/E range during periods of stable growth, reflecting the quality of its market-leading franchises and consistent free cash flow generation. The current 21.7x multiple is at the low end of this range, which prices in some of the execution risk but does not yet discount a scenario where VITAS mix shift or Roto-Rooter weakness persists beyond 2026.

**52-week range:** $385 to $623.61. Current price is just 3.5% above the 52-week low of $385, which was hit on February 26, 2026 (the day after the earnings miss). The stock has stabilized in the $395-425 range since early March.

**Buyback floor:** $300M authorized buyback at current prices absorbs ~11 weeks of average daily volume. Management's willingness to deploy capital here provides a meaningful price floor, particularly given Chemed's track record of follow-through on buyback authorizations.

**Valuation verdict:** At 21.7x with flat-to-modest growth, the stock is fairly valued for the near term. A BUY would require either (a) a lower price (~18-19x, implying ~$330-350), or (b) evidence that the back-half earnings recovery is materializing.

## Competitive Landscape

**VITAS:** Market leader in US hospice care with significant scale advantages — referral network breadth, regulatory compliance infrastructure, geographic coverage. Hospice is a fragmented industry with the top 10 providers accounting for less than 30% of the market. VITAS benefits from scale in compliance costs, nurse recruitment, and payer negotiation. The main competitive dynamic is the proliferation of smaller, often for-profit hospice providers that may cherry-pick higher-acuity patients or geographic markets, which could contribute to the mix shift problem.

**Roto-Rooter:** Dominant national brand in a highly fragmented plumbing services market. Brand recognition creates meaningful demand generation advantages — Roto-Rooter is one of the few plumbing companies with genuine consumer brand awareness. The competitive moat is in brand + national dispatch infrastructure. Local competitors cannot match the 24/7 national service network.

Both businesses benefit from aging infrastructure (plumbing) and aging population (hospice), creating a durable secular demand backdrop.

## Risk Assessment

**1. VITAS Admissions Mix (HIGH — Active):** The 143bp negative acuity mix shift is the most important operational risk. If VITAS continues to grow admissions through hospital-based short-stay patients rather than home-based longer-stay patients, per-patient revenue and margin will remain compressed. Management acknowledged this on the earnings call, but reversing admissions mix requires changes to referral relationships and clinical programming that take quarters to implement.

**2. Medicare Reimbursement (MODERATE):** VITAS derives the majority of revenue from Medicare. Any changes to hospice reimbursement rates, Medicare Cap calculations, or eligibility criteria directly impact revenue. The $2.4M Medicare Cap accrual in Q4 is a reminder of this sensitivity. In a stagflationary environment where government budgets are under pressure, future Medicare rate increases may lag cost inflation.

**3. Labor Costs (MODERATE):** Nurse and aide recruitment/retention is a structural challenge in healthcare. Wage inflation in healthcare has been running above general inflation, compressing margins for service providers. VITAS requires skilled clinical staff; Roto-Rooter requires skilled tradespeople. Both labor markets are tight.

**4. CEO Selling (MODERATE NEGATIVE SIGNAL):** Kevin McNamara sold 2,000 shares at $403.18 on March 12, 2026 — $806K. He also sold 2,000 shares at $437.55 in November 2025. He retains 93,719 shares (~$37M at current prices), so these sales are less than 2% of his holdings each. The pattern of regular small sales is not alarming in isolation, but selling shortly after a major earnings miss and during a period when management is asking investors to trust a back-half recovery is suboptimal optics.

**5. Macro Sensitivity (LOW-MODERATE):** Hospice care is non-discretionary and Medicare-funded, providing insulation from consumer spending cycles. Roto-Rooter's emergency services are similarly non-discretionary. However, the current stagflationary environment with rising energy costs flows through to fleet costs (Roto-Rooter trucks) and healthcare supply costs.

**6. Low Liquidity:** 13.77M shares outstanding with average daily volume of ~173K shares. This amplifies price moves on earnings surprises (as demonstrated by the 14.7% drop on February 26) and creates execution risk for institutional position changes.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- The stock is 37% off highs and trading near the low end of its historical P/E range, creating a potential entry point if earnings recovery materializes in H2 2026.
- $300M buyback authorization provides 5.5% of market cap in mechanical support.
- Demographic tailwinds for hospice (aging population) and plumbing services (aging infrastructure) remain intact.
- VITAS's 5.5-6.5% revenue growth guidance is achievable given market position and demand backdrop.
- Both businesses have genuine competitive moats — national scale in fragmented markets.

### Bear Case
- Two consecutive earnings misses suggest operational execution problems that may take more than one or two quarters to resolve.
- The 143bp VITAS mix shift is structural (hospital-based referral patterns) and may persist, keeping margins compressed even as revenue grows.
- Back-half 2026 earnings weighting creates multiple quarters of weak reported results before the thesis can be validated, with risk of further multiple compression.
- CEO open-market selling during the recovery period undermines conviction in near-term improvement.
- In a stagflationary macro environment, labor cost inflation and Medicare reimbursement adequacy are both headwinds.
- No analyst price targets available to anchor valuation expectations.

## Conclusion

Chemed operates two high-quality, market-leading businesses with genuine competitive advantages. The 37% drawdown from highs prices in meaningful execution risk, but the near-term earnings trajectory is genuinely uncertain — two consecutive misses, back-half weighted 2026 guidance, and CEO selling create a "show me" setup. At 21.7x forward earnings with flat growth, the stock requires either a lower price or visible evidence of operational recovery to justify a BUY.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The entry point for a BUY would be either (a) price approaching ~$330-350 (18-19x estimated forward earnings), providing a meaningful margin of safety, or (b) Q1 2026 results showing sequential improvement in VITAS admissions acuity mix and positive Roto-Rooter revenue trajectory. Until one of these conditions is met, the risk/reward is balanced rather than compelling.

**Why Not BUY:** The mandatory "Why Not BUY?" gate applies here (score 6.15). Specific reasons: (1) Two consecutive earnings misses with no Q1 2026 data yet to confirm recovery; (2) CEO selling 2,000 shares at $403 on March 12 during the recovery period; (3) Forward P/E of 21.7x with flat growth is fair value, not cheap; (4) Back-half weighted guidance means 2-3 more quarters of potentially weak results before thesis validation.
