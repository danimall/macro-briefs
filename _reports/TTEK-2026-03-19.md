## Executive Summary

Tetra Tech is a consulting and engineering services firm focused on water, environment, infrastructure, renewable energy, and international development, serving both government and commercial clients. The stock has experienced extreme volatility over the past year, rallying from ~$29 in mid-March 2025 to $43.14 in February 2026, then collapsing back to ~$30.78 as of March 18, 2026 — a 29% drawdown from the peak in roughly five weeks.

At ~23x forward earnings with a $8.0B market cap, TTEK sits in a valuation range that is neither cheap nor expensive for its sector. The company benefits from long-duration secular tailwinds (water infrastructure, environmental remediation, energy transition) and recently won a position on a five-year U.S. Army Corps of Engineers contract. However, the absence of detailed financial statements in the available data, combined with the current stagflationary macro regime where federal spending faces DOGE-related scrutiny and rising rates compress multiples for government-adjacent firms, limits conviction in either direction.

## Competitive Landscape

Tetra Tech operates in a fragmented market but holds a top-tier position in water and environmental consulting. Key competitors include AECOM, Jacobs Solutions, Arcadis, WSP Global, and Stantec.

**Competitive advantages:**
- Deep federal government relationships and past performance record, which are critical for winning contract recompetes (incumbency advantage is strong in federal services).
- Specialization in water — the most regulatory-driven and recession-resistant segment of infrastructure.
- Asset-light business model (consulting/engineering) generates higher margins and lower capital intensity than construction-heavy peers.

**Competitive risks:**

## Risk Assessment

**Federal spending risk (HIGH):** The current administration's DOGE initiative and federal workforce reductions create direct risk to the Government Services segment. Even if TTEK's specific contracts are protected, a broader slowdown in new task order issuance would reduce near-term revenue.

**Macro/stagflation risk (MODERATE-HIGH):** Rising rates compress multiples for government-adjacent firms. If the Fed hikes (now 10-15% probability), discount rates rise and TTEK's 23x multiple contracts. Meanwhile, commercial clients may defer discretionary environmental spending in a recession.

**Oil price impact (LOW-MODERATE):** Oil at $119 is net neutral to slightly positive for TTEK. Higher energy costs increase demand for energy efficiency and environmental compliance consulting, but they also pressure commercial clients' budgets.

**Concentration risk (MODERATE):** Heavy exposure to U.S. federal government. International development (USAID) exposure adds geopolitical risk.

**Data gap risk (MATERIAL):** The absence of balance sheet and cash flow data in the provided dataset prevents assessment of leverage, liquidity, and free cash flow generation. This limits conviction.
