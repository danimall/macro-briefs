## Executive Summary

Rocket Lab reported record FY2025 revenue of $602M, up 38% year-over-year, with Q4 revenue of $180M beating estimates. The company's backlog reached $1.85B, providing roughly three years of revenue visibility at current run rates. These are genuine operational milestones for a company transitioning from a small-launch-vehicle provider into a vertically integrated space systems platform, with the Neutron medium-lift vehicle targeted for first launch in Q4 2026.

The problem is price. At $68.41 per share and a $38.8B market cap, RKLB trades at approximately 64x trailing revenue. The company has no trailing or forward P/E because it remains unprofitable, and the financial data provided contains no income statement or balance sheet details to assess the path to profitability. Meanwhile, insiders have been selling at scale: CEO Peter Beck, CFO Adam Spice, COO Frank Klein, General Counsel Arjun Kampani, and two directors collectively disposed of tens of millions of dollars in stock between January and early March 2026. CFO Spice alone exercised options on 731K shares at $1.09 and sold over $100M worth of stock in a single day (January 5). These are not small portfolio rebalancing transactions.

The secular growth thesis — defense-tech spending cycle, space-based national security demand, Neutron's addressable market expansion — is supported by multiple independent data points. But at current valuation, the stock requires Neutron to launch on time, achieve rapid commercial cadence, and for margins to expand materially, all without execution missteps. That is a lot of things that need to go right simultaneously for shares priced at 64x revenue.

## Company Overview

Rocket Lab is a vertically integrated space company headquartered in the US (with significant operations in New Zealand). The company operates across two segments: **Launch Services** (Electron small launch vehicle, HASTE suborbital vehicle, and the in-development Neutron medium-lift vehicle) and **Space Systems** (satellite manufacturing, spacecraft components, mission management software, and on-orbit management).

FY2025 revenue of $602M reflects the growing Space Systems segment, which now likely represents the majority of revenue, alongside increasing Electron launch cadence. The $1.85B backlog is heavily weighted toward Space Systems, per the Cantor Fitzgerald analyst note. The company has completed over 50 Electron launches to date, making it the second-most-frequent orbital launch provider globally behind SpaceX.

Neutron is the key strategic initiative. A reusable medium-lift vehicle designed to carry ~13,000 kg to LEO, Neutron targets the commercial constellation deployment and national security launch markets currently dominated by SpaceX's Falcon 9 and ULA's Vulcan. First launch is targeted for Q4 2026.

**DCF framework (rough):** To justify $38.8B market cap at a 10% discount rate, RKLB would need to generate approximately $3.9B in terminal free cash flow (at a 10x terminal multiple) or demonstrate a path to $2B+ in revenue at 20%+ FCF margins within 5-7 years. Possible but requires multiple assumptions to compound favorably.

**Score: 2.0.** The valuation embeds best-case execution across multiple dimensions over a multi-year horizon. At 64x revenue for an unprofitable company, the risk-reward is asymmetric to the downside on any execution stumble.

## Competitive Landscape

Rocket Lab occupies a strong competitive position as the second-most-active orbital launch provider globally.

**Strengths:**
- Proven Electron vehicle with 50+ launches (highest cadence after SpaceX)
- Vertical integration across launch, spacecraft, components, and software
- Sole credible Western alternative to SpaceX for small/medium-class launches once Neutron operational
- Defense customer relationships deepening (HASTE hypersonic missions for DoD)
- $1.85B backlog provides demand visibility

**Competitive risks:**
- SpaceX dominance: Falcon 9 launch costs are declining, and Starship will further compress pricing. SpaceX's vertical integration (launch + Starlink) creates ecosystem advantages RKLB cannot match.
- ULA's Vulcan, Blue Origin's New Glenn, and Relativity Space are all pursuing the medium-lift market. Neutron arrives into a more competitive field than Electron did.
- Chinese launch providers (CASC, Galactic Energy) offer lower-cost alternatives in non-restricted markets.

**Moat assessment:** Moderate and narrowing. Electron's heritage and reliability create switching costs for existing customers. Vertical integration in space systems is genuinely difficult to replicate. But Neutron competes against incumbents with years of operational data. The competitive position score reflects proven capability partially offset by the SpaceX overhang.

## Conclusion

Rocket Lab is a real company with a real competitive position, genuine growth, and legitimate defense-tech tailwinds. The $602M in FY2025 revenue, 38% growth, and $1.85B backlog are substantive operational achievements. The defense spending cycle, confirmed by multiple independent data points in the world model, provides structural demand support.

The valuation, however, prices in successful Neutron development, on-time launch, rapid commercial adoption, and material margin expansion — simultaneously. At 64x trailing revenue with no profitability, the stock offers minimal margin of safety. Insiders agree: five of the company's six most senior executives and directors sold significant stock in January-March 2026, with the CFO monetizing $104M in a single day.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** For existing holders, the secular thesis justifies maintaining a position with tight risk management. For new capital, the risk-reward at $68 is poor. A better entry point would be either (a) a meaningful pullback to 30-40x revenue ($30-45 range), or (b) successful Neutron first launch confirming the execution thesis. Buying at 64x revenue into a 2.31-beta stock during confirmed stagflation with recession probability approaching 40% is a high-risk proposition that the growth trajectory, however impressive, does not adequately compensate for.
