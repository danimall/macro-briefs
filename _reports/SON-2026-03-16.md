## Executive Summary

Sonoco Products is a global packaging manufacturer that recently reported Q4 2025 results showing 47.1% year-over-year growth in adjusted operating profit, driven primarily by the Eviosys acquisition completed in late 2024. The stock surged to a 52-week high of $58.44 in mid-February 2026, supported by strong 2026 guidance and an investor day that reinforced volumetric growth expectations. At the current price of ~$53.32, shares trade at approximately 9x forward earnings with a 4% dividend yield.

The company is actively implementing price increases — $70/ton on uncoated recycled paperboard and 8% across converted paperboard products effective April 2026 — signaling both pricing power and input cost pressure. The Eviosys acquisition drove record Consumer Packaging segment results and expanded Sonoco's European metal packaging footprint. Truist Financial reiterated a Buy rating in early February with a raised price target.

The fundamental concern is that the easy part of the post-acquisition re-rating has likely already occurred. The stock has appreciated approximately 19% from its March 2025 lows to current levels, and 30%+ year-to-date through the February peak. In a stagflationary macro environment with $100+ oil, rising input costs across petrochemicals and energy, and weakening consumer spending, a packaging company dependent on industrial and consumer volumes faces meaningful cyclical headwinds. The balance of risk/reward at current levels is neutral.

## Company Overview

Sonoco Products Company, headquartered in Hartsville, SC, is a diversified packaging company operating across two primary segments: Consumer Packaging and Industrial Paper Packaging. The company produces tubes, cores, cones, protective packaging, rigid paper containers, metal packaging (via Eviosys), and various other packaging solutions. Sonoco has approximately 99 million shares outstanding with a market capitalization of ~$5.3 billion.

The transformative event in recent history is the Eviosys acquisition, a European metal packaging business that substantially expanded Sonoco's Consumer Packaging revenue base and drove the record operating profit reported in Q4 2025. The company files annually with the SEC (most recent 10-K filed February 26, 2026) and maintains a consistent quarterly dividend of $0.53/share.

## Competitive Landscape

Sonoco competes in fragmented packaging markets against International Paper, Graphic Packaging, Berry Global, Amcor, and Sealed Air, among others. The company's competitive advantages include:

1. **Vertical integration** in recycled paperboard: Sonoco produces its own URB feedstock, providing cost advantages and supply security.
2. **Customer switching costs** in industrial tubes/cores: These are often custom-specified and embedded in customer production lines.
3. **Geographic diversification** enhanced by Eviosys: European metal packaging adds a complementary product line serving food and beverage end markets.

The competitive position is moderate. Packaging is a scale business with meaningful logistics economics (heavy products favor regional production), but barriers to entry are moderate and customers exert price pressure during downturns. The ability to implement $70/ton and 8% price increases demonstrates some near-term pricing power, though the durability depends on industry discipline.

## Risk Assessment

**Macro/cyclical risk (HIGH):** The confirmed stagflationary regime — with oil above $100, core PCE at 3.1%, and negative payrolls — directly threatens both the cost and demand sides of Sonoco's business. Packaging volumes correlate with industrial production and consumer spending, both under pressure.

**Input cost risk (MODERATE-HIGH):** Oil-linked resin costs, energy costs for paperboard mills, and transportation costs are all elevated. The $70/ton URB price increase and 8% converted product increase are defensive moves to protect margins, but there's a lag between cost absorption and price realization.

**Integration risk (MODERATE):** Eviosys is a large acquisition relative to Sonoco's pre-deal size. Integration costs, cultural alignment (European operations), and synergy realization represent execution risk through 2026-2027.

**Leverage risk (MODERATE):** The acquisition likely elevated debt levels significantly. Without balance sheet data, the 35% payout ratio on dividends and the focus on price increases suggest management is prioritizing cash generation, but rising rates increase debt service costs.

**Tariff/trade risk (LOW-MODERATE):** Section 301 probes targeting 60 economies could affect Sonoco's international operations and customer supply chains, though packaging is primarily local production for local consumption.

## Investment Thesis

**Bull Case:**
- Eviosys delivers above-plan synergies, and 2026 becomes a breakout year for operating profit and free cash flow
- Price increases stick without volume loss, expanding margins
- Packaging volumes stabilize or recover as consumer spending normalizes
- Deleveraging accelerates, potential for dividend increases or buybacks by 2027
- At 9x earnings with 4% yield, the stock offers meaningful upside if earnings grow even modestly toward $6.50-7.00

**Bear Case:**
- Stagflationary macro environment compresses volumes in both consumer and industrial packaging segments
- $100+ oil drives input costs higher faster than price increases can compensate
- Eviosys integration proves more costly or complex than projected, particularly in a weaker European economy
- Recession probability at 38-42% would hit cyclical packaging demand directly
- The stock has already re-rated from 6.5x trough to 9x on the Q4 beat — further multiple expansion requires demonstrated earnings growth that may not materialize in a downturn

## Conclusion

Sonoco Products presents a mixed picture. The Q4 2025 earnings beat and Eviosys acquisition success are genuine positives, and the company's pricing actions demonstrate operational discipline. At 9x earnings with a 4% yield, the valuation is not demanding.

However, the stock has already captured most of the post-acquisition re-rating. The macro environment is hostile to cyclical industrial companies: rising input costs, declining consumer spending, and elevated recession probability all create headwinds for packaging volumes. The balance of evidence suggests fair value sits near current levels, with meaningful upside requiring either macro improvement or above-consensus earnings delivery. Better entry would be below $47 (8x earnings), which is plausible if the stagflationary regime persists or deepens.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** Existing holders should maintain positions for the 4% yield and monitor Q1 2026 results for evidence that price increases are sticking without volume deterioration. New positions should wait for a pullback toward the lower end of the $47-50 range.
