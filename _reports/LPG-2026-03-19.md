## Executive Summary

Dorian LPG is a pure-play owner and operator of Very Large Gas Carriers (VLGCs) focused on transporting liquefied petroleum gas globally. The stock trades at $32.80 with a market cap of $1.45B, a forward P/E of 11.6x, and a trailing dividend yield of approximately 10%. The company declared a $0.70/share irregular dividend in January 2026, reflecting its variable distribution policy tied to cash flow generation.

The current geopolitical environment — specifically the Iran conflict escalation, Strait of Hormuz disruption, and strikes on Qatar LNG facilities and Saudi export infrastructure — creates an unusually favorable backdrop for VLGC operators. Longer voyage distances as LPG trade flows reroute away from the Middle East, combined with elevated energy prices broadly, support charter rates. However, Q3 FY2026 earnings of $1.11/share missed the $1.17 consensus estimate, and the stock has already appreciated 58% from its March 2025 lows. Insider selling by both the CFO ($348K at $34.84) and CCO ($1.95M across two sales at $28.85 and $35.19) at recent highs introduces a cautionary signal.

This is a cyclical shipping name with substantial upside if Middle East disruptions persist or intensify, but equally exposed to a sharp reversal if geopolitical normalization compresses charter rates. The 10% dividend yield provides a meaningful carry while waiting, but the yield is irregular and not guaranteed. HOLD at current levels; the risk-reward profile was substantially more attractive at $20-24 where the stock traded through much of Q4 2025.

## Company Overview

Dorian LPG operates a fleet of modern, ECO-designed VLGCs — the largest class of LPG carriers, each capable of transporting approximately 84,000 cubic meters. The company is incorporated in the Marshall Islands, headquartered in Stamford, Connecticut, and listed on the NYSE. Its fiscal year ends March 31.

VLGCs primarily transport LPG (propane and butane) from export terminals in the US Gulf Coast, Middle East, and West Africa to demand centers in Asia (primarily China, India, Japan, South Korea). The VLGC market is characterized by high capital intensity, long asset lives (25+ years), and charter rate volatility driven by LPG supply/demand fundamentals, fleet utilization, ton-mile demand, and geopolitical factors.

## Growth Analysis

Growth for Dorian LPG is principally a function of:

1. **Charter rate environment:** VLGC spot rates are the primary earnings driver. The current geopolitical environment (Hormuz disruption, Gulf infrastructure attacks) extends ton-mile demand as cargoes reroute. This is a cyclical tailwind, not structural growth.

2. **US LPG export expansion:** The US has become the world's largest LPG exporter. Continued growth in shale gas production and export terminal capacity (Enterprise, Energy Transfer facilities along the Gulf Coast) supports long-haul VLGC demand on the US-Asia route.

3. **Fleet composition:** Dorian's emphasis on ECO vessels provides fuel cost advantages (~15-20% lower consumption) and compliance with IMO 2023 carbon intensity regulations, positioning the fleet favorably for charter preference.

4. **Limited fleet growth optionality:** VLGC newbuilding prices are elevated ($90M+), and Dorian's capital allocation has favored dividends/buybacks over fleet expansion. This constrains growth but supports per-share economics.

The forward P/E of 11.6x implies the market expects no earnings growth — essentially pricing in current charter rates continuing. If rates strengthen further due to prolonged Middle East disruption, there's upside to estimates. If rates normalize, earnings could decline materially.

## Valuation Assessment

At 11.6x forward P/E with a 10% dividend yield, Dorian LPG appears inexpensive on a headline basis. However, context matters:

- **Cyclicality discount is warranted.** VLGC charter rates can swing 50%+ in a single year. The appropriate P/E for a cyclical at mid-to-peak earnings is lower than for a stable business. A 10-12x multiple is typical for shipping companies during elevated rate environments.
- **Dividend yield is not sustainable at 10%.** The irregular dividend structure means yield will decline if charter rates fall. Investors should not capitalize this as a bond-like yield.
- **52-week range context:** The stock hit $16.66 in April 2025 and $38.40 in March 2026 — a 130% range. At $32.80, it sits at the 77th percentile of this range, suggesting much of the geopolitical premium is priced in.
- **Price trajectory:** The stock rallied from ~$20 (March 2025) to $38.40 (March 2, 2026), then pulled back 15% to $32.80. The February 6 earnings release at $32.35 (up from $30.06 the day prior) suggests the market digested the earnings miss and refocused on the macro/geopolitical tailwind.

Compared to BW LPG (BWLP), the world's largest VLGC operator (~50 vessels), Dorian trades at a slight premium reflecting its younger, more fuel-efficient fleet but smaller scale.

## Competitive Landscape

The VLGC market is served by ~300 vessels globally. Key competitors include:

- **BW LPG (BWLP):** ~50 VLGCs, largest fleet globally. Reports Q4 2025 results March 3, 2026. Has invested in LPG dual-fuel propulsion technology.
- **Avance Gas:** Norway-listed, ~15 VLGCs.
- **Petredec, Navigator Holdings:** Smaller fleet segments.

Dorian's competitive advantages are: (1) a modern, ECO fleet with lower operating costs; (2) a focused pure-play strategy without diversification into smaller vessel classes; (3) experienced management with deep relationships in the LPG trading community. The disadvantage is scale — BW LPG's fleet is roughly 2x Dorian's, providing more chartering flexibility and counterparty diversity.

The VLGC market is structurally supply-constrained in the near term. Orderbook-to-fleet ratios are moderate (~15-20%), and shipyard capacity is absorbed by LNG carriers, container ships, and tankers. New VLGC deliveries are staggered through 2027-2028, limiting supply growth.

## Risk Assessment

**Geopolitical binary risk (HIGH):** The stock's rally from $20 to $33+ is substantially driven by Middle East conflict premium. If Hormuz reopens or a ceasefire emerges, charter rates and the stock could retrace 20-30% rapidly. Per the world model, rapid de-escalation probability is only 3-5%, but negotiated wind-down (months) is at 22%.

**Charter rate cyclicality (HIGH):** VLGC spot rates are volatile. A reversion to pre-conflict rate levels would compress earnings by 30-50%. The forward P/E of 11.6x offers limited cushion against a rate downturn.

**Insider selling (MODERATE):** CFO Theodore Young sold 10,000 shares at $34.84 ($348K) and CCO Tim Hansen sold 60,000 shares across two transactions at $28.85 and $35.19 ($1.95M combined) in January-February 2026. CEO John Hadjipateras bought 15,000 shares at $27.30 ($409K) in January 2026. The net insider activity is mixed — CEO buying is constructive, but the CFO and CCO selling at recent highs is a cautionary data point.

**Concentration risk (MODERATE):** Revenue is concentrated in a single vessel class (VLGCs) transporting a single commodity (LPG) on a limited number of trade routes. Any disruption to US LPG exports or Asian import demand directly impacts earnings.

**Marshall Islands incorporation (LOW-MODERATE):** Common in shipping but provides weaker shareholder protections than US incorporation.

**Oil price impact (MIXED):** Oil at $119 (per world model) increases LPG production as a byproduct of crude/gas processing, supporting cargo availability. However, it also raises bunker fuel costs for vessels not on time charter (though ECO vessels partially mitigate this). Net effect is modestly positive for VLGC demand through ton-mile expansion.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- Middle East disruption persists beyond 6 months (40% probability per world model for acute phase extending beyond 6 weeks, 25% for Gulf-wide escalation). This extends ton-mile demand and elevates charter rates above current levels.
- US LPG export capacity continues expanding, supporting long-haul VLGC demand on the highest-margin trade route (US Gulf to Asia via Cape of Good Hope).
- The 10% dividend yield, while variable, provides meaningful cash returns while the geopolitical premium persists.
- CEO buying at $27.30 signals management confidence in fundamentals.
- VLGC supply growth is constrained through 2027, limiting competitive pressure even if demand moderates.

### Bear Case
- Geopolitical de-escalation compresses charter rates and earnings by 30-50%. At $32.80, the stock is priced for elevated rates to continue.
- Q3 FY2026 already missed estimates at $1.11 vs. $1.17 consensus — suggesting rate capture may be below expectations even in a favorable environment.
- CFO and CCO selling a combined $2.3M in stock at $28-35 over three transactions in 8 weeks suggests informed insiders view the stock as fairly-to-fully valued.
- Irregular dividend could be cut significantly if charter rates normalize, removing the yield support.
- A global recession (40-45% probability per world model) would reduce LPG demand from petrochemical feedstock and residential heating, compressing rates regardless of geopolitical factors.

## Conclusion

Dorian LPG is a well-run VLGC operator benefiting from a geopolitical premium that has driven the stock up 58% since March 2025. At $32.80, the valuation (11.6x forward P/E, 10% dividend yield) looks reasonable but not compelling — the stock is at the 77th percentile of its 52-week range, the most recent quarter missed estimates, and two of three C-suite officers with recent transactions were sellers at or near current prices.

The macro backdrop per the world model (oil at $119, every Middle East export pathway threatened, SPR exhaustion probability 70-80%) supports continued elevated LPG shipping rates for at least the next quarter. However, this is already substantially reflected in the price. The risk-reward was far more attractive at $20-24 where the stock traded through late 2025.

**HOLD.** The stock offers a strong carry via dividends while the geopolitical premium persists, but the entry point for new positions is suboptimal. Upgrade to BUY on a pullback to $24-26 (roughly 7-8x forward earnings, representing a reversion to pre-escalation sentiment) or on evidence that charter rates are sustainably above current levels independent of the Hormuz disruption.
