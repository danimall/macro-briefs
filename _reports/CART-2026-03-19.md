## Executive Summary

Maplebear Inc., operating as Instacart, is a $9B market cap online grocery delivery and pickup platform trading at $38.17, roughly 29% below its 52-week high of $53.50 reached in August 2025. The company reported Q4 2025 earnings of $0.53/share (adjusted), in line with prior year and marginally above the $0.52 consensus, on revenue that beat estimates. CNBC reported "the strongest quarter of gross transaction value in three years," and the stock initially popped 14% on the earnings release before giving back most of the gains.

The fundamental picture is mixed. Instacart has a defensible position in online grocery with growing enterprise partnerships, and the ~24x forward P/E is not demanding for a platform business. But the stock has been in a persistent downtrend since August, falling from $51+ to the high-$30s, and insider selling has been broad-based — the General Counsel sold over $4.7M in March alone, the CAO sold $364K, and a director sold shares in February. In a stagflationary macro regime where consumer spending is contracting and diesel costs are surging (directly compressing last-mile delivery economics), Instacart faces real headwinds that the market appears to be pricing progressively.

## Company Overview

Maplebear Inc. operates the Instacart platform, connecting consumers with personal shoppers for on-demand grocery delivery and pickup across North America. Revenue streams include transaction fees, advertising (a high-margin business from CPG brands targeting Instacart's user base), and enterprise technology solutions for grocers. The company went public via IPO in September 2023 and has been working to diversify beyond its core delivery marketplace into advertising and enterprise software (Instacart Platform).

- Shares outstanding: 240.6M
- Trailing/Forward P/E: ~24x
- EPS (trailing): $1.60
- No dividend
- 52-week range: $32.73 – $53.50

## Valuation Assessment

At ~24x forward earnings, CART trades at a modest premium to the broader market but a significant discount to high-growth tech platforms. The question is whether CART deserves a growth multiple or a utility-like multiple.

Arguments for fair/cheap:
- 24x is below the typical range for ad-supported marketplace businesses (DoorDash trades at much higher multiples)
- If advertising revenue continues to scale at 30%+ while marketplace grows at 10-15%, blended growth could support this multiple
- Stock is down 29% from highs, compressing valuation

Arguments for expensive:
- Flat YoY adjusted EPS ($0.53 in Q4 2025 vs. $0.53 in Q4 2024) suggests limited earnings growth
- In a stagflationary environment, P/E compression is likely for consumer-facing platforms
- No visibility on free cash flow generation, buyback programs, or capital return

The stock touched $32.73 (52-week low) in February 2026, just before the earnings beat sent it temporarily higher. Current price at $38.17 is 17% above that trough, suggesting some of the positive earnings signal has been absorbed.

## Competitive Landscape

Instacart operates in a three-way competitive dynamic:
1. **DoorDash (DASH):** Direct competitor in grocery delivery, with broader food delivery platform creating cross-sell advantages
2. **Amazon Fresh / Whole Foods:** Amazon's grocery delivery infrastructure represents the most formidable long-term threat
3. **Retailer-owned delivery:** Walmart, Kroger, and other grocers are investing in their own delivery capabilities, potentially disintermediating Instacart

Instacart's competitive advantages include:
- Largest dedicated grocery delivery network with deep retailer partnerships
- Growing advertising platform that creates a dual-sided revenue model (consumers pay fees, CPG brands pay for placement)
- Enterprise technology offerings that embed Instacart deeper into retailer operations

The risk is that as major retailers build their own digital capabilities, Instacart becomes less essential. The enterprise platform strategy is designed to counter this by making Instacart infrastructure rather than a competitor.

## Risk Assessment

**Macro/Consumer risk (HIGH):** In the current stagflationary regime — unemployment at 4.4%, core PCE at 3.1%, diesel at $5/gallon — grocery delivery is vulnerable. Delivery fees and tips represent a ~20-30% premium over in-store shopping. As consumers feel squeezed, the first cut is often convenience services. This is the dominant near-term risk.

**Diesel/energy cost risk (MODERATE-HIGH):** Instacart relies on gig workers driving personal vehicles. At $5/gallon diesel (and elevated gasoline prices), shoppers demand higher compensation or reduce availability. This creates either margin pressure (Instacart subsidizes) or consumer price increases (demand destruction).

**Competitive risk (MODERATE):** DoorDash, Amazon, and retailer-owned solutions all represent ongoing competitive pressure. Instacart's advertising moat provides defensibility, but the delivery marketplace itself has limited switching costs.

**Insider selling (NOTABLE):** General Counsel Morgan Fong exercised options and sold ~$4.7M in stock in March 2026. CAO Lisa Blackwood-Kapral sold $364K. Director Lily Sarafan sold $128K. Director Mary Beth Laughton sold $165K in November. CEO Chris Rogers had $1.65M in tax withholdings in February. While some of this is routine (tax withholdings, option exercises), the pattern of open-market sales across multiple executives is a moderate negative signal. No insider buying was observed in the dataset.

**Hedge fund disposition:** Incline Global sold its entire $15.5M position, counterbalanced by D1 Capital maintaining CART as its largest position. Mixed institutional sentiment.

**Price action:** The stock fell from $51 to $33 between August 2025 and February 2026 (a 35% decline), partially recovered post-earnings, then drifted back to $38. Volume spiked to 17-21M shares on several decline days (Sep 19, Sep 30, Aug 13), indicating institutional distribution. The downtrend remains intact.

## Investment Thesis

**Bull Case:**
- Advertising revenue is the hidden gem — high-margin, growing faster than marketplace, and creates a defensible competitive moat as CPG brands build measurement frameworks around Instacart's platform
- Enterprise technology deepens retailer relationships, converting Instacart from a channel competitor to infrastructure provider
- 24x forward P/E is reasonable if the company can accelerate earnings growth toward 15-20% annually
- Strongest GTV quarter in three years suggests underlying demand momentum despite macro headwinds
- D1 Capital's conviction ($829M position) implies sophisticated long-term thesis

**Bear Case:**
- Flat YoY EPS growth despite revenue beats suggests the company is investing heavily without earnings leverage
- Consumer spending contraction in a stagflationary environment directly impacts willingness to pay delivery premiums
- Diesel at $5/gallon creates a structural cost headwind for the gig delivery model
- Broad-based insider selling with no insider buying
- DoorDash and Amazon are both investing aggressively in grocery, and major retailers are internalizing delivery capabilities
- Stock has been in a clear downtrend since August 2025 with no technical evidence of reversal

## Conclusion

CART is a HOLD. The business has genuine competitive advantages through its advertising platform and retailer partnerships, and the Q4 2025 results demonstrated improving GTV trends. At ~24x forward earnings, the stock is not expensive for a platform business. However, the stagflationary macro environment directly pressures the consumer delivery model through both demand destruction (consumers cutting delivery spending) and cost inflation (diesel/fuel costs for gig workers). Insider selling is consistent and broad-based, and the price trend remains negative. The right entry point is likely $30-33 (near the 52-week low), where the valuation would compress to ~20x forward and provide a margin of safety against continued consumer weakness. For investors with existing positions, the earnings quality and advertising growth story justify holding through the macro turbulence, but adding at current levels lacks a clear catalyst.
