## Executive Summary

Range Resources is a pure-play Appalachian Basin natural gas producer focused on the Marcellus Shale in southwest Pennsylvania. The company reported Q4 2025 EPS of $0.82 vs. $0.68 consensus, generated over $650M in full-year 2025 free cash flow, and received an IBD Composite Rating upgrade to 96 (top 4% of all stocks). Morningstar describes the company as having "decades of inventory," which provides long-duration production visibility in a basin with the lowest-cost gas production economics in the U.S.

The stock is trading at $43.51, within $0.14 of its 52-week high of $43.65, at 15.6x forward earnings. This valuation is mid-range for E&P companies and reflects an improvement in natural gas fundamentals relative to 2024's depressed pricing environment. The current macro backdrop — oil at $98-108, geopolitical supply disruption through Hormuz, and rising energy prices globally — provides a tailwind for natural gas producers, though RRC's direct exposure is to Henry Hub gas prices and NGL pricing rather than crude oil.

The core concern is insider selling activity: CFO Mark Scucchi sold $1.25M in stock on March 2, and VP/Principal Accounting Officer Ashley Kavanaugh sold her entire vested position ($814K across two transactions). Insider sales immediately after equity award vesting are common, but the CFO selling 30,000 shares at $41.66 while the stock approaches multi-year highs warrants monitoring. I rate RRC a HOLD — the operational fundamentals are solid, but the stock is priced near fair value at current gas prices, and insider activity suggests limited near-term upside from management's perspective.

## Company Overview

Range Resources operates exclusively in the Marcellus Shale play in southwest Pennsylvania, producing approximately two-thirds natural gas and one-third natural gas liquids (NGLs including ethane, propane, butane, and condensate). The NGL component is important because it diversifies revenue away from pure Henry Hub gas pricing and provides higher-value commodity exposure.

Key characteristics:
- **Market cap:** $10.2B
- **Beta:** 0.60 (notably low for an E&P company, reflecting gas-weighted production vs. oil-weighted peers)
- **Forward P/E:** 15.6x
- **Dividend yield:** 0.93%
- **52-week range:** $30.32 - $43.65
- **Current price:** $43.51 (at 52-week high)

The Marcellus Shale is the largest natural gas producing basin in the United States, and Range's acreage position in the core of the play provides low breakeven costs. Morningstar's characterization of "decades of inventory" suggests 20+ years of drilling locations at current activity levels, which is among the longest inventory durations in the E&P sector.

## Growth Analysis

### Production Growth
The "record drilling and completion" performance referenced in the earnings call suggests RRC achieved per-well productivity improvements or faster drilling times (or both). For Marcellus operators, efficiency gains typically manifest as:
1. Longer lateral lengths (reducing per-Mcf development costs)
2. Faster drilling days (more wells per rig per year)
3. Optimized completion designs (higher initial production rates)

### Market Positioning
Natural gas demand growth has two structural drivers relevant to RRC:
1. **LNG export capacity expansion:** U.S. LNG export terminals under construction will add demand for Appalachian gas
2. **Power generation demand:** AI data center buildout and coal plant retirements are increasing gas-fired power demand

Both drivers are multi-year in nature. RRC's Appalachian production is well-positioned to serve East Coast LNG export projects and East Coast power generation demand.

### NGL Optionality
The NGL component (~one-third of production) provides exposure to petrochemical feedstock demand and export markets. NGL prices have been supported by export demand from European and Asian buyers. Sulphur disruption from the Iran conflict could indirectly support NGL pricing through broader energy complex tightness.

### Growth Score: 6.5/10
Moderate growth trajectory. Production growth likely low-to-mid single digits given capital discipline. Revenue growth depends heavily on commodity prices. Decades of inventory provides duration but not acceleration.

## Risk Assessment

### Commodity Price Risk (PRIMARY)
RRC's earnings are directly tied to Henry Hub natural gas prices and NGL pricing. Natural gas is among the most volatile commodities, with seasonal swings of 50%+ common. A return to sub-$2.50/Mcf gas (seen as recently as 2024) would compress earnings significantly from current levels.

### Macro/Geopolitical Context
Per the world model, the current energy supply disruption (Hormuz, Saudi production cut, Kharg Island strikes) primarily impacts oil markets. Natural gas benefits indirectly through:
1. Coal-to-gas switching economics improving when gas is relatively cheaper than oil
2. Global LNG demand increasing as European and Asian buyers diversify from Russian/Middle Eastern supply
3. NGL pricing rising with the broader energy complex

However, RRC is predominantly a domestic gas producer. A recession (38-42% probability per world model) would reduce industrial gas demand and power generation load, partially offsetting geopolitical supply tightness.

### Regulatory Risk
Appalachian production faces ongoing regulatory attention around methane emissions, water use, and drilling permits in Pennsylvania. The Section 301 trade probes (60 economies) have no direct impact on domestic gas production but could indirectly affect demand through recession transmission.

### Pipeline/Basis Risk
Appalachian gas producers are vulnerable to basis differentials — the spread between local wellhead prices and Henry Hub. Insufficient pipeline takeaway capacity can force Appalachian gas to trade at significant discounts. Mountain Valley Pipeline completion has partially alleviated this constraint, but it remains a structural risk.

### Risk Score: 6.0/10
Moderate risk. Commodity price volatility is the dominant factor. Geographic concentration in one basin adds operational risk (weather, regulatory). Low beta (0.60) partially mitigates portfolio-level risk. The geopolitical backdrop is net positive for energy prices but primarily benefits oil-weighted producers more directly.

## Conclusion

Range Resources is a well-run, low-cost Appalachian gas producer that delivered strong Q4 2025 results and generated substantial free cash flow. The stock's 0.60 beta makes it less volatile than oil-weighted peers, and its decades of inventory provide long-term production visibility. However, at $43.51 (99.7% of 52-week high), 15.6x forward earnings, and with the CFO selling $1.25M in stock at these levels, the risk-reward is balanced rather than compelling. The current geopolitical energy disruption benefits natural gas producers indirectly, but the primary commodity exposure is to Henry Hub gas pricing, which is more sensitive to domestic weather and demand than to Hormuz.

I would look to accumulate RRC on a pullback to the $36-38 range (12-13x forward earnings), where the FCF yield would expand to ~8% and provide a more adequate margin of safety. At current levels, the stock is fairly valued for a $3.50-4.00/Mcf gas environment. **HOLD.**
