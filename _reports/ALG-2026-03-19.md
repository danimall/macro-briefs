## Executive Summary

Alamo Group is a mid-cap ($2.0B market cap) manufacturer of industrial and vegetation management equipment headquartered in Seguin, Texas. The company reported Q4 2025 results on March 2, 2026 that missed consensus on both lines: adjusted EPS of $1.70 vs. $2.06 expected, on revenue of $373.7M vs. $385.3M in the prior year quarter. Full-year Vegetation Management division revenue fell 16.7% to $654.1M, reflecting cyclical weakness in agricultural equipment markets. Adjusted EBITDA margin of 12.0% in Q4 shows the company is maintaining reasonable profitability despite the top-line contraction.

The stock has traded down from a 52-week high of $233.29 (reached in August 2025) to $166.79 currently, a 28.5% decline. The post-earnings selloff was severe: the stock dropped from $218.47 on March 2 to $184.78 the next day (a 15.4% single-day decline), and has continued to drift lower. At ~19.5x trailing EPS of $8.59, the valuation is not distressed but reflects compression from recent-year peaks. William Blair initiated coverage with an Outperform rating on March 16, citing 27.8% upside to a consensus target, which provides a modest positive signal. CEO Hureau's open-market purchase of 460 shares at $164.40 on March 9 is a small but directionally positive insider signal.

The core investment tension is between ALG's strong competitive position in niche industrial/municipal equipment markets and the multi-quarter earnings decline driven by agricultural cyclicality, operational inefficiencies in consolidated facilities, and a macro environment characterized by rising input costs (steel, diesel) and potential tariff headwinds. The company's strategic pivot in its snow business toward margin quality over market share is sensible but will take time to execute.

## Company Overview

Alamo Group operates in two primary segments: Vegetation Management (mowing, clearing, forestry equipment) and Industrial Equipment (street sweepers, vacuum trucks, snow removal, infrastructure maintenance). The company serves municipalities, governmental agencies, agricultural customers, and industrial end markets. It has historically grown through a combination of organic operations and bolt-on acquisitions.

The January 2026 acquisition of Petersen Industries, a manufacturer of equipment (likely vacuum trucks or refuse handling based on ALG's portfolio), extends the Industrial Equipment segment. The company has a long track record of acquiring and integrating niche industrial manufacturers.

## Financial Analysis

**Revenue:** Q4 2025 net sales of $373.7M represent a 3.0% decline from Q4 2024's $385.3M. The full-year picture is more concerning: Vegetation Management revenue fell 16.7% to $654.1M from $785M (implied), a $131M decline. This segment is the company's larger division and is directly exposed to the agricultural equipment downturn.

**Earnings:** Q4 2025 GAAP EPS was $1.28 (net income $15.5M). Adjusted EPS was $1.70, down 28.9% from $2.39 in Q4 2024. The gap between GAAP and adjusted ($0.42/share) indicates restructuring charges, likely related to the facility consolidation referenced in the earnings call summary. Full-year trailing EPS of $8.59 implies the remaining three quarters of 2025 averaged approximately $2.30 adjusted EPS per quarter — suggesting the Q4 miss represents an accelerating decline rather than a one-time event.

**EBITDA Margins:** Q4 adjusted EBITDA of $44.8M on $373.7M revenue = 12.0% margin. This is a reasonable level for industrial equipment manufacturing, though it likely compresses from mid-cycle levels. Management's emphasis on "optimizing" operations and addressing "operational inefficiencies in consolidated facilities" suggests margin recovery is a medium-term project.

**Balance Sheet:** Detailed financial statements were not available in the data provided. The company completed the Petersen Industries acquisition in January 2026, which likely involved debt financing given ALG's acquisition-driven growth model. The 10-K filed March 2, 2026 would contain current balance sheet detail. Market cap of $2.0B with 12.1M shares outstanding suggests a relatively concentrated float.

**Capital Return:** The company pays a dividend yielding approximately 0.8%, indicating a modest but consistent cash return to shareholders.

## Growth Analysis

**Near-term outlook is challenged.** The 16.7% decline in Vegetation Management revenue reflects the agricultural equipment cycle downturn that has affected the broader ag-equipment sector. This cycle typically lasts 2-3 years from peak to trough. With the current macro regime featuring elevated oil prices ($98-108 per the world model), rising diesel costs ($5/gallon), and potential tariff disruptions under Section 301 probes, the recovery timeline could extend.

**Industrial Equipment offers partial offset.** Municipal and governmental customers tend to have more stable budgets, and infrastructure maintenance is less discretionary than agricultural equipment purchases. The Petersen Industries acquisition expands this segment.

**Management's strategic pivot in snow business** — prioritizing margin quality over market share — is a rational response to cyclical weakness but will constrain near-term revenue recovery in that sub-segment.

**Fertilizer cost inflation** (CF Industries at high levels per macro context) feeds through to farmer economics, which in turn affects agricultural equipment demand. This is a negative second-order effect for ALG's Vegetation Management division.

**The William Blair Outperform initiation** suggests at least one institutional analyst sees value emerging after the selloff, with an implied consensus target roughly 28% above the February 25 price (approximately $213 at that date, implying a target around $272). However, this target predates the Q4 earnings miss and subsequent selloff.

## Valuation Assessment

At $166.79 with trailing EPS of $8.59, ALG trades at approximately 19.4x trailing earnings. The forward P/E of 19.4x (per the company profile data) suggests consensus expects roughly flat earnings over the next twelve months.

**Historical context:** ALG traded at a 52-week high of $233.29, implying the stock has lost ~28% of its peak value. At its August 2025 high, the stock traded at approximately 27x trailing EPS — significantly stretched for an industrial cyclical. The current 19.4x is more reasonable but still not cheap for a company in the declining phase of its earnings cycle.

**For context:** At a normalized mid-cycle EPS (if we assume $10-11/share based on the trajectory from prior years), the stock at $167 would trade at 15-17x earnings, which is more attractive. But the path to earnings normalization is unclear given the macro headwinds.

**Relative to sector:** Small-cap industrial equipment manufacturers typically trade at 12-18x earnings. At 19.4x on declining earnings, ALG does not screen as cheap. A more compelling entry point would be in the 14-16x range, implying approximately $120-137 on current trailing EPS, or approximately $150-165 on a normalized $10.50 EPS.

## Competitive Landscape

Alamo Group has built a defensible position in niche industrial and vegetation management equipment through decades of acquisitions and brand building. Key competitive attributes:

- **Diversified product portfolio** across mowing, clearing, vacuum trucks, street sweepers, and snow removal equipment
- **Strong municipal/government customer relationships** with recurring maintenance and replacement cycles
- **Acquisition integration capability** demonstrated over a long track record (Petersen Industries being the latest example)
- **Limited direct competition** in many niches — ALG competes with specialized regional manufacturers rather than large diversified industrials

The primary competitive risk is from agricultural OEMs (Deere, AGCO, CNH) that could expand into ALG's vegetation management niches during their own cyclical downturns.

## Risk Assessment

**Cyclical agricultural downturn (HIGH):** The 16.7% decline in Vegetation Management revenue is the primary risk. Agricultural equipment cycles typically last 2-3 years from peak to trough, and there is limited visibility on when demand stabilizes.

**Tariff exposure (MODERATE-HIGH):** As a manufacturer sourcing steel, components, and potentially finished goods from international supply chains, ALG faces direct cost pressure from Section 301 tariffs targeting 60 economies. The company also exports equipment internationally.

**Input cost inflation (MODERATE-HIGH):** Steel, diesel, and freight costs are elevated. Diesel at $5/gallon increases both manufacturing and logistics costs. The company's ability to pass through these costs depends on demand elasticity, which is weakened during a cyclical downturn.

**Acquisition integration risk (MODERATE):** The Petersen Industries acquisition was completed in January 2026, during a period of operational challenges with existing facility consolidation. Concurrent integration and restructuring increases execution risk.

**Operational execution (MODERATE):** Management explicitly acknowledged "operational inefficiencies in consolidated facilities" and is implementing KPI-driven improvements. This suggests the restructuring is not yet delivering planned benefits.

**Insider activity (MIXED):** CEO Hureau's $75,623 open-market purchase on March 9 at $164.40 is a positive signal, though modest in absolute terms. Director Grooms sold 499 shares ($87,422) on March 11 at $175.20. The CFO's dispositions were tax-withholding related (non-discretionary). Net insider signal is neutral to slightly positive.

## Conclusion

Alamo Group is a quality industrial compounder going through a cyclical downturn in its largest segment. The stock has already declined meaningfully (28% from highs), but at 19.4x declining earnings, the valuation does not yet reflect a compelling entry point for a stock facing agricultural cyclical weakness, rising input costs, tariff uncertainty, and incomplete operational restructuring.

The William Blair initiation and CEO purchase provide modest positive signals, but the macro environment (stagflation, $5 diesel, tariff probes across 60 economies) creates persistent headwinds to both the Vegetation Management recovery timeline and the company's cost structure. The Q4 miss suggests the earnings trajectory has not yet stabilized.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The stock becomes more interesting at 15-16x trailing EPS ($129-137) or at demonstrated evidence of earnings stabilization. Monitoring for: (1) Q1 2026 earnings showing sequential improvement in Vegetation Management, (2) backlog trends confirming demand stabilization, (3) Petersen integration progress, and (4) macro developments in diesel/steel costs and tariff policy.

**Why not BUY (mandatory gate for score >6.0):** The valuation at 19.4x declining earnings is not sufficiently discounted for the magnitude of cyclical and macro headwinds. The Q4 miss was substantial and the earnings trajectory is still negative. Consumer/end-market demand recovery requires multiple macro conditions (lower diesel, stabilized farm economics, reduced tariff uncertainty) that the current world model does not support in the near term. Better entry exists at $130-145.
