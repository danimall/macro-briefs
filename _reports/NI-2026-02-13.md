## Executive Summary

NiSource Inc is a regulated natural gas and electric utility serving approximately 2.9 million residential, commercial, and industrial customers across several U.S. states. The company operates through two primary segments: Columbia Operations (natural gas distribution) and NIPSCO Operations (electric and gas in Indiana). With a market capitalization of approximately $21.6 billion, NiSource has demonstrated solid earnings momentum, reporting full-year 2025 GAAP net income of $929.5 million ($1.95 EPS), a substantial increase from $739 million in the prior year — representing approximately 26% year-over-year earnings growth.

The company's investment thesis centers on its ambitious $28 billion five-year capital plan, which includes $6–$7 billion dedicated to supporting Amazon's data center development, driving an estimated 11% rate base CAGR. Management has emphasized customer affordability, disciplined capital deployment, and expanding large-load opportunities, while reaffirming 2026 guidance. NiSource beat Q4 2025 consensus EPS estimates ($0.51 actual vs. $0.49 expected), reinforcing confidence in execution.

While the stock has performed well — trading near its 52-week high of $45.62 at $45.18 — the forward P/E of ~28.5x is above the utility sector average, suggesting the market has already priced in much of the near-term growth. The ~2.7% dividend yield provides modest income, and the low beta of 0.64 offers defensive characteristics. The key question for investors is whether the data center-driven growth narrative justifies the premium valuation relative to peers.

## Company Overview

NiSource Inc is a pure-play regulated utility headquartered in the United States. The company delivers natural gas to approximately 2.4–2.9 million customers across multiple states and provides electric service through its NIPSCO subsidiary in northern Indiana. As a regulated utility, NiSource earns returns on its invested capital (rate base) as approved by state regulatory commissions, providing a relatively predictable earnings stream.

**Key Operating Segments:**
- **Columbia Operations** — Natural gas distribution across several states (Ohio, Pennsylvania, Virginia, Kentucky, Maryland, and Indiana)
- **NIPSCO Operations** — Electric generation and distribution plus natural gas distribution in northern Indiana

The company has been undergoing a significant strategic transformation, including the innovative "GenCo" structure for power generation assets and a pivot toward supporting large-load customers such as data centers, particularly through its partnership with Amazon.

## Growth Analysis

NiSource presents one of the more compelling growth stories in the regulated utility space:

1. **$28 Billion Five-Year Capital Plan:** This massive investment program is expected to drive 11% rate base CAGR, well above the typical utility range of 6–8%. The capital plan spans infrastructure modernization, pipeline replacement, and generation investments.

2. **Data Center Opportunity:** The $6–$7 billion earmarked for Amazon's data center project represents a transformative growth driver. Data center demand is surging nationally, and NiSource's service territory and regulatory positioning make it an attractive partner for hyperscale operators.

3. **GenCo Structure:** NiSource's innovative generation company structure has been highlighted by analysts (Seeking Alpha) as offering data center upside with reduced political risk compared to peers.

4. **Earnings Trajectory:** The ~26% YoY earnings growth from FY2024 to FY2025 is exceptional for a utility. While this pace is unlikely to be sustained, the underlying rate base growth of 11% should translate into high-single-digit to low-double-digit EPS growth over the medium term.

5. **Reaffirmed 2026 Guidance:** Management's reaffirmation of 2026 guidance during the Q4 earnings call provides near-term visibility and confidence.

### Growth Risks
- Execution on the massive capital plan requires continued regulatory approval
- Data center demand could shift geographically or face permitting headwinds
- The forward P/E of 28.5x being higher than trailing 24.2x may indicate an expected earnings dip or dilution in the near term

## Competitive Landscape

NiSource operates in the regulated utility space, where competition is limited by franchise territories. Key competitive dynamics include:

**Strengths:**
- **Regulatory Moat:** As a regulated utility, NiSource has exclusive franchise rights in its service territories, creating a natural monopoly
- **Geographic Diversification:** Multi-state gas distribution operations reduce single-jurisdiction regulatory risk
- **Data Center Positioning:** The Amazon partnership and GenCo structure differentiate NiSource from peers like Atmos Energy, WGL Holdings, and CenterPoint Energy
- **Scale:** Serving ~2.9 million customers provides scale advantages in operations and regulatory proceedings

**Challenges:**
- **Natural Gas Transition Risk:** Long-term policy shifts toward electrification could pressure gas distribution volumes
- **Peer Competition for Data Centers:** Other utilities (Dominion, Duke, AEP) are also pursuing data center load growth
- **Limited Electric Franchise:** Unlike diversified utility peers, NiSource's electric operations are limited to Indiana (NIPSCO)
