## Executive Summary

Robert Half Inc trades at $22.72 with a market cap of $2.26B after a 57.6% decline from its 52-week high of $55.24. The staffing and consulting company faces a dual headwind: cyclical pressure from a weakening labor market (NFP -92K, unemployment 4.4%) and secular pressure from AI-driven automation of white-collar tasks that directly threatens its core temporary staffing business. Trailing EPS of $1.33 implies profitability has already compressed significantly from peak levels, and analysts have been steadily cutting price targets — Barclays to $25 (March 6), BMO Capital to $32 (February 17).

The stock's apparent cheapness at 17x trailing P/E is misleading. RHI's earnings power is declining, and the forward trajectory points lower given macro conditions. The $0.59/quarter dividend ($2.36 annualized, 10.4% yield) signals either deep value or an unsustainable payout — with limited financial statement data available to distinguish between the two, the burden of proof falls on the bull case, which lacks supporting evidence. Director Kempthorne sold $188K in stock at $28.87 in October 2025, and no insider has made open-market purchases.

## Company Overview

Robert Half operates through three segments: Contract Talent Solutions (temporary/contract staffing in finance, technology, and administrative roles), Permanent Placement Talent Solutions, and Protiviti (a global consulting firm specializing in internal audit, risk, and technology consulting). The company was founded in 1948 and is headquartered in Menlo Park, California. It has historically been considered one of the highest-quality staffing firms, with particular strength in specialized finance and accounting placement.

## Financial Analysis

The financial statement data provided is empty, which severely limits quantitative analysis. What can be inferred from available data:

**Earnings:** Trailing EPS of $1.33 on 99.4M diluted shares implies net income of approximately $132M. Forward P/E equals trailing P/E at 17.07x, suggesting the market expects flat earnings — a generous assumption given the macro environment.

**Dividend:** The quarterly dividend of $0.59/share ($2.36 annualized) represents a payout ratio of approximately 178% of trailing EPS ($2.36 / $1.33). This is unsustainable at current earnings levels. Either earnings must recover materially, or the dividend will be cut. A dividend cut would remove a key support for the stock price and could trigger forced selling by income-oriented funds.

**Market Cap Compression:** At $2.26B, RHI is valued at roughly 17x what appears to be trough-adjacent earnings. Historical staffing company valuations at cycle troughs tend to range 10-15x trough earnings, suggesting additional downside risk if earnings haven't bottomed.

**Insider Activity:** Director Kempthorne sold 6,505 shares at $28.87 in October 2025 — 36% of his post-sale holdings. CEO Waddell received equity grants of ~90K shares in March 2025 at ~$53 (now underwater by 57%). Protiviti President Tarantino had tax withholding dispositions at $24.42 in February 2026. No open-market insider purchases are visible, which is notable given the stock's steep decline.

## Growth Analysis

Robert Half's growth prospects are impaired on multiple fronts:

**Cyclical Headwinds:** The staffing industry is a leading indicator of labor market health. With NFP at -92K, unemployment at 4.4%, and recession probability at 38-42%, demand for temporary and permanent placement services contracts. Hiring freezes at client firms directly reduce RHI's revenue. The company's high exposure to finance, accounting, and technology staffing means it is particularly vulnerable to white-collar job market weakness.

**AI Displacement:** Barclays noted "limited AI risk" in its March 2026 coverage, but this assessment deserves scrutiny. AI tools are automating tasks in exactly the domains where RHI places temporary workers — bookkeeping, data entry, basic financial analysis, administrative support, and entry-level technology roles. The long-term demand for temporary white-collar workers in these categories is structurally declining. Protiviti's consulting business provides some offset, but the core staffing business generates the majority of revenue.

**BMO Capital's Assessment:** BMO lowered its target to $32 (from $35) citing "profit recovery slows." This confirms that even the analyst community, which tends toward optimism, sees earnings recovery as pushed out.

**Bill Rate Trends:** A Zacks article from March 11 noted "rising bill rates" as a positive, which would improve revenue per placement even as placement volumes decline. This partially offsets volume pressure but cannot fully compensate for a shrinking addressable market.

Staffing companies at cycle troughs historically trade at 10-15x trough earnings, implying potential downside to $8-15 in a severe scenario.

The 10.4% dividend yield looks like a deep-value signal, but the math shows the payout exceeds earnings. Without access to the balance sheet, it is impossible to determine how long RHI can fund the dividend from cash reserves and free cash flow. Barclays' $25 target (now only 10% above the current price) and BMO's $32 target bracket the near-term range. The consensus appears to be $25-$36, but given the continued deterioration in macro conditions since these targets were set, the lower end is more credible.

## Competitive Landscape

Robert Half competes with Hays plc, Adecco, Randstad, ManpowerGroup, Kelly Services, and Kforce in staffing, plus the Big Four accounting firms and various boutique consultancies through Protiviti. RHI has historically commanded premium bill rates due to its specialization in finance/accounting talent and its brand reputation.

However, competitive advantages in staffing are inherently narrow. Switching costs for clients are low — a finance director can call three staffing firms in an afternoon. RHI's differentiation comes from its database of pre-vetted candidates and speed of placement, but AI-powered matching platforms (LinkedIn, Indeed, Upwork, Fiverr) are eroding this advantage. The emergence of AI agents that can perform entry-level finance and administrative tasks reduces the total addressable market for temporary workers in RHI's core verticals.

Protiviti provides some strategic moat through deeper client relationships and specialized expertise in risk/compliance consulting, but it is a smaller segment and faces competition from established consultancies with broader capabilities.

## Risk Assessment

**Primary Risks:**

1. **Recession Transmission (HIGH):** Staffing companies are among the first to feel recessionary pressure. Clients cut temporary workers before permanent staff. With recession probability at 38-42% and NFP already negative, RHI's revenue trajectory is directly exposed.

2. **Dividend Sustainability (HIGH):** The 178% payout ratio on trailing earnings is unsustainable. A cut would remove the primary bull case for income investors and could trigger 10-15% additional downside.

3. **AI Structural Displacement (MEDIUM-HIGH):** While Barclays characterized AI risk as "limited," the 3-5 year trajectory of AI automation in finance, accounting, and administrative functions directly reduces the addressable market for RHI's core staffing segments.

4. **Earnings Still Declining (MEDIUM-HIGH):** The convergence of trailing and forward P/E at 17x suggests the market prices flat earnings, but the macro backdrop (stagflation, negative payrolls, rising unemployment) makes further earnings deterioration likely.

5. **No Insider Buying (MEDIUM):** Despite a 57% price decline, no insider has made open-market purchases. Director Kempthorne sold in October at ~$29. This is a negative signal about internal confidence.

**Mitigating Factors:**

- Protiviti provides diversification and is less cyclically sensitive than staffing
- Rising bill rates partially offset volume declines
- RHI's brand and specialized focus in finance/accounting provide some defensibility
- Historically, staffing stocks can rally sharply off cycle troughs (50-100%+ recoveries are common)

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
RHI is a high-quality, asset-light staffing franchise trading at a multi-year low. If recession is avoided or proves shallow, earnings stabilize near current levels, and the stock re-rates to 20-25x as the cycle turns. Protiviti provides structural growth. The dividend yield attracts income investors. At $22.72, the stock prices in a severe downturn that may not fully materialize. Historical precedent: RHI recovered from $28 to $90+ after the 2020 COVID trough.

### Bear Case
Earnings haven't bottomed. The staffing industry faces both cyclical contraction (recession, hiring freezes) and secular disruption (AI automation of white-collar tasks). The 10.4% dividend yield is a trap — the 178% payout ratio forces a cut within 2-3 quarters unless earnings recover. No insider is buying. The stock is at 17x earnings that are still declining, meaning the true trough multiple hasn't been set. Fair value in a recession scenario is $12-18, implying 20-45% additional downside.

## Conclusion

Robert Half fits the profile of a value trap rather than a deep value opportunity. The stock has declined 57.6% in twelve months, but the decline is driven by fundamental deterioration (declining staffing volumes, rising unemployment, AI displacement of white-collar temp work) rather than temporary dislocation. The dividend yield of 10.4% is mathematically unsustainable at a 178% payout ratio, and a cut would remove the primary support for the stock. No insider has made open-market purchases despite the steep decline. Analysts are cutting targets — Barclays to $25, BMO to $32 — and the macro regime (stagflation, negative NFP, 38-42% recession probability) directly impairs RHI's core business.

Per calibration guidance, AVOID calls should target deteriorating trajectories rather than static weakness. RHI meets this criterion: earnings are declining, the macro is worsening for staffing demand, AI displacement is structural, the dividend is at risk, and insiders aren't buying. The contrarian rebound checklist does not trigger — there is no evidence of abnormal short interest, no insider buying in the trailing 90 days, and while estimates have been cut, the cuts may prove insufficient.

**Recommendation: AVOID.**

Appropriate entry for reconsideration would require: (1) evidence of staffing volume stabilization (positive sequential growth), (2) dividend coverage returning above 100% of earnings, (3) price below $15 (10-12x genuine trough earnings), or (4) insider open-market purchases.
