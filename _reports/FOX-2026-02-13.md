## Executive Summary

Fox Corporation is a focused media company operating across cable news (Fox News), broadcast television (Fox Broadcasting), sports programming, and the fast-growing Tubi streaming platform. The company recently reported strong fiscal Q2 results, hitting $5.18 billion in revenue on the back of ad gains, cable growth, and a seven-game World Series in October. The stock currently trades at $51.39, representing a trailing P/E of ~12.1x and a forward P/E of ~10.1x — both meaningfully below broader market multiples.

The investment case for Fox is anchored in its dominant competitive position in cable news, its high-value sports rights portfolio, and the rapid growth of Tubi as a free ad-supported streaming service. These strengths are partially offset by the secular decline in linear television viewership, a dual-class share structure that concentrates control with the Murdoch family, and cyclical exposure to advertising and political ad spending. The stock's low beta of 0.52 and ~0.9% dividend yield position it as a relatively defensive media holding.

Fox has been one of the stronger-performing media stocks, with the broader media sector seeing significant gains in 2025. The company's strategic pivot toward sports, news, and streaming — while avoiding the capital-intensive scripted entertainment model — provides a differentiated and arguably more resilient business model compared to peers.

## Company Overview

Fox Corporation was spun off from 21st Century Fox in March 2019 following the sale of most entertainment assets to The Walt Disney Company. The remaining entity focuses on:

- **Fox News Media**: The #1 rated cable news network in the U.S., including Fox News Channel, Fox Business Network, and Fox News Digital.
- **Fox Sports**: Premium sports rights including NFL, MLB (World Series), college football, NASCAR, and soccer (FIFA World Cup rights).
- **Fox Television Stations**: A portfolio of 29 owned-and-operated broadcast TV stations in major markets.
- **Tubi**: A free, ad-supported streaming platform that has become one of the fastest-growing AVOD services in the U.S.
- **Fox One**: A newer bundled subscription product combining Fox's news and sports content.

The company is headquartered in New York, trades on the NASDAQ, and has a market capitalization of approximately $22.8 billion. The Murdoch family maintains voting control through the Class A share structure.

## Growth Analysis

### Key Growth Drivers

1. **Tubi Expansion**: Tubi has emerged as a major growth engine for Fox. The free ad-supported streaming platform has been gaining significant market share in the AVOD space, competing with Pluto TV (Paramount), Roku Channel, and Amazon Freevee. Tubi benefits from Fox's content library and promotional capabilities across its broadcast and cable networks.

2. **Fox One Launch**: Fox's new bundled subscription product, Fox One, is an important strategic initiative. According to recent reporting, sports and live news have been primary drivers of sign-ups, with news consumers showing particularly strong retention ("stickier" behavior). This represents a direct-to-consumer monetization opportunity.

3. **Sports Rights Portfolio**: Fox holds premium sports rights including NFL (Thursday Night Football through Amazon, Sunday games), MLB (World Series), college football, NASCAR, and FIFA World Cup 2026. The upcoming 2026 FIFA World Cup, co-hosted by the U.S., represents a significant potential revenue catalyst.

4. **Political Advertising Cycles**: While fiscal Q2 saw a year-over-year decline in political advertising (post-election cycle), the cyclical nature of political ads provides periodic revenue boosts. Fox News and Fox Television Stations are prime beneficiaries during election years.

5. **Digital Transformation**: Fox News Digital has been among the most-visited news websites in the U.S., providing additional advertising revenue and data monetization opportunities.

### Growth Concerns
- Linear TV subscriber declines continue to pressure affiliate fee revenue
- Sports rights costs are escalating across the industry
- Tubi's profitability timeline remains uncertain as it scales

**Estimated earnings growth**: The gap between trailing P/E (12.07x) and forward P/E (10.09x) implies approximately 19.6% expected earnings growth, suggesting the market expects meaningful near-term improvement.

## Competitive Landscape

### Strengths
- **Fox News Dominance**: Fox News has maintained its position as the #1 rated cable news network for over two decades. This provides a highly predictable and profitable revenue stream with strong viewer loyalty.
- **Sports Moat**: Premium live sports rights are among the most valuable content in media, providing must-watch programming that commands premium advertising rates and resists cord-cutting pressures.
- **Tubi's AVOD Position**: Tubi has carved out a strong position in the free streaming market, differentiating Fox from peers pursuing expensive SVOD strategies.

### Competitive Threats
- **Cord-cutting**: Linear TV subscriber declines continue to erode traditional cable/broadcast economics.
- **Streaming competition**: Netflix, YouTube, Amazon, and Apple are competing aggressively for sports rights and viewer attention.
- **News competition**: CNN, MSNBC, and digital-native news outlets compete for audiences, though Fox News has shown remarkable audience resilience.
- **Sports rights inflation**: The cost of securing premium sports content continues to escalate, potentially compressing margins.

### Industry Dynamics
The media industry is undergoing significant consolidation (e.g., Paramount/Skydance, potential WBD deals). Fox's focused portfolio of news, sports, and stations — deliberately narrower than diversified peers — may make it either an acquisition target or a disciplined operator that avoids value-destructive content spending.

## Risk Assessment

### Key Risks

1. **Secular Decline in Linear TV** (High): Cord-cutting continues to accelerate. Fox's cable and broadcast businesses face structural subscriber decline, though premium content (news, sports) provides relative resilience.

2. **Dual-Class Share Structure** (Moderate-High): The Murdoch family controls Fox Corporation through supervoting Class A shares, creating governance risk. Minority Class B shareholders have limited influence over corporate decisions.

3. **Advertising Cyclicality** (Moderate): Fox is heavily dependent on advertising revenue, which is sensitive to economic cycles. A recession would likely impact ad spending across Fox's properties.

4. **Political/Regulatory Risk** (Moderate): Fox News's strong partisan positioning creates brand risk and has led to significant litigation (e.g., the Dominion Voting Systems settlement). Future legal or regulatory challenges cannot be ruled out.

5. **Sports Rights Cost Escalation** (Moderate): As Fox's current sports rights deals expire, renewals are likely to come at significantly higher costs, potentially pressuring margins.

6. **Key Person Risk** (Moderate): The Murdoch family succession dynamics and leadership transitions create uncertainty about long-term strategic direction.

7. **Concentration Risk** (Moderate): Heavy dependence on Fox News as the primary profit center means that any significant disruption to that franchise would disproportionately impact overall results.

### Mitigating Factors
- Low beta (0.52) suggests the stock is less volatile than the broader market
- Diversification into streaming via Tubi reduces linear TV dependence
- Strong free cash flow generation supports shareholder returns
- FIFA World Cup 2026 provides a near-term catalyst

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- **Valuation re-rating**: At ~10x forward earnings, Fox is meaningfully undervalued relative to the quality of its assets. A re-rating toward 13-14x forward earnings would imply ~30-40% upside.
- **Tubi monetization**: As Tubi scales and improves its ad technology, it could become a significant profit contributor, potentially worth $5-10 billion as a standalone property.
- **FIFA World Cup 2026**: The U.S.-hosted World Cup represents a once-in-a-generation sporting event that will drive massive viewership and advertising revenue for Fox.
- **Fox One growth**: Successful scaling of Fox One as a DTC product could create a high-margin recurring revenue stream.
- **Share buybacks**: Continued aggressive buyback activity reduces share count and enhances per-share metrics.
- **Defensive positioning**: In a potential recession, Fox's low beta and stable news/sports viewership provide relative safety.

### Bear Case
- **Cord-cutting acceleration**: If linear TV subscriber declines accelerate beyond expectations, affiliate fee revenue could fall sharply.
- **Litigation overhang**: Future legal challenges similar to the Dominion case could result in significant financial penalties and reputational damage.
- **Governance discount**: The dual-class structure permanently limits Class B shareholder rights and may warrant a sustained valuation discount.
- **Sports rights inflation**: Rising costs for sports content could erode the profitability advantage Fox currently enjoys from its sports programming.
- **Advertising downturn**: A macro-driven decline in ad spending would directly impact Fox's largest revenue source.
- **Peak Fox News**: Any structural decline in Fox News viewership or ratings dominance would remove the company's most important competitive advantage.

## Conclusion

**Overall Recommendation: MODERATE BUY**

Fox Corporation presents a compelling value opportunity in the media sector. At approximately 10x forward earnings with a low beta of 0.52 and a ~0.92% dividend yield, the stock offers an attractive entry point for investors seeking media exposure with defensive characteristics. The company's dominant position in cable news, premium sports portfolio, and rapidly growing Tubi platform provide multiple avenues for value creation.

The near-term catalysts are strong: the 2026 FIFA World Cup, continued Tubi scaling, Fox One subscriber growth, and robust sports programming. The recent revenue beat of $5.18 billion demonstrates that Fox's core businesses remain healthy despite headwinds from political ad cyclicality and cord-cutting.

However, investors must be mindful of the dual-class share structure, litigation risks, secular linear TV pressures, and advertising cyclicality. The stock is appropriate for investors with a 1-3 year time horizon who can tolerate these risks in exchange for what appears to be a discounted valuation on high-quality media assets.

**Target Price Range**: $60-$68 (representing 12-13x forward earnings)
**Upside Potential**: 17%-32% from current levels
