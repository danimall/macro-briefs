## Executive Summary

Inspire Medical Systems is a single-product medtech company focused on neurostimulation therapy for obstructive sleep apnea (OSA). The company delivered a strong Q4 2025 with 12% revenue growth, adjusted EPS of $1.65 (beating estimates by 139%), and expanding gross margins. Full-year 2025 revenue grew 14% with reported EPS of $4.89. These results were overshadowed by 2026 revenue guidance of $950M-$1B, which disappointed the market primarily because of coding and reimbursement uncertainty around the company's next-generation Inspire V system.

The stock has declined approximately 67% from its 52-week high of $172.95 to $55.15 as of March 17, 2026. The selloff accelerated in three distinct legs: August 2025 (Q2 results/guidance concerns, -32% in one day), December 2025 (further reimbursement clarity deterioration), and February 2026 (post-Q4 earnings guidance disappointment). A law firm investigation into officers and directors was initiated in March 2026, adding governance risk to an already stressed situation. The company also has an interim CFO, which reduces confidence in financial stewardship during a critical product transition.

At ~11x forward P/E, the stock is trading at levels historically reserved for structurally impaired medtech businesses. If the reimbursement issues are temporary and coding gets resolved within 2-3 quarters, the current price represents meaningful upside. If Inspire V adoption stalls or payers use the coding ambiguity to reduce reimbursement rates permanently, there is further downside. The risk/reward is balanced at current levels — cheap enough to merit monitoring, uncertain enough to avoid building a position until the coding resolution path becomes visible.

## Company Overview

Inspire Medical Systems manufactures and sells an implantable neurostimulation system for the treatment of moderate-to-severe obstructive sleep apnea. The Inspire system is the only FDA-approved device of its kind — a hypoglossal nerve stimulator that patients activate nightly to maintain airway patency during sleep. The company has benefited from strong clinical data, growing physician adoption, and a large untreated patient population (estimated 22M+ moderate-to-severe OSA patients in the US alone, most of whom are non-compliant with CPAP therapy).

The company is headquartered in Minnesota and traded on NYSE. Market capitalization stands at approximately $1.6 billion on ~28.6 million shares outstanding.

## Growth Analysis

Inspire's growth trajectory has been strong historically — 14% revenue growth in FY2025, with consistent double-digit growth over prior years based on the product adoption curve. The OSA market is large and underpenetrated; CPAP non-compliance rates remain high (estimated 30-50% of patients), and Inspire's clinical data supporting efficacy has expanded.

The critical growth headwind is the Inspire V product transition. CEO Timothy Herbert described the coding challenge directly: healthcare centers and physicians face ambiguity about which procedure codes to use for the Inspire V system. This coding uncertainty means:

1. **Payer pre-authorization delays** — hospitals may defer procedures until coding clarity exists
2. **Reimbursement rate uncertainty** — incorrect codes could result in lower payments or claim denials
3. **Physician adoption hesitation** — surgeons may continue implanting the older system to avoid billing complexity

The $950M-$1B guidance range implies management expects these issues to persist through much of 2026. If resolved in H1 2026, the upper end becomes achievable. If coding disputes extend into H2, the lower end or worse becomes likely.

The company's long-term growth drivers remain intact: OSA prevalence is increasing with obesity trends, competitive alternatives remain limited, and international expansion (particularly Europe) provides a second growth leg. But these longer-term drivers are discounted when the near-term reimbursement pathway is unclear.

## Valuation Assessment

At 11.3x trailing earnings, INSP is trading at a significant discount to both its own history and the medtech sector median (typically 20-30x for growth-stage medtech). This discount reflects three things:

1. **Earnings quality uncertainty** — the Q4 beat included what appears to be a large non-recurring item (GAAP EPS of $4.66 in Q4 alone vs. $4.89 for the full year suggests unusual items in prior quarters, likely losses or charges)
2. **Forward growth compression** — if 2026 revenue grows only 0-5%, the P/E on normalized earnings should be lower than for a 14%+ grower
3. **Structural reimbursement risk** — the market is pricing some probability that Inspire V coding issues are a permanent reimbursement headwind rather than a temporary transition friction

**Scenario analysis:**
- **Bull case (coding resolved Q2 2026):** Revenue hits $1B+, EPS recovers to $5-6 range, P/E re-rates to 18-22x → stock worth $90-130. Roughly 60-135% upside.
- **Base case (coding resolved H2 2026):** Revenue lands at $950-975M, EPS flat to slightly down → $4.50-5.00, P/E stays compressed at 12-15x → stock worth $55-75. Roughly flat to 36% upside.
- **Bear case (permanent reimbursement impairment):** Revenue declines or stagnates, margin compression, EPS falls to $3-4 range, P/E stays 10-12x → stock worth $30-48. 13-45% downside.

The current price approximately discounts the base case. Upside requires resolution of coding uncertainty; downside requires permanent impairment.

## Competitive Landscape

Inspire's competitive position is its strongest attribute. The Inspire system is the only FDA-approved hypoglossal nerve stimulator for OSA. Competitors include:

- **CPAP devices** (ResMed, Philips) — dominant standard of care, but 30-50% non-compliance creates Inspire's addressable market
- **Oral appliances** — less effective for moderate-to-severe OSA
- **Surgical alternatives** (UPPP, maxillomandibular advancement) — invasive with variable efficacy
- **Emerging competitors** — Nyxoah (NYXH) has a competing hypoglossal nerve stimulator with dual-sided stimulation, but has not yet achieved comparable reimbursement coverage or clinical evidence base

The moat is real but narrowing. Nyxoah's DREAM trial data and potential US approval could introduce meaningful competition within 12-24 months. Inspire's first-mover advantage in physician relationships, reimbursement infrastructure, and clinical data remains significant, but the Inspire V coding issues highlight the fragility of reimbursement-dependent moats.

## Risk Assessment

**High-severity risks:**

1. **Reimbursement/coding resolution timeline (PRIMARY RISK):** The Inspire V coding issue is binary in nature. If CMS and commercial payers establish clear, favorable coding, the overhang lifts. If coding remains ambiguous or results in lower reimbursement rates, procedure volumes will be structurally impaired. No clear timeline for resolution has been communicated.

2. **Law firm investigation (Kahn Swick & Foti):** Announced March 2, 2026, this investigation targets officers and directors related to events beginning in August 2025 (the first major selloff). While ambulance-chasing law firms routinely file these investigations after stock declines, the timing — referencing specific August 2025 events — suggests potential claims around disclosure adequacy or insider knowledge of reimbursement headwinds before the August selloff.

3. **Interim CFO:** Richard Buchholz is serving as interim CFO. A medtech company navigating its most significant product transition without a permanent CFO reduces confidence in financial guidance credibility and capital allocation decisions.

4. **Single-product concentration:** 100% of revenue derives from the Inspire system for OSA. Any regulatory, reimbursement, or safety issue with this one product line would be existential.

5. **Macro environment:** In a stagflationary environment with rising healthcare cost consciousness, payers and hospital systems may defer elective surgical procedures. Inspire implants are typically elective — patients can continue using (or not using) CPAP. Procedure deferrals directly reduce revenue.

**Insider activity:** Recent transactions are all grant/award or tax withholding — no open market purchases or sales. CEO Herbert received 62,153 shares in a grant and sold 4,010 for tax withholding at $59.53. No insider has bought shares on the open market at these depressed levels, which is a mildly negative signal for a stock that has declined 67%.

## Investment Thesis

- International expansion continues as a second growth vector

- Law firm investigation uncovers disclosure deficiencies, leading to securities litigation

## Conclusion

INSP presents a classically difficult analytical problem: a fundamentally strong franchise (sole FDA-approved product, large underpenetrated market, expanding margins, profitable) with a near-term uncertainty that has created a significant valuation dislocation. The stock is 3.8% above its 52-week low and trades at 11x earnings — historically cheap for medtech.

The missing piece is visibility into coding resolution. Without a timeline or mechanism for resolving the Inspire V reimbursement ambiguity, the market cannot confidently underwrite forward earnings. The law firm investigation and interim CFO add governance uncertainty. No insider has purchased shares on the open market.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The valuation is attractive enough to prevent an AVOID classification — 11x P/E with a dominant market position is not a broken business. But the reimbursement uncertainty is structural enough to prevent a BUY — the coding issue could resolve favorably (H2 2026 recovery thesis) or could reveal permanent payer pushback on Inspire V economics. The right entry point is after CMS/AMA provides coding guidance or after a quarter where Inspire V adoption metrics demonstrate that reimbursement is functioning. A compelling BUY zone would be below $45 (where the stock would trade at ~9x trailing earnings, pricing in meaningful permanent impairment) or above $70 on confirmed coding resolution (where de-risked growth would justify a higher multiple). Between those levels, the risk/reward is roughly balanced.
