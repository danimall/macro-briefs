## Executive Summary

AAR Corp is a mid-cap aerospace MRO (Maintenance, Repair, and Overhaul) and defense services provider trading at $101.91, representing a $4.0B market cap. The company has delivered strong stock performance over the past year (+66.3% per Zacks), driven by rising commercial aviation aftermarket demand, government contract wins (including a $159.7M Air Force contract announced March 9, 2026), and favorable industry dynamics as aging aircraft fleets require more maintenance.

The core analytical problem is valuation. The trailing P/E of 42.6x is elevated for an aerospace services company, and the forward P/E of 320.9x signals either a dramatic expected earnings decline or a structural charge/transition that will temporarily depress profitability. Without detailed financial statements (which were unavailable in the data feed), it is impossible to determine the cause with precision. The stock sits 16% below its 52-week high of $121.64 and 119% above its 52-week low of $46.51. Given the macro backdrop of confirmed stagflation, rising oil costs (which increase airline operating expenses and could compress MRO budgets), and the extreme forward multiple, initiating a position here carries unfavorable risk-reward.

## Company Overview

AAR Corp provides aviation services to commercial airlines, government operators, MROs, and OEMs. Its business segments include parts supply, repair and engineering, integrated solutions, and government services (expeditionary airlift, maintenance, and logistics). The company is headquartered in Wood Dale, Illinois and trades on the NYSE under ticker AIR.

Key recent developments:
- **$159.7M Air Force contract** (March 9, 2026) won by AAR Manufacturing LLC subsidiary
- **New CFO appointment:** Dylan Wolin named CFO on February 11, 2026
- **Stock outperformance:** +66.3% over the trailing year vs. +17.7% for the S&P 500

4. **Revenue growth context:** The stock's +66% move over the past year reflects substantial revenue/earnings growth that has already occurred. The question is whether the forward P/E disconnect signals the end of that growth phase.

## Competitive Landscape

AAR operates in a fragmented MRO market with both OEM-affiliated competitors (e.g., Lufthansa Technik, ST Engineering, GE Aviation Services) and independent providers. Key competitive advantages:

- **Scale and breadth:** AAR's combined parts supply + repair capabilities create cross-selling opportunities that smaller independents lack.
- **Government services diversification:** The Air Force contract demonstrates continued trust from DoD procurement. Government services provide revenue stability during commercial aviation downturns.
- **Inventory positioning:** In a parts-constrained environment, AAR's existing inventory and supplier relationships represent a meaningful competitive advantage that is difficult to replicate quickly.

Competitive risks include OEM vertical integration (Boeing and Airbus expanding their own aftermarket services), price competition from lower-cost Asian MRO providers, and contract concentration risk on the government side.

## Risk Assessment

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
AAR is positioned at the intersection of two structural growth vectors: commercial MRO demand from aging fleets and rising defense spending from an extended Middle East conflict. The $159.7M Air Force contract validates government revenue potential. The MRO market has regulatory-mandated demand (aircraft must be maintained regardless of economic conditions), providing more downside protection than typical cyclicals. If the forward P/E anomaly reflects a one-time charge rather than sustained earnings decline, the stock could re-rate to ~25-30x normalized earnings, implying $60-70 in share value from trailing earnings alone, or higher if growth continues.

### Bear Case
The forward P/E of 320.9x implies consensus expects an ~87% earnings decline. Even if this reflects a temporary disruption, buying at 42.6x trailing earnings in a stagflationary environment with $100+ oil (which directly pressures airline MRO budgets) is buying at peak cycle multiples. Beta of 1.30 means the stock falls harder than the market in a correction (40-50% probability of further equity correction per world model). The CFO change adds execution risk. Airlines under margin pressure from fuel costs will renegotiate MRO contracts downward, compressing AAR's margins.

## Conclusion

AAR Corp has strong structural positioning in a growing MRO market with genuine defense spending tailwinds. The $159.7M Air Force contract and +66% trailing stock performance reflect real fundamental improvement. However, the 42.6x trailing P/E is premium for the sector, the 320.9x forward P/E signals severe near-term earnings compression, the macro environment (stagflation, $100+ oil) pressures AAR's airline customers, and the lack of detailed financial statement data prevents independent verification of balance sheet and cash flow claims. **HOLD** with a potential upgrade if: (1) the forward earnings picture clarifies and normalizes, (2) the stock pulls back to $70-80 (implying ~30x trailing), or (3) oil prices decline below $85 removing the airline margin pressure overhang.
