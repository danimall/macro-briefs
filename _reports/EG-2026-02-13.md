## Executive Summary

Everest Group Ltd (EG) is a Bermuda-domiciled global reinsurance and insurance company with a market capitalization of approximately $14.1 billion. The company recently reported full-year 2025 results that demonstrated a significant recovery from a challenging 2024, delivering annual net income of $1.6 billion (10.5% ROE) and net operating income of $1.9 billion (12.4% operating ROE). Management returned $397 million to shareholders through share repurchases during the year, signaling confidence in the company's intrinsic value.

However, the most recent quarter (Q4 2025) showed some headwinds, with revenue declining 4.6% year-over-year to $4.42 billion and EPS of $13.26 missing consensus estimates by approximately 4.1%. Gross written premiums also declined year-over-year, suggesting the company is exercising underwriting discipline in a softening market rather than chasing volume. This strategic restraint, while potentially limiting near-term growth, could benefit long-term profitability if pricing cycles turn unfavorable for undisciplined competitors.

Trading at a forward P/E of approximately 10.3x with a 2.4% dividend yield and a remarkably low beta of 0.34, Everest Group presents a compelling value proposition for investors seeking insurance sector exposure with defensive characteristics. The stock currently trades at $336.12, well within its 52-week range of $302.44–$370.21, suggesting it is neither at a deep discount nor at stretched levels.

## Company Overview

Everest Group Ltd is a global provider of reinsurance and insurance solutions, headquartered in Hamilton, Bermuda. The company operates across multiple lines of business and geographies, serving as one of the world's leading reinsurance platforms. Everest underwrites property, casualty, and specialty insurance and reinsurance lines, serving clients worldwide through offices in the Americas, Europe, and Asia.

The company is listed on the NYSE under ticker "EG" and has a long track record in the global reinsurance market. As a Bermuda-domiciled entity, Everest benefits from a favorable tax and regulatory environment, which is common among major reinsurers.

## Financial Analysis

### Profitability and Earnings

Everest Group reported strong full-year 2025 results:

- **Annual Net Income:** $1.6 billion
- **Annual Net Operating Income:** $1.9 billion
- **Net Income ROE:** 10.5%
- **Net Operating Income ROE:** 12.4%
- **Total Shareholder Return (TSR):** 13.1%

These figures represent a dramatic recovery from the prior year, when Q4 2024 alone posted a loss of $18.39 per share — likely driven by elevated catastrophe losses. The Q4 2025 operating income of $13.26 per share marks a significant swing back to profitability, though it fell slightly short of consensus estimates ($13.53).

The gap between the trailing P/E (25.55x) and the forward P/E (10.25x) is notable. This discrepancy suggests that 2024 earnings were substantially depressed (likely due to catastrophe events), and 2025/2026 earnings are expected to normalize at significantly higher levels. Based on the forward P/E of 10.25x and the current price of $336.12, implied forward earnings are approximately $32.78 per share.

### Revenue Trends

Q4 2025 revenue came in at $4.42 billion, representing a **4.6% year-over-year decline**. Gross written premiums also declined year-over-year during the quarter. While this may appear concerning, it is consistent with a disciplined underwriting strategy — Everest appears to be pulling back from business that does not meet its return thresholds rather than growing for growth's sake. This is a hallmark of well-managed reinsurance companies during potentially softening pricing cycles.

### Capital Allocation

The company repurchased **$397 million** in common shares during 2025, demonstrating a shareholder-friendly capital allocation strategy. Combined with the 2.41% dividend yield, total capital return to shareholders was substantial. For a company with a ~$14.1 billion market cap, $397 million in buybacks represents roughly 2.8% of market cap, implying a combined shareholder yield of approximately **5.2%** (dividends + buybacks).

### Balance Sheet

While detailed balance sheet data was not available from the data sources, several indicators suggest reasonable financial health:
- A 10.5% net income ROE suggests adequate but not excessive leverage.
- The ability to repurchase nearly $400 million in shares while maintaining dividend payments suggests strong surplus capital generation.
- The low beta of 0.34 indicates the market views EG as a defensive, financially stable holding.

## Growth Analysis

### Revenue Growth

The near-term growth outlook is mixed. Q4 2025 saw a 4.6% decline in revenue year-over-year, and gross written premiums also contracted. Wall Street had anticipated earnings growth for Q4 but on lower revenues, which materialized. This suggests the market is not expecting aggressive top-line expansion.

### Strategic Positioning

News from the Q4 2025 earnings call highlights that Everest is "navigating challenges with strategic moves," including:
- Maintaining underwriting discipline in a competitive market
- Strong investment income partially offsetting premium declines
- Strategic share repurchases to enhance per-share value

Investment income is likely a meaningful contributor to overall results, as higher interest rates have benefited insurance company investment portfolios significantly.

### Growth Catalysts

- **Hardening reinsurance pricing cycles:** If catastrophe losses increase industry-wide, reinsurance pricing could firm, benefiting Everest's top and bottom lines.
- **Investment income:** A higher-for-longer interest rate environment supports the investment portfolio.
- **Geographic expansion:** Everest has been expanding internationally, which provides diversification and access to new markets.
- **Share buybacks:** Continued repurchases at reasonable valuations mechanically grow EPS even without top-line growth.

### Growth Risks

- Softening reinsurance pricing could compress margins.
- Decline in gross written premiums may persist if Everest continues to prioritize discipline over volume.

## Competitive Landscape

Everest Group competes in the global reinsurance and insurance markets alongside major players such as:

- **Munich Re** — The world's largest reinsurer
- **Swiss Re** — Another global reinsurance leader
- **RenaissanceRe** — A specialty reinsurance peer
- **Arch Capital Group** — A diversified reinsurer and insurer
- **Berkshire Hathaway Reinsurance** — Warren Buffett's formidable reinsurance operation

### Competitive Strengths
- **Scale and diversification:** Everest operates across multiple lines and geographies, providing earnings diversification.
- **Bermuda domicile:** Tax and regulatory advantages relative to some competitors.
- **Underwriting discipline:** The willingness to shrink premiums when pricing is inadequate is a sign of competitive maturity.
- **Strong capital position:** Adequate surplus to capitalize on market dislocations.

### Competitive Challenges
- The reinsurance industry is cyclical and competitive, with well-capitalized players limiting pricing power.
- Alternative capital (insurance-linked securities, catastrophe bonds) continues to grow, adding supply to the market.
- Everest lacks the brand recognition and scale of Munich Re or Swiss Re.

## Risk Assessment

### Catastrophe Risk
As a major reinsurer, Everest is inherently exposed to large catastrophe losses. The Q4 2024 loss of $18.39 per share demonstrates the potential earnings volatility from catastrophe events. Climate change may increase the frequency and severity of such events over time.

### Pricing Cycle Risk
Reinsurance pricing is cyclical. If the current environment moves toward softer pricing, Everest's combined ratio could deteriorate, pressuring profitability. The decline in gross written premiums already suggests some competitive pressure on pricing.

### Regulatory and Domicile Risk
As a Bermuda-domiciled company, Everest faces potential regulatory changes in its home jurisdiction and may be subject to additional scrutiny or tax policy changes in the jurisdictions where it operates (notably the U.S. and Europe). SEC filing availability is also limited due to the Bermuda domicile, reducing transparency for some investors.

### Investment Portfolio Risk
A significant portion of Everest's earnings comes from investment income. While the current rate environment is favorable, a sharp decline in interest rates or credit deterioration could adversely impact results.

### Concentration Risk
Reinsurance losses tend to be correlated across lines during major catastrophic events, creating potential for outsized losses in any single quarter.

### ESG Considerations
As an insurer/reinsurer, climate change represents both a risk (increased claims) and an opportunity (rising demand for risk transfer). Everest's ESG positioning was not detailed in available data.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case 🐂
- **Attractive valuation:** A forward P/E of 10.25x and estimated P/B below 1.0x offer a margin of safety for patient investors.
- **Strong capital return:** ~5.2% combined shareholder yield (dividends + buybacks) provides tangible returns while waiting for valuation re-rating.
- **Earnings normalization:** After a catastrophe-affected 2024, 2025 results demonstrate the company's earning power ($1.9B operating income), and 2026 could see further improvement.
- **Defensive characteristics:** A beta of 0.34 offers portfolio diversification and reduced volatility.
- **Underwriting discipline:** Management's willingness to shrink premiums in a softening market protects long-term profitability.
- **Investment income tailwind:** Higher interest rates continue to support the investment portfolio.

### Bear Case 🐻
- **Declining top line:** A 4.6% revenue decline and shrinking gross written premiums may persist, limiting growth.
- **Catastrophe exposure:** A single severe catastrophe season could dramatically impair earnings, as seen in 2024.
- **Pricing cycle headwinds:** Increased competition and alternative capital could drive reinsurance pricing lower.
- **Limited data transparency:** The inability to access detailed SEC filings and full financial statements makes thorough due diligence more challenging.
- **Earnings miss trend:** The Q4 2025 miss on both top and bottom line could signal deteriorating fundamentals or overly optimistic estimates.
- **Climate change:** Increasing frequency and severity of natural disasters could structurally impair reinsurance profitability.

## Conclusion

**Overall Recommendation: HOLD / Moderate Buy**

Everest Group presents a solid value investment opportunity for investors with appropriate risk tolerance for the reinsurance sector. The forward P/E of 10.25x, estimated P/B below 1.0x, and combined shareholder yield of ~5.2% offer attractive entry points for a quality reinsurer generating double-digit ROEs. The low beta of 0.34 adds portfolio diversification benefits.

However, the declining revenue trend, catastrophe risk exposure, and limited financial data transparency warrant caution. The Q4 2025 earnings miss, while modest (4.1% below consensus), combined with a 4.6% revenue decline, suggests that near-term momentum is not strongly positive.

For value-oriented and income-focused investors, EG represents a reasonable allocation at current levels, particularly if the company can sustain its ~12% operating ROE and continue returning capital to shareholders. Investors should monitor reinsurance pricing trends, catastrophe loss developments, and management's commentary on the competitive environment before increasing position sizes. A more aggressive buy signal would emerge if the stock approaches the lower end of its 52-week range (~$302) or if reinsurance pricing shows signs of hardening.
