## Executive Summary

Cooper Companies is a medical device company operating through two segments: CooperVision (contact lenses, ~70% of revenue) and CooperSurgical (fertility and women's health, ~30%). The stock trades at approximately $83 (implied from ~$16.4B market cap on ~198M diluted shares), representing a trailing and forward P/E of ~44x. This valuation embeds expectations for sustained mid-to-high single digit revenue growth and margin expansion that recent data suggests is moderating.

The company occupies a defensible #2 position in the global contact lens market behind Johnson & Johnson Vision and is a leader in myopia management, a secular growth category. CooperSurgical's fertility business benefits from demographic trends (delayed childbearing, expanding IVF access). However, per Seeking Alpha's February 2026 analysis, revenue growth is moderating and margin leverage is becoming harder to achieve. A comprehensive strategic review is underway — including potential divestitures — which introduces both optionality and execution risk. In the current macro regime (oil-driven inflation, higher-for-longer rates, consumer spending compression), a 44x P/E medical device company with decelerating growth faces meaningful multiple compression risk.

## Company Overview

Cooper Companies operates as a mid-cap medical device company (~$16.4B market cap) headquartered in San Ramon, California. The two operating segments serve distinct but complementary healthcare markets:

**CooperVision:** Manufactures and markets contact lenses, with particular strength in specialty lenses (toric, multifocal) and the MiSight myopia management lens. The global contact lens market is approximately $10-11B annually, growing at low-to-mid single digits organically. CooperVision competes with J&J Vision (Acuvue), Alcon, and Bausch + Lomb.

**CooperSurgical:** Provides fertility solutions (IVF equipment, media, genetic testing) and office/surgical products for OB/GYN practices. The fertility segment is the higher-growth component, benefiting from increasing IVF utilization rates globally.

The company has a fiscal year ending October 31. The most recent 10-K was filed December 5, 2025 (FY2025), and Q1 FY2026 results are scheduled for March 5, 2026.

## Growth Analysis

**Secular Tailwinds:**
- Global myopia prevalence is increasing (projected to affect ~50% of the world population by 2050), supporting demand for MiSight and other myopia management products
- IVF utilization rates are expanding globally, particularly in emerging markets; delayed childbearing in developed markets adds structural demand
- Contact lens market benefits from trade-up to daily disposables (higher revenue per wearer)

**Growth Headwinds:**
- Per Seeking Alpha, revenue growth is moderating — the contact lens market is mature in developed markets, limiting organic growth to mid-single digits
- FX headwinds are a persistent drag; Cooper derives approximately 50% of revenue from international markets, and a strong dollar compresses reported results
- CooperSurgical faces competitive pressure from new entrants in fertility technology
- The current macro environment (oil-driven inflation, consumer spending compression) may slow discretionary vision correction spending, though contact lenses are more necessity than discretionary

**Strategic Review:** The Seeking Alpha article mentions a "comprehensive strategic review with all options including divestitures" under consideration. This could unlock value if CooperSurgical is separated (it trades at a conglomerate discount embedded in a contact lens multiple), but introduces execution risk and potential for suboptimal outcomes.

## Valuation Assessment

At 43.7x forward earnings, Cooper trades at a substantial premium to both the S&P 500 (~20x) and the broader medical device sector (typically 25-30x for mid-cap medtech). The premium is historically justified by (a) contact lens recurring revenue characteristics, (b) myopia management growth optionality, and (c) fertility market expansion. However:

- **Moderating growth undermines the premium.** A 44x multiple typically requires 15%+ earnings growth. If Cooper is delivering high-single-digit revenue growth with limited margin expansion, sustainable earnings growth is likely 8-12%, insufficient to justify the current multiple.
- **Higher-for-longer rates compress growth multiples mechanically.** With the Fed unlikely to cut in 2026 and potential hike probability at 20-25%, the discount rate applied to future earnings increases, disproportionately penalizing high-multiple stocks.
- **Acquisition-related debt limits buyback capacity** that could support per-share earnings growth independent of operating performance.

The stock sits 10.5% below its 52-week high, which provides some cushion, but the 52-week low of $61.78 (25.5% below current) illustrates meaningful downside in a risk-off environment. For context, during broad market selloffs, a 1.03 beta stock with a 44x multiple typically declines more than the index because multiple compression compounds beta-driven selling.

## Competitive Landscape

**CooperVision's competitive position is strong within a concentrated oligopoly.** The global contact lens market is effectively a four-player market (J&J, Alcon, CooperVision, Bausch + Lomb), with combined share exceeding 90%. This structure limits pricing pressure and creates high barriers to entry (manufacturing scale, regulatory approvals, distribution relationships). CooperVision has been gaining share, particularly in specialty lenses where clinical differentiation matters.

**MiSight myopia management** is an important competitive differentiator — it was the first FDA-approved soft contact lens for myopia control in children. Competition is increasing (Essilor's Stellest spectacle lens, other pharmaceutical approaches), but CooperVision has first-mover advantage in the soft lens category.

**CooperSurgical** operates in a more fragmented fertility market, competing with Vitrolife, Hamilton Thorne, and others. The fertility space is consolidating, and Cooper's scale (largest pure-play fertility device company) provides procurement and cross-selling advantages.

Score: 7.0 — the oligopolistic contact lens structure and myopia management positioning provide durable competitive advantages, partially offset by CooperSurgical's more contested market.

## Risk Assessment

**Macro/Multiple Compression Risk:** The dominant near-term risk. A 44x P/E in a stagflationary environment where rates remain elevated is vulnerable. If the forward P/E compresses to 35x (still above medtech average), the stock declines approximately 20%.

**FX Risk:** ~50% international revenue exposure means dollar strength (likely in a risk-off/higher-rate environment) mechanically reduces reported earnings.

**Strategic Review Uncertainty:** "All options including divestitures" introduces a range of outcomes. A CooperSurgical divestiture could be value-creating (if priced at fertility-market multiples of 20-25x EBITDA) or value-destructive (if sold at a discount to manage debt). Timeline and execution quality are unknowns.

**Acquisition Integration:** Historical debt from the Cook Medical deal constrains balance sheet flexibility.

**Regulatory Risk:** MiSight's competitive advantage depends partly on FDA approval barriers. If regulators approve competing myopia management contact lenses, CooperVision's pricing power diminishes.

**Consumer Spending:** While contact lenses are relatively non-discretionary, trade-down to glasses or reduced replacement frequency is possible in severe consumer stress.

**Positive Risk (Upside):** Strategic review could unlock 15-25% upside if CooperSurgical is divested at attractive multiples. Myopia management could accelerate faster than expected, driving CooperVision growth above market expectations.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- Strategic review results in value-unlocking divestiture of CooperSurgical, potentially raising $3-5B to deleverage and repurchase shares
- Myopia management market inflects to $2B+ TAM, with CooperVision capturing 30%+ share
- Margin expansion resumes as manufacturing scale improves and acquisition integration costs roll off
- Contact lens trade-up to dailies accelerates, driving above-market organic growth
- Fair value at 35-40x a higher earnings base could reach $95-110

### Bear Case
- Revenue growth decelerates to low-single-digits as contact lens market matures and FX headwinds persist
- Strategic review yields no actionable outcome or results in a poorly-timed divestiture at depressed multiples
- Higher-for-longer rate environment compresses the forward P/E from 44x toward 30x — on flat earnings, that implies $57, approximately 31% downside
- Acquisition debt limits financial flexibility during a credit tightening cycle
- Oil-driven inflation increases operating costs (manufacturing, logistics) while moderating pricing power
- The 52-week low of $61.78 may be tested in a broader market selloff

## Conclusion

Cooper Companies is a quality medical device business with defensible competitive positions in two attractive end markets. The fundamental issue is valuation: 44x forward earnings requires sustained double-digit earnings growth that current trajectory does not support. In a macro environment characterized by stagflation, higher-for-longer rates, and potential consumer spending compression, growth multiples face headwinds that disproportionately affect stocks in COO's valuation range.

The strategic review introduces meaningful optionality — a well-executed CooperSurgical divestiture could be a significant catalyst. Absent that, moderating growth at a premium multiple in a hostile macro regime creates an unfavorable risk/reward skew.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The stock is not expensive enough relative to its quality to warrant AVOID, but the 44x multiple with decelerating growth in a rising-rate environment provides insufficient margin of safety for BUY. Reassess after (a) Q1 FY2026 earnings on March 5 provide updated growth trajectory, (b) strategic review reaches conclusion, or (c) the stock approaches $65-70, where the risk/reward improves materially. An entry below $68 (roughly 30x forward earnings assuming flat EPS) would warrant upgrading to BUY.
