## Executive Summary

Carlyle Group reported record 2025 results and outlined an ambitious three-year growth roadmap at its February 2026 shareholder update, targeting $200B in new capital inflows, >$1.9B in Fee Related Earnings (FRE), and committing to a new $2B share buyback program through 2028. Q4 2025 distributable EPS of $1.01 met estimates (consensus $1.03 initially, revised to $1.00-$1.01), and AUM grew YoY. Barclays maintained an Overweight rating with a $68 price target (lowered from $71).

The stock trades at $45.64, a 33% discount to Barclays' target, reflecting two structural headwinds: (1) the macro regime of confirmed stagflation with 38-42% recession probability, which directly impairs realization activity, fundraising velocity, and mark-to-market portfolio valuations for alternative asset managers; and (2) sector-specific stress evidenced by BX facing withdrawal pressure, Blue Owl at a 34.9% NAV discount, and the broader credit cascade probability at 55-60%. With a 2.07 beta, CG amplifies every macro downturn. The $1.07B insider disposal on March 10 at $41/share by a 10%+ owner compounds the concern. Carlyle's fundamental turnaround under CEO Harvey Schwartz is real, but the timing of this entry point is poor given the macro environment. Calibration data shows the highest-score BUY call in the system's history was APO at 7.55, which then lost 16.9% — a direct warning against high-conviction calls on economically sensitive alt managers in deteriorating conditions.

## Company Overview

Carlyle Group is a global alternative asset manager with diversified operations across private equity, credit, real assets, and investment solutions. Under CEO Harvey Schwartz (appointed 2023, formerly Goldman Sachs CFO/President), the firm has undergone a strategic turnaround focused on scaling fee-earning AUM, improving margin discipline, and expanding credit and insurance-linked strategies. The firm manages capital across global platforms with a focus on corporate private equity, real assets, and global credit.

Market cap: $16.5B. Current price: $45.64. 52-week range: $33.02–$69.85 (current price sits at 35% below the high, 38% above the low). Forward P/E: 20.4x. Dividend yield: 3.09%. Beta: 2.07.

## Financial Analysis

**Earnings:** Q4 2025 post-tax distributable EPS of $1.01 slightly missed the initial consensus of $1.03 but matched revised expectations. Shares rose 5.9% on the report, suggesting the market was positioned for worse. For FY2025, management described results as "record-breaking," though specific aggregate figures were not disclosed in the available data.

**2028 Targets:** FRE >$1.9B, $200B in capital inflows, new $2B buyback authorization. These targets imply significant scaling — current market cap of $16.5B implies FRE yield of ~11.5% if achieved, which would be attractive. The fundraising target of $200B over three years is aggressive relative to CG's historical pace and would require meaningful acceleration from 2025 inflow levels (which beat prior targets).

**Valuation Metrics:** At 20.4x forward P/E, CG trades at a discount to top-tier peers (KKR ~25-30x, APO ~14x, BX ~25-30x). The 3.09% dividend yield provides modest income support. However, the P/E multiple should be evaluated against the quality and predictability of earnings — distributable earnings for alt managers are inherently lumpy, driven by realization timing.

**Balance Sheet:** Detailed financial statements were unavailable in the data feed. This is a material gap. Alt-manager balance sheet health depends on investment portfolio marks, fund-level leverage, and GP commitment obligations — none of which can be assessed from available data.

**Insider Activity:** The most significant signal is the $1.07B sale on March 10 by a 10%+ owner (the firm itself or legacy partnership entities) at $41/share — 10% below the current $45.64 price. This was preceded by a $624M sale on February 26 at $31.46/share (though this lower price may reflect a different class or block discount). The conversion of Class B to Class A shares and subsequent sales suggest ongoing partnership restructuring/monetization by legacy founders. CEO Schwartz received a 17,457-share grant on Feb 20, and other executives received smaller grants — standard compensation, no open-market purchases by management.

## Growth Analysis

**Fundraising Momentum:** Management stated inflows beat 2025 targets and set a $200B goal through 2028. If achieved, this would represent a step-change in CG's competitive position, potentially narrowing the AUM gap with KKR and BX. Credit strategies and insurance-linked capital are the primary growth vectors across the alt-manager industry.

**FRE Expansion:** The >$1.9B FRE target implies meaningful margin improvement alongside AUM growth. FRE is the highest-quality revenue stream for alt managers (management fees less operating expenses), so achieving this would structurally improve earnings quality.

**Headwinds to Growth Targets:** The 2028 targets were set against a more favorable macro backdrop. Current conditions — stagflation, credit cascade risk at 55-60%, recession probability at 38-42%, rising oil prices, and capital markets uncertainty — directly impair fundraising (institutional allocators slow commitments during volatility), realization activity (exit markets freeze), and portfolio valuations (marks decline). The $200B target assumed normalized capital markets; that assumption is increasingly stressed.

## Valuation Assessment

At $45.64 with a 20.4x forward P/E, CG appears moderately valued relative to its own history (traded as low as $33 in the past year). Barclays' $68 target implies 49% upside, which is substantial, though the target revision from $71 to $68 reflects incremental caution.

The bull case for valuation rests on 2028 FRE targets — if CG hits >$1.9B FRE on ~360M diluted shares, that implies ~$5.28 FRE/share. At a 15x FRE multiple (reasonable for a scaling alt manager), the stock would be worth ~$79. At 12x (more conservative given macro), ~$63. Current market cap prices CG at roughly 8.7x the 2028 FRE target, which is cheap if the target is achievable.

The bear case: current earnings power is closer to $4/share in distributable EPS annually (4 × $1.01 = ~$4.04). At 20x, fair value is ~$81. But if realization activity slows and AUM growth stalls, distributable EPS could compress to $3.00-3.50 through a downturn, putting fair value at $60-70 on a 20x multiple, or $45-52 on a more conservative 15x. The stock is currently pricing the stress scenario.

## Competitive Landscape

Carlyle ranks as a mid-tier alternative asset manager by AUM — behind Blackstone, Apollo, and KKR, roughly comparable to TPG and Ares in certain segments. Under Schwartz, the firm has been executing a credible turnaround from years of underperformance relative to peers.

Key competitive dynamics:
- **APO** has differentiated through credit/insurance origination and trades at 14x forward — cheaper than CG with a stronger balance sheet and more durable earnings stream. APO remains the preferred alt-manager long per the world model.
- **BX** faces withdrawal pressure in semi-liquid vehicles, a direct read-across risk for CG's own retail/semi-liquid product ambitions.
- **KKR** has the broadest platform and strongest brand momentum but trades at a premium valuation.
- Carlyle's competitive edge lies in its global PE franchise and real assets capabilities, but it lacks the insurance-linked capital base (Athene for APO) that provides structural funding advantage.

The broader alt-manager sector faces the credit cascade risk identified in the world model (55-60% probability). If Ares or KKR restrict semi-liquid fund redemptions, the probability rises to ~70%, creating a sector-wide de-rating event.

## Risk Assessment

**Macro Sensitivity (Critical):** Beta of 2.07 makes CG one of the most macro-sensitive financial services stocks available. In the current regime — GDP 0.7%, NFP -92K, core PCE 3.1%, oil $98-108 — this beta is a liability. A 10% further decline in the S&P 500 implies ~20% downside for CG, to ~$36-37, below the 52-week low of $33.

**Credit Cascade (High):** 18+ independent data points supporting 55-60% probability of a credit cascade. Alt managers are directly exposed through fund-level leverage, portfolio company refinancing needs, and potential redemption pressure in semi-liquid vehicles. CG's credit business would face both mark-to-market losses and fundraising headwinds.

**Insider Selling (Moderate-High):** The $1.07B sale at $41/share on March 10 by a 10%+ owner is a large-scale disposition. While much of this likely reflects legacy partnership restructuring (Class B to A conversions), the volume and timing are notable — during a macro downturn with the stock already down 35% from highs.

**Realization Risk:** PE exit activity depends on functional IPO and M&A markets. Both are impaired by current volatility and credit conditions. Delayed exits reduce performance fees and distributable earnings.

**Fundraising Risk:** The $200B target through 2028 requires institutional allocators to maintain or increase PE/credit allocations during a period of rising macro uncertainty. Historically, institutional commitment pace slows during downturns with a 6-12 month lag.

**Concentration:** Heavy reliance on carried interest and performance fee revenue creates earnings volatility that the FRE expansion strategy is intended to reduce — but the transition is incomplete.

- Record 2025 results and $200B fundraising target through 2028 represent credible acceleration under Schwartz's leadership.

- $1.07B insider sale at $41/share on March 10 — large-scale selling by a 10%+ owner during the decline.
- Recession probability 38-42% with timing accelerated to Q1-Q2 2026 impairs realization activity, fundraising, and portfolio company earnings.
- $200B fundraising target set during more favorable conditions; institutional allocator behavior during stress typically involves slowing commitments.
- APO at 14x forward P/E with insurance-linked capital base is a structurally better way to express alt-manager exposure. The world model explicitly identifies Long APO / Short BX as a core pair — CG sits between these two in quality and is closer to BX in risk profile.

## Conclusion

Carlyle Group is executing a credible turnaround under Harvey Schwartz, with record 2025 results and ambitious 2028 targets. The valuation at 20x forward is reasonable in isolation, and the $2B buyback and 3.1% dividend yield provide some support. Barclays' $68 target implies meaningful upside.

However, the macro regime is hostile to the alt-manager business model across multiple dimensions: stagflation compresses realization activity and fundraising simultaneously, credit cascade risk at 55-60% threatens the sector broadly, and CG's 2.07 beta means any further equity decline gets roughly doubled. The $1.07B insider sale at $41 on March 10 is a meaningful negative signal, and APO remains the structurally superior way to express alt-manager exposure (lower P/E, insurance-linked capital moat, preferred by the world model at max conviction).

For investors who want alt-manager exposure, APO is the better risk-adjusted position. For investors considering CG specifically, the stock likely needs to trade closer to its 52-week low of $33 (roughly 15x forward) before the risk/reward becomes compelling relative to the macro headwinds. At current levels, the probability-weighted outcome is roughly symmetric — potential upside to $55-60 in a normalization scenario, potential downside to $33-37 in a recession/credit cascade scenario.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** Wait for either (a) credit cascade risk to resolve, (b) macro stabilization allowing fundraising targets to remain credible, or (c) a price closer to $35 that provides adequate margin of safety for the high-beta risk profile.
