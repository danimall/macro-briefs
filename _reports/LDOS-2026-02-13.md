## Executive Summary

Leidos Holdings Inc (LDOS) is a major U.S. defense, aviation, information technology, and professional services company headquartered in Reston, Virginia, serving both government and commercial clients. With a market capitalization of approximately $21.9 billion and a current share price of ~$171.44, the stock sits roughly 17% below its 52-week high of $205.77, reflecting recent market volatility and investor uncertainty surrounding a major acquisition and the evolving government spending landscape.

The company recently announced a transformative $2.4 billion acquisition of Entrust Solutions Group, which will double the size of its energy infrastructure engineering business. This strategic move diversifies Leidos beyond its traditional defense and IT government contracting base into the high-growth energy sector. However, the deal will be funded through a combination of new debt, cash on hand, and commercial paper, raising near-term leverage concerns.

Wall Street consensus remains constructive, with Citigroup initiating coverage with a Buy rating in December 2025 and analysts projecting Q4 EPS of $2.57 (an 8.4% year-over-year increase). The forward P/E of ~17.8x is attractive relative to its trailing P/E of ~29.4x, suggesting meaningful earnings growth is expected. The stock's low beta of 0.62 provides defensive characteristics, while the ~1.0% dividend yield offers modest income. Balancing the growth opportunity against acquisition integration risk and government budget uncertainty, we view LDOS as a moderate-conviction Buy.

## Company Overview

Leidos Holdings Inc is one of the largest defense and IT services contractors in the United States. The company operates across several critical domains:

- **National Security & Digital** — Cybersecurity, intelligence, and defense solutions
- **Health & Civil** — Federal health IT, civil agency modernization
- **Commercial & International** — Energy infrastructure, international defense
- **Defense Systems** — Systems integration, logistics, and training

The company serves the U.S. Department of Defense, intelligence community, Department of Homeland Security, and other federal civilian agencies. It also has a growing commercial and international footprint, particularly in energy infrastructure — a segment poised to expand significantly with the Entrust acquisition.

### Balance Sheet Considerations

The debt-funded acquisition will push leverage higher in the near term, likely to 3.5-4.0x, which will need to be monitored carefully.

Leidos has historically generated strong free cash flow, typically converting 80-100% of net income to free cash flow. The company pays a modest dividend (~1.0% yield) and has engaged in share repurchases. Post-acquisition, we expect free cash flow to be prioritized toward deleveraging.

### Dividend

- **SAIC** — Leidos' former parent company, focused on government IT
- **CACI International** — Strong in intelligence and cyber
- **General Dynamics IT (GDIT)** — Large-scale IT services
- **Accenture Federal Services** — Growing competitor in modernization

### Competitive Advantages (Moat Assessment)

1. **Scale & Security Clearances:** Leidos' workforce holds extensive security clearances, creating a significant barrier to entry
2. **Incumbent Advantage:** Long-standing relationships with defense and intelligence agencies provide reblue advantages
3. **Diversification:** Revenue spread across defense, civil, health, and now energy reduces concentration risk
4. **Technical Depth:** Deep capabilities in systems integration, cyber, and digital modernization
5. **Entrust Differentiation:** The energy infrastructure expansion creates a unique cross-sector positioning among defense peers

## Risk Assessment

### Key Risks

1. **Government Spending Uncertainty (High):** DOGE and government efficiency initiatives could pressure certain contract types. Potential for continuing resolutions or budget cuts creates near-term revenue uncertainty.

2. **Acquisition Integration Risk (Medium-High):** The $2.4 billion Entrust deal is the largest in recent memory. Integration challenges, cultural mismatches, or revenue dis-synergies could undermine the thesis.

3. **Leverage Risk (Medium):** Post-acquisition debt levels will be elevated. Rising interest rates or slower-than-expected cash flow could strain the balance sheet.

4. **Contract Risk (Medium):** Loss of major re-compete contracts could materially impact revenue. Government contracts are inherently subject to protest, delay, and termination.

5. **Margin Pressure (Medium):** Competitive bidding dynamics in government contracting can compress margins, particularly in cost-plus contracts.

6. **Key Personnel & Clearance Risk (Low-Medium):** The business depends on cleared personnel in a tight labor market.

### Mitigating Factors

- Low beta (0.62) indicates historically lower volatility than the market
- Diversified revenue across multiple agencies and sectors
- Backlog visibility provides revenue predictability
- Energy infrastructure is a bipartisan priority, reducing political risk of the Entrust business
