## Executive Summary

Sanmina is an electronics manufacturing services (EMS) company that has successfully pivoted toward AI data center infrastructure, positioning itself as a contract manufacturer for server systems, optical interconnects, and related hardware. The company's Q1 FY2026 results (reported January 26, 2026) showed meaningful acceleration: EPS of $2.38 beat consensus by $0.23 (10.7%), operating cash flow tripled YoY to $178.7M, and free cash flow reached $92M in the quarter. The ZT Systems integration (linked to AMD's server business) has shifted Sanmina's revenue mix toward higher-growth server products, giving it a credible claim as an AI infrastructure beneficiary.

The stock has appreciated approximately 64% over the trailing twelve months, moving from ~$76 in mid-March 2025 to ~$125 currently. At $124.77, the stock trades at roughly 29.9x trailing EPS of $4.46. This multiple is elevated relative to Sanmina's historical range (typically 10-15x) and relative to EMS peers like Flex or Jabil which trade at lower multiples. The premium reflects the AI narrative, which is real but already substantially priced in. The sharp ~30% pullback from the January 22 high of $185.29 suggests the market is already repricing risk around execution and sustainability of AI-driven revenue growth.

Insider activity is a mild negative signal: CFO Jonathan Faust sold $1.6M in stock at ~$159 on February 24, and Director David Hedley sold $166K the same day, both well above the current price. These were not routine scheduled sales during a period when the stock was near highs. The recent director grants on March 16 are standard compensation awards and carry no directional signal.

## Company Overview

Sanmina Corporation is a global EMS provider headquartered in San Jose, California, operating across two segments: Integrated Manufacturing Solutions (IMS) and Components, Products & Services (CPS). The company manufactures printed circuit board assemblies, backplane assemblies, cable assemblies, enclosures, and optical modules for customers in communications networks, cloud infrastructure, industrial, medical, defense, and automotive end markets.

The AI-driven transformation of the business centers on its role as a contract manufacturer for server rack systems and optical interconnect components used in hyperscale data centers. The ZT Systems integration (ZT was acquired by AMD) has channeled AMD's server hardware manufacturing through Sanmina, creating a direct linkage between AMD's AI accelerator growth and Sanmina's revenue trajectory. This positions Sanmina as what Seeking Alpha describes as "the cheapest proxy to AMD's explosive growth."

Market cap stands at $6.8B on 54.6M shares outstanding. The company pays no dividend. The 52-week range spans $63.67 to $185.29, placing the current price at approximately 44% of the high-to-low range from the top.

## Growth Analysis

Sanmina's growth thesis rests on three pillars:

1. **AI Data Center Infrastructure:** The company manufactures server rack systems, optical modules, and interconnect solutions for hyperscale data centers. With the $1T+ AI infrastructure pipeline referenced in Nvidia's GTC guidance through 2027, the total addressable market for EMS providers serving this buildout is expanding rapidly.

2. **ZT Systems/AMD Channel:** AMD's acquisition of ZT Systems has consolidated server manufacturing relationships. Sanmina, as a primary contract manufacturer for ZT/AMD server products, benefits directly from AMD's data center revenue growth. AMD's data center GPU revenue grew 40%+ in recent quarters.

3. **Revenue Mix Shift:** Higher-margin server and optical products are replacing lower-margin legacy EMS work, which should structurally improve profitability even on flat revenue.

**Risks to Growth:**
- AI capex spending could decelerate if hyperscalers pull back (macro headwind, recession probability 38-42%)
- Customer concentration risk: dependence on AMD/ZT channel creates single-point-of-failure exposure
- Section 301 trade probes targeting 60 economies could disrupt Sanmina's global manufacturing footprint
- Semiconductor supply chain triple risk (Chinese petrochemical force majeure, helium scarcity, wafer start constraints) could constrain inputs

## Competitive Landscape

Sanmina competes in the EMS space against Foxconn, Flex, Jabil, Celestica, and Benchmark Electronics. Its competitive advantages include:

- **Vertical integration:** In-house PCB fabrication and optical component manufacturing
- **AI infrastructure positioning:** ZT Systems relationship provides differentiated access to AMD server builds
- **Engineering capability:** Advanced thermal management and high-density interconnect expertise

Weaknesses relative to competitors:
- **Scale:** Significantly smaller than Foxconn and Jabil, limiting bargaining power
- **Customer concentration:** AMD/ZT channel dependency creates vulnerability
- **Geographic exposure:** Global manufacturing footprint exposed to tariff/trade disruption

Ken Fisher's fund taking a position in Q3 2025 (per news) provides a signal of institutional interest but Fisher's portfolio is highly diversified and the position is unlikely to be concentrated.

## Conclusion

Sanmina's AI infrastructure positioning is legitimate, supported by a specific customer channel (AMD/ZT Systems), verifiable earnings acceleration (Q1 beat), and structural shifts in revenue mix. The stock has already corrected 33% from its January highs, which removes some of the froth. However, at ~30x trailing earnings, the valuation assumes sustained execution on AI-driven growth that has only been demonstrated for one quarter. The CFO's $1.6M sale at $159 in late February, followed by a 22% decline, suggests even management viewed the post-earnings price as stretched.

The critical question is whether Q1's $2.38 EPS is the new baseline or an outlier. Without additional quarterly data to confirm the trajectory, paying 30x trailing earnings for an EMS company in a stagflationary macro environment with elevated trade policy uncertainty carries meaningful downside risk if the AI buildout decelerates or customer-specific factors weaken.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The AI thesis provides a credible path to higher earnings, but the current price requires sustained multi-quarter confirmation that hasn't yet arrived. A more attractive entry would be near $100-110 (15-18x on a conservative $6-7 annualized EPS scenario), which would provide margin of safety while still participating in AI upside. Conversely, two consecutive quarters at Q1-level EPS would justify upgrading to BUY even at higher prices, because the earnings power would be validated rather than assumed.
