## Executive Summary

Jabil is a global electronic manufacturing services (EMS) company that has successfully repositioned itself as a primary beneficiary of AI infrastructure buildout. AI-related revenue surged 80% YoY in FY2025 and management guides 34.4% growth in FY2026, with AI expected to reach 36% of total revenue. The stock hit an all-time high of $259.74 in February 2026 and has returned over 54% YoY, outperforming the broader S&P 500 and its EMS peer group by a wide margin.

The core tension is valuation versus growth trajectory. At ~40x forward earnings, JBL trades at roughly 2x the historical EMS sector multiple. The AI revenue ramp is real and supported by multiple analyst initiations (Baird Outperform, average target $262.75), but the current price embeds aggressive expectations. With a 1.22 beta, the stock is meaningfully exposed to the stagflationary macro environment: $100+ oil raising input/logistics costs, Section 301 tariff probes across 60 economies threatening Jabil's globally distributed manufacturing footprint, and a potential credit cascade tightening customer capex budgets. CEO Michael Dastoor sold ~$3.7M in stock on January 22, 2026 — modest relative to his 262K remaining shares but worth noting at all-time highs.

The risk-reward at current levels favors patience. The secular AI infrastructure thesis is intact and Jabil is well-positioned within it, but paying 40x forward earnings for a contract manufacturer — even one with improving mix — requires near-perfect execution in a deteriorating macro environment.

## Company Overview

Jabil Inc. is one of the world's largest EMS providers, operating manufacturing facilities across 100+ sites in 30+ countries. The company serves diversified end markets including cloud/data center infrastructure, automotive, healthcare, industrial, and consumer electronics. Following the divestiture of its mobility business (selling the iPhone casing business to BYD Electronic in 2023), Jabil has concentrated on higher-margin, higher-complexity manufacturing with a deliberate pivot toward AI infrastructure.

The company's fiscal year ends in August. The most recent quarterly filing (10-Q, January 9, 2026) covers Q1 FY2026. Market cap stands at approximately $26.5B.

## Financial Analysis

The provided dataset lacks detailed income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow data, which limits granular financial analysis. Working from available data points:

**Earnings Power:** Analysts project Q2 FY2026 EPS of $2.50, representing 28.9% YoY growth. Revenues are projected at approximately $7B for the quarter. The trailing P/E of 37.6x implies trailing twelve-month EPS of roughly $6.60-6.70 (based on a ~$248 share price). The forward P/E of 40.3x implies forward EPS of approximately $6.15-6.20 for the next twelve months — the seeming paradox of a higher forward P/E despite growing earnings likely reflects share price appreciation outpacing consensus estimate revisions, or fiscal year timing differences.

**Capital Return:** JBL pays a modest dividend yielding 0.13%, which is immaterial to the investment case. The company has historically been an active repurchaser of shares, and the declining share count has been a significant contributor to EPS growth.

**Balance Sheet Context:** Without detailed balance sheet data, the $26.5B market cap against what is historically a capital-intensive, low-margin EMS business suggests the market is pricing in a structural margin expansion tied to AI mix shift. Historically, Jabil has carried moderate leverage (2-3x net debt/EBITDA range), which is typical for EMS companies that use working capital financing extensively.

## Growth Analysis

**AI Revenue Acceleration — the Central Thesis:**
- FY2025 AI-related revenue grew 80% YoY
- FY2026 AI-related revenue guided to grow 34.4%
- AI expected to reach 36% of total revenue in FY2026, up from approximately 20% in FY2024
- Q2 FY2026 consensus revenue of ~$7B would imply mid-to-high single digit total revenue growth

The AI growth driver is concentrated in cloud/data center infrastructure manufacturing, where Jabil produces server racks, liquid cooling systems, optical networking components, and power distribution hardware for hyperscaler customers. This positions the company within the "picks and shovels" layer of AI infrastructure — building the physical systems that house and cool GPU clusters.

**Growth Quality Assessment:** The 80% → 34.4% deceleration in AI revenue growth is expected given base effects but still represents a strong absolute rate. The key question is whether AI revenue growth can sustain 20%+ annually once it crosses 40%+ of total revenue, or whether it normalizes to the 10-15% range as hyperscaler buildout matures. The latter scenario would compress the multiple significantly.

**Non-AI Segments:** Traditional end markets (automotive, healthcare, industrial) face mixed conditions. Automotive is weakening globally, healthcare is stable, and industrial/energy is benefiting from electrification but faces macro headwinds. The divestiture of the mobility segment in 2023 removed a significant revenue contributor but improved margins.

## Valuation Assessment

**Current Multiples:**
- Forward P/E: 40.3x
- Trailing P/E: 37.6x
- Market Cap: $26.5B

**Historical Context:** Jabil has historically traded at 8-15x forward earnings, consistent with the EMS industry's low-margin, asset-intensive profile. The current 40x forward P/E represents a 3-4x expansion from historical norms. This premium is justified only if Jabil's margin profile is structurally shifting higher (AI manufacturing commands higher margins than traditional EMS work) and if AI revenue growth sustains for multiple years.

**Peer Comparison:** Traditional EMS peers like Flex (FLEX) trade at 15-20x forward earnings. The premium JBL commands reflects the AI mix shift narrative, but at 40x, the stock is priced more like an AI-adjacent technology company than a contract manufacturer.

**Analyst Targets:** The consensus range is $244-$283, with an average of $262.75 (Benzinga, 4 analysts). Baird initiated at Outperform with an implied target of ~$262 (5.55% upside from the $248 area). These targets suggest limited upside from current levels — a 6% average upside from consensus is a modest risk-reward proposition, particularly for a stock with 1.22 beta in a high-volatility environment.

**Valuation Verdict:** At 40x forward earnings, the stock prices in approximately 3-4 years of above-trend growth with margin expansion. Any stumble in AI revenue growth or margin trajectory would trigger a multiple compression of 30-40%, implying $150-175 downside in a bear scenario.

## Competitive Landscape

Jabil's competitive position within AI infrastructure manufacturing is strong but faces several structural constraints:

**Advantages:**
- Scale: One of few EMS providers with the capacity, clean room environments, and engineering capability to manufacture complex AI infrastructure at hyperscaler volumes
- Customer relationships: Long-standing relationships with major cloud providers provide visibility and switching cost protection
- Vertical integration: Capabilities spanning design, prototyping, and volume manufacturing
- Geographic diversification: 100+ facilities across 30+ countries, though this is also a tariff liability

**Vulnerabilities:**
- Customer concentration: Hyperscaler customers have significant bargaining power and can in-source manufacturing or diversify across competitors
- Margin ceiling: Even premium EMS work operates at structurally lower margins (4-6% operating) than pure-play technology companies, limiting ultimate earnings power per dollar of revenue
- Competition: Flex, Celestica (CLS), and Foxconn all compete for AI infrastructure manufacturing contracts. Celestica in particular has been gaining share in AI-adjacent manufacturing

## Risk Assessment

**Macro Sensitivity (HIGH):** At 1.22 beta, JBL is meaningfully exposed to equity market drawdowns. The stagflation environment creates a triple threat: (1) rising input costs from $100+ oil and disrupted supply chains, (2) potential tariff exposure across Jabil's 30+ country manufacturing footprint from Section 301 probes, and (3) the possibility that hyperscaler capex budgets moderate if recession materializes (38-42% probability per world model).

**Tariff Risk (MEDIUM-HIGH):** Jabil's globally distributed manufacturing base — a strategic advantage in normal times — becomes a significant liability if the Section 301 probes targeting 60 economies result in tariff actions. Manufacturing in China, Malaysia, Vietnam, Mexico, and Eastern Europe could all face incremental duty exposure. The company's ability to shift production is an advantage relative to less diversified competitors, but not a complete hedge.

**Supply Chain Disruption (MEDIUM):** The Hormuz Strait closure and broader Middle East conflict affect Jabil through petrochemical feedstock availability (circuit boards, enclosures, connectors all use petroleum-derived inputs), logistics costs, and potential component shortages. The helium scarcity channel from Qatar LNG disruption could affect semiconductor supply to Jabil's manufacturing lines.

**Customer Concentration (MEDIUM):** Heavy reliance on a small number of hyperscaler customers for AI revenue growth. If any major customer delays or cancels data center buildout plans, the impact on Jabil's AI growth narrative would be significant.

**Insider Activity (LOW-MODERATE):** CEO Michael Dastoor sold approximately $3.7M in shares on January 22, 2026, across multiple transactions at prices ranging from $245-$257. He retains approximately 262,566 shares (~$65M at current prices), so the sale represents roughly 5% of holdings. SVP Gary Schick sold approximately $103K in early February. These are modest, likely tax/diversification-motivated sales, but they occurred at or near all-time highs.

## Investment Thesis

**Bull Case:**
- AI infrastructure spending continues to grow 30%+ annually through FY2028, driving JBL's AI revenue from 36% to 50%+ of total
- Margin mix shift from AI manufacturing commands 200-300bps higher operating margin than traditional EMS, structurally lifting company-wide profitability to 6-8% operating margins
- Share repurchases continue to compound EPS growth above revenue growth
- Tariff disruption actually benefits Jabil relative to less diversified competitors, as Jabil can reroute production across its global footprint
- Fair value in this scenario: $280-320 (25-35x elevated forward EPS of $8-10)

**Bear Case:**
- Hyperscaler capex budgets moderate in a recessionary environment, AI revenue growth decelerates to 10-15%
- Section 301 tariffs materially increase costs across Jabil's manufacturing base
- Oil at $100+ and supply chain disruption compress margins through higher logistics and input costs
- Multiple contracts from historical 40x to 15-20x (still a premium to historical EMS multiples)
- Fair value in this scenario: $130-170 (15-20x forward EPS of $7-8.50)

## Conclusion

Jabil's AI infrastructure positioning is legitimate and well-supported by revenue data and analyst coverage. The 80% YoY AI revenue growth in FY2025 and 34% guided growth in FY2026 represent a genuine business transformation from a generic EMS provider to a strategically positioned AI infrastructure manufacturer.

The problem is price. At 40x forward earnings, the stock already reflects this transformation. The analyst consensus average target of $262.75 offers only 6% upside, while the macro environment creates multiple paths to 30-50% downside (tariffs, recession, supply chain disruption). The 1.22 beta ensures JBL will amplify any broad market correction.

For existing holders, the position merits retention with a trailing stop around $210-215 (approximately 15% below current levels). For new capital, waiting for either a pullback to the $190-200 range (bringing the forward P/E below 30x) or confirmation that AI revenue growth is sustaining above 30% in FY2027 guidance would provide a more favorable risk-reward entry point. **HOLD.**
