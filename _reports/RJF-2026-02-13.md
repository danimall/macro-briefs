## Executive Summary

Raymond James Financial Inc (RJF) is a diversified financial services firm with a market capitalization of approximately $31 billion, currently trading at $157.41 per share — roughly 11% below its 52-week high of $177.66. The company reported mixed Q1 fiscal 2026 results, with adjusted EPS of $2.86 beating consensus estimates while delivering an impressive annualized return on common equity of 18.0% and adjusted return on tangible common equity of 21.4%. Client assets under administration reached a record $1.77 trillion in November 2025, representing a 10% year-over-year increase.

The stock experienced a notable 8.8% single-day decline in February 2026 amid concerns that new artificial intelligence tools could disrupt the traditional financial advisory business. However, at a forward P/E of approximately 14.2x — below its historical average — the current valuation appears to present an attractive entry point. Bank of America Securities maintains a Neutral rating with an average analyst price target of $189.91, implying roughly 21% upside from current levels.

Raymond James has demonstrated consistent long-term value creation, with an annualized return of 12.05% over the past 20 years, outperforming the broader market by approximately 3.27% annually. The combination of record asset levels, disciplined capital allocation, a reasonable valuation, and a modest dividend yield of ~1.36% makes RJF an interesting consideration for investors seeking exposure to the financial services sector.

## Financial Analysis

### Profitability & Returns
Raymond James reported strong profitability metrics for Q1 fiscal 2026 (quarter ended December 31, 2025):

- **Adjusted EPS:** $2.86, beating the Zacks Consensus Estimate of $2.83
- **Annualized ROE:** 18.0%
- **Annualized Adjusted ROTCE:** 21.4%

While the adjusted EPS beat consensus, the bottom line declined 2% year-over-year, suggesting some margin pressure. The ROE of 18% is well above the cost of equity for financial services firms, indicating the company is creating significant shareholder value. The 21.4% adjusted ROTCE is particularly impressive and ranks favorably among peers.

### Revenue Growth
The Q1 fiscal 2026 results were characterized as "mixed" with "solid revenue growth," indicating that top-line momentum remains intact even as earnings growth moderated. The company's record $1.77 trillion in client assets under administration (as of November 2025) — up 10% year-over-year and 1% month-over-month — provides a strong foundation for recurring fee-based revenues.

### Capital Returns
The company pays a dividend yielding approximately 1.36%, which, while modest, is consistent with its capital-intensive financial services business model. Given the trailing P/E of 14.5x, the payout ratio appears conservative, leaving room for reinvestment in growth and share buybacks.

### Balance Sheet Considerations
While detailed balance sheet data was not available in the provided dataset, the company's ability to sustain an 18% ROE and 21.4% adjusted ROTCE suggests a well-capitalized balance sheet with efficient capital deployment. The banking segment provides interest-rate sensitivity, which has been a tailwind in the higher-rate environment.

## Risk Assessment

### 1. AI Disruption Risk (High)
The 8.8% single-day sell-off on February 10, 2026, underscores the market's concern that AI tools could disrupt traditional financial advisory models. While the threat is real, it is likely to evolve gradually rather than suddenly, and firms with strong advisor relationships may be better positioned to integrate AI as a tool rather than a replacement.

### 2. Interest Rate Sensitivity (Moderate)
Raymond James' banking segment benefits from higher interest rates. Any meaningful decline in rates could compress net interest margins and reduce banking segment profitability.

### 3. Market Cyclicality (Moderate)
As a financial services firm with asset-based fees, RJF's revenues are inherently tied to equity market levels. A significant market downturn would reduce AUA and corresponding fee revenues. The beta of 1.01 indicates roughly market-level sensitivity.

### 4. Investment Banking Cyclicality (Moderate)
The current weakness in IB revenues could persist if deal-making activity remains subdued. Conversely, a pickup in M&A and capital markets activity would provide a meaningful tailwind.

### 5. Regulatory Risk (Low-Moderate)
Financial services firms face ongoing regulatory scrutiny, including potential changes to fiduciary standards, capital requirements, and compliance obligations.

### 6. Concentration Risk (Low)
The diversified revenue base across four major segments mitigates concentration risk. Geographic concentration in the U.S. is partially offset by broad client diversification.

## Conclusion

**Overall Recommendation: Moderate Buy / Accumulate**

Raymond James Financial presents a compelling risk/reward profile at current levels. The stock trades at an undemanding forward P/E of 14.2x despite delivering an 18% ROE and managing record client assets of $1.77 trillion. The recent AI-driven sell-off appears to have created an attractive entry point, with the stock sitting roughly 11% below its 52-week high and roughly 21% below the average analyst price target of $189.91.

The key debate centers on whether AI will fundamentally disrupt the financial advisory business or whether it will serve as a productivity tool that enhances advisor capabilities. Given Raymond James' advisor-centric culture and long track record of adaptation, we lean toward the latter view but acknowledge the uncertainty.

For investors with a 2-3 year time horizon, the combination of reasonable valuation, strong profitability, growing asset base, modest dividend yield (~1.36%), and potential IB revenue recovery makes RJF an attractive holding in a diversified portfolio. The primary risks to monitor are the pace of AI adoption in financial advisory, interest rate trajectory, and equity market conditions. We would become more cautious above 17x forward earnings and more aggressive below 12x forward earnings.
