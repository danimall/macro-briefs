## Executive Summary

Nucor is the largest steel producer in North America by capacity, operating primarily through electric arc furnace (EAF) technology that provides cost flexibility relative to integrated blast furnace competitors. The stock has rallied 24% over the past year from its April 2025 low of $97.59, currently trading at $161.16 with a market capitalization of $36.6B. The company just issued Q1 2026 EPS guidance of $2.70-$2.80, modestly above the $2.71 consensus, signaling improving steel demand.

However, the investment case is constrained by several factors. Trailing EPS is $7.52, implying a 21.4x P/E ratio that sits well above Nucor's historical mid-cycle multiple of 8-12x. Net income fell 75% from its 2022 peak to $2.04B in 2025, and the company generated negative free cash flow of -$188M in 2025 while paying $512M in dividends. The current macro regime — stagflation with oil at $119, recession probability at 40-45%, and the Fed now discussing rate hikes — is structurally unfavorable for cyclical steel demand tied to construction, manufacturing, and infrastructure activity.

The Q1 guidance beat and active buyback program provide near-term support, but the valuation already prices in a meaningful earnings recovery that may not materialize if the macro environment deteriorates further. At 21x forward earnings for a deeply cyclical business at a cyclical trough, the risk/reward is balanced rather than compelling.

## Company Overview

Nucor operates approximately 300 facilities across the US and internationally, producing a diversified mix of steel products including sheet, plate, bar, structural, and tubular steel. The company also has downstream operations in steel products manufacturing (joists, deck, rebar fabrication) and raw materials (DRI plants, scrap processing). Its EAF-based model uses primarily recycled scrap steel as feedstock, providing inherent cost advantages versus integrated producers during scrap price downturns and alignment with decarbonization trends.

Key operational metrics: ~228M shares outstanding, $36.6B market cap, 1.38% dividend yield, and a 52-week range of $97.59-$196.90.

## Financial Analysis

**Earnings Trajectory:** The critical financial fact is that Nucor's earnings have declined sharply from peak levels. Net income fell 75% from its 2022 peak to approximately $2.04B in FY2025, with trailing EPS at $7.52. Q1 2026 guidance of $2.70-$2.80 implies an annualized run rate of roughly $10.80-$11.20, which would represent meaningful recovery but remains well below peak earnings of ~$30+ EPS in 2022.

**Free Cash Flow:** Nucor generated negative FCF of -$188M in 2025, driven by elevated capital expenditures on expansion projects (including the West Virginia sheet mill and other greenfield investments). The company paid $512M in dividends against negative FCF, funding the distribution from the balance sheet rather than operations. This is sustainable in the near term given $2.70B in cash and a $2.25B undrawn revolver, but the negative FCF position means the company is consuming its capital cushion.

**Balance Sheet:** With $2.70B cash and access to a $2.25B credit facility, Nucor has approximately $5B in total liquidity. Nucor has historically maintained a conservative balance sheet relative to peers, with investment-grade credit ratings. The liquidity position provides buffer for the current cyclical trough.

**Capital Allocation:** Nucor announced significant buyback activity — news references indicate the company is "revving its buyback engine." At $161/share, buybacks at current levels consume capital that could otherwise rebuild cash reserves during a period of negative FCF. The $512M annual dividend represents a 1.38% yield.

**Annualized Q1 Guidance Math:** $2.75 midpoint × 4 = $11.00 annualized EPS. At $161 share price, that's a 14.6x P/E on the forward guidance run rate — more reasonable than the 21.4x trailing figure, but assumes Q1's improving demand trajectory persists through the year.

## Growth Analysis

**Near-Term Catalysts:** Q1 2026 guidance above consensus suggests steel pricing and volumes are recovering from 2025's trough. Improved demand likely reflects a combination of infrastructure spending (IIJA project ramp), potential restocking by service centers, and tariff-related import substitution.

**Infrastructure Spending:** The Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act continues to drive multi-year demand for steel in bridges, highways, and transit projects. This provides a secular demand floor but the spending timeline is long and the volume impact is incremental rather than transformative for a producer of Nucor's scale.

**Capacity Expansion:** Nucor has been investing heavily in new capacity, including its West Virginia sheet mill. These investments position the company for higher revenue once demand recovers, but they also increase fixed cost exposure during downturns and have contributed to the negative FCF position.

**Macro Headwinds to Growth:** The stagflationary macro environment presents material risks to steel demand growth:
- Oil at $119 raises input costs (energy, transportation) and reduces end-market demand in construction and manufacturing.
- Recession probability at 40-45% directly threatens steel consumption volumes.
- Rate hike discussions (10-15% probability) would further suppress construction and housing activity.
- Homebuilder AVOIDs across the sector signal structural weakness in a major steel end market.

**Steel Pricing Dynamics:** US HRC (hot-rolled coil) prices have been volatile, and while tariff protection supports domestic pricing, the threat of recession-driven demand destruction could overwhelm supply-side support. Section 232 tariffs on steel remain in place, providing a price floor versus imports, but they cannot prevent domestic demand contraction.

## Valuation Assessment

**Current Multiples:**
- Trailing P/E: 21.4x (on trough earnings of $7.52)
- Forward P/E: 21.4x (same figure in data; likely reflects consensus below Q1 guidance)
- Implied P/E on annualized Q1 guidance: ~14.6x

**Historical Context:** Nucor has historically traded at 6-12x earnings during mid-cycle periods. The current 21.4x trailing multiple reflects trough earnings, which is mechanically expected. The more relevant question is what normalized mid-cycle earnings look like and what multiple applies.

**Normalized Earnings Estimate:** If we assume mid-cycle EPS of $12-15 (between the $7.52 trough and the $30+ peak), the stock trades at 10.7-13.4x normalized earnings. This is at the upper end of historical mid-cycle multiples, suggesting limited margin of safety.

**Dividend Yield:** 1.38% is modest and below the 10-year Treasury yield, providing minimal income support for holding through volatility.

**Buyback Support:** Active buybacks provide a technical floor and signal management confidence, but at 21x trailing earnings and negative FCF, the buyback is funded by drawing down the balance sheet rather than excess cash generation.

**Valuation Verdict:** At $161, Nucor is fairly valued if the earnings recovery to $11-12 EPS materializes and sustains. It is expensive if recession pushes earnings back toward $5-7. It is cheap only if you believe a strong recovery toward $15+ EPS is imminent — which the macro backdrop does not support.

## Competitive Landscape

**Market Position:** Nucor is the largest steel producer in North America by capacity, with a diversified product mix and geographic footprint. Its EAF technology provides meaningful competitive advantages: lower capital intensity than blast furnace producers, ability to modulate production with demand (shorter lead times to restart/idle), lower emissions per ton, and cost flexibility via scrap-based feedstock.

**Competitive Moat:** Nucor's moat derives from: (1) scale advantages across 300+ facilities, (2) EAF cost structure that is variable-cost-heavy (approximately 65-70% variable costs), allowing margin preservation in downturns, (3) vertical integration into scrap processing and DRI production, (4) decentralized management culture that incentivizes operational efficiency at the plant level, and (5) tariff protection via Section 232 that limits import competition.

**Key Competitors:** Steel Dynamics (STLD) and Commercial Metals Company (CMC) are the closest domestic EAF peers. Cleveland-Cliffs (CLF) represents the integrated producer model. Nucor trades at a premium to STLD and CMC, reflecting its scale and diversification advantage.

**Tariff Dependency:** Section 232 tariffs provide structural protection for domestic steel pricing. Any trade policy changes that reduce or eliminate these tariffs would compress Nucor's pricing power. The current administration's trade posture (escalating tariffs broadly via Section 301) is generally supportive of continued steel protection.

## Risk Assessment

**Cyclical Risk (HIGH):** Steel is among the most cyclical industries. With recession probability at 40-45% and the Fed discussing rate hikes, the primary risk is that the Q1 improvement proves transient and earnings deteriorate further rather than recovering. Historical precedent: in the 2008-2009 recession, Nucor's EPS fell from $8.94 to a loss.

**Energy Cost Risk (MODERATE-HIGH):** EAF steelmaking is energy-intensive. Oil at $119 and rising energy costs directly pressure operating margins. While EAFs primarily use electricity rather than coking coal, electricity costs correlate with broader energy prices. Natural gas prices also affect costs for DRI production.

**Negative FCF Risk (MODERATE):** The company is burning cash while maintaining dividends and buybacks. If the earnings recovery stalls, Nucor will face a choice between reducing capital returns and further depleting its $2.7B cash cushion. The $2.25B undrawn revolver provides additional buffer but using it would represent a deterioration in financial position.

**Construction Demand Risk (MODERATE-HIGH):** Homebuilder AVOIDs, 7-month high mortgage rates, and potential rate hikes threaten residential construction demand. Commercial construction faces headwinds from rising real yields and office vacancy. Infrastructure spending provides partial offset but cannot fully compensate.

**Executive Transition Risk (LOW-MODERATE):** EVP Daniel Needham is retiring in June 2026, and a new CFO (Jack Sullivan) took over March 1, 2026. Both transitions are orderly and internal, limiting disruption risk, but two simultaneous C-suite changes warrant monitoring.

**Trade Policy Risk (LOW in current environment):** Section 232 tariffs are supportive under the current administration. Risk would increase under a future administration with different trade priorities.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- Q1 guidance above consensus marks the beginning of a sustained earnings recovery
- Infrastructure spending (IIJA) provides multi-year demand support independent of the business cycle
- EAF cost flexibility allows Nucor to maintain profitability even in a mild recession
- Active buybacks reduce share count, amplifying per-share earnings recovery
- Section 232 tariffs protect domestic pricing; broader tariff escalation could further restrict imports
- $5B in liquidity provides a comfortable buffer to weather the cyclical trough
- If EPS recovers to $12-15, the stock is worth $150-180 at 12x mid-cycle earnings, suggesting limited downside at current levels

- 21.4x trailing P/E on trough earnings already prices in substantial recovery that may not come
- Recession probability at 40-45% directly threatens steel demand across construction, manufacturing, and automotive end markets
- Negative FCF of -$188M in 2025 while paying $512M in dividends is unsustainable without earnings recovery
- Oil at $119 raises energy input costs and suppresses end-market demand simultaneously
- Rate hike discussions (10-15% probability) would further compress construction activity
- The 24% rally from April 2025 lows has already captured much of the recovery trade
- If recession hits, EPS could decline to $3-5 range, making the stock very expensive at current prices (32-54x)
- Normalized mid-cycle valuation of 10-13x on $12-15 EPS yields a range of $120-195, with current price near the midpoint

## Conclusion

Nucor is a high-quality cyclical business with genuine competitive advantages in its EAF-based cost structure, scale, and domestic tariff protection. The Q1 2026 guidance beat is a positive signal for near-term demand improvement.

However, at $161 and 21.4x trailing earnings, the stock prices in a meaningful earnings recovery that faces material headwinds from the stagflationary macro environment: $119 oil, 40-45% recession probability, potential rate hikes, and declining construction activity. The negative FCF position, while manageable given the liquidity cushion, constrains capital return sustainability if the recovery stalls.

The calibration framework's cyclical score ceiling (7.0 max for cyclically sensitive sectors) applies here. Per calibration rules, cyclical stocks require supportive macro indicators (PMI >52, positive yield curve, stable unemployment) for high-conviction calls — conditions not currently met. The risk/reward at current levels is balanced: meaningful downside if recession materializes, moderate upside if recovery continues.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The entry point for a BUY would be closer to $120-130, where mid-cycle valuation provides a genuine margin of safety, or upon clear evidence that the macro environment is stabilizing (improving PMI, stable employment, declining oil prices).
