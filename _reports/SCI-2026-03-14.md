## Executive Summary

Service Corporation International is the largest funeral and cemetery operator in North America, with approximately 17% market share. Q4 2025 adjusted EPS came in at $1.14, up 8% YoY on revenues of ~$1.12B (+2.4% YoY). Management guided 2026 normalized EPS of $4.05-$4.35, implying mid-to-high-single-digit earnings growth driven by margin expansion and disciplined capital deployment rather than volume.

The company's competitive position is genuinely strong: consolidation in a fragmented industry, pre-need sales generating a large trust float, and an aging U.S. population provide structural support. At ~20x forward earnings on $4.20 midpoint guidance, the stock is neither cheap nor expensive for a business with this level of defensiveness. The primary concern is low organic revenue growth (2.4% in Q4, guided flat-to-down funeral volumes for 2026) in a macro environment where inflation is running above target, making real revenue growth effectively negative.

The stock trades at $79.51, within 8% of its 52-week high of $86.67 and 11% above its 52-week low of $71.75. Given the narrow trading range, limited near-term catalysts, and a macro regime that doesn't specifically favor or threaten the business, this is a hold position. The dividend yield of ~1.7% provides modest income while waiting for either a better entry point or evidence that the "post-pandemic normalization" growth inflection materializes in reported numbers.

## Company Overview

SCI operates approximately 1,900 funeral homes and 500 cemeteries across the U.S. and Canada. Revenue comes from two segments: Funeral (at-need and pre-need services) and Cemetery (pre-need lot sales, interment services, memorialization). The business model has two key structural advantages: (1) geographic density creates local market dominance and cost synergies, and (2) pre-need sales generate a substantial trust float that earns investment income before services are delivered.

CEO Thomas Ryan has led the company since 2005. The management team has consistently emphasized capital allocation discipline — share buybacks, bolt-on acquisitions, and debt reduction — over aggressive revenue expansion.

## Financial Analysis

**Earnings:** Q4 2025 adjusted EPS of $1.14, up 8% from the prior year period. Q4 net income was $159.4 million, or $1.13 GAAP. Full-year 2025 results mark what management describes as the completion of "post-COVID normalization" — the elevated mortality pull-forward during 2020-2021 created a subsequent trough in volumes that has now run its course.

**Revenue:** Q4 revenue of approximately $1.12B, up 2.4% YoY. Management noted "moderate increases in revenues and gross profit in both the funeral and cemetery segments." Revenue growth is driven primarily by average revenue per case increasing at inflationary rates rather than volume growth. For 2026, management expects flat to slightly down funeral volumes.

- Trailing P/E: 20.4x

SCI historically carries meaningful debt (~$4-5B range in prior years) to fund acquisitions and buybacks. The company's pre-need trust fund assets partially offset this leverage, as they represent future revenue already contracted. The lack of current balance sheet data is a gap in this analysis.

**Capital Allocation:** Management's 2026 guidance emphasizing "margin expansion and disciplined capital deployment" suggests continued buybacks and selective M&A. The company has historically been an aggressive share repurchaser, which has been accretive given the stability of cash flows.

## Growth Analysis

**2026 Guidance:** Normalized EPS of $4.05-$4.35, with midpoint $4.20. Management frames this as 5-13% growth. The range is wide, reflecting uncertainty around funeral volumes.

**Volume Dynamics:** Flat-to-slightly-down funeral volumes for 2026 is the key constraint. U.S. death rates are a function of demographics (aging baby boomers = structural tailwind over the next 10-15 years) and year-to-year variability in mortality (flu seasons, COVID cohort effects). The demographic tailwind is real but gradual — the CDC projects deaths increasing from ~3.4M annually to ~3.8M by 2035, roughly 1% annual growth.

**Pricing Power:** Average revenue per funeral case has consistently grown at or above inflation. SCI benefits from limited price sensitivity in death care — families make purchasing decisions under emotional duress with limited comparison shopping. This gives SCI meaningful pricing power, which is the primary driver of organic revenue growth given flat volumes.

**Pre-Need Float:** SCI's pre-need sales model generates a large pool of trust assets (~$10B+ historically) that earn investment returns. In a higher-rate environment, this float becomes more valuable. This mechanism partially offsets the margin pressure from higher labor and materials costs.

**Cremation Mix Shift:** The ongoing shift from burial to cremation (now ~60% of U.S. dispositions) is both a headwind and opportunity. Cremation generates lower average revenue per case, but SCI has been building out cremation-focused service packages and memorial products to capture more revenue per cremation. Whether they fully offset the mix shift is an ongoing question.

## Valuation Assessment

At $79.51 with 2026 EPS guidance midpoint of $4.20, SCI trades at ~18.9x forward earnings. Using the profile's forward P/E of 20.4x (which may use a different earnings estimate), the stock is in a reasonable range for a business with these characteristics.

**Comparables context:** Death care is a niche sector with limited pure-play comps. The closest publicly traded competitor, Park Lawn Corporation (PLC in Canada), is significantly smaller. SCI's multiple has historically ranged from 16-25x forward earnings, with the higher end during periods of elevated mortality (COVID) and the lower end during normalization troughs. At ~19-20x, the stock is at the midpoint of its historical range.

**Dividend:** 1.73% yield provides modest income support. The payout ratio on $4.20 EPS would be approximately 33%, leaving ample room for continued buybacks and dividend increases.

**DCF Considerations:** Without detailed cash flow statements, a precise DCF is not possible. However, assuming $600-700M in annual operating cash flow (consistent with historical levels for SCI's revenue base), a 9-10% WACC, and low-single-digit perpetuity growth, fair value likely falls in the $75-90 range, putting the current price roughly at the middle.

## Competitive Landscape

SCI's 17% market share in a highly fragmented industry (there are ~19,000 funeral homes in the U.S.) is a significant competitive advantage. The next largest public competitor has less than 3% share. Key competitive dynamics:

1. **Consolidation moat:** SCI has spent decades acquiring independent funeral homes and cemeteries, creating geographic density that provides cost advantages (shared facilities, staff, vehicles, marketing). New entrants cannot replicate this network.

2. **Brand portfolio:** SCI operates under hundreds of local brand names (families often don't know their local funeral home is SCI-owned), preserving local relationships while extracting corporate-level efficiencies.

3. **Pre-need sales force:** SCI's scale allows it to maintain a dedicated pre-need sales organization that smaller operators cannot afford, creating a self-reinforcing cycle of future contracted revenue.

4. **Regulatory barriers:** Funeral home licensing, zoning restrictions, and cemetery perpetual care requirements create meaningful barriers to new capacity.

The primary competitive threat is not from other funeral operators but from secular shifts: direct cremation providers (which skip funeral services entirely), online memorial platforms, and cultural shifts away from traditional funerals. SCI is addressing these by offering cremation-compatible service packages, but the average revenue per cremation remains below burial.

## Risk Assessment

**Macro Sensitivity:** SCI has relatively low macro sensitivity — people die regardless of economic conditions, and families generally maintain funeral spending during recessions (SCI's revenue grew through 2008-2009). The 1.01 beta confirms this. In the current stagflationary environment, SCI is more defensive than most consumer services companies.

**Volume Risk:** The post-COVID normalization trough may not be fully over. If funeral volumes decline more than the "flat to slightly down" guidance, EPS could miss the low end of the $4.05-$4.35 range.

**Debt Levels:** SCI historically operates with ~$4-5B in debt. Rising interest rates increase refinancing costs. Without current balance sheet data, the precise risk is difficult to quantify, but SCI's predictable cash flows generally support this leverage level.

**California Legal Reserve:** Q4 2024 included a $20.3M reduction in California legal reserves, suggesting prior litigation exposure. The "primary claims period expired" language suggests this specific risk has diminished, but regulatory and litigation risk in the funeral industry is ongoing.

**Cremation Mix Shift:** Each percentage point shift from burial to cremation compresses average revenue per case. If SCI's cremation service packages don't offset this, organic revenue growth decelerates.

**Insider Activity:** Director Tony Coelho sold $615K in stock on March 10, 2026. Most other insider transactions were tax withholding (routine) or gifts. The Coelho sale is modestly negative but not alarming given the small size relative to SCI's market cap. CEO Ryan holds over 1M shares, indicating strong alignment.

**Oil/Inflation Impact:** Rising oil prices increase transportation costs (fleet vehicles) and energy costs for facilities. SCI's pricing power mitigates this, but margin compression is possible if cost inflation exceeds revenue-per-case growth.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- Post-pandemic normalization is complete, and 2026 marks the beginning of a demographic-driven growth cycle as baby boomer mortality increases 1-2% annually for the next decade.
- Pre-need trust float benefits from higher rates, adding incremental investment income.
- Share buybacks at current levels are accretive (FCF yield likely 5-6%), reducing share count and mechanically growing EPS.
- Margin expansion guidance suggests SCI is finding operating efficiencies, potentially through technology and cremation-focused service standardization.
- Defensive characteristics (1.01 beta, inelastic demand, 1.73% yield) make SCI attractive as a stagflation hedge.

### Bear Case
- 2.4% revenue growth and flat-to-declining volumes don't justify a 20x multiple. SCI is a mature, slow-growth business trading at a growth premium.
- Cremation mix shift continues to pressure average revenue per case, and SCI's cremation service packages may not fully offset the structural headwind.
- Debt load (~$4-5B) creates refinancing risk if credit markets tighten (credit cascade probability 55-60% per world model).
- The stock is 8% below its 52-week high with limited catalysts until the aging demographic tailwind becomes more visible in reported volumes (2027-2028 likely timing).
- Director selling suggests insiders don't see meaningful near-term upside.

## Conclusion

SCI is a high-quality defensive business in a structurally favorable industry, trading at a fair valuation. The 17% market share, pricing power, and demographic tailwinds are genuine competitive advantages. However, at ~19-20x forward earnings on guidance that implies flat volumes and inflationary pricing, the stock offers limited upside unless volume growth accelerates sooner than expected.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The defensive characteristics make SCI worth owning in a stagflationary environment, but the current price doesn't offer a compelling entry point. Accumulation at $72-74 (near the 52-week low, representing ~17x midpoint guidance) would improve risk-reward meaningfully. Conversely, a move above $85 without earnings acceleration would warrant trimming.

Per calibration: Why not BUY? The specific reason is that 2.4% revenue growth and flat-to-declining volumes at ~20x forward earnings provides insufficient margin of safety. The demographic growth catalyst (accelerating baby boomer mortality) is real but likely 12-24 months from showing up in volume data. The bull case requires patience without catalysts, and the entry point is mid-range rather than discounted.
