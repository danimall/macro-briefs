## Executive Summary

Service Corporation International is the largest provider of funeral and cemetery services in North America, operating approximately 1,900 funeral homes and 500 cemeteries. The company reported Q4 2025 adjusted EPS of $1.14, up 8% year-over-year, and guided 2026 normalized EPS of $4.05-$4.35 (midpoint $4.20), implying 5-13% growth. Revenue grew 2.4% in Q4 to approximately $1.12 billion. Management emphasized margin expansion and disciplined capital deployment.

The investment case rests on SCI's dominant competitive position (17% market share in a fragmented industry), pricing power at inflationary rates, and a pre-need "float engine" that generates investable cash flows from advance funeral and cemetery contracts. The stock trades at ~20x forward earnings at $79.51, which is fair for a company with this quality of competitive moat but does not offer a compelling margin of safety given the current macro environment of stagflation, rising consumer cost pressures from $100+ oil, and limited top-line growth (flat to slightly declining funeral volumes in 2026). The absence of detailed financial statements in the available data limits the depth of balance sheet and cash flow analysis.

## Company Overview

SCI operates in the death-care industry, providing funeral services (caskets, cremation, memorial services) and cemetery services (burial plots, mausoleum crypts, memorialization products). The business model has several structural advantages: demand is non-discretionary, demographics (aging baby boomers) provide a long-term volume tailwind, and SCI's scale enables procurement efficiencies and brand recognition unavailable to the ~85% of the market served by independent operators.

The company generates revenue through at-need services (services rendered at time of death) and pre-need contracts (advance funeral and cemetery purchases). Pre-need contracts create a float — cash received today for services delivered years or decades later — which SCI invests in a trust portfolio. This float mechanism, referenced in Seeking Alpha analysis as the "Float Engine," provides a meaningful and often underappreciated source of investment income.

CEO Thomas Ryan has led the company since 2005, providing management continuity. The company is headquartered in Houston, Texas.

## Growth Analysis

SCI's growth profile is modest but predictable. The key drivers:

1. **Volume:** Funeral volumes are guided flat to slightly down in 2026. Post-COVID normalization (the pull-forward of deaths during 2020-2022 created a subsequent volume deficit) appears complete based on management commentary that 2025 marks a "transition into a new growth cycle." The aging baby boomer cohort (born 1946-1964, now ages 62-80) provides a structural volume tailwind that should begin accelerating in the late 2020s.

2. **Pricing:** Average revenue per funeral case is expected to grow at inflationary rates. In a stagflationary environment with CPI running 3.1%+ and potentially rising further (world model estimates 3.2-5.2% peak CPI), this provides a natural revenue hedge. Funeral services have low price elasticity — consumers rarely comparison-shop aggressively during bereavement.

3. **Pre-need and Cemetery:** Cemetery revenue growth tends to be more variable but has been trending positively. Pre-need sales lock in future revenue at current prices, providing forward visibility.

4. **Margin expansion:** Management explicitly guided for margin expansion in 2026, which combined with flat-to-modestly-growing revenue implies operating leverage through cost discipline and pricing.

5. **Capital deployment:** Share buybacks reduce share count over time, amplifying per-share earnings growth beyond operating growth. SCI has historically been an active repurchaser.

The 5-13% EPS growth range for 2026 is credible given these drivers. The upper end requires either stronger-than-expected volume, higher pricing, or more aggressive buybacks.

Historical trading range has typically been 16-24x forward earnings. The stock sits 8.3% below its 52-week high of $86.67 and 10.8% above its 52-week low of $71.75.

The 1.73% dividend yield provides modest income support. Combined with 5-13% EPS growth and a ~19x multiple, the total return potential is approximately 7-15% annualized if the multiple holds steady.

In the current macro regime where consumer discretionary stocks are under pressure and defensive names are being bid, SCI's non-discretionary demand profile deserves a premium to the S&P 500's forward multiple. The stock is approximately fairly valued — modestly cheap on the low end of the range, modestly expensive on the high end.

## Competitive Landscape

SCI's competitive position is its strongest attribute. Key factors:

- **17% market share** in a highly fragmented industry where ~85% of providers are independent, single-location operators. The next largest competitor (Park Lawn Corporation, recently acquired by Carriage Services) is a fraction of SCI's size.
- **Scale advantages** in procurement (caskets, embalming supplies), technology, marketing, and regulatory compliance that independents cannot replicate.
- **Pre-need sales network** is a proprietary distribution channel. SCI's pre-need backlog creates significant switching costs and forward revenue visibility.
- **Brand portfolio** includes Dignity Memorial, the only nationally recognized funeral brand.
- **Barriers to entry** are high: cemetery capacity is physically constrained (zoning restrictions prevent new cemetery development in most markets), and funeral home locations require regulatory approvals and community relationships built over decades.

The death-care industry is also relatively immune to technological disruption. While cremation rates continue to rise (now approximately 60% nationally), SCI has adapted its service mix and pricing to maintain revenue per case even as cremation increases.

## Risk Assessment

**Macro risks — Moderate:**
- SCI's demand is non-discretionary, providing genuine insulation from the stagflationary environment. People do not defer funerals.
- However, consumers can trade down within death care: choosing cremation over burial, selecting less expensive caskets, or reducing memorial service scope. In a recession (38-42% probability per world model), this trade-down effect could compress average revenue per case below inflationary growth expectations.
- Rising interest rates affect the pre-need trust portfolio's mark-to-market value and could impair the float engine's contribution to earnings if credit spreads widen materially.

**Balance sheet risk — Unknown but historically elevated:**
- SCI typically carries $4-5 billion in total debt. In a credit cascade scenario (55-60% probability per world model), companies with significant leverage face refinancing risk. Without current balance sheet data, I cannot assess maturity schedules or covenant proximity.

**Insider activity — Neutral to mildly negative:**
- Director Tony Coelho sold 7,700 shares ($615K) on March 10, 2026. This is not large relative to institutional position sizes but occurred at a price ($79.87) close to the current level.
- Multiple executives had routine tax withholding dispositions on March 5.
- CEO Thomas Ryan gifted 10,000 shares in December 2025 but retains over 1 million shares, demonstrating substantial alignment.

**Regulatory risk — Low:**
- The FTC's Funeral Rule governs pricing disclosure. Changes are possible but unlikely to materially disrupt SCI's business model.
- The California legal reserve reduction mentioned in Q4 2024 suggests resolution of a prior regulatory headwind.

**Demographic risk — Favorable long-term:**
- US death counts are projected to rise from ~3.3 million annually to ~3.8-4.0 million by the mid-2030s as baby boomers age. This provides a structural volume tailwind that begins to accelerate in 2-3 years.

## Investment Thesis

**Bull Case:**
Post-pandemic normalization is complete, and SCI enters a sustained growth phase driven by demographic tailwinds, pricing power at or above inflation, margin expansion from scale, and continued share buybacks. The pre-need float engine generates compounding value as the trust portfolio grows. If 2026 EPS hits the upper end ($4.35) and the market assigns a 21-22x multiple for defensive quality, the stock reaches $91-96 — 15-20% upside. The defensive demand profile warrants a premium in a stagflationary environment where most consumer-facing companies are seeing demand destruction.

**Bear Case:**
Recession drives consumer trade-down in funeral services, compressing average revenue per case. Funeral volumes come in below "flat" guidance if the post-COVID normalization deficit lingers. Elevated leverage (typically $4-5B debt) creates refinancing risk if the credit cascade scenario materializes. At ~19x forward earnings, there is limited valuation cushion — if EPS comes in at the low end ($4.05) and the multiple compresses to 17x (lower end of historical range), the stock trades to $69, representing 13% downside. Rising interest rates could also impair pre-need trust portfolio returns.

## Conclusion

SCI is a high-quality defensive business with the strongest competitive position in its industry. The 17% market share, pricing power, pre-need float mechanism, and demographic tailwinds create a durable franchise. At ~19x forward earnings, the stock is fairly valued — not cheap enough to warrant a high-conviction BUY, and not expensive enough for an AVOID.

The primary constraint on upgrading this to BUY is the absence of detailed financial statement data, which prevents verification of the balance sheet and cash flow profile in a macro environment where leverage matters significantly (credit cascade probability 55-60%). The 2.4% revenue growth and flat volume guidance also suggest limited near-term catalysts. For a stock where the best growth catalyst (boomer demographics) is 2-3 years away, paying 19x for 5-13% EPS growth is adequate but not compelling.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The defensive demand profile provides portfolio ballast in the current stagflationary regime, and existing holders should maintain positions. New capital should wait for either (a) a pullback to the $71-73 range (16.5-17.5x forward), which would provide a more attractive entry, or (b) confirmation that the demographic volume inflection is beginning to accelerate.
