## Risk Assessment

**Macro risks (HIGH):** The stagflationary environment is broadly hostile to bank earnings. Higher-for-longer rates compress net interest margins when deposit costs rise faster than loan yields reprice. Simultaneously, a 38-42% recession probability increases credit loss expectations. Texas-specific risks include energy sector volatility (oil at $98-108 creates mixed effects: benefits energy borrowers but increases broader economic uncertainty) and CRE exposure.

**Insider selling (MODERATE-HIGH):** This is the most concerning signal in the available data. Ned Holmes has sold shares in small but regular tranches (typically 1,000-1,100 shares per week across multiple accounts) every week from January 14 through March 11, 2026 — eight consecutive weeks of selling. Perry Mueller Jr. sold 21,900 shares (~$1.6M) in a concentrated burst on February 12-17, 2026, near the stock's 52-week high. Combined insider dispositions exceed $2M with zero insider purchases during the period. While Holmes's selling pattern (small, regular lots) could reflect a 10b5-1 plan or estate/tax planning, the complete absence of any insider buying is a negative signal. Mueller's concentrated selling near the top is more concerning.

**Credit cycle risk:** Without loan book composition data, we cannot size this risk precisely. Texas banks historically carry above-average energy loan exposure. In a recession scenario with oil at $98-108, the effect is ambiguous: energy borrowers benefit from high prices, but non-energy commercial and consumer borrowers face margin compression from fuel costs.

**Regulatory risk:** Standard for all banks. No unusual filings or disclosures flagged.

**Liquidity risk:** Average daily volume of ~950K shares on $64 stock = ~$61M daily turnover. Adequate for institutional participation but not highly liquid.

## Conclusion

Prosperity Bancshares presents a mixed picture: reasonable valuation (11.3x, 3.7% yield) against a concerning macro backdrop and negative insider activity signals. The critical analytical limitation is the absence of financial statement data, which prevents assessment of asset quality, capital adequacy, and NIM trends — the three variables that matter most for bank stock valuation.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The valuation provides a floor against catastrophic loss, and the dividend yield compensates for patience. But the combination of (1) persistent insider selling with zero purchases, (2) a post-earnings selloff that has not been recovered, (3) a stagflationary macro environment hostile to bank earnings, and (4) insufficient data to evaluate credit quality prevents a BUY recommendation. An entry below $60 (10.5x earnings, approaching the 52-week low) would offer better risk/reward. Investors should wait for the next quarterly filing (likely May 2026) to assess Q1 results and NIM trajectory before committing capital.
