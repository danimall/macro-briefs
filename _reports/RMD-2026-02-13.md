## Executive Summary

ResMed Inc is the global leader in sleep apnea devices and respiratory care, commanding an estimated 50-60% share of the worldwide sleep apnea device market. The company reported strong Q2 FY2026 results with revenue of $1.42 billion (up 11% YoY), EPS of $2.81 (beating the consensus estimate of $2.69), and gross margin expansion of 320 basis points to 61.8%. The company's razor-razorblade business model, which pairs device sales with recurring consumable revenue (masks, accessories), provides resilient cash flows.

Despite these operational strengths, ResMed faces a potentially transformative risk from GLP-1 receptor agonist drugs (such as Eli Lilly's tirzepatide), which have demonstrated efficacy in treating obstructive sleep apnea by addressing the underlying obesity that contributes to the condition. This risk has weighed on the stock, which currently trades at $245.73—approximately 16% below its 52-week high of $293.81. At a trailing P/E of ~24.3x and forward P/E of ~25.7x, the stock is not cheap enough to fully compensate for this structural overhang.

The investment case hinges on whether GLP-1 drugs will meaningfully cannibalize CPAP demand or whether the massive undiagnosed sleep apnea population (estimated at ~80% of sufferers remain undiagnosed) creates enough runway for continued growth. We rate ResMed as a **Hold** with a neutral-to-cautious stance, acknowledging its best-in-class competitive position but flagging valuation and disruptive risks.

## Company Overview

ResMed Inc (NYSE: RMD) is a US-headquartered global medical device company that develops, manufactures, and distributes cloud-connected devices and digital health solutions for the treatment of sleep apnea, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and other respiratory conditions. The company is dual-listed on the NYSE and the ASX (via CHESS Depositary Interests at a 10:1 ratio).

**Key Business Segments:**
- **Devices**: CPAP and bilevel machines for sleep apnea treatment (e.g., AirSense platform)
- **Masks & Accessories**: Replacement masks, tubing, humidifiers — recurring revenue stream
- **Software-as-a-Service (SaaS)**: Cloud-based patient management platforms (AirView, myAir, Brightree, MatrixCare)

## Financial Analysis

### Revenue Performance
ResMed reported Q2 FY2026 (quarter ended December 2025) revenue of **$1.42 billion**, representing an **11% year-over-year increase** (9% on a constant currency basis). This exceeded analyst expectations by approximately 2.3%. The prior quarter (Q1 FY2026) generated $1.34 billion in revenue, up 9.1% YoY, indicating an acceleration in growth.

**Annualized revenue run rate**: Based on the last two quarters (~$2.76 billion in H1 FY2026), ResMed is tracking toward approximately **$5.5–5.6 billion** in annual revenue for FY2026.

### Profitability
- **Gross Margin**: 61.8% in Q2 FY2026, up **320 basis points** YoY. Non-GAAP gross margin expanded by 310 bps. This is a significant improvement and signals operating leverage, favorable product mix, and potentially lower supply chain costs.
- **EPS**: $2.81 (adjusted) in Q2 FY2026, up from $2.43 in the year-ago period — a **15.6% YoY increase**. This handily beat the consensus estimate of $2.69.
- **Prior quarter EPS**: $2.55 (Q1 FY2026), suggesting sequential improvement as well.

### Implied Trailing EPS and Valuation Cross-Check
With the stock at $245.73 and a trailing P/E of 24.25, the implied trailing twelve-month EPS is approximately **$10.13**. Given the Q2 FY2026 EPS of $2.81 and Q1 FY2026 EPS of $2.55, the first half alone totals $5.36, suggesting a full-year FY2026 EPS potentially in the **$10.50–$11.00** range.

### Balance Sheet & Cash Flow
While detailed balance sheet and cash flow statements were not available in the provided data, several indicators suggest financial health:
- The company pays a regular dividend (yield ~0.92%), indicating consistent free cash flow generation
- The market cap of ~$35.8 billion with consistent profitability implies a well-capitalized business
- ResMed has historically maintained moderate leverage, having used debt for acquisitions (MatrixCare, Brightree)

**Financial Health Assessment**: Strong gross margin expansion, double-digit revenue growth, and consistent earnings beats suggest solid financial health, though the lack of detailed balance sheet data limits full assessment.

## Growth Analysis

### Revenue Growth Trajectory
ResMed has demonstrated consistent growth:
- Q2 FY2026: +11% YoY revenue growth
- Q1 FY2026: +9.1% YoY revenue growth
- The acceleration from Q1 to Q2 is encouraging

### Growth Drivers
1. **Massive Undiagnosed Population**: An estimated 936 million people globally suffer from sleep apnea, with approximately 80% undiagnosed. Increased awareness and screening could drive sustained demand.
2. **Mask Replacement Cycle**: The recurring revenue from mask and accessories provides visibility and resilience.
3. **Digital Health/SaaS**: Cloud-connected devices and patient management platforms (AirView manages >22 million patients) create switching costs and data advantages.
4. **Geographic Expansion**: Growth in international markets, though constant currency growth (9%) trailed reported growth (11%), suggesting some FX tailwind.
5. **New Product Innovation**: Continued iteration on the AirSense platform and mask designs.

### Growth Headwinds
- **GLP-1 Weight-Loss Drugs**: This is the most significant growth concern. Eli Lilly's tirzepatide has been FDA-approved for moderate-to-severe obstructive sleep apnea in patients with obesity. If GLP-1 drugs gain wide adoption, some portion of the addressable market for CPAP devices could shrink.
- **Philips Recall Wind-Down**: ResMed benefited significantly from Philips' massive device recall in 2021–2024. As Philips re-enters the market, this tailwind fades.

## Competitive Landscape

### Market Position
ResMed holds an estimated **50-60% global market share** in sleep apnea devices, making it the clear market leader. This dominance is particularly strong in the CPAP/bilevel device category.

### Key Competitors
| Competitor | Position | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Philips Respironics** | #2 | Severely weakened by 2021 recall; slowly recovering |
| **Fisher & Paykel Healthcare** | #3 | Strong in masks; New Zealand-based |
| **Inspire Medical Systems** | Niche | Implantable neurostimulation; surgical alternative to CPAP |
| **GLP-1 Drug Makers** (Lilly, Novo) | Disruptive threat | Pharmaceutical alternative to devices |

### Competitive Moat
- **Razor-Razorblade Model**: Device placement creates years of recurring mask/accessory revenue
- **Data Ecosystem**: 22+ million patients on AirView; data lock-in for providers
- **Brand & Distribution**: Decades of relationships with sleep labs, DMEs, and payers
- **Scale & Manufacturing**: Cost advantages from market leadership
- **Switching Costs**: Patients and providers are reluctant to switch due to familiarity and data continuity

**Moat Rating**: Strong, but not impregnable. The GLP-1 threat represents a potential "moat bypass" if drugs can eliminate the need for devices in a significant patient subset.

## Risk Assessment

### High-Impact Risks

1. **GLP-1/Obesity Drug Disruption** (HIGH): This is the most significant risk. Tirzepatide's approval for sleep apnea in obese patients could reduce the addressable market. While current data suggests CPAP remains superior for severe apnea and compliance with daily injections may be challenging, the trajectory of obesity drug development is accelerating. One Seeking Alpha analyst rates the stock a "Strong Sell" primarily on this thesis.

2. **Philips Re-Entry** (MODERATE): As Philips rehabilitates its Respironics business and clears recalled devices, ResMed will lose the extraordinary market share gains from the recall period.

3. **Regulatory/Reimbursement Risk** (MODERATE): Changes to Medicare/Medicaid reimbursement rates for CPAP devices and supplies could pressure revenues.

### Moderate Risks

4. **Currency Risk**: With significant international operations, FX fluctuations impact results (11% reported vs. 9% constant currency growth in Q2).

5. **Product Liability**: As a medical device company, potential recalls or safety issues could be damaging.

6. **Concentration Risk**: Heavy dependence on the sleep apnea market; limited diversification.

### Mitigating Factors
- Beta of 0.89 suggests lower-than-market volatility
- Dividend provides a small yield floor
- Recurring revenue from masks/accessories provides resilience
- Vast undiagnosed population provides growth buffer even if some patients shift to drugs

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case 🐂
- **GLP-1 fears are overblown**: CPAP adherence is well-established, drug compliance may be lower (daily injections, cost, side effects), and GLP-1 drugs may actually increase diagnosis rates by raising awareness of sleep apnea, expanding the treatable population
- **Massive undiagnosed market**: With ~80% of sleep apnea patients undiagnosed, even modest increases in diagnosis rates drive years of device growth
- **Margin expansion continues**: Gross margins at 61.8% and rising suggest further operating leverage; EPS growth could outpace revenue growth
- **Philips competitor weakness**: Philips' brand damage may be permanent, leaving ResMed with structurally higher market share
- **Valuation has reset**: At ~24x earnings vs. historical 30-40x, much of the GLP-1 risk may already be priced in
- **Digital health optionality**: SaaS platforms create additional value and recurring revenue streams
- **Fair value target**: $280–$300 (15-20% upside)

### Bear Case 🐻
- **GLP-1 drugs transform sleep apnea treatment**: If obesity drugs meaningfully reduce sleep apnea severity in a large patient cohort, device demand could plateau or decline
- **Philips recovery accelerates**: A rehabilitated Philips could recapture significant market share, pressuring ResMed's pricing and volume
- **Valuation still not cheap enough**: At ~24x earnings, the stock doesn't offer sufficient margin of safety for the structural risks it faces
- **Growth deceleration**: As the Philips recall tailwind fades, organic growth may slow to mid-single digits
- **Reimbursement headwinds**: Potential Medicare competitive bidding changes could compress margins
- **Fair value target**: $180–$200 (18-27% downside)

## Conclusion

**Overall Rating: HOLD (Score: 6.85/10)**

ResMed is an exceptionally well-run company with dominant market share, expanding margins, and a proven recurring-revenue business model. The Q2 FY2026 results — 11% revenue growth, 320 bps gross margin expansion, and a $2.81 EPS beat — demonstrate continued operational excellence.

However, the stock operates under a significant structural overhang from GLP-1 obesity drugs that could meaningfully disrupt the sleep apnea device market over the medium to long term. While the current ~24x P/E reflects some discount for this risk versus historical multiples, we believe the risk-reward is approximately balanced at current levels.

**Recommendation**: We rate RMD a **Hold** for existing shareholders and suggest prospective buyers wait for either (a) further valuation compression to ~20x earnings ($210-$215 range) to provide a more attractive entry point, or (b) clearer evidence that GLP-1 drugs are not materially cannibalizing CPAP demand. Investors with high conviction in the "awareness thesis" (that GLP-1 drugs will expand the diagnosed population more than they cannibalize device demand) may find the current price attractive for a long-term position.
