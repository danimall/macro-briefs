## Executive Summary

Neogen is a food and animal safety company with a ~$2.0B market cap, trading at $9.09 as of March 14, 2026. The company has been in a protracted post-acquisition integration phase following the September 2022 combination with 3M's Food Safety division, which roughly tripled Neogen's revenue base but introduced significant operational complexity and diluted margins. The most recent quarter (Q2 FY2026, reported January 8) showed EPS of $0.10 vs. $0.07 consensus — a beat, but on a very low base that implies annualized earnings power of roughly $0.30-$0.40, yielding a 23-30x implied P/E for a business growing in the low-to-mid single digits organically.

The strategic divestiture of the animal genomics business to Zoetis for $160M (announced March 2, 2026) is a meaningful event for a company of this size. It signals management's intent to refocus the portfolio on core food safety testing and should provide capital for debt reduction or reinvestment. However, the genomics business was likely one of Neogen's higher-growth segments, so the divestiture trades near-term growth for balance sheet flexibility. With a 1.99 beta, no dividend, and the current stagflation macro regime, NEOG carries outsized volatility risk relative to its fundamental profile.

## Company Overview

Neogen operates in food safety diagnostics and animal safety products. Its core offerings include pathogen detection (Listeria, Salmonella, E. coli), mycotoxin testing, allergen detection kits, culture media, and environmental monitoring solutions for food processors. The 2022 3M Food Safety acquisition was transformative — it brought global scale, a large installed base, and the Petrifilm product line but also approximately $1.3B in acquisition-related goodwill/intangibles and integration costs that have weighed on reported profitability for over three years.

The company's fiscal year ends in May. The most recent filing is the 10-Q for Q2 FY2026 (filed January 8, 2026), with a 10-K for FY2025 filed July 30, 2025.

## Growth Analysis

Organic growth in food safety testing is typically 4-7% annually, driven by regulatory tightening (FSMA implementation in the US, EU food safety directives), supply chain complexity, and consumer demand for food transparency. Neogen's specific growth rate is obscured by acquisition noise, but the Q2 FY2026 earnings beat suggests the integration may be tracking ahead of Street expectations.

The genomics divestiture removes a segment that likely had higher growth characteristics (genomic testing in livestock is growing 8-12% annually). This is a negative for the consolidated growth profile but may improve margin consistency and strategic focus.

The current macro regime creates a nuanced growth picture for Neogen. Food safety testing is relatively non-discretionary — food processors must test regardless of economic conditions due to regulatory requirements and liability exposure. However, smaller food companies under financial pressure from $100+ oil (transportation costs, input costs) may defer discretionary testing upgrades or consumable orders at the margin. This partially insulates NEOG from the worst of the stagflation cycle, but does not make it immune.

## Competitive Landscape

Neogen competes in a fragmented food safety testing market. Key competitors include bioMerieux (pathogen detection), Roper Technologies (food quality), Mettler-Toledo (inspection), and Thermo Fisher (environmental testing). The 3M acquisition gave Neogen the Petrifilm franchise — one of the most recognized brands in indicator organism testing — and a global distribution network spanning 150+ countries.

The competitive position is solid but faces margin pressure from the need to invest in digital platform migration (moving customers from manual to automated testing workflows) while servicing acquisition-related debt. The food safety testing market has meaningful switching costs once a testing protocol is validated by a food processor's quality assurance team, which provides some recurring revenue stability.

## Risk Assessment

**High Beta (1.99):** In the current macro environment with VIX below capitulation and further equity correction probability at 40-50%, a 1.99 beta stock with limited earnings power faces amplified drawdown risk. A 10% S&P 500 decline could translate to a 20% NEOG decline, taking shares back toward $7.

**Integration Risk:** Three years post the 3M acquisition, integration is still the primary narrative. Margins have not yet recovered to standalone Neogen levels. Further delays or cost overruns remain plausible.

**Debt Level Uncertainty:** Without balance sheet data, the debt burden from the 3M deal remains a key unknown. If interest costs are $30-50M annually at current rates, that represents a significant drag on a business earning ~$80-90M pre-interest.

**Product Recall (January 28, 2026):** Voluntary recall of all lots of HYCOAT Hyaluronate Sodium represents reputational and financial risk, though this appears to be in the animal safety segment and the dollar amount is likely manageable.

**Macro Sensitivity:** While food safety testing is relatively non-discretionary, Neogen has exposure to the animal safety market, which is more cyclical. Oil at $100+ raises input costs for Neogen's consumables manufacturing.

**Section 301 Tariff Exposure:** Neogen has global operations and supply chains. Depending on sourcing of reagents and manufacturing inputs, Section 301 probes across 60 economies could create cost headwinds.

## Management Quality

The directional signal is positive — the CEO is buying, not selling. However, the dollar amounts are negligible (total ~$3,100 across five transactions). This could reflect a new CEO building a position through an automatic investment plan rather than high-conviction block purchases. For context, the SVP received a $375K stock grant and the new CFO received a $1.1M stock grant in January and November 2025, respectively, indicating the company is using equity extensively to retain new leadership during the turnaround phase.

The genomics divestiture decision appears strategically rational — exiting a non-core segment at an apparently fair price ($160M) to refocus and deleverage. The new CFO (Richard Bryan Riggsbee, starting November 2025) brings fresh perspective to the financial turnaround.

## Conclusion

NEOG is a mid-cap turnaround story with identifiable strategic catalysts (genomics divestiture, integration completion) offset by meaningful execution and macro risks. The stock's 135% rally from its 52-week low has already priced in significant improvement, leaving limited margin of safety at $9.09. The 1.99 beta is particularly problematic in the current stagflation environment where further equity correction probability is 40-50%.

The calibration data warns against issuing BUY calls on cyclically sensitive or high-beta names without strong macro support, and the overall score of 4.7 falls near the hard AVOID threshold of 5.0. However, the food safety testing end-market is sufficiently non-discretionary and the strategic actions (divestiture, new CFO, CEO buying) are directionally positive enough to keep this in HOLD territory rather than AVOID. The primary reason this is not a BUY: at $9.09 and ~23x annualized earnings with 1.99 beta entering a stagflation cycle, risk-reward is unfavorable. The primary reason this is not an AVOID: the business is strategically defensible, management is taking rational actions, and the valuation is not extreme on an EV/revenue basis for the category.

Wait for either: (a) a pullback to $6-7 that rebuilds margin of safety, or (b) two consecutive quarters demonstrating EBITDA margin expansion post-genomics divestiture.
