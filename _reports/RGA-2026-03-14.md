RGA reported Q4 2025 revenue of $6.77B (+23.4% YoY) and EPS of $7.75 (+55.3% vs. $4.99 a year ago), beating Zacks consensus estimates. Management reiterated intermediate-term targets of 8-10% annual EPS growth and 13-15% ROE, while announcing a strategic exit from group health care. AM Best affirmed the company's A+ (Superior) financial strength rating in February 2026. The company recently issued $400M in subordinated debentures at 6.375%, indicating continued access to capital markets on reasonable terms.

At $201.51, the stock trades at approximately 11.2x both trailing and forward earnings — a substantial discount to the broader market and reasonable for a life reinsurer delivering mid-teens ROE. With a beta of 0.54, a 1.8% dividend yield, and direct benefits from elevated interest rates on its investment portfolio, RGA presents a compelling risk-adjusted return profile in the current macro environment where GDP is decelerating, inflation remains sticky, and equity market volatility is elevated.

The primary concern is the limited granularity of available financial statement data, which constrains balance sheet analysis. However, the combination of strong Q4 results, credit rating affirmation, disciplined capital allocation (group health exit), and valuation well below analyst targets ($208-$223) supports a BUY recommendation.

## Company Overview

Reinsurance Group of America is one of the largest global life and health reinsurers, headquartered in St. Louis, Missouri. The company provides life reinsurance, annuity reinsurance, financial solutions, and group reinsurance to ceding insurance companies. RGA operates across the Americas, EMEA, and Asia Pacific, with executive leadership spanning all three regions (evidenced by insider filings from EVPs for EMEA, Asia Pacific, and the US).

RGA's business model is B2B: it assumes mortality, morbidity, and longevity risk from primary insurers. Revenue is driven by net premiums on in-force blocks, new treaty production, investment income on reserves, and fee income from financial solutions transactions. The company's recent decision to exit group health care signals a strategic narrowing toward its core life reinsurance competency where it has the deepest expertise and most defensible competitive position.

Market cap stands at $13.2B. CEO Tony Cheng holds approximately 32,735 shares (~$6.6M at current prices), representing meaningful personal alignment.

## Growth Analysis

**Revenue Growth:** The 23.4% YoY revenue increase in Q4 2025 is exceptional for a reinsurer. This likely reflects a combination of new treaty production, favorable mortality experience, and higher investment income from the elevated rate environment. Structural growth drivers:

1. **Aging demographics globally** — increasing demand for life and longevity reinsurance across all three operating regions. Japan (Asia Pacific head Arthur Ozeki's territory), continental Europe (EMEA head Cormac Galvin's territory), and the US all face accelerating demographic aging.

2. **Primary insurer capital relief** — Solvency II and risk-based capital requirements continue to push primary insurers to cede more risk. RGA's A+ rating makes it a preferred counterparty for these transactions.

3. **Financial solutions / block transactions** — this segment has been growing industry-wide as insurance companies seek to monetize or restructure legacy blocks. RGA's actuarial expertise gives it pricing advantages.

4. **Investment income tailwind** — with the current rate environment (FOMC holding amid stagflation), RGA's large investment portfolio benefits from elevated reinvestment rates. Management's 7% variable investment income assumption for 2026 reflects this.

**Management's targets:** 8-10% annual EPS growth and 13-15% ROE on an intermediate-term basis. These targets were reiterated on the Q4 2025 earnings call, suggesting confidence in the pipeline despite macro uncertainty.

**Group health exit:** Removing a lower-margin, higher-volatility business line should improve both ROE and earnings predictability over time. The revenue impact will be negative in the near term but accretive to margins.

## Competitive Landscape

RGA is the largest publicly traded life reinsurer. Its primary competitors include:

- **Swiss Re (Life & Health)** — larger overall but RGA has deeper US life market penetration
- **Munich Re (Life)** — strong European franchise; RGA competes more directly in Asia and the Americas
- **SCOR (Life & Health)** — smaller, more European-focused
- **Hannover Re (Life)** — growing competitor, particularly in Asia

RGA's competitive advantages:

1. **AM Best A+ rating** — critical for counterparty acceptance. Primary insurers evaluate reinsurer credit quality as a first-order screening criterion. Few life reinsurers carry this rating.

2. **Actuarial and data analytics expertise** — RGA has invested heavily in proprietary mortality and morbidity databases, which allows more precise pricing of complex risks. This is the primary moat in life reinsurance.

3. **Geographic diversification** — operations across Americas, EMEA, and Asia Pacific reduce concentration risk and allow cross-subsidization of growth in emerging markets.

4. **Client relationships** — reinsurance treaties are typically long-term (5-30+ year durations). Switching costs are high because of the complexity of transferring reserve calculations and regulatory approvals. This creates sticky revenue.

The group health exit narrows the competitive surface area to where RGA has the strongest positioning, which should improve win rates and margins in core segments.

## Risk Assessment

**Mortality/Morbidity Risk:** RGA's core business involves assuming mortality and morbidity risk. A pandemic or unexpected health event could cause claims to spike materially. COVID demonstrated this vulnerability — RGA's earnings were significantly depressed in 2020-2021. However, the company has since strengthened its reserves and risk models.

**Interest Rate Risk (Dual-Edged):** Higher rates benefit investment income but create mark-to-market losses on fixed income portfolios (unrealized losses in held-to-maturity may not appear in income but affect economic book value). A rapid rate decline would reduce investment income, though it would improve fixed income portfolio valuations. The current stagflationary environment with rates likely to remain elevated for an extended period is net positive.

**Credit Risk on Investment Portfolio:** Life reinsurers hold large fixed-income portfolios. A credit cascade (55-60% probability per macro framework) could impair investment portfolio values. The $400M subordinated debt issuance may partially be intended to build liquidity buffers against this scenario.

**Regulatory Risk:** Life reinsurance is regulated across multiple jurisdictions. Changes to reserve requirements, capital standards, or tax treatment of reinsurance transactions could affect profitability. The Bermuda subsidiary (RGA Americas Reinsurance Company) suggests some regulatory arbitrage that could be targeted.

**Concentration Risk:** The exit from group health care increases concentration in individual life reinsurance. While this is the highest-margin segment, it reduces diversification.

**Macro Sensitivity:** Beta of 0.54 indicates below-average market sensitivity. Life reinsurance earnings are driven more by actuarial experience and investment income than by economic cycles. In a stagflationary environment with 38-42% recession probability, this low beta is a genuine asset.

**Conflict/Geopolitical:** RGA has limited direct exposure to energy, shipping, or Middle Eastern operations. However, the broader credit cascade risk and equity market drawdown could affect capital markets access and client demand for block transactions.
