## Executive Summary

Carvana Co has delivered one of the most dramatic turnaround stories in recent market history, with shares surging approximately 3,100% over three years from the brink of collapse in 2022 to joining the S&P 500 Index in late 2025. The company has grown into the second-largest used car retailer in the United States, demonstrating improving business fundamentals including profitability and revenue growth. Management has guided for a strong finish to 2025 and the stock more than doubled during that year.

However, the investment case is severely complicated by several factors. In late January 2026, short-seller Gotham City Research published a detailed report alleging that Carvana overstated its 2023-2024 earnings by more than $1 billion through undisclosed related-party transactions with DriveTime and Bridgecrest—entities controlled by the father of Carvana's CEO. The report claims that over 73% of Carvana's adjusted EBITDA is propped up by DriveTime's leverage, which reportedly sits at 20-40x earnings. While JPMorgan has pushed back on the short report, calling it a "significant misrepresentation of facts," the allegations have triggered sharp sell-offs and raise genuine governance questions.

At a market capitalization of approximately $74.9 billion and a trailing P/E of ~119x (forward P/E of ~357x), the stock is priced for perfection in a scenario where the short-seller allegations prove baseless and growth continues unabated. Given the limited detailed financial data available for our analysis and the significant overhang from the Gotham City report, we believe the risk/reward is tilted to the downside at current levels.

## Company Overview

Carvana Co operates an e-commerce platform for buying and selling used cars in the United States. The company pioneered the online used car buying experience, featuring its iconic car vending machines and a fully digital transaction process including financing, trade-ins, and home delivery. Founded in 2012 and headquartered in Tempe, Arizona, Carvana trades on the NYSE under the ticker CVNA.

**Key facts:**
- **Market Capitalization:** ~$74.9 billion
- **Current Stock Price:** $344.14 (as of February 12, 2026)
- **52-Week Range:** $148.25 – $486.89
- **Beta:** 3.65 (extremely high volatility relative to market)
- **Trailing P/E:** 119.0x
- **Forward P/E:** 356.5x
- **Dividend Yield:** None
- **S&P 500 Member:** Yes (added December 2025)

The company's relationship with DriveTime Automotive Group, a private used car dealership chain controlled by Ernest Garcia II (father of Carvana CEO Ernest Garcia III), is a central feature of its business model and the source of current controversy.

## Financial Analysis

### Profitability & Earnings
Unfortunately, detailed income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow data were not available in the provided dataset, limiting our ability to conduct granular financial analysis. However, based on the market data and news reports, we can draw several conclusions:

- **Trailing P/E of 119x** confirms that Carvana has achieved GAAP profitability, a significant milestone given that the company was burning cash and facing potential bankruptcy as recently as 2022.
- **Forward P/E of ~357x** suggests that consensus earnings are expected to decline or that the current price significantly exceeds forward earnings expectations, which is unusual for a "growth" story.
- The divergence between trailing P/E (119x) and forward P/E (357x) is a red flag—it could indicate analysts expect earnings compression, one-time items inflating trailing earnings, or the accounting concerns raised by Gotham City.

### Related-Party Concerns
According to the Gotham City Research report:
- DriveTime burned over **$1 billion in cash flow from operating activities** in 2023-2024
- DriveTime's leverage is alleged to be at **20-40x earnings**, compared to a historical cap of 10.3x before 2023
- Gotham alleges that **73% of Carvana's adjusted EBITDA** is attributable to DriveTime subsidies
- The short report alleges **over $1 billion in overstated earnings** for the 2023-2024 period

### Balance Sheet Considerations

- The company still carries significant long-term debt from its rapid expansion and the 2022 acquisition of ADESA's physical auction business

## Valuation Assessment

Carvana's valuation is, by any traditional metric, extremely stretched:

- **Trailing P/E of 119x** is approximately 6-12x the typical valuation for used car retailers like CarMax (historically 15-20x P/E)
- **Forward P/E of 357x** implies minimal near-term earnings relative to the stock price
- **Market cap of ~$75 billion** for a used car retailer—even one growing rapidly—requires extraordinary assumptions about future profitability
- For context, **CarMax**, the largest used car retailer, trades at a market cap of roughly $12-15 billion with substantially higher revenue

A rough back-of-the-envelope calculation: at 119x trailing P/E and a ~$75B market cap, trailing net income is approximately $630 million. To justify a 20x P/E (fair for a mature retailer), Carvana would need ~$3.75 billion in net income—a level achieved by very few retailers in history.

Even if we grant a 40x P/E for above-average growth, the company would need ~$1.9 billion in earnings, roughly 3x current levels. This is achievable but requires flawless execution over several years with no margin compression.

**Valuation Score: 2.5/10** — The stock is priced for a best-case scenario with no margin for error.

## Risk Assessment

### Critical Risks

1. **Gotham City Research Allegations (HIGH):** The allegation that 73% of adjusted EBITDA comes from related-party subsidies and that over $1B in earnings were overstated is potentially existential if proven true. Even partial validation could trigger SEC investigation, restatements, and massive stock decline.

2. **Regulatory/Governance Risk (HIGH):** The related-party transactions between Carvana and DriveTime/Bridgecrest (controlled by the CEO's father) create inherent conflicts of interest. Gotham City specifically predicted "regulatory trouble."

3. **Extreme Valuation Risk (HIGH):** With a beta of 3.65 and P/E multiples far exceeding peers, any earnings miss, guidance cut, or negative headline can trigger outsized declines. The stock has already fallen from $486.89 to $344.14 (-29%).

4. **Interest Rate Sensitivity (MEDIUM-HIGH):** Used car financing is core to the business model. Higher rates reduce affordability and compress loan margins.

5. **Cyclical Risk (MEDIUM):** Used car demand is cyclical. An economic downturn would reduce unit sales and potentially increase credit losses on originated loans.

6. **Concentration Risk (MEDIUM):** Heavy dependence on related-party entities (DriveTime/Bridgecrest) for reconditioning, financing, and other services creates operational and reputational risk.

7. **Short-Seller Targeting (MEDIUM):** The stock's high short interest and the active short-seller reports create volatility and can become self-fulfilling through investor panic.

## Conclusion

**Overall Rating: 4.4/10 — UNDERWEIGHT / AVOID at current levels**

Carvana's operational turnaround is genuine and impressive—the company has gone from the brink of bankruptcy to the second-largest used car retailer and an S&P 500 constituent. However, the investment case at current prices is highly unfavorable:

1. **The valuation leaves no margin of safety.** At 119x trailing and 357x forward earnings, the stock is priced for a decade of flawless execution. Used car retail simply does not warrant this type of premium.

2. **The Gotham City allegations create material downside risk.** Even if the allegations are only partially true, the impact on investor confidence and potential regulatory action could be severe. The related-party structure with DriveTime is, at minimum, a governance concern that warrants a discount.

3. **The risk/reward is asymmetric to the downside.** With a beta of 3.65, limited earnings visibility, and an active short thesis, the stock could easily revisit the $200 level in a bearish scenario while upside to the $486 high requires all risks to dissipate.

**Recommendation:** Investors should avoid initiating new positions at current levels. Existing holders should consider reducing exposure. The stock may present a better entry point if (a) the Gotham City allegations are thoroughly disproven by an independent audit, (b) the company demonstrates sustained profitability excluding related-party benefits, and (c) the stock pulls back to a more reasonable valuation (below 40x forward earnings).

For investors who believe strongly in the turnaround narrative, a small speculative position with strict stop-losses may be appropriate, but this is a high-conviction, high-risk trade rather than a fundamental investment.
