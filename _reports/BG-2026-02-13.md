## Executive Summary

Bunge Global SA is a premier global agribusiness and food company operating across the agricultural value chain, connecting farmers to consumer markets worldwide. The company recently completed its landmark merger with Viterra, significantly expanding its global origination and processing footprint. Management has guided for 2026 adjusted EPS of $7.50–$8.00, representing modest growth over 2025's $7.30–$7.60 range, with the Viterra integration described as "already delivering results."

Despite these positive developments, the stock currently trades at approximately $121.74, implying a trailing and forward P/E of ~28.8x — a premium valuation for a commodity-oriented agribusiness company. This elevated multiple comes amid a challenging commodity environment characterized by record soybean oversupply that is compressing processing margins. The bull case rests on Viterra synergy realization and eventual margin recovery, while the bear case centers on prolonged commodity headwinds and an overstretched valuation.

Overall, Bunge presents a mixed investment profile: a strong competitive position enhanced by the Viterra merger, but with near-term earnings headwinds and a valuation that appears to price in much of the synergy upside already.

## Company Overview

Bunge Global SA (NYSE: BG) is one of the world's leading agribusiness and food companies, headquartered in Missouri with global operations. The company operates through several key segments:

- **Agribusiness** — Grain origination, oilseed processing, and global commodity trading
- **Refined & Specialty Oils** — Production of edible oils and specialty fats for food manufacturers
- **Milling** — Wheat and corn milling operations
- **Sugar & Bioenergy** — Sugar production and renewable energy (primarily in Brazil)
- **Corporate & Other**

The company has a market capitalization of approximately $23.5 billion. Its stock has a beta of 0.78, indicating below-average market sensitivity, consistent with a defensive food/commodity profile. The dividend yield stands at approximately 2.29%. The 52-week range is $68.33–$122.19, with the current price of $121.74 sitting near the top of that range.

The transformative Viterra merger, completed in 2025, significantly expanded Bunge's global grain origination and logistics network, making it one of the four dominant "ABCD" agricultural trading houses globally.

## Growth Analysis

1. **Viterra Synergies:** Management has shifted language from "embracing integration" to "unlocking synergies and delivering results," suggesting the integration is progressing well. The 2026 EPS guidance of $7.50–$8.00 (vs. 2025's $7.30–$7.60) incorporates a full year of Viterra contributions.

2. **EPS Growth:** The midpoint of 2026 guidance ($7.75) represents approximately 4–6% growth over the 2025 midpoint ($7.45), modest but positive given the challenging commodity backdrop.

1. **Soybean Oversupply:** Global soybean supply is at record levels, compressing crush margins. Seeking Alpha analysis notes that the 2026 outlook "does not show major catalysts" and that the Viterra effect may already be priced in.

2. **Commodity Cyclicality:** Agribusiness earnings are inherently volatile and tied to crop cycles, weather patterns, and global trade flows.

### Long-Term Growth Opportunities
- Expanding biofuel/renewable diesel demand creating structural support for oilseed processing
- Growing global food demand driven by population and protein consumption trends
- Enhanced global origination network post-Viterra providing better risk management and margin optimization

**Growth Prospects Score: 6.0/10** — Modest near-term earnings growth supported by merger synergies, but commodity headwinds limit upside.

## Competitive Landscape

Bunge operates in the highly concentrated global agricultural commodity trading and processing industry, often referred to as the "ABCD" companies:

- **A**rcher-Daniels-Midland (ADM)
- **B**unge Global (BG)
- **C**argill (private)
- **D**reyfus (Louis Dreyfus Company, private)

### Competitive Advantages
1. **Scale Post-Viterra:** The merger with Viterra significantly expanded Bunge's origination network, particularly in Canada, Australia, and Eastern Europe, creating one of the most diversified agricultural supply chains globally
2. **Processing Infrastructure:** Extensive oilseed crushing and refining capacity provides margin capture across the value chain
3. **Logistics Moat:** Global port, transportation, and storage infrastructure creates high barriers to entry
4. **Diversified Geography:** Presence across key agricultural regions (North America, South America, Europe, Asia-Pacific) provides natural hedging

### Competitive Risks
- ADM remains a formidable competitor with a more diversified portfolio including nutrition/specialty ingredients
- Smaller regional players and cooperatives compete in specific geographies
- Consolidation risk if regulatory scrutiny increases on ABCD concentration

**Competitive Position Score: 7.5/10** — The Viterra merger solidifies Bunge's position as a top-tier global agribusiness player with significant infrastructure moats.

## Risk Assessment

### Key Risks

1. **Commodity Price and Margin Risk (HIGH):** Soybean oversupply is at record levels, compressing crush margins. Extended oversupply could pressure earnings well below guidance.

2. **Integration Risk (MODERATE-HIGH):** The Viterra merger is transformative in scale. While early indications are positive, large-scale integrations carry execution risk, potential cultural clashes, and hidden liabilities.

3. **Regulatory/Trade Risk (MODERATE-HIGH):** Agribusiness is highly exposed to trade policy changes, tariffs, and export restrictions. Geopolitical tensions (e.g., US-China relations, Russia-Ukraine conflict) directly impact grain trade flows.

4. **Currency Risk (MODERATE):** Significant operations in Brazil, Argentina, and other emerging markets expose the company to FX volatility.

5. **Debt/Financial Risk (MODERATE):** Post-merger leverage is likely elevated, increasing financial risk during commodity downturns.

6. **Environmental/ESG Risk (MODERATE):** Deforestation concerns in South American soybean production, increasing regulatory and investor scrutiny on supply chain sustainability.

7. **Concentration Risk (LOW-MODERATE):** Heavy reliance on oilseed/grain commodities limits diversification relative to peers like ADM.

### Mitigating Factors
- Low beta (0.78) suggests relatively defensive characteristics
- Dividend yield of 2.3% provides downside support
- Active share buyback program demonstrates capital discipline
- Diversified global operations provide natural hedges

**Risk Factors Score: 5.5/10** — Significant commodity and integration risks partially offset by diversification and defensive characteristics.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case 🐂
- **Viterra Synergies Exceed Expectations:** The combined entity could generate significant cost and revenue synergies beyond initial estimates, driving earnings above the $8.00 upper end of 2026 guidance
- **Commodity Cycle Recovery:** A normalization of soybean supply (weather event, demand surge) could dramatically improve crush margins and earnings power
- **Structural Demand Growth:** Biofuel mandates and growing global protein consumption provide secular tailwinds for oilseed processing
- **Multiple Expansion:** If management delivers on synergies, the market may assign a premium multiple to the enhanced platform, driving stock above $140
- **Defensive Portfolio Positioning:** Low beta and dividend yield provide relative safety in volatile market environments

### Bear Case 🐻
- **Prolonged Oversupply:** Record global soybean production could persist for multiple years, keeping margins depressed and earnings below consensus
- **Integration Challenges:** Viterra integration could prove more complex and costly than anticipated, with delayed synergy realization
- **Trade Policy Disruption:** Escalating tariffs or export bans could disrupt grain trade flows and harm Bunge's intermediary business model
- **Valuation Risk:** At $121.74, the stock is trading above the $107.79 average analyst target, suggesting limited near-term upside and meaningful downside risk
- **Debt Burden:** Post-merger leverage in a compressed margin environment could strain the balance sheet and force cuts to buybacks or dividends

## Conclusion

**Overall Score: 6.3/10 — HOLD with cautious optimism**

Bunge Global is a fundamentally strong agribusiness company that has enhanced its competitive position through the Viterra merger. Management appears capable, synergies are beginning to materialize, and the long-term structural outlook for agricultural processing is favorable.

However, the current valuation presents a challenge. At $121.74, the stock trades near its 52-week high and above consensus analyst targets. The adjusted P/E of 15–16x is at the upper end of the historical range for agribusiness companies, suggesting the market has already priced in much of the Viterra upside. Meanwhile, record soybean oversupply creates a near-term earnings headwind that could persist.

**Recommendation:** HOLD for existing shareholders; new investors should consider waiting for a pullback to the $100–$110 range (representing 13–14x 2026E adjusted EPS) for a more favorable risk/reward entry point. The 2.3% dividend yield provides modest income while waiting for commodity market conditions to improve and Viterra synergies to fully materialize.

**Key catalysts to watch:**
- Quarterly earnings progression vs. $7.50–$8.00 2026 guidance
- Specific synergy realization targets and timelines
- Global soybean supply/demand balance and crush margin trends
- Trade policy developments affecting agricultural commodity flows
