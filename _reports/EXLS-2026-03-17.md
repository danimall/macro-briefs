## Executive Summary

ExlService Holdings reported a strong Q4 2025 with revenue of $542.6M (+12.7% YoY) and full-year revenue of $2.09B (+13-14% YoY), beating consensus on both revenue and adjusted EPS ($0.50 vs. $0.46 estimate). The data and AI segment grew 21% in 2025, and management guided for 9-11% organic growth in 2026. These are solid operating results for a mid-cap IT services and analytics firm serving insurance, healthcare, and financial services verticals.

The stock tells a different story. EXLS has declined from ~$48.80 (52-week high) to $29.88 as of March 16, 2026 — a 36% drawdown over the past year and a 39% decline from the high. A $68M institutional exit was disclosed in early March 2026, adding selling pressure. The disconnect between improving fundamentals and deteriorating share price reflects broader IT services sector compression, concerns about AI-driven disruption to traditional BPO/operations revenue, and the stagflationary macro regime reducing enterprise discretionary spending budgets.

At ~19.4x trailing and forward P/E, the stock trades at a discount to its historical range but within the context of sector-wide multiple compression affecting ACN, CTSH, EPAM, and peers. The 9-11% organic growth guidance is credible given the AI/data pivot, but a single small insider sale ($51K by the General Counsel) and no insider buying at depressed levels are neutral-to-mildly-negative signals. The risk/reward is balanced — the stock could re-rate if growth execution continues, but macro and sector headwinds argue against conviction at current levels.

## Company Overview

ExlService Holdings provides digital operations and analytics solutions primarily to insurance, healthcare, banking/financial services, and life sciences clients. The company operates through two main segments: (1) Digital Operations, which includes BPO and domain-specific outsourced services, and (2) Analytics/Data & AI, which includes data management, advanced analytics, and AI-driven solutions. The company is headquartered in New York with significant delivery operations in India.

FY2025 revenue reached $2.09B, with Q4 at $542.6M. Market capitalization is approximately $4.8B based on 156.4M shares outstanding at the current price of ~$30.83 (profile data) / $29.88 (latest price history). The company does not pay a dividend.

## Financial Analysis

**Revenue:** FY2025 revenue of $2.09B represents 13-14% growth over FY2024 (implied ~$1.85B). Q4 revenue of $542.6M was up 12.7% YoY. The data and AI segment grew 21% for the full year, indicating the higher-value analytics business is taking an increasing share of the revenue mix.

**Earnings:** Q4 GAAP EPS was $0.38, up 21.8% from $0.31 in Q4 2024. Q4 adjusted EPS was $0.50, up 15.0% from $0.44. The company reported Q4 net income of $60.2M. Trailing EPS is $1.54 per the profile data. The gap between GAAP ($0.38) and adjusted ($0.50) EPS in Q4 implies meaningful stock-based compensation and/or restructuring charges — approximately $0.12/share or roughly 24% of adjusted earnings.

**2026 Guidance:** Management guided for 9-11% organic revenue growth in 2026, which would imply approximately $2.28-2.32B in revenue. This represents a deceleration from the 13-14% growth in FY2025, which is typical as the base grows. The guidance range appears achievable given pipeline commentary around data and AI adoption.

**Balance Sheet / Cash Flow:** Detailed balance sheet and cash flow data were not available in the financial statements provided. However, the company's market cap of ~$4.8B against $2.09B in revenue implies a price-to-sales ratio of approximately 2.3x. The absence of a dividend and the company's consistent RSU issuance to executives suggest the company prioritizes reinvestment and share-based compensation over direct shareholder returns.

**Profitability:** Based on Q4 net income of $60.2M on $542.6M revenue, the GAAP net margin is approximately 11.1%. This is respectable for IT services but lower than pure-play analytics firms and below the margins of larger peers like ACN (~11-12% net margin on significantly higher scale).

## Growth Analysis

The 21% growth in data and AI revenue is the most important metric in the report. It demonstrates EXLS is successfully repositioning from a traditional BPO/operations outsourcer toward higher-value analytics and AI-driven services. CEO Rohit Kapoor specifically highlighted "accelerating client adoption of data and AI initiatives" as the primary growth driver.

The 9-11% organic growth guidance for 2026 implies management expects the AI/data acceleration to partially offset potential softness in traditional operations outsourcing. In the current stagflationary macro environment, enterprise IT spending is under pressure: CIOs are prioritizing cost reduction and efficiency (which could benefit EXLS's BPO) but also delaying discretionary transformation projects (which could slow analytics adoption). These forces partially offset.

The key growth risk is that EXLS's traditional operations business — likely still the majority of revenue — faces structural disruption from generative AI tools that reduce the need for human-in-the-loop BPO processes. If AI tools can automate claims processing, data entry, and routine analytics at lower cost than offshore labor, EXLS's core value proposition erodes. The company is attempting to get ahead of this by selling AI-enabled solutions rather than labor arbitrage, but the transition creates execution risk.

## Valuation Assessment

At $29.88 with trailing EPS of $1.54, EXLS trades at 19.4x trailing P/E. With the forward P/E also listed at 19.4x (implying consensus forward EPS roughly flat to trailing), the stock prices in modest earnings growth despite the revenue growth guidance. This likely reflects margin pressure expectations or continued SBC dilution.

**Historical context:** EXLS historically traded at 25-35x earnings during its growth phase. The current 19.4x represents significant multiple compression.

**Peer comparison:** ACN trades at ~25x forward P/E, CTSH at ~15x, WIT at ~20x. EXLS at 19.4x sits in the middle of the IT services peer group, which is appropriate given its growth rate is above CTSH but below pure-play AI/analytics firms.

**Price-to-revenue:** At ~2.3x revenue, EXLS is cheaper than ACN (~3x) but carries more execution risk. The valuation is reasonable given the growth rate but does not provide a large margin of safety if growth decelerates below the 9-11% guidance range.

The stock is down 36% over the past year. At the 52-week low of $26.94, the trailing P/E would be approximately 17.5x — closer to a genuine value entry point. At current levels, the stock is neither expensive nor compellingly cheap.

## Competitive Landscape

EXLS competes in a fragmented market against Accenture, Cognizant, Genpact, WNS Holdings, and EXL-specific smaller analytics firms. The company's competitive advantages include:

1. **Domain expertise in insurance and healthcare:** Deep vertical knowledge creates switching costs and positions EXLS for cross-selling analytics alongside operations.
2. **Data and AI capabilities:** The 21% growth rate suggests the company is winning against peers in AI-enabled solutions, though the absolute dollar base remains modest relative to ACN or CTSH.
3. **India delivery cost structure:** Provides labor arbitrage, though this advantage is structurally declining as AI tools reduce the labor intensity of BPO.

The competitive position is adequate but not dominant. EXLS lacks the scale of ACN or the pure-play analytics positioning of firms like Palantir. It occupies a middle ground that works in the current environment but faces long-term structural questions about the sustainability of operations outsourcing.

## Risk Assessment

**1. AI displacement of BPO revenue (HIGH).** This is the central risk. EXLS's traditional operations business relies on Indian labor performing rules-based processes for Western clients. Generative AI tools from Microsoft, Google, and specialized vendors are increasingly capable of automating these processes. EXLS is attempting to pivot to selling AI solutions rather than labor, but the transition may compress margins and reduce total addressable market.

**2. Macro/enterprise spending slowdown (MODERATE-HIGH).** In the current stagflationary environment with recession probability at 38-42%, enterprise IT budgets face pressure. Insurance and healthcare clients — EXLS's core verticals — may delay transformation projects. The company's BPO business is somewhat counter-cyclical (clients outsource more during downturns to cut costs), which provides partial offset.

**3. Institutional selling pressure (MODERATE).** A $68M investor exit was reported in March 2026, and the stock's volume spiked to 27.7M shares on June 27, 2025 (vs. 1.7M average), suggesting an earlier large block trade. Continued institutional liquidation could create persistent selling pressure.

**4. Insider activity (NEUTRAL-NEGATIVE).** The insider transactions are dominated by routine RSU vesting and tax withholding (Code M and F transactions). The only open-market sale was by General Counsel Ajay Ayyappan — two small sales totaling ~$87K across February-March 2026. CEO Rohit Kapoor converted 33,525 RSUs but no open-market sales or purchases. The absence of insider buying at 52-week lows is a mildly negative signal.

**5. Currency risk (MODERATE).** With significant India-based costs and USD-denominated revenue, INR appreciation reduces margins. Conversely, INR weakness benefits the company.

**6. Stock-based compensation dilution (MODERATE).** The gap between GAAP and adjusted EPS ($0.38 vs. $0.50 in Q4) and the extensive RSU grants visible in insider filings suggest meaningful ongoing dilution. The 156.4M share count should be monitored for creep.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- Data and AI segment growing 21% annually positions EXLS for multiple re-rating if AI adoption accelerates across insurance and healthcare clients
- 9-11% organic growth guidance is credible and would compound revenue past $2.5B by 2027
- At 19.4x earnings, significant multiple compression has already occurred — downside is more limited after a 36% decline
- Counter-cyclical BPO demand could stabilize revenue during macro weakness
- If EXLS successfully transitions from labor arbitrage to AI-enabled platform sales, margins could expand materially

### Bear Case
- Generative AI tools erode the value of traditional BPO operations, which likely represent 50%+ of revenue
- Enterprise spending compression in a stagflationary environment delays analytics deals and compresses growth below guidance
- Institutional selling continues — the $68M exit suggests large holders are reducing exposure
- SBC dilution reduces per-share earnings growth relative to revenue growth
- Competition from larger firms (ACN, CTSH) and pure-play AI companies (PLTR, specialized vendors) squeezes EXLS's mid-market positioning
- IT services sector is in the world model's AVOID category (software/SaaS short side) — while EXLS is more operations than SaaS, sentiment contagion affects the sector broadly

## Conclusion

EXLS presents a mixed picture: strong operational execution (14% revenue growth, 21% AI/data growth, Q4 beat) colliding with severe share price weakness (36% decline), institutional selling, and structural questions about AI displacement of traditional BPO. The 19.4x forward P/E is reasonable but does not offer a compelling margin of safety given the macro regime and sector headwinds.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The fundamental growth trajectory is intact and the valuation has compressed meaningfully. However, three factors prevent a BUY recommendation: (1) the ongoing institutional selling pressure with no countervailing insider buying, (2) the structural risk that AI tools erode the core operations business faster than the data/AI business can scale, and (3) the stagflationary macro environment creating headwinds for enterprise IT spending. A more attractive entry point would be ~17-18x forward earnings (~$27-28), which would also require confirmation that Q1 2026 revenue growth tracks within the 9-11% organic guidance range.
