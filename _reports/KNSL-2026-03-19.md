## Executive Summary

Kinsale Capital Group is a pure-play excess and surplus (E&S) lines insurer that has been among the best-performing insurance stocks over the past decade, driven by a technology-enabled low-cost underwriting model and disciplined capital allocation. Q4 2025 results were strong: revenue of $483.3M (+17.3% YoY), EPS of $5.81 (beat the $5.30 consensus by 9.6%), and full-year EPS of $21.65 (+28% YoY from the implied $16.89 figure based on the Q4 2024 run rate). The combined ratio has historically been among the best in the E&S space, and the company's proprietary technology platform provides a structural cost advantage.

The stock has declined approximately 30% from its 52-week high of $512.76, now trading at $355.74 as of March 17, 2026. The drawdown accelerated after Q4 earnings on February 12-13 (stock dropped from ~$401 to $371 in a single session despite a beat), suggesting the market is repricing growth expectations downward. A recent fair value estimate cut from $433 to $422 by at least one research firm reflects slowing premium growth and sector headwinds. CFO Bryan Petrucelli exercised options and sold 5,100 shares at $378.35 on February 27, and Director Frederick Russell exercised and sold 4,000 shares at $379.50 on February 26 — totaling ~$3.5M in open-market dispositions within two weeks of the earnings release.

At 16.4x forward P/E, the stock is historically cheap for Kinsale, which has typically traded at 25-40x earnings during its growth phase. The question is whether the compression reflects appropriate repricing of decelerating growth or an overcorrection that creates a buying opportunity. Given the insider selling pattern, continued price deterioration, and lack of a clear technical floor, HOLD is appropriate with a more compelling entry likely in the $300-330 range.

## Company Overview

Kinsale Capital Group, headquartered in Richmond, Virginia, is a specialty insurance company focused exclusively on the E&S market. E&S insurers cover risks that standard admitted carriers decline — construction, environmental, professional liability, excess casualty, and other hard-to-place risks. The E&S market has been structurally growing as admitted carriers retreat from complex risk classes, particularly after catastrophe events and social inflation trends.

Kinsale differentiates through its proprietary technology platform, which automates much of the underwriting and quoting process. This enables lower expense ratios than peers (typically 20-23% vs. 30%+ for competitors), faster turnaround times for brokers, and better data-driven risk selection. The company writes across approximately 100 lines of E&S business through roughly 50 underwriting divisions.

The company has approximately 23.15 million diluted shares outstanding, giving it a market capitalization of approximately $8.2 billion at the current price.

## Growth Analysis

### Decelerating Premium Growth

The central concern driving the stock's decline is growth deceleration. Q4 revenue grew 17.3%, a respectable rate but well below the 30-50% growth rates of 2021-2023. Multiple factors contribute:

1. **Hard market cycle maturation.** The E&S hard market that began in 2019 is aging. Rate increases are moderating across most lines. Kinsale's growth was turbocharged by both rate and new business submission volume during the hardest phase of the cycle.

2. **Base effect.** Growing 17% on a much larger premium base ($483M quarterly revenue) requires substantially more absolute premium dollars than growing 40% off a $250M base.

3. **Competitive re-entry.** As E&S pricing has improved, some admitted carriers and new E&S entrants are competing more aggressively for business that Kinsale previously captured with less competition.

The fair value estimate reduction from $433 to $422 cited in news articles explicitly references "slower premium growth and sector headwinds." This is the correct framing.

### Structural Tailwinds Remain Intact

Despite cyclical deceleration, the E&S market continues to benefit from structural tailwinds: social inflation (rising jury awards), climate-driven catastrophe frequency, and ongoing withdrawal of admitted carriers from difficult risk classes. In the current macro environment, the Iran conflict and Hormuz disruption are generating new categories of war-risk and energy-related E&S demand. The stagflationary regime also tends to support insurance pricing power (higher replacement costs → higher insured values → higher premiums).

Kinsale's technology moat means it captures a disproportionate share of incremental E&S submissions. As long as the E&S market continues its secular expansion, Kinsale should grow above the industry average — the question is the magnitude of outperformance.

## Competitive Landscape

Kinsale's competitive position within E&S remains strong. The company's technology platform provides three distinct advantages:

1. **Expense ratio superiority.** Kinsale consistently operates 800-1,200 basis points below peer expense ratios. This advantage is durable because it's embedded in the company's operating model, not in cyclical factors.

2. **Speed-to-quote.** E&S brokers prioritize carriers that respond quickly. Kinsale's automated underwriting platform delivers quotes faster than competitors, attracting more submissions.

3. **Data quality.** Ten years of granular underwriting data across 100+ lines creates a compounding informational advantage in risk selection. Competitors without comparable data infrastructure make worse risk selection decisions on average.

Key competitors include Markel (MKL), RLI Corp (RLI), James River Group (JRVR), and the E&S divisions of large carriers like Berkshire Hathaway, AIG, and Chubb. None have replicated Kinsale's technology-driven cost structure.

The E&S market is estimated at approximately $100-120B in gross written premiums and has been growing at approximately 15% annually. Kinsale's market share remains in the low single digits, providing substantial runway for continued share gains even as overall market growth moderates.

## Risk Assessment

### 1. Growth Deceleration Risk (Primary)
The stock's 30%+ decline from highs is entirely driven by growth repricing. If Q1 2026 results show further deceleration below 15% revenue growth, the multiple could compress to 14-15x, implying further downside to the $300-325 range. This is the highest-probability near-term risk.

### 2. Catastrophe Exposure
As an E&S insurer, Kinsale has meaningful catastrophe exposure in property lines. The current macro environment (Iran conflict, climate volatility) increases the probability of outsized cat losses. A single large cat event could swing quarterly results from a significant beat to a miss. The 2025 hurricane season and ongoing geopolitical disruptions create above-average cat risk.

### 3. Reserving Risk
E&S reserves are inherently more uncertain than admitted lines due to longer-tail liability classes (excess casualty, professional liability). Social inflation continues to put upward pressure on loss development. If prior-year reserves develop adversely, Kinsale would need to increase reserves, directly reducing reported earnings.

### 4. Insider Selling Signal
The CFO and a director selling a combined ~$3.5M in shares within two weeks of a strong earnings report is a negative signal. Director Russell's reduction to 460 shares (from 4,460) is particularly bearish — this is a departure from material alignment with shareholders.

### 5. Macro/Sector Headwinds
The world model's stagflationary regime is mixed for Kinsale. Positive: higher replacement costs and risk aversion support E&S pricing. Negative: higher interest rates → higher discount rates → lower equity valuations for growth stocks. Bond safe-haven failure and credit stress could affect Kinsale's investment portfolio, though as a P&C insurer its fixed-income portfolio is typically high-quality and short-duration.

### 6. Key-Person Risk
CEO Michael Kehoe founded Kinsale and has been instrumental in building the culture and technology platform. He holds 308,048 shares (~$110M), demonstrating alignment, but any leadership transition would create uncertainty given the company's founder-led nature.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- At 16.4x earnings, Kinsale is trading at its cheapest valuation in years. Historical mean reversion to 20-25x would imply $430-540, representing 20-50% upside.
- The E&S market's structural expansion continues. Kinsale's technology moat ensures above-average market share gains even as the overall growth rate normalizes.
- Q4 results demonstrate continued underwriting discipline: 17% revenue growth with 28% EPS growth means margins are expanding.
- The stagflationary macro environment supports insurance pricing power. Rising cat risk and social inflation keep E&S rates firm.
- Investment income benefits from higher interest rates on the fixed-income portfolio, providing an earnings tailwind that partially offsets premium growth deceleration.

### Bear Case
- Growth is decelerating, and the market is correctly repricing from a growth multiple to a quality-specialty multiple (12-14x). At 13x EPS, the stock would trade near $280.
- Insider selling immediately post-earnings by the CFO and a director suggests those with the best information are monetizing.
- The E&S hard market is maturing. Competition is intensifying. Submission flow growth may slow further.
- Catastrophe losses in the current elevated-risk environment could produce significant quarterly earnings volatility.
- The stock has no technical support — it has made new 52-week lows repeatedly since October 2025, declining from $502 to $356 in under 5 months.

## Conclusion

Kinsale Capital is a high-quality business trading at a historically compressed valuation after a 30%+ drawdown. The fundamental story — best-in-class E&S underwriter with durable technology moat, expanding margins, and strong capital allocation — remains intact. Q4 2025 results confirmed that the company continues to execute well operationally.

The valuation at 16.4x is attractive relative to Kinsale's own history but still carries a premium over P&C peers, justified only if above-average growth continues. The combination of growth deceleration, insider selling, and sustained price deterioration suggests the market has not yet found equilibrium. The P&C insurance sector is structurally attractive in the current macro environment (world model confirms overweight thesis for P&C broadly), and Kinsale is among the highest-quality names in the space.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The business quality merits a position, but current momentum is negative and insider selling adds caution. A more compelling entry point is likely in the $300-330 range, where the stock would trade at approximately 14-15x trailing earnings and provide a margin of safety against further growth deceleration. If the stock stabilizes in the $350-370 range for 4-6 weeks and insider selling ceases, an upgrade to BUY would be warranted. Conversely, if Q1 2026 results (expected May 2026) show revenue growth below 12% or combined ratio deterioration, further downside is likely.

**Why Not BUY:** (Per calibration mandate for HOLD scores ≥6.2) Three specific factors prevent a BUY: (1) CFO sold $1.93M and Director Russell sold $1.52M in open-market transactions within two weeks of the Q4 beat — this is the strongest negative signal available; (2) the stock has not established a technical floor, making new 52-week lows as recently as February 13 ($341); (3) growth deceleration from 30%+ to 17% may not be complete, and the next data point (Q1 2026 earnings) is months away.
