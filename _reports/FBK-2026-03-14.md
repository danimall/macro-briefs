## Valuation Assessment

At 21.5x trailing and forward earnings, FBK is expensive relative to community bank peers. The KBW Regional Banking Index typically trades at 10-14x forward earnings. Even high-quality community banks in premium growth markets (like Nashville) rarely sustain multiples above 15-16x for extended periods.

**Possible explanations for the premium:**
1. Market expectations for above-average earnings growth that the forward P/E doesn't capture (i.e., consensus estimates may project significant earnings acceleration beyond one year)
2. Nashville market scarcity premium — few publicly traded pure-play Nashville banks
3. Potential M&A target premium — $2.6B market cap is attractive for larger regional acquirers looking for Southeast exposure
4. The P/E figures may be distorted by non-recurring items depressing trailing earnings

Without tangible book value data, price-to-TBV analysis is impossible. For reference, community banks typically trade at 1.2-2.0x TBV; a $2.65B market cap for a bank of this profile would likely imply 1.5-2.0x TBV, which is reasonable but not cheap.

**At 21.5x earnings, the stock needs to deliver meaningful earnings growth to justify the multiple.** The macro environment makes this unlikely in 2026. If earnings are flat (as the identical trailing/forward P/E suggests) and the multiple compresses toward 16-18x (still a premium to peers), downside of 15-25% is plausible.

## Conclusion

FBK is a HOLD. The fundamental quality of the Nashville franchise is likely above average for community banks, but the valuation at 21.5x earnings provides no margin of safety in a deteriorating macro environment. The stock has already corrected 18% from its 52-week high, which prices in some deterioration, but the recession probability (38-42%) and credit cascade risk (55-60%) suggest further downside is plausible if earnings contract while the multiple compresses.

The critical data gap — no income statement, balance sheet, or cash flow data — prevents upgrading or downgrading with confidence. An analyst with access to FBK's actual financial statements should focus on: (1) CRE concentration as a percentage of total capital, (2) deposit composition (insured vs. uninsured), (3) NIM trend over the last four quarters, (4) provision expense trajectory, and (5) tangible book value per share to assess whether the current price represents a reasonable TBV multiple.

For investors holding FBK, the position deserves closer scrutiny given the macro headwinds. For prospective buyers, better entry points likely exist at 14-16x forward earnings ($33-38), which would require either a further correction of 25-35% or meaningful earnings growth. Neither path is imminent.
