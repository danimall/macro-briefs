## Executive Summary

Ryder System is a fleet management, supply chain solutions, and dedicated transportation provider with a $7.4B market cap trading at $193.48, approximately 16.1x trailing and forward earnings of ~$11.98. The company guided 2026 comparable EPS of $13.45-$14.45, representing 12% growth at the high end on approximately 3% operating revenue growth, with a targeted improvement in return on equity. Q4 2025 EPS of $3.26 missed consensus expectations of $3.66, which triggered a volatile reaction around the February 11 earnings release.

The stock has appreciated approximately 39% from its 52-week low of $125.54 (hit during the April 2025 tariff/oil shock) but remains 16% below its 52-week high of $230.39. Coordinated insider selling totaling over $30M occurred in the two weeks following the Q4 report, involving the CEO, COO, CLO, multiple EVPs, and a director. In the current macro environment — oil at $119, recession probability at 40-45%, potential Fed rate hikes, and deteriorating consumer/industrial activity — Ryder's asset-heavy transportation model faces margin compression risk from fuel costs and volume pressure from slowing freight demand.

## Company Overview

Ryder operates three segments: Fleet Management Solutions (FMS), which provides full-service leasing, contract maintenance, and commercial rental of trucks; Supply Chain Solutions (SCS), which manages warehousing, distribution, and transportation management; and Dedicated Transportation Solutions (DTS), which provides vehicles with drivers as outsourced fleet operations. The business model is capital-intensive, requiring continuous investment in fleet vehicles, with revenue tied to commercial transportation volumes and industrial activity.

The company is undergoing a leadership transition, with CEO Robert Sanchez presenting alongside incoming President and COO John Diez at recent investor conferences. Diez outlined the 2026 guidance framework at the Q4 call.

## Financial Analysis

**Earnings:** Trailing EPS is $11.98. Q4 2025 reported EPS of $3.26 missed consensus of $3.66 by approximately 11%. Full-year 2026 guidance is $13.45-$14.45, implying the midpoint at ~$13.95 represents approximately 16% growth from FY2025 levels.

**Valuation Multiples:** At $193.48, the stock trades at 16.1x trailing EPS and approximately 13.4-14.4x the 2026 guidance range midpoint. The forward P/E on guidance is reasonable for an industrial services company.

**Market Cap:** $7.45B on 39.2M shares outstanding.

**Dividend:** Yield of approximately 1.9%, providing modest income.

**Balance Sheet / Cash Flow:** Detailed financial statements were unavailable in the data provided. However, Ryder's business model is inherently capital-intensive: fleet vehicles sit on the balance sheet as assets funded by significant debt. The company typically carries $7-9B in total debt against a fleet asset base of comparable magnitude. Rising interest rates (with Fed now discussing hikes) increase Ryder's cost of debt on refinancing.

**Revenue Growth Guidance:** ~3% operating revenue growth for 2026, modest and consistent with a mature fleet services business in a slowing economy.

## Growth Analysis

Ryder's growth trajectory is tied to three dynamics:

1. **Secular outsourcing trend:** Companies increasingly outsource fleet management and supply chain operations rather than maintaining owned fleets. This provides a multi-year tailwind, though growth rates are single-digit.

2. **Used vehicle pricing cycle:** Ryder's FMS segment generates significant gains/losses on disposal of used vehicles after their lease terms. This is highly cyclical. Used truck prices have been normalizing from post-COVID peaks, creating a headwind that contributed to the Q4 miss.

3. **Technology investment:** Management has emphasized investments in fleet technology, telematics, and supply chain optimization tools at recent conferences (JPMorgan Industrials, Citi Global Industrial Tech). These investments improve customer stickiness but require capital.

The 2026 EPS guidance range of $13.45-$14.45 implies 12% earnings growth at the top end, driven by operating leverage on modest revenue growth plus cost discipline. The ROE improvement target mentioned in guidance suggests management expects to extract more from the existing asset base. However, achieving the top end of guidance in a stagflationary environment with $119 oil and declining freight volumes would be challenging.

## Valuation Assessment

At $193.48 and 2026 guidance midpoint of ~$13.95, Ryder trades at approximately 13.9x forward earnings. This is reasonable for an asset-heavy industrial services company. Historical Ryder P/E ranges have typically been 10-18x, placing the current valuation in the middle of that range.

The stock's 52-week range of $125.54 to $230.39 reflects extreme volatility. The February earnings reaction (stock surged from ~$191 to $230 intraday on February 11, then pulled back to $208 by February 12) suggests the market is uncertain about the earnings trajectory. At $193, the stock has given back approximately half the post-guidance rally.

**Bull case valuation:** If Ryder achieves $14.45 EPS and the market applies 16x, fair value is ~$231, roughly the 52-week high.

**Bear case valuation:** If EPS comes in at $12.00 (below guidance due to recession/oil impact) and the market compresses to 12x, downside is ~$144.

The current price sits roughly in the middle of these scenarios, suggesting balanced risk/reward.

## Competitive Landscape

Ryder competes with Penske Truck Leasing (private), J.B. Hunt Transport Services (JBHT) in dedicated and intermodal, XPO Logistics, and Werner Enterprises. In supply chain solutions, the competitive set includes C.H. Robinson, GXO Logistics, and major 3PLs.

Ryder's competitive advantages include: (1) one of the largest commercial truck fleets in North America with national service coverage, (2) long-term customer relationships with high switching costs due to integrated fleet management technology, (3) diversification across FMS, SCS, and DTS segments that provides revenue stability relative to pure-play truckers.

Weaknesses include: (1) capital intensity requiring continuous fleet investment and significant debt, (2) exposure to used vehicle residual value cycles, (3) sensitivity to fuel costs (oil at $119 directly increases fleet operating expenses, though fuel surcharges provide partial offset with a lag).

## Risk Assessment

**Macro/Cyclical Risk (HIGH):** Ryder is directly exposed to industrial production, freight volumes, and consumer goods movement. With recession probability at 40-45%, GDP at 0.7%, and NFP at -92K, the freight demand environment is deteriorating. The Cass Freight Index and truck tonnage data have been weakening.

**Oil/Fuel Cost Risk (HIGH):** Oil at $119 and central range of $108-120 creates significant cost pressure. Ryder passes fuel costs through surcharges on certain contracts, but there is a lag and incomplete passthrough, particularly in FMS rental and used vehicle operations. Diesel at current levels compresses margins on non-surcharge revenue.

**Interest Rate Risk (MODERATE-HIGH):** Ryder carries substantial debt to finance its fleet. The Fed discussing rate hikes (10-15% probability of a hike in 2026) means refinancing costs could increase materially. Even the "no cuts" scenario (25-30% probability) keeps borrowing costs elevated.

**Insider Selling (ELEVATED):** The coordinated insider selling in February 2026 is significant:
- CEO Sanchez sold ~$9.3M in stock (plus $14M in tax withholding on option exercises), reducing his position to just 40,828 shares
- COO Diez sold $2.1M, retaining 183,810 shares
- EVPs Sensing, Fatovic, Ravindran, Havens, Jones, and Regan collectively sold millions
- Director Smith liquidated most of his position ($6.7M in sales)

This is not routine diversification — the CEO's remaining position of 40,828 shares (~$7.9M) is notably small for a $7.4B company's chief executive. The breadth and timing (immediately after the stock's post-earnings surge to $220+) suggests insiders viewed the $220+ level as fully valued or overvalued.

**Used Vehicle Residual Risk:** Softening used truck prices in a recessionary environment would pressure FMS segment earnings, as Ryder books gains/losses on fleet disposals.

**Leadership Transition Risk:** CEO succession from Sanchez to Diez introduces execution uncertainty.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- 13.9x forward P/E on guidance midpoint is attractive for a company with strong market position and secular outsourcing tailwinds
- 2026 guidance implies meaningful earnings growth despite Q4 miss
- Supply chain solutions and dedicated transportation provide recurring revenue characteristics
- Dividend yield of ~1.9% adds total return
- If freight cycle recovers, Ryder's leverage to volume improvement is significant

### Bear Case
- $119 oil directly compresses margins; diesel passthrough lag means Q2-Q3 2026 results could disappoint
- Recession probability at 40-45% threatens freight volumes and fleet utilization rates
- Potential Fed rate hikes increase cost of debt on a highly leveraged balance sheet
- Coordinated insider selling of $30M+ across 8 executives and a director, with CEO reducing to minimal position
- Q4 2025 already missed by $0.40/share; 2026 guidance may prove optimistic in current macro
- Used vehicle values declining in a soft freight market

## Conclusion

Ryder presents a balanced risk/reward profile at $193.48. The valuation at ~14x forward guided earnings is not expensive, the business has genuine competitive advantages in fleet management, and the outsourcing secular trend provides structural support. However, the macro headwinds are severe: $119 oil, potential rate hikes, 40-45% recession probability, and weakening freight demand all directly threaten Ryder's cost structure and revenue base. The coordinated insider selling — particularly the CEO reducing to a minimal holding — is a meaningful negative signal that the people closest to the business did not want exposure at $220+.

The stock is 16% below its high and 39% above its low. At current levels, it neither offers the margin of safety needed for a BUY in this environment nor presents the deteriorating trajectory that would warrant an AVOID. A freight cycle recovery or oil pullback toward $90-100 would improve the thesis; conversely, a recession confirmation or sustained oil above $120 would justify downgrading.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** Monitor Q1 2026 results (likely reported May 2026) for freight volume trends and margin impact from elevated fuel costs. Revisit if the stock pulls back toward $160-165 (offering ~12x forward earnings) where risk/reward would shift more favorably.
