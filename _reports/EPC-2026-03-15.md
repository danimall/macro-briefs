## Executive Summary

Edgewell Personal Care is a ~$923M market cap consumer products company whose core brands include Schick/Wilkinson Sword (wet shave), Banana Boat/Hawaiian Tropic (sun care), and Playtex/Carefree (feminine care, now being divested). The stock trades at $19.77, down 38% from its 52-week high of $31.72 but up 25% from its 52-week low of $15.88. Q1 FY2026 earnings came in at -$0.16 EPS, beating the -$0.18 consensus by $0.02, which is a marginal positive on an absolutely weak number.

The company is undergoing a strategic pivot: divesting its feminine care unit to reduce debt and concentrate on razors and sun care. This is a reasonable capital allocation decision for a company with limited pricing power competing against Procter & Gamble's Gillette and private label alternatives. However, the forward P/E of 36.3x is difficult to justify for a business with no demonstrated organic growth trajectory, and the current macro environment — stagflation with rising petrochemical input costs — directly pressures Edgewell's margin structure. The 3.2% dividend yield provides a floor, but the risk-reward at current levels is unattractive for new money.

## Company Overview

Edgewell Personal Care was spun off from Energizer Holdings in 2015. The company operates in three segments: Wet Shave (Schick, Wilkinson Sword, Intuition, Hydro), Sun and Skin Care (Banana Boat, Hawaiian Tropic, Bulldog, Jack Black), and Feminine Care (Playtex, Carefree, Stayfree — now being divested). The company is headquartered in Shelton, Connecticut.

The wet shave category has been structurally challenged for years by DTC disruptors (Dollar Shave Club, Harry's), private label growth, and reduced shaving frequency. Sun care is more defensible seasonally but faces intense competition from private label and specialty brands. The feminine care divestiture signals management's recognition that the business lacks scale in this category to compete effectively.

## Growth Analysis

Growth prospects are limited across all three segments:

**Wet Shave (largest segment):** The global wet shave market has been flat to declining in volume terms for the past decade. Schick/Wilkinson Sword holds the #2 position behind Gillette but with significantly less scale and marketing firepower. Private label penetration continues to rise. Dollar Shave Club (Unilever) commoditized the category. Edgewell's Hydro brand has not gained meaningful share. There is no visible catalyst for category acceleration.

**Sun and Skin Care:** This is the more promising segment. Banana Boat and Hawaiian Tropic are well-recognized brands, and growing consumer awareness of UV protection provides a modest tailwind. The Bulldog and Jack Black acquisitions added premium men's grooming exposure. However, sun care is highly seasonal, commoditized at the mass market level, and faces reformulation headwinds from evolving FDA sunscreen regulations.

**Post-Divestiture:** Removing feminine care simplifies the portfolio but also removes revenue, leaving a smaller company with higher fixed cost absorption requirements. Unless divestiture proceeds materially reduce debt, the pro-forma entity may have worse operating leverage.

**Growth Score:** 3.5/10. No organic growth drivers, structurally challenged categories, limited pricing power.

## Competitive Landscape

Edgewell competes against substantially larger, better-capitalized companies:

- **Wet Shave:** P&G's Gillette has ~60% global market share vs. Edgewell's ~15-20%. Scale advantages in R&D, manufacturing, and advertising are enormous. Private label (e.g., Target's Goodfellow, Amazon Basics) continues to grow.
- **Sun Care:** L'Oréal, Beiersdorf (Coppertone), Johnson & Johnson, and dozens of indie/premium brands compete in a fragmented market. Banana Boat is well-known but associated with mass-market/value positioning.
- **Men's Grooming:** Bulldog and Jack Black are niche brands with limited distribution relative to P&G, Unilever, and L'Oréal's men's portfolios.

Edgewell lacks meaningful moat. Brand awareness exists but doesn't translate to pricing power. Distribution relationships (Walmart, Target, Amazon) are shared with all competitors. R&D spend is a fraction of P&G's.

**Competitive Position Score:** 4.0/10. Recognizable brands without pricing power or scale advantages.

## Risk Assessment

**Macro/Input Cost Risk (HIGH):** Current oil prices at $98-108/barrel directly increase costs for petrochemical-derived inputs (razor blade coatings, plastic handles, packaging, sunscreen chemicals). Edgewell lacks the pricing power to fully pass through costs given private label competition and consumer trade-down behavior in a stagflationary environment.

**Consumer Spending Risk (MODERATE-HIGH):** GDP at 0.7%, retail sales -0.2%, gasoline +21%. Consumer staples are more defensive than discretionary, but personal care is more deferrable than food/beverage. Consumers can extend razor blade usage, switch to private label, or reduce sun care purchases.

**Divestiture Execution Risk (MODERATE):** The feminine care sale must close at an attractive price, proceeds must reduce debt meaningfully, and the remaining business must demonstrate improved profitability. Failure on any dimension would be negative.

**Insider Activity (CAUTIONARY):** Chief Accounting Officer John Dunham sold his entire position (333 shares, shares remaining: 0) at $17.21 on November 20, 2025. While the dollar amount is small ($5,731), a CAO selling 100% of their holdings is a negative signal about their assessment of the stock's value. Board members received restricted stock equivalent grants on February 5, 2026 (7,817 shares each to 8 directors), which is routine compensation. No open-market insider purchases are visible.

**Dividend Sustainability Risk:** With negative trailing EPS, the 3.2% dividend yield requires scrutiny. If FCF doesn't cover the dividend (which we cannot verify from the available data), a cut would remove a key support pillar and likely trigger a 10-15% price decline.

**Risk Score:** 4.5/10. Multiple macro and company-specific risks partially offset by low beta and defensive category positioning.

## Conclusion

Edgewell Personal Care is a structurally challenged consumer products company executing a defensible but unexciting portfolio simplification strategy. The stock may be cheap on normalized earnings, but normalization timing is uncertain, the macro environment actively works against the company's margin structure, and there is no visible catalyst for organic growth acceleration. The 3.2% dividend yield and 0.59 beta provide defensive utility, but the absence of insider buying at depressed levels, the CAO's complete exit, and the 36x forward P/E on currently negative earnings argue against new capital deployment.

Per calibration guidance: consumer discretionary/staples BUY calls require positive sequential acceleration in comparable revenue metrics, FCF yield above 5%, and non-deteriorating consumer data. None of these conditions are met. The stock scores 4.3 overall, which under calibration guidelines creates a presumptive AVOID. However, the low beta, dividend yield, and potential for normalized earnings recovery on divestiture completion provide sufficient uncertainty to warrant HOLD rather than AVOID. The specific falsifiable condition for upgrade to BUY: two consecutive quarters of positive organic revenue growth and reported EPS above $0.40/quarter.

**Recommendation: HOLD**
