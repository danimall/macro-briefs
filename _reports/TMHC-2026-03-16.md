## Executive Summary

Taylor Morrison Home reported Q4 2025 adjusted EPS of $1.91, beating consensus of $1.73 but declining 28% from $2.64 in Q4 2024. Full-year 2025 revenue came in around $8.1-8.3B based on available quarterly run rates, with Q4 profit of $174M. Management is guiding 2026 as a "deliberate reset year" with ~11,000 home deliveries and 100+ new community openings, while explicitly managing margin pressures. The stock trades at 7.6x forward earnings, a ~$5.8B market cap, and sits 18% below its 52-week high of $72.50.

The valuation looks cheap in isolation, but homebuilders as a sector face compounding structural headwinds in the current macro regime: mortgage rates at 7-month highs driven by bond safe-haven failure, core PCE at 3.1% removing any near-term rate cut catalyst, oil at $98-108 inflating construction input costs, and consumer spending under five simultaneous pressures including -92K NFP and declining retail sales. Lennar's Q1 results (-13% revenue, -53% EPS) and its CEO's insider selling provide sector-level confirmation of deteriorating fundamentals. TMHC's own 28% YoY EPS decline is part of this same pattern.

## Company Overview

Taylor Morrison Home Corporation, headquartered in Scottsdale, Arizona, is a mid-cap homebuilder operating primarily in Sun Belt markets. The company focuses on three buyer segments: entry-level, move-up, and resort/lifestyle. Per CEO Sheryl Palmer's Q4 2025 commentary, TMHC is strategically pivoting away from "commoditized, incentive-heavy entry-level submarkets" toward core move-up and resort-lifestyle buyers where pricing power is more durable. The company has ~96.3M shares outstanding and does not pay a dividend.

## Growth Analysis

**2026 guidance:** Management targets ~11,000 home deliveries and 100+ new community openings. The strategic shift toward move-up/resort-lifestyle segments implies lower volume but higher ASPs and potentially better margins per unit — if demand holds.

**Key growth risks in current macro:**
1. **Mortgage rates at 7-month highs.** Bond safe-haven failure (Treasuries selling off during active geopolitical crisis) mechanically pushes yields higher → higher mortgage rates → reduced affordability → lower housing demand. The sub-6% mortgage window has closed for the entirety of 2026 in our base case.
2. **Consumer spending deterioration.** NFP at -92K, retail sales -0.2%, unemployment 4.4%. TMHC's target move-up buyer requires existing home equity and job confidence — both eroding.
3. **Input cost inflation.** Oil at $98-108 raises lumber transport costs, petroleum-based materials (roofing, insulation, PVC), and equipment operating costs. Fertilizer supply disruptions (confirmed by Reuters) will increase landscaping costs for new communities.
4. **Spring selling season already impaired.** Lennar's Q1 2026 results (-13% revenue, -53% EPS) indicate that the spring selling season — typically the strongest seasonal period for homebuilders — has failed to materialize at sector level.

The 100+ new community openings create cost commitments (land development, infrastructure, model homes) regardless of whether demand materializes. In a deteriorating environment, this creates operating leverage to the downside.

## Valuation Assessment

At 7.6x forward earnings, TMHC trades roughly in line with homebuilder peer multiples (LEN ~8x, DHI ~7.5x, PHM ~6.5x, TOL ~7x). The sector compression reflects the market correctly pricing in structurally lower earnings power as rates remain elevated.

**Why cheap doesn't equal undervalued here:**
- Forward EPS of ~$7.85 assumes stable margins. Management explicitly acknowledged "margin pressures" in 2026 guidance. If home price/mix adjustments or incentives compress gross margins by 200bps, EPS could fall to $6.50-7.00, making the effective forward P/E 8.5-9.1x — less compelling.
- Book value per share is unavailable from the data provided, but homebuilder book values are heavily influenced by land inventory marks that may not reflect current market clearing prices.
- The stock is 18% below its 52-week high of $72.50 and 14% above its 52-week low of $51.90. Current price of $59.20 is roughly mid-range, not a distressed level.

BofA downgraded TMHC to Neutral on January 16, 2026, citing "EPS and delivery risks despite valuation appeal." The subsequent price target boost (March 8, 2026) after Q4 results is consistent with a maintain-Neutral-but-acknowledge-beat pattern, not a reversal of the fundamental concern.

## Competitive Landscape

Taylor Morrison occupies a mid-tier position among national homebuilders. Its strategic pivot toward move-up and resort/lifestyle buyers differentiates it from pure entry-level builders (DR Horton, Meritage) but puts it in competition with Toll Brothers and Pulte's move-up segments.

**Structural challenge:** In a rising-rate environment, move-up buyers face a "lock-in effect" — they hold sub-4% mortgages on existing homes and are reluctant to sell and finance at 6.5-7%+. This constrains TMHC's addressable market precisely where they are concentrating resources.

The company's Sun Belt geographic focus (Arizona, Texas, Florida, Carolinas) provides demographic tailwinds from domestic migration patterns, but these markets also had the most aggressive builder supply additions during 2021-2023, creating elevated inventory competition.

## Investment Thesis

**Bull Case:**
TMHC at 7.6x forward earnings with 100+ community openings in growing Sun Belt markets offers significant upside if rates decline. The strategic pivot to move-up/resort-lifestyle segments positions TMHC for higher margins as the entry-level market becomes margin-destructive. If a ceasefire occurs (5-8% probability per world model), the resulting oil price decline and bond rally could produce a rapid 20%+ revaluation. BofA's post-Q4 price target increase suggests institutional recognition of execution quality.

**Bear Case:**
TMHC is a cyclically exposed homebuilder entering a confirmed stagflation with bond safe-haven failure ensuring mortgage rates remain elevated throughout 2026. Q4 EPS declined 28% YoY despite beating a lowered consensus bar. The "reset year" framing masks fundamental demand weakness. LEN's -53% Q1 EPS decline provides the sector trajectory. Consumer spending is deteriorating across five channels. Input costs are rising from oil, materials, and labor. The 100+ new community openings create fixed cost commitments that amplify downside operating leverage. The stock could trade to $45-48 (6x a compressed ~$7.50 EPS) in a recession scenario, representing 20-24% downside.

## Conclusion

TMHC is a structurally AVOID in the current macro regime. The calibration document shows Consumer Discretionary BUY calls average -1.54% — the worst sector in the track record. None of these conditions is currently met.
