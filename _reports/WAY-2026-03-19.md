## Executive Summary

Waystar is a healthcare revenue cycle management (RCM) software provider based in Lehi, Utah, that IPO'd on NASDAQ and has guided for 17% revenue growth in 2026, reaching a midpoint of $1.284 billion. The growth target reflects ~10% organic expansion plus contributions from the July 2025 acquisition of clinical visitation capabilities. Despite record bookings and a growing backlog, the stock has declined 32% over the trailing 12 months from $36.55 to $24.75, driven by a Q4 2025 EPS miss ($0.36 reported vs. $0.39 expected) and broader multiple compression in growth software names.

The core tension is between a genuinely attractive end market (healthcare payments digitization, large remaining manual-process TAM) and a valuation that remains demanding at ~40x forward earnings on $0.61 trailing EPS. Without access to full financial statements — income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow data are all empty in the provided dataset — the analysis necessarily relies on market data, news, price action, and insider activity. The combination of incomplete financial visibility, a recent earnings miss, and a stretched multiple in a hostile macro environment for growth software warrants a HOLD rating.

## Company Overview

Waystar provides cloud-based software for healthcare revenue cycle management. Its platform automates administrative and financial workflows for healthcare providers — claims submission, payment processing, denial management, and patient financial engagement. The company is investing in AI-driven automation and recently completed an acquisition to add clinical visitation capabilities.

## Competitive Landscape

Healthcare RCM is a fragmented market with multiple competitors:

- **Change Healthcare** (now part of Optum/UnitedHealth): dominant scale player
- **R1 RCM** (RCM): direct competitor in end-to-end RCM
- **Veracyte, nThrive, Experian Health, Availity**: various niche competitors
- **Epic Systems**: EHR vendor increasingly building native RCM capabilities

Waystar's advantage lies in its platform approach — connecting providers with payers through a single cloud platform for the full revenue cycle. The company claims over 30,000 clients. Mizuho's competitive risk flagging is worth noting: as EHR vendors like Epic build native RCM features, the standalone RCM software market faces potential disintermediation over a 3-5 year horizon.

## Risk Assessment

**Key risks:**

1. **Incomplete financial visibility:** Without balance sheet data, the debt load is unknown. PE-backed healthcare IT companies frequently carry leverage ratios of 4-6x EBITDA. If Waystar has $2-3 billion in net debt (plausible for a ~$5B EV company), the equity is more leveraged to operational performance than the headline market cap suggests.

2. **Competitive disintermediation:** Epic and other EHR vendors building native RCM features could erode Waystar's addressable market over time.

3. **Earnings quality:** The Q4 miss ($0.36 vs. $0.39) on an adjusted basis suggests the path to profitability may be lumpier than guided. GAAP EPS of $0.10 for Q4 vs. adjusted $0.36 indicates significant adjustments — the gap between GAAP and adjusted merits scrutiny.

4. **Macro sensitivity:** While healthcare spending is generally defensive, hospital capital expenditure budgets for IT projects can be deferred in stressed environments. The current stagflationary regime with rising input costs for health systems could delay purchasing decisions.

5. **Valuation risk:** At 40x forward earnings, even modest negative revisions could trigger disproportionate price declines. The stock already fell from $42.55 to $21.13 — a 50% drawdown — in roughly 7 months.

6. **Lock-up and PE overhang:** As a relatively recent IPO with PE sponsorship, potential secondary sales by pre-IPO shareholders create supply overhang. The September 2025 volume spike (23.2 million shares on 9/19) may reflect a lock-up expiration or secondary offering.

- 17% guided revenue growth with record bookings/backlog suggests accelerating momentum.

### Bear Case

- Unknown debt load from PE heritage could make equity more fragile than it appears.
- Competitive risk from Epic and Optum/Change Healthcare is structural and growing.
- Insider selling by CBO at $30 (now proven well-timed) and lack of broader insider buying beyond one small director purchase.

- The GAAP-to-adjusted EPS gap ($0.10 vs. $0.36 in Q4) suggests heavy stock-based compensation or amortization that dilutes true economic earnings.

## Conclusion

Waystar operates in an attractive, growing end market with genuine competitive strengths in healthcare RCM automation. The 17% revenue growth guidance and record bookings are legitimate positives. However, the stock remains expensive at 40x forward earnings for a company with thin GAAP profitability, a recent earnings miss, unknown balance sheet characteristics, and growing competitive risks. The 32% price decline has improved the risk/reward but has not yet brought the valuation to levels that compensate for the uncertainties.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The stock becomes more interesting below $20 (~33x forward if EPS grows to $0.75-0.80), where the growth premium would be more defensible relative to the risk profile. Investors with higher risk tolerance could initiate a small position near $22-23 (close to the 52-week low of $21.13), but the incomplete financial data and 40x multiple prevent a full BUY recommendation under current conditions.
