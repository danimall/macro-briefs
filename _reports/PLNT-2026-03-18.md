## Executive Summary

Planet Fitness has declined 35% from its July 2025 52-week high of $114.47 to $75.30, driven by a convergence of company-specific and macro headwinds. Q4 2025 results beat consensus (revenue up 10.5% YoY to $376.3M, EPS of $0.83 beating by 4.8%), but 2026 revenue growth guidance of ~9% disappointed investors expecting continuation of double-digit expansion. The stock gapped down ~9% on earnings day (February 24, 2026) and has continued declining.

Three distinct risk vectors are compressing the multiple simultaneously: (1) an active Schall Law Firm securities fraud investigation announced March 14, 2026; (2) CFO turnover, with Jay Stasz departing and former CFO Tom Fitzgerald appointed as interim replacement; and (3) net debt of $1.7B at 3.3x EBITDA, which becomes increasingly problematic as the stagflationary macro regime raises refinancing risk and limits financial flexibility. The franchise-asset-light model generates strong cash flows, but the balance sheet impairs the company's ability to weather sustained consumer weakness.

The current ~28.7x forward P/E remains elevated for a company with this leverage profile and decelerating growth trajectory. Jefferies maintains a $175 target (Buy rating), implying 130%+ upside, which appears aggressive given the overhang from litigation and management instability. The stock warrants monitoring for a better entry point rather than commitment at current levels.

## Company Overview

Planet Fitness operates the largest fitness franchise system in the United States, with approximately 2,900 clubs globally and 20.8 million members as of year-end 2025. The company's "Judgement Free Zone" positioning targets casual fitness consumers at a $10-$15/month price point ($24.99 for the Black Card tier), creating a wide addressable market among price-sensitive demographics.

Revenue is derived from three streams: franchise segment (royalties, placement fees), corporate-owned stores, and equipment sales to franchisees. The asset-light franchise model means ~95% of locations are franchisee-operated, generating high-margin royalty income with limited capital expenditure requirements from the parent company.

## Financial Analysis

**Revenue and Growth:** Q4 2025 revenue reached $376.3M, up 10.5% YoY. Full-year 2025 performance was described by CEO Colleen Keating as reflecting "strong performance" driven by strategic discipline. However, 2026 guidance targets ~9% revenue growth, representing a meaningful deceleration from the 10.5% Q4 run rate. Same-club sales data was referenced positively by management but specific figures were not provided in available data.

**Profitability:** Trailing EPS of $2.62 on a market cap of $5.9B yields a 28.7x trailing P/E. Non-GAAP Q4 EPS of $0.83 beat consensus by 4.8%. The franchise model supports high incremental margins on royalty revenue, but the company's overall profitability is constrained by interest expense on its $1.7B net debt load.

**Balance Sheet — The Central Concern:** Net debt of $1.7B at 3.3x net debt/EBITDA is the most significant financial risk. A Seeking Alpha analysis from February 21 specifically flagged this, noting that on an FCF basis, the leverage ratio is approximately 8x. In the current rate environment (10-year yields elevated, bond safe-haven status questioned), refinancing this debt becomes more expensive. Planet Fitness carries no dividend, which is appropriate given the leverage, but it limits the shareholder return toolkit to buybacks funded by operating cash flow after debt service.

**Cash Flow:** The franchise model should generate consistent FCF given low capex requirements for company-operated infrastructure. However, with FCF/net debt at roughly 8x per the Seeking Alpha analysis, deleveraging will be slow absent significant membership or pricing gains.

## Growth Analysis

**Member Growth:** 20.8M members represents continued expansion of the installed base. The low monthly price point ($10-15) provides resilience during consumer downturns relative to premium gym competitors charging $50-200/month. GLP-1 weight-loss drug adoption could actually benefit Planet Fitness by bringing health-conscious consumers who want to complement pharmaceutical weight loss with exercise, though this is a contested thesis.

**Unit Growth:** Nearly 2,900 clubs globally with continued expansion planned. International represents a long runway but requires capital from franchisees willing to invest, which becomes harder in a stagflationary environment where small business lending tightens and consumer spending contracts.

**2026 Guidance:** ~9% revenue growth target. In the context of the current macro regime (38-42% recession probability, consumer spending in active contraction phase, gasoline up 21% in a month squeezing discretionary budgets), even this target carries execution risk. Gym memberships are among the first discretionary expenses consumers cut when budgets tighten — despite the low absolute dollar amount, the "I'll just run outside" substitution effect is real.

**AI-Driven Retention:** Management highlighted "AI-driven member retention initiatives" in February 2026 guidance. This is a single data point with no measurable impact yet. Monitor for concrete metrics in Q1 2026 reporting.

## Valuation Assessment

At $75.30 with trailing EPS of $2.62, the stock trades at ~28.7x earnings. This is the same as the forward P/E listed (28.74x), suggesting flat or marginal EPS growth expected for 2026 — consistent with 9% revenue growth partially offset by interest expense pressure.

**Valuation context:** For a franchise business carrying 3.3x net debt/EBITDA, 28.7x earnings is expensive. Comparable asset-light franchise models (YUM, QSR, DPZ) trade at similar or lower multiples with more manageable leverage profiles. The 35% drawdown has compressed the multiple from what was likely 40-45x at peak, but the stock has not reached a level that adequately compensates for the combination of leverage risk, litigation overhang, and management turnover.

**Entry Framework:** At ~22-24x forward earnings (~$58-63 per share assuming $2.62-2.65 EPS), the risk/reward would become more attractive, pricing in the litigation and leverage risks while still giving credit to the franchise model's durability. Current price does not offer sufficient margin of safety.

Jefferies' $175 target (maintained March 9) implies roughly 38-40x forward earnings, which requires sustained double-digit earnings growth and assumes the litigation/CFO issues resolve cleanly. This target appears disconnected from the current risk profile.

## Competitive Landscape

Planet Fitness holds the strongest competitive position in the value gym segment. Key advantages:

1. **Scale:** 2,900 clubs and 20.8M members create network effects in brand recognition and real estate negotiation leverage.
2. **Price anchor:** The $10/month entry point is difficult for competitors to undercut profitably, creating a durable floor on market share.
3. **Franchise economics:** Franchisees bear the capex and operating risk of individual locations; Planet Fitness extracts royalties with minimal capital at risk.
4. **Brand recognition:** "Judgement Free Zone" positioning differentiates from intimidation-factor concerns that keep casual consumers from traditional gyms.

**Competitive risks:** Home fitness (Peloton, free YouTube workouts) provides zero-cost substitution. Boutique fitness (Barry's, Orangetheory) competes at the higher end but doesn't directly overlap with Planet Fitness's core demographic. The more relevant competitive threat in a downturn is the "do nothing" option — consumers deciding they don't need a gym at all.

## Risk Assessment

**1. Securities Fraud Investigation (HIGH IMPACT, UNCERTAIN PROBABILITY)**
The Schall Law Firm announced a fraud investigation on March 14, 2026, investigating "violations of the securities laws." Ambulance-chasing law firms file these investigations routinely after stock drops, and most amount to nothing. However, coupled with the CFO departure and the stock's continued decline post-earnings, this creates a persistent overhang. Key question: was there a material misrepresentation in pre-earnings disclosures? The gap between "beat expectations" Q4 results and the stock's 9% decline on earnings day suggests the market was surprised by something in the guidance or balance sheet detail.

**2. CFO Turnover (MODERATE-HIGH)**
Jay Stasz's departure and replacement by interim CFO Tom Fitzgerald (former CFO returning) is a yellow flag. CFO departures at companies with $1.7B in net debt warrant scrutiny. The appointment of a predecessor as interim suggests this may be a planned transition, but the timing — immediately after reporting annual results — raises questions.

**3. Leverage Risk (HIGH)**
$1.7B net debt at 3.3x EBITDA in a rising-rate environment. If refinancing windows narrow due to credit market stress (55-60% cascade probability per the macro model), Planet Fitness could face materially higher interest costs on its next debt maturity.

**4. Consumer Spending Contraction (MODERATE)**
The macro regime features active consumer contraction: January retail sales -0.2%, gasoline up 21%, unemployment at 4.4%. Planet Fitness's low price point provides relative insulation, but it does not make the company immune. Membership cancellations tend to lag economic deterioration by 2-3 quarters as consumers initially maintain habits before cutting back.

**5. Stagflationary Macro (MODERATE)**
Diesel at $5/gallon and inflation tracking toward 3.2-5.2% CPI means real wages are negative for many Planet Fitness customers, who skew toward lower-income demographics most sensitive to energy price increases.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- Asset-light franchise model generates durable royalty income with high incremental margins
- 20.8M member base at $10-15/month price point provides relative resilience in consumer downturn vs. premium competitors
- 35% drawdown from highs already prices in meaningful pessimism
- Jefferies $175 target implies substantial upside if litigation/CFO issues prove transient
- International expansion runway remains long
- GLP-1 adoption may increase gym participation as newly health-conscious consumers seek complementary fitness

### Bear Case
- $1.7B net debt at 3.3x EBITDA (8x on FCF basis) is dangerous in a tightening credit market
- Securities fraud investigation creates legal overhang and may reveal undisclosed issues
- CFO departure timing raises governance questions
- 2026 guidance deceleration (9% vs. 10.5% in Q4) signals management sees headwinds
- Stagflationary macro compresses discretionary spending; gym memberships are among earliest cuts
- 28.7x earnings remains expensive for this risk profile — stock could trade to 22-24x in a risk-off environment, implying further 15-20% downside
- No insider buying at current levels despite the drawdown

## Conclusion

Planet Fitness possesses a genuinely differentiated franchise model with strong competitive positioning in the value gym segment. The 20.8M member base and nearly 2,900-club footprint represent meaningful barriers to entry. However, the combination of elevated leverage ($1.7B net debt), active securities litigation, CFO departure, decelerating growth guidance, and a stagflationary macro environment that pressures the company's core low-income demographic creates a risk profile that the current 28.7x multiple does not adequately compensate for.

The stock has fallen 35% from its high but has not reached a valuation level where the risk/reward is compelling. At ~22-24x forward earnings ($58-63 range), the franchise model's durability and the resolution of near-term overhangs would make this an attractive entry. At $75, downside risk from litigation developments, credit market stress, or consumer spending deterioration outweighs the probability of a near-term re-rating higher.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** Monitor for: (1) resolution or escalation of the Schall Law securities investigation, (2) permanent CFO appointment and any related disclosures, (3) Q1 2026 same-club sales trends for early signs of consumer pullback, (4) debt maturity schedule and refinancing activity. Consider upgrading to BUY if the stock reaches the $58-63 range or if the litigation overhang clears with no material findings.
