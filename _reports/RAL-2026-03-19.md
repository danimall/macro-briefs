## Executive Summary

The negative earnings are primarily attributable to a $1.4B non-cash goodwill impairment charge in the Test & Measurement segment related to the EA Elektro-Automatik acquisition. Q4 2025 revenue of $555M beat guidance but grew only 1% YoY, with highly uneven segment performance — Communications grew 29% while other segments apparently lagged.

## Conclusion

Ralliant Corp presents a highly uncertain risk/reward profile. The 29% Communications segment growth is genuinely attractive, and the stock's 25% decline from highs creates a more favorable entry point. However, the combination of (1) a $1.4B goodwill impairment signaling acquisition misjudgment, (2) active securities litigation, (3) consolidated revenue growth of only 1%, (4) negative forward earnings, (5) net insider selling, and (6) complete absence of financial statement data makes a BUY recommendation impossible at this time.

The activist involvement from Irenic Capital could be a positive catalyst if it drives cost discipline and capital return, but it also introduces governance uncertainty. The analyst target of $48.35 implies 13% upside, which is insufficient compensation for the litigation risk and fundamental opacity.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The stock requires resolution of at least two conditions before upgrade consideration: (1) next quarterly earnings demonstrating consolidated revenue growth acceleration and positive adjusted EPS, and (2) clarity on litigation trajectory and balance sheet health. In the current stagflationary macro environment with rising real yields, loss-making industrial tech companies with active litigation face an uphill battle for multiple expansion.
