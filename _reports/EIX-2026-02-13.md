## Executive Summary

Edison International, the parent company of Southern California Edison (SCE), is one of the largest electric utility holding companies in the United States, serving approximately 5 million customer accounts across a vast Southern California service territory. As of mid-February 2026, the stock trades at approximately $69.08, representing a market capitalization of roughly $26.6 billion. The company offers an attractive trailing P/E of ~8.3x and a forward P/E of ~17.1x, alongside a robust dividend yield of approximately 5.2%.

However, the investment thesis for EIX is heavily influenced by the ongoing wildfire liability exposure that has plagued the company for several years. The January 2025 Los Angeles-area wildfires resulted in significant property destruction and loss of life, and SCE is navigating complex settlements and securitization strategies to manage associated costs. Morgan Stanley recently raised its price target to $61 but maintained an Underweight rating, reflecting the tension between the company's underlying utility earnings power and its extraordinary risk profile. UBS, by contrast, reaffirmed a Buy rating with a $70 price target, citing deleveraging progress.

The divergence in analyst sentiment underscores the core challenge: EIX offers attractive yield and value metrics for income-oriented investors willing to accept elevated risk, but the wildfire liability overhang creates meaningful uncertainty around book value, earnings trajectory, and capital allocation flexibility. Investors must weigh the regulated utility's steady earnings base against the tail risks that are unique to California's fire-prone utility landscape.

## Company Overview

Edison International is headquartered in Rosemead, California, and operates primarily through its subsidiary Southern California Edison (SCE), a regulated electric utility. SCE generates, transmits, and distributes electricity to a service territory spanning approximately 50,000 square miles across central, coastal, and southern California. The company does not operate a natural gas utility, distinguishing it from some diversified peers.

Key characteristics:
- **Sector:** Utilities
- **Market Cap:** ~$26.6 billion
- **Beta:** 0.82 (lower than market average, consistent with utility sector)
- **52-Week Range:** $47.73 – $67.21
- **Current Price:** $69.08 (above 52-week high, suggesting recent positive momentum)
- **Dividend Yield:** ~5.2%
- **Forward P/E:** 17.1x | **Trailing P/E:** 8.3x

The significant gap between trailing P/E (8.3x) and forward P/E (17.1x) suggests that trailing earnings were inflated by non-recurring items or that forward earnings expectations incorporate significant charges or lower profitability—a pattern consistent with wildfire-related accounting adjustments.

## Financial Analysis

### Earnings and Valuation Metrics

The trailing P/E of 8.3x is notably low for a regulated utility, where peer companies typically trade at 15–20x trailing earnings. This suggests that trailing twelve-month earnings included significant one-time gains, possibly from insurance recoveries, asset sales, or favorable accounting treatments related to wildfire cost recovery. The forward P/E of 17.1x is more in line with utility sector averages, indicating that normalized earnings expectations are more modest.

### Dividend Profile

The dividend yield of approximately 5.2% is compelling and sits above the utility sector median (~3.5–4.0%). This elevated yield reflects both the company's commitment to shareholder returns and the market's pricing-in of risk. Edison International has been identified as a "Dividend Contender" and was named a Top 10 energy dividend stock by Dividend Channel with a yield cited as high as 5.84% at certain points in late 2025.

### Balance Sheet Considerations

While detailed financial statements were not available in the data provided, the broader narrative from news sources indicates that Edison International has been actively pursuing **deleveraging initiatives** and **wildfire cost securitization**. UBS analyst Gregg Orrill highlighted these efforts as a key reason for maintaining a Buy rating. Securitization of wildfire costs allows the company to spread liabilities over time through ratepayer-backed bonds, reducing near-term balance sheet pressure.

### Capital Structure

Utilities are inherently capital-intensive, and EIX faces additional capital demands from:
- Grid hardening and wildfire mitigation infrastructure
- Clean energy transition investments
- Settlement and legal costs from wildfire liabilities

The company's beta of 0.82 reflects lower-than-market volatility, typical for utilities, though EIX's beta has likely been elevated relative to peers due to wildfire event risk.

## Growth Analysis

### Regulatory Rate Base Growth

Edison International's growth is primarily driven by regulated rate base expansion at SCE. The utility has substantial ongoing capital expenditure programs focused on:

1. **Grid modernization and wildfire mitigation** — Undergrounding power lines, installing covered conductors, deploying advanced sensors and monitoring technology
2. **Clean energy transition** — California's aggressive renewable energy mandates (100% clean electricity by 2045) require significant transmission and distribution infrastructure investment
3. **Electrification** — Growing adoption of electric vehicles and building electrification in California drives load growth

### Earnings Growth Outlook

Zacks identified EIX as a "Strong Growth Stock" in late December 2025, suggesting consensus expectations for above-average EPS growth. Utility sector growth rates typically range from 5–8% annually based on rate base expansion. EIX's growth may be at the higher end given California's ambitious clean energy policies, though this is partially offset by regulatory lag and wildfire-related disruptions.

### Market Expansion

EIX's growth is geographically limited to its Southern California service territory—it does not have the geographic diversification of multi-state utility holding companies like NextEra Energy or Duke Energy. However, Southern California is one of the largest and wealthiest electricity markets in the U.S., providing a strong demand foundation.

## Competitive Landscape

Edison International, through SCE, is one of the largest investor-owned electric utilities in the United States. Its primary competitive advantages include:

- **Regulated monopoly:** SCE operates as a regulated monopoly in its service territory, providing predictable revenue streams
- **Essential service:** Electricity demand is relatively inelastic
- **California market:** Serving one of the most populous and economically dynamic states

### Peer Comparison

Key peers include:

- **Sempra (SRE):** Parent of San Diego Gas & Electric; also California-exposed but more diversified
- **NextEra Energy (NEE):** Premium-valued utility with cleaner risk profile
- **Duke Energy (DUK):** Large regulated utility in southeastern U.S.

EIX's competitive position is solid from a market fundamentals perspective but is undermined by the unique wildfire risk premium that California utilities bear. The company's beta of 0.82 is notably higher than typical regulated utilities (0.4–0.6), reflecting this elevated risk.

### Moat Assessment

EIX has a **narrow to moderate moat** based on its regulated monopoly status and the essential nature of its services. However, the moat is narrowed by:
- Regulatory risk from California's complex regulatory environment
- Political risk from wildfire liability frameworks
- Emerging distributed energy competition (rooftop solar, battery storage)

## Risk Assessment

### Critical Risks

1. **Wildfire Liability (SEVERE):** This is the dominant risk factor. The January 2025 LA-area wildfires destroyed thousands of homes and resulted in fatalities. SCE has committed to paying "several million dollars each to families who lost loved ones," with total liabilities potentially reaching billions. California's inverse condemnation doctrine holds utilities strictly liable for fire damages caused by their equipment, regardless of negligence.

2. **Regulatory/Political Risk (HIGH):** California's regulatory environment is complex and politically charged. Rate recovery for wildfire costs is not guaranteed, and regulatory proceedings can be prolonged and contentious.

3. **Climate Change/Drought Risk (HIGH):** Climate change is increasing the frequency and severity of wildfires in California. Extended drought, Santa Ana winds, and urban-wildland interface expansion create a structural increase in fire risk.

4. **Interest Rate Sensitivity (MODERATE):** As a capital-intensive, dividend-paying utility, EIX is sensitive to interest rate movements. Higher rates increase borrowing costs and make the dividend yield less competitive relative to fixed-income alternatives.

5. **Balance Sheet Leverage (MODERATE):** Wildfire liabilities and ongoing capital investments strain the balance sheet. The deleveraging strategy is positive, but execution risk remains.

6. **Concentration Risk (MODERATE):** Unlike multi-state utility holding companies, EIX operates solely in Southern California, concentrating geographic, regulatory, and climate risks.

### ESG Considerations

EIX faces a complex ESG profile. On the positive side, California's clean energy mandates position SCE as a leader in renewable energy procurement and grid decarbonization. On the negative side, wildfire events create environmental destruction and raise governance questions about infrastructure maintenance and risk management.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case

- **Attractive yield:** At ~5.2%, EIX offers one of the highest dividend yields in the utility sector, providing substantial income support
- **Normalized valuation is reasonable:** Forward P/E of 17.1x is in line with peers, and the stock may re-rate higher as wildfire liabilities are resolved
- **Deleveraging progress:** Wildfire cost securitization provides a path to balance sheet repair without dilutive equity issuances
- **Clean energy tailwinds:** California's ambitious clean energy policies drive long-term rate base growth
- **Mean reversion potential:** If wildfire risk perception improves (through grid hardening results, favorable legislation, or benign fire seasons), the risk premium could contract significantly, driving stock appreciation
- **Stock trading above 52-week high:** Recent momentum of +3% suggests improving market sentiment

### Bear Case

- **Perpetual wildfire risk:** Climate change and California's fire-prone geography create an ongoing, potentially escalating liability risk that cannot be fully mitigated
- **Regulatory uncertainty:** Cost recovery for wildfire liabilities is subject to complex regulatory proceedings; adverse outcomes could materially impact earnings and dividends
- **Morgan Stanley Underweight:** Experienced utility analysts maintain a cautious stance with a $61 price target, implying ~12% downside from current levels
- **Concentration risk:** Single-state, single-utility exposure limits diversification benefits
- **Rising interest rates:** If rates remain elevated, the dividend yield advantage narrows and borrowing costs increase
- **Earnings quality concerns:** The wide gap between trailing (8.3x) and forward (17.1x) P/E suggests earnings volatility and potential for negative surprises

## Conclusion

Edison International presents a classic high-yield, moderate-risk utility investment with an outsized wildfire risk premium. The ~5.2% dividend yield and forward P/E of 17.1x offer reasonable value for income-oriented investors, and the company's deleveraging and securitization strategy provides a credible path to improved financial health. However, the wildfire liability overhang remains a significant and potentially recurring risk that distinguishes EIX from lower-risk utility peers.

**Overall Recommendation: HOLD / Neutral**

The stock is appropriate for risk-tolerant income investors who are willing to accept wildfire-related volatility in exchange for an above-average dividend yield. The recent price strength (trading above the prior 52-week high) suggests the market is becoming more constructive, but the divergent analyst views (UBS Buy at $70 vs. Morgan Stanley Underweight at $61) highlight the ongoing uncertainty. Investors should monitor wildfire season developments, regulatory proceedings, and balance sheet deleveraging progress as key catalysts. Position sizing should reflect the elevated risk profile relative to typical utility investments.

**Overall Score: 5.6 / 10** — Reflecting a fairly valued utility with attractive income characteristics offset by above-average risk factors.
