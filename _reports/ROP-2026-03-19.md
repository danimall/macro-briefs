## Competitive Landscape

Roper's competitive advantage is structural and durable:

1. **Niche vertical software monopolies:** Each portfolio company serves a specific vertical with high switching costs. Legal practice management, freight matching, insurance underwriting — these are deeply embedded workflow tools where replacement cost and disruption risk far exceed annual subscription fees.

2. **Decentralized operating model:** Unlike traditional conglomerates, Roper gives portfolio companies operational autonomy, retaining entrepreneurial culture while extracting capital allocation efficiency at the parent level.

3. **Recurring revenue:** Estimated 70-80%+ of revenue is recurring (SaaS, maintenance, subscription). This provides visibility and reduces cyclical exposure relative to project-based or transactional models.

4. **Capital-light business model:** Software businesses require minimal capex, enabling Roper to convert a high percentage of EBITDA to free cash flow.

The primary competitive risk is valuation discipline in acquisitions. If Roper overpays for targets (as multiples for vertical SaaS businesses have expanded significantly over the past five years), returns on deployed capital will compress.
