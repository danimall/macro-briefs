## Executive Summary

BancFirst Corp is an Oklahoma-based bank holding company with a market capitalization of ~$3.4 billion, trading at $103.36 as of March 18, 2026. The stock trades at approximately 14.5x both trailing and forward earnings with an EPS of $7.11 and a dividend yield of ~1.85%. The stock has declined roughly 25% from its 52-week high of $138.77 reached in early September 2025, underperforming the broader market during a period of heightened macro uncertainty.

The fundamental challenge for BANF is its position as a community bank concentrated in Oklahoma during a stagflationary environment where the Fed has now discussed rate hikes. Rising rates would compress net interest margins if deposit costs reprice faster than loan yields, while simultaneously increasing credit risk in an energy-dependent state. Oil at $119/bbl is directionally positive for Oklahoma's economy, but the volatility and recession risk (40-45% probability) create offsetting headwinds for loan quality.

The data available is limited — financial statements are empty in the provided dataset, meaning this analysis relies on profile metrics, price action, insider transactions, and SEC filing cadence. This constrains the depth of financial analysis possible and contributes to a cautious HOLD recommendation.

## Company Overview

Services include commercial, real estate, agricultural, and consumer lending, along with depository services, funds transfer, and collections. The company files 10-K and 10-Q reports on a regular cadence, with the most recent 10-K filed February 26, 2026, covering fiscal year 2025. The last reported earnings date was January 22, 2026.

## Growth Analysis

**Growth signals are limited:**

1. **Forward vs. trailing P/E convergence:** Both at 14.54x, indicating consensus expects EPS to remain roughly flat at ~$7.11. For a community bank, this is not unusual — these are slow-growth, dividend-yielding franchises.

2. **Oklahoma economic sensitivity:** Oklahoma's economy is heavily tied to energy. Oil at $119/bbl supports drilling activity, employment, and commercial loan demand in the state. However, the current geopolitical volatility creating this price level also introduces tail risks (rapid price declines if conflict de-escalates, or demand destruction if recession materializes).

3. **Acquisition history:** BancFirst has historically grown through community bank acquisitions in Oklahoma. The SEC filing cadence (annual 10-Ks every February) is consistent and unremarkable.

4. **Rate environment implications:** The FOMC's discussion of rate hikes is a negative for bank growth prospects. Higher rates typically benefit banks initially through wider NIM, but at this stage of the cycle — with deposit costs already elevated and competition for deposits fierce among community banks — further rate increases may compress NIM rather than expand it. Additionally, higher rates increase the probability of loan losses as borrowers face higher refinancing costs.

## Valuation Assessment

At 14.5x earnings and 1.85% dividend yield, BANF is modestly valued relative to its own historical range but not cheap enough to provide a margin of safety in the current environment.

**Peer context:** Community and regional banks in the $3-5 billion market cap range typically trade at 10-16x earnings. BANF is mid-range. Oklahoma-concentrated banks arguably deserve a discount to diversified peers given single-state economic exposure, though the energy tailwind partially offsets this.

**Price action context:** The stock peaked at $138.77 in early September 2025 and has declined to $103.36 — a 25.5% drawdown. The decline accelerated in October 2025 (from ~$125 to ~$109) and again in March 2026 (from ~$113 to ~$103). ## Competitive Landscape

BancFirst is the largest state-chartered bank headquartered in Oklahoma, giving it a local franchise advantage. Community banks compete on relationship lending, local knowledge, and branch convenience rather than scale or technology.

**Strengths:** Dominant local brand, deep agricultural and energy lending expertise, relationship-driven deposit base that tends to be stickier than national banks.

**Weaknesses:** Single-state concentration (Oklahoma), limited diversification across industries and geographies, smaller scale means higher per-unit technology and compliance costs relative to money-center banks.

The competitive position is stable but unremarkable. There is no identifiable moat widening or narrowing.

For a bank with a $3.4B market cap concentrated in community banking, the impact depends on the composition of its loan and deposit book. **3. Credit cycle risk (MODERATE):** Recession probability at 40-45% with NFP at -92K. Community banks are disproportionately exposed to small business and agricultural borrowers who tend to be more cyclically sensitive. Provisions for credit losses would likely increase.

BANF is a reasonably valued community bank franchise in Oklahoma with stable operations but no clear catalyst for appreciation. The 14.5x P/E and 1.85% yield provide modest compensation for holding a single-state bank through a stagflationary environment where rate hikes are now on the table.

The absence of detailed financial data — no income statement, balance sheet, or cash flow information — prevents a higher-conviction assessment. **Recommendation: HOLD.** The valuation is not compelling enough to warrant a BUY given the macro risks (rate hike potential, recession probability, energy volatility), and the stock is not deteriorating fast enough or facing structural enough problems to warrant an AVOID. Investors already positioned should hold for the dividend and wait for Q1 2026 earnings clarity. New investors should wait for either (a) a price below $95, providing a more meaningful margin of safety, or (b) evidence of NIM stability in upcoming quarterly results.
