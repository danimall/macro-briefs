## Executive Summary

TG Therapeutics is a commercial-stage biopharmaceutical company whose business is almost entirely driven by BRIUMVI (ublituximab), an anti-CD20 monoclonal antibody approved for relapsing forms of multiple sclerosis. Full year 2025 revenue reached $616.3 million, with Q4 2025 revenue of $192.6 million. Management guided 2026 total global revenue to $875-900 million, implying approximately 42-46% year-over-year growth. The company reported Q4 2025 net income of $23 million ($0.14/share) and trailing twelve-month EPS of $2.77, putting the stock at roughly 10x trailing and forward earnings at the current price of ~$28.

The stock has declined approximately 39% from its 52-week high of $46.48, creating a valuation entry point that is unusual for a biotech growing revenue at 40%+ annually. Analyst opinion is sharply divided: Goldman Sachs holds a Neutral rating with a $39 target, while Bank of America maintains a Sell with a $15 target. This divergence reflects the core analytical tension — whether BRIUMVI's commercial trajectory will sustain growth against entrenched competitors (Ocrevus from Roche) and whether the pipeline (azer-cel for progressive MS) provides adequate diversification. At 10x earnings with 42% guided revenue growth, the risk/reward skews favorably for investors willing to accept single-product concentration risk.

## Company Overview

TG Therapeutics, headquartered in Morrisville, North Carolina, is focused on the development and commercialization of treatments for B-cell diseases, primarily multiple sclerosis. BRIUMVI received FDA approval in December 2022 and has been in a rapid commercial ramp since launch. The company's pipeline includes azercabtagene zapreleucel (azer-cel), a CAR-T therapy licensed from Precision BioSciences for progressive multiple sclerosis — a milestone payment of $7 million was recently triggered in early March 2026 upon reaching a clinical milestone.

The company has approximately 147.7 million shares outstanding, producing a market capitalization of approximately $4.2 billion at the current price. It trades on NASDAQ.

## Growth Analysis

### BRIUMVI Commercial Ramp

BRIUMVI's growth trajectory is the central investment thesis. Going from $616M to $875-900M represents 42-46% growth, driven by:

1. **US market share gains** against Ocrevus (ocrelizumab, Roche), which generated ~$7.6B globally in 2024. BRIUMVI offers a faster infusion time (1 hour vs. Ocrevus's 3.5-5 hours for loading doses) as its primary commercial differentiation.
2. **International expansion** — the European approval of BRIUMVI occurred in 2024, and revenue contribution appears early-stage based on the US/total revenue gap.
3. **Patient switching** — as more neurologists gain experience with the product.

The guidance implies ~$220M average quarterly revenue in 2026, versus $192.6M in Q4 2025 — a reasonable glide path that does not require a step-function change.

### Pipeline Optionality

The azer-cel program (CAR-T for progressive MS) represents genuine pipeline optionality in an indication with limited effective treatments. The $7M milestone payment to Precision BioSciences in March 2026 confirms clinical progress. This is early-stage, likely years from commercialization, but addresses a large unmet medical need and could substantially expand TGTX's addressable market beyond relapsing MS.

## Valuation Assessment

At $28.48 per share:
- **Trailing P/E:** 10.1x ($2.77 EPS)
- **Forward P/E:** 10.1x (per Yahoo Finance data)
- **Market cap:** $4.2 billion
- **Market cap / 2026 guided revenue:** ~4.7x at midpoint ($887.5M)

For a biotech growing revenue at 42%+ annually, 10x earnings is cheap by sector standards. Comparable commercial-stage biotechs with single-product revenue in the $500M-$1B range typically trade at 15-25x forward earnings. The discount reflects: (1) single-product concentration risk, (2) the BofA $15 Sell rating creating a valuation overhang, (3) broader market selloff in biotech, and (4) questions about the durability of current margins.

If the company hits $900M revenue in 2026 with a 35% net margin (conservative relative to the implied FY2025 margin), that produces approximately $315M net income, or roughly $2.13/share. At 15x, fair value would be ~$32. At 20x, ~$43. The current price of $28 prices in limited growth beyond 2026 and assumes some margin of safety for execution risk.

The 39% decline from the $46.48 high creates a more favorable entry than existed three months ago. The stock touched $25.28 (52-week low) in August 2025, suggesting current levels are in the lower quartile of the recent trading range.

## Competitive Landscape

BRIUMVI competes in the anti-CD20 MS market, which is dominated by Roche's Ocrevus. Key competitive dynamics:

- **Ocrevus** has first-mover advantage, deep neurologist relationships, and broad payer coverage. Annual sales of ~$7.6B dwarf BRIUMVI's $594M, but this also means TGTX is taking share in a very large pool.
- **BRIUMVI's differentiation** is primarily infusion convenience (1-hour infusion after loading doses) and comparable efficacy data from the ULTIMATE I and II trials.
- **Kesimpta** (ofatumumab, Novartis) is a subcutaneous anti-CD20 that competes on at-home administration convenience.
- **Pipeline competition** from BTK inhibitors (tolebrutinib, fenebrutinib) could reshape the MS treatment landscape if approved, potentially compressing the addressable market for all anti-CD20 therapies.

TGTX's competitive position is viable but narrow. It depends on continued execution in a market where Roche has decades of neurologist relationships and Novartis offers a different convenience vector.

## Risk Assessment

### Product Concentration (HIGH)
Essentially 100% of revenue comes from BRIUMVI. Any safety signal, manufacturing issue, payer pushback, or competitive disruption would have an outsized impact. This is the single most important risk factor.

### Analyst Divergence
Goldman Sachs at $39 (Neutral) and Bank of America at $15 (Sell) represents an extraordinarily wide spread — nearly 3:1. BofA's bearish thesis likely centers on sustainability of margins, competitive pressure, or concerns about whether the revenue trajectory can be maintained. This divergence creates volatility and limits institutional consensus buying.

### Insider Activity
CEO Michael Weiss gifted 4.67M shares in November 2025 — a large block transfer that, while not a sale, reduces his direct holdings. Director sales have been small ($162K and $369K). The CEO's January 2026 grant of 622K shares maintains alignment. No aggressive insider selling pattern, but the gift transactions are notable for their size.

### Macro Sensitivity
Biotech stocks with real earnings are relatively insulated from the current stagflationary macro environment. TGTX has no commodity input exposure, no tariff risk, and limited sensitivity to consumer spending cycles. Healthcare spending is non-discretionary. However, rising interest rates increase the discount rate applied to growth companies, which partially explains the multiple compression.

### Short Interest
The stock was flagged as one of the "10 most shorted biotech stocks" per hedge fund data — elevated short interest creates both downside pressure and potential for sharp short squeezes (the January 14, 2026 price action — opening at $31.16 with an intraday range of $28.45-$33.24 on 8.7M volume — may reflect short covering).

### Pipeline Risk
The azer-cel program is early-stage. Failure would eliminate the primary pipeline diversification optionality, further concentrating the investment thesis on BRIUMVI alone.

The risk profile is concentrated — a single product, a divided analyst community, and elevated short interest — but the valuation already reflects substantial skepticism. A company growing revenue at 42% does not typically trade at 10x earnings unless the market is pricing in either margin compression or growth deceleration. The question is whether that skepticism is warranted or overdone.
