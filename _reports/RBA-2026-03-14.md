## Executive Summary

RB Global is the world's largest auctioneer of used heavy equipment, trucks, and other industrial assets, operating through the Ritchie Bros. and IAA platforms following the 2023 merger. The company reported Q4 2025 EPS of $1.11, beating the $0.97 consensus by 14.4%, with revenue up $61M year-over-year. Management guided 2026 gross transaction value (GTV) growth of 5-8% and adjusted EBITDA of $1.47-1.53B, while announcing a $500M share repurchase program and the acquisition of BigIron to expand into U.S. agricultural equipment auctions.

At $98.68, the stock trades at approximately 42x trailing earnings — a premium multiple that embeds significant growth expectations. The ~40% decline from its 52-week high of $164.85 reflects both the broader market correction and concerns about the cyclical sensitivity of used equipment transaction volumes in a deteriorating macro environment. With GDP at 0.7%, NFP at -92K, and oil above $100, the construction and transportation end markets that drive RBA's GTV are vulnerable to contraction, making the current valuation difficult to justify on near-term fundamentals.

## Company Overview

RB Global operates a multi-channel marketplace for used heavy equipment, trucks, and automotive salvage vehicles. The business model is predominantly commission-based: the company earns fees on transactions without taking inventory risk on most of its volume. Key segments include:

- **Ritchie Bros. Auctioneers**: Industrial equipment auctions (construction, mining, agriculture, transportation)
- **IAA (Insurance Auto Auctions)**: Salvage vehicle auctions for insurance companies, acquired in 2023
- **SmartEquip/Rouse**: Data analytics and parts procurement services

The company is dual-listed on NYSE and TSX. Market cap stands at $24.8B. Beta of 0.75 indicates lower-than-market volatility, consistent with the platform/marketplace business model where revenue is partially insulated from asset price fluctuations.

## Growth Analysis

**Positive signals:**
- 5-8% GTV growth guidance for 2026 implies continued market share gains across industrial equipment and automotive segments
- BigIron acquisition opens the U.S. agricultural equipment market, a fragmented sector where RBA's digital platform and brand have competitive advantages
- Renewed automotive contracts with improved terms provide revenue visibility
- AI-driven initiatives mentioned in earnings call (pricing optimization, inspection automation)

**Growth constraints in current macro:**
- Construction equipment volumes are cyclically tied to construction starts, which correlate with GDP growth. At 0.7% GDP and contracting employment, new equipment purchases slow → fewer used units entering the secondary market within 12-18 months
- Transportation equipment (trucks) faces the same dynamic; freight volumes contract during recession
- Oil above $100 raises operating costs for equipment-heavy industries, potentially delaying equipment replacement cycles
- The 5-8% GTV growth target may prove aggressive if recession materializes in Q1-Q2 2026 as estimated at 38-42% probability

**Counter-cyclical offset:** During recessions, fleet liquidations increase as companies fail or downsize, which can increase auction volumes even as per-unit prices decline. RBA's commission model means it earns on transaction volume regardless of price direction, though dollar-value-based commissions do decline with lower prices.

## Valuation Assessment

At 42x trailing earnings and 42x forward earnings, RBA is priced for sustained high-single-digit to low-double-digit earnings growth. The stock at $98.68 sits 40% below its 52-week high of $164.85, which suggests the market has already repriced downward but may not be finished.

**Valuation context:**
- The 42x P/E is a premium to the broader market (S&P 500 ~20x) and to most commercial services peers
- The premium is partially justified by the asset-light marketplace model, recurring revenue characteristics, and dominant market position
- However, 42x requires ~$2.35/share in forward earnings to justify at current price. Given the GAAP/adjusted gap, the actual GAAP earnings power may be closer to $2.35, making the adjusted multiple lower but the GAAP multiple stretched
- The $500M buyback (~2% of market cap) provides modest support but is insufficient to materially move EPS at this valuation

**Downside scenario:** If recession drives GTV growth to 0-2% instead of 5-8%, and EBITDA comes in at $1.35-1.40B instead of $1.47-1.53B, a 15-17x EV/EBITDA multiple (recession discount) implies 15-25% downside from current levels.

**Upside scenario:** If macro stabilizes and GTV growth hits the high end (8%+), with continued margin expansion from IAA synergies and digital initiatives, EPS could reach $3.50-4.00 by 2027. At 30x forward P/E, that implies $105-120.

The risk-reward skew is roughly symmetric to slightly negative at current prices, which argues against initiating a position.

## Competitive Landscape

RBA's competitive position is its strongest attribute:

- **Global leader in used heavy equipment auctions** with no comparable-scale competitor. The Ritchie Bros. brand has decades of trust with buyers and sellers
- **IAA merger created a dual-vertical platform** spanning industrial equipment and automotive salvage, with potential for cross-selling data and services
- **Network effects**: More buyers attract more sellers, which attracts more buyers. Auction platforms exhibit natural monopoly tendencies within geographic and asset-class verticals
- **Digital transformation**: RBA has shifted substantially to online/hybrid auctions, reducing geographic constraints and expanding the buyer base globally
- **Data moat**: Rouse analytics provides proprietary pricing data used by lenders and equipment owners, creating switching costs

Competitors include Euro Auctions (smaller, regional), Copart (automotive salvage, direct IAA competitor), and various regional equipment dealers. Copart is the primary competitive threat in the auto salvage segment.

## Risk Assessment

**Macro/cyclical risk (HIGH):** The current stagflationary environment directly threatens RBA's growth trajectory. Construction spending, trucking volumes, and equipment replacement cycles all correlate with GDP. A 38-42% recession probability and $100+ oil create meaningful headwinds to the 5-8% GTV growth target.

**Acquisition integration risk (MODERATE):** The IAA merger was completed in 2023 and the GAAP/adjusted EPS gap suggests significant ongoing integration costs. Adding BigIron introduces additional execution risk, though the deal appears small relative to the overall business.

**Leverage risk (MODERATE):** The IAA acquisition was debt-financed. Specific debt figures are unavailable in the provided data, but elevated leverage in a rising-rate environment increases financial risk. The $500M buyback could compete with debt reduction priorities.

**Valuation risk (HIGH):** At 42x earnings, the stock has limited margin of safety. Any guidance miss or macro deterioration could trigger disproportionate selling given the premium multiple.

**Currency risk (LOW-MODERATE):** RBA generates significant Canadian and international revenue. USD strength (typical during risk-off periods) creates translation headwinds.

**Positive risk offset:** Beta of 0.75 and the asset-light marketplace model provide some downside protection relative to asset-heavy industrials. The counter-cyclical liquidation dynamic partially buffers recession impacts.

## Conclusion

RB Global has a strong competitive position as the dominant global platform for used industrial equipment auctions, and Q4 2025 results demonstrated operational execution with a 14% earnings beat. The 2026 guidance, $500M buyback, and BigIron acquisition reflect management confidence in the growth trajectory.

However, at 42x forward earnings in a confirmed stagflationary environment with 38-42% recession probability, the valuation leaves minimal margin of safety. The stock has corrected meaningfully from its highs, but the risk-reward remains roughly balanced. The GAAP/adjusted earnings gap warrants investigation, and the macro sensitivity of GTV growth introduces downside risk to guidance.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The competitive moat and marketplace model are genuinely attractive assets, but the combination of premium valuation, cyclical exposure to deteriorating end markets, and macro uncertainty argues against initiating a new position. Existing holders should maintain positions but monitor Q1 2026 GTV trends closely — a miss on the 5-8% growth target would likely trigger further multiple compression. Accumulation below $85 (roughly 35x estimated 2026 adjusted earnings) would offer a more attractive entry point.
