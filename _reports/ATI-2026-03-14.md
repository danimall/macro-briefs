## Executive Summary

ATI Inc is a specialty materials producer focused on nickel-based alloys, titanium, and other high-performance materials used predominantly in jet engines and aerospace applications. The company reported Q4 2025 adjusted EPS of $0.93 (beating the $0.89 consensus), and full-year 2026 guidance reflects sustained aerospace demand, with consensus expecting approximately 48% EPS growth in 2026. The stock has rallied 45% over three months and roughly 262% from its 52-week low of $39.23, now trading at $141.97 with a market cap of $19.4B.

The core tension is between a genuinely strong demand cycle — jet engine production rates at Boeing and Airbus remain elevated, and ATI's content per engine is increasing through its specialty alloy mix — and a valuation that at ~48.5x trailing earnings already prices in substantial execution. Executive Chairman Robert Wetherbee sold over $12M in stock across February transactions, and CEO Kimberly Fields disposed of shares via tax withholding on large equity grants. The insider activity pattern is consistent with routine diversification following large vesting events but does not signal aggressive conviction in further upside.

Given the elevated valuation, insider selling, and the possibility that the current aerospace upcycle normalizes post-2027 (as flagged by Seeking Alpha), this is a HOLD. The demand thesis is real, but the stock price already reflects it.

## Company Overview

ATI Inc (formerly Allegheny Technologies) produces specialty materials — nickel-based alloys, titanium alloys, and other advanced materials — primarily for aerospace and defense applications. The company operates through two segments: High Performance Materials & Components (HPMC) and Advanced Alloys & Solutions (AA&S). Key end markets include commercial aerospace (jet engines, airframes), defense, energy, and medical. ATI is headquartered in Dallas, TX.

ATI's competitive position derives from its qualification on major engine platforms. Once qualified as a supplier for a specific engine program (e.g., Pratt & Whitney's GTF or GE's LEAP), switching costs are extremely high due to the multi-year certification process for safety-critical components. This creates a quasi-monopolistic position on specific alloy compositions for specific engine variants.

## Growth Analysis

The growth case for ATI rests on three pillars:

1. **Jet engine production ramp:** Boeing and Airbus production rate increases drive direct demand for ATI's nickel superalloys and titanium. The industry is in a multi-year recovery from COVID-era production troughs, with significant backlogs at both OEMs.

2. **Content per engine growth:** ATI has been increasing its share of materials content per engine through advanced alloy development. Newer engine programs (GTF, LEAP, GE9X) use more high-performance alloys per unit, benefiting ATI's revenue per engine shipped.

3. **Defense spending tailwind:** Per the world model, nine independent data points confirm elevated defense spending through multi-year munitions replenishment, and the current Iran conflict has led to ceasefire rejection, extending demand. ATI supplies materials for defense applications including missile components.

**Consensus expects ~48% EPS growth in 2026**, per Seeking Alpha. If Q4 2025 adjusted EPS annualizes to roughly $3.40-3.60, a 48% increase implies ~$5.00-5.30 for 2026. At $141.97, that puts the forward P/E on 2026 consensus at roughly 27-28x — more reasonable than the reported 48.5x trailing figure, but still demanding for a cyclical materials company.

**Risk to growth:** Seeking Alpha flags "normalization risks" post-2027. Aerospace production cycles historically mean-revert. If Boeing/Airbus production plateaus or encounters further supply chain bottlenecks (as has occurred repeatedly), ATI's growth rate decelerates materially.

## Competitive Landscape

ATI's competitive moat is narrow but deep within its specific qualified programs. Key competitors include:

- **Carpenter Technology (CRS):** Overlapping specialty alloys portfolio
- **Haynes International:** Nickel and cobalt-based superalloys
- **Precision Castparts (Berkshire Hathaway subsidiary):** Dominant in forged/cast components
- **VSMPO-AVISMA (Russia):** Titanium sponge supplier — partially sanctioned, creating opportunity for Western suppliers like ATI

The Russian sanctions environment is a structural positive for ATI. VSMPO-AVISMA supplied significant titanium to Western aerospace, and sanctions/self-sanctions have created substitution demand that benefits ATI and Carpenter. This tailwind persists as long as geopolitical tensions with Russia remain elevated, which the world model suggests is highly likely through 2026.

The qualification barrier is ATI's strongest competitive advantage. Switching alloy suppliers on a qualified engine program takes 2-5 years and requires extensive testing. This provides revenue visibility once programs are won.

## Risk Assessment

**Cyclicality:** ATI is a cyclical materials company correlated to aerospace production rates. The current upcycle has driven a 262% stock appreciation. Cycles turn. Boeing production challenges, macroeconomic slowdown (world model: recession probability 38-42%), or airline fleet retirement deferrals could reduce demand.

**Macro sensitivity:** Oil at $98-108 (world model central range) has mixed implications. Higher oil prices increase airline operating costs, potentially reducing new aircraft orders at the margin. However, newer fuel-efficient engines (which use more ATI content) become more attractive, partially offsetting.

**Insider selling:** Executive Chairman Wetherbee sold approximately $12.5M in stock across February 10-17 transactions. CEO Fields had approximately $9.5M in tax withholding dispositions on January 5 equity grants. While much of this is routine tax-related selling around vesting events, Wetherbee's February open-market sales ($1.8M + $1.2M + $4.2M + $1.5M) are discretionary and occurred at $144-146, near current levels. Additional sales by David Morehouse ($1.0M) at $153-155 in early March add to the pattern. No insider purchases are present in the data.

**Input cost risk:** ATI uses significant energy, nickel, titanium sponge, and other raw materials. Oil at $100+ and sulphur disruption (per world model) could increase production costs. Ability to pass through via escalation clauses in long-term contracts is the key mitigant.

**Concentration risk:** Heavy dependence on aerospace/defense. If commercial aerospace delivery rates disappoint (Boeing has repeatedly delayed production ramp-ups), revenue falls disproportionately.

**Valuation risk:** At 48.5x trailing earnings, any earnings miss or guidance cut would likely result in significant multiple compression. The stock's beta of 1.03 understates this risk — ATI behaves like a higher-beta stock in aerospace downturns.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- Aerospace production rates continue rising through 2027+, driving 48% EPS growth in 2026 and sustained high-teens growth thereafter
- Content per engine increases accelerate as newer programs (GE9X, GTF Advantage) enter production
- Russian titanium sanctions create durable substitution demand
- Defense spending remains elevated for 2-3+ years due to munitions restocking (world model: nine independent confirmations)
- If 2026 EPS reaches $5.50+ and the market assigns 30x for growth durability, the stock reaches $165+

### Bear Case
- Valuation at 48.5x trailing already prices in the demand upcycle
- Boeing production ramp delays (historically persistent) compress near-term revenue
- Recession probability at 38-42% per world model reduces aircraft demand and triggers airline order deferrals
- Aerospace cycle normalizes post-2027, and multiple compresses from peak-cycle levels
- Insider selling by Chairman and CEO signals informed belief that near-term appreciation is limited
- Net income already fell in late 2025 vs. prior year and prior quarter despite stable sales — suggesting margin headwinds that may persist
- At 20-25x normalized mid-cycle earnings (~$3.50), fair value is $70-88, implying significant downside if the cycle turns

## Conclusion

ATI has legitimate structural advantages in a favorable demand environment. The aerospace supply chain position, qualification moats, and defense spending tailwind are real. However, the stock at $141.97 and 48.5x trailing earnings has already captured the bulk of the upcycle appreciation. The 45% three-month rally, significant insider selling by senior executives, and the Forbes article explicitly noting RTX as a "better play... for growth and value" all argue that better entry points exist.

The calibration document warns against overconfidence on cyclical names (APO at 7.55 was the worst BUY loss). ATI at peak-cycle multiples with executive selling fits this pattern. The demand cycle is real, but the price already reflects it.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** Wait for a pullback to $100-110 range (20-22x estimated 2026 EPS) for a more favorable risk/reward entry, or for evidence that the growth cycle extends beyond 2027 consensus expectations.
