## Executive Summary

ATI Inc is a specialty materials company (nickel-based alloys, titanium, specialty stainless steels) with dominant exposure to jet engine and aerospace structural content. The company reported Q4 2025 adjusted EPS of $0.93, beating consensus of $0.89, and management guided for sustained demand in core markets through 2026. The stock has rallied 45% in three months through mid-March 2026, driven by aerospace production rate increases and engine content growth per aircraft.

The investment thesis hinges on whether ATI's multi-year aerospace supercycle justifies a 48.5x forward P/E — a valuation that embeds years of above-trend growth and leaves minimal margin of safety. Consensus expects ~48% EPS growth in 2026, but growth is forecast to decelerate materially in 2027+. Combined with significant insider selling ($10M+ by the Executive Chairman in February alone), $100+ oil creating airline margin pressure (which could eventually slow aircraft orders), and limited financial statement data to verify balance sheet strength, the risk-reward at $142 is balanced rather than compelling.

## Company Overview

ATI Inc (formerly Allegheny Technologies) is headquartered in Dallas, TX and produces advanced specialty materials including nickel-based alloys and superalloys, titanium and titanium alloys, and specialty stainless steels. The company's primary end markets are commercial aerospace (jet engines and airframes), defense, and energy. ATI's materials are critical components in next-generation jet engines (GE LEAP, Pratt & Whitney GTF), where content per engine is increasing due to higher temperature requirements and fuel efficiency demands.

Market capitalization stands at $19.4 billion. The company has a beta of 1.03, suggesting roughly market-level systematic risk. ATI pays no dividend, directing capital toward growth capex and debt reduction. The 52-week range of $39.23 to $168.14 reflects substantial volatility, with the current price of $141.97 sitting approximately 16% below the 52-week high.

1. **Aerospace engine content growth:** ATI's nickel superalloy and titanium content per next-generation engine is higher than legacy platforms. As Boeing and Airbus ramp production rates, ATI benefits from both volume and mix improvement.

## Competitive Landscape

ATI operates in a structurally concentrated market for aerospace-grade specialty alloys. Key competitors include:
- **Carpenter Technology (CRS):** Direct competitor in specialty alloys, also benefiting from aerospace demand
- **Haynes International:** Nickel-based alloys for high-temperature applications
- **Precision Castparts (Berkshire Hathaway):** Vertically integrated; both supplier and customer competitor
- **TIMET (PCC subsidiary):** Titanium alloys

ATI's competitive advantages include:
1. **Qualification barriers:** Aerospace alloy suppliers must be qualified by OEMs, a process taking 2-5 years. This creates a durable installed base advantage.
2. **Technical differentiation:** Proprietary alloy formulations for next-generation engine requirements
3. **Long-term supply agreements:** Multi-year contracts with major OEMs provide revenue visibility
4. **Increasing content per platform:** Next-gen engines require more high-temperature alloy content, expanding ATI's addressable market per aircraft

The qualification barrier is the most important moat. New entrants face multi-year timelines to become certified suppliers, and engine OEMs are reluctant to change suppliers mid-program due to quality and reliability requirements.

## Risk Assessment

**Cycle Risk (Primary):** ATI is a cyclical materials company trading at a growth multiple. The aerospace cycle has been strong, but cycles turn. At 48.5x forward earnings, the stock has no buffer against a cyclical slowdown. Historical precedent: ATI's stock declined from ~$120 (pre-split adjusted) to under $10 during the 2015-2016 energy/industrial downturn and again during COVID.

**Insider Selling (Elevated Concern):**
- Executive Chairman Robert Wetherbee sold ~$8.6 million in open-market sales during February 2026, after receiving large equity grants in January
- Director David Morehouse sold ~$1.0 million in early March 2026
- CEO Kimberly Fields had large tax withholding sales ($9.5M) on January 5 equity conversions, which are routine
- The Wetherbee open-market sales are discretionary and occurred at $144-$147 per share — within 5% of the current price. Selling $8.6M while retaining ~$39M worth of shares ($269K shares × ~$146) is not panic selling, but it signals the Executive Chairman does not view the stock as significantly undervalued at these levels.

**Oil Price Risk:** The world model has oil at $98-108 central case with ceiling $115-130. Sustained $100+ oil compresses airline profitability → airlines defer new aircraft orders → Boeing/Airbus slow production rates → ATI volume declines. This is a 12-24 month lag effect, not immediate, but it undermines the multi-year growth assumption embedded in the valuation.

**Macro Stagflation Risk:** GDP at 0.7%, recession probability 38-42%. While defense spending is counter-cyclical, commercial aerospace spending is pro-cyclical. ATI's revenue mix is heavily commercial aerospace, making it vulnerable to a recession-driven order slowdown.

**Limited Financial Transparency in Available Data:** The absence of detailed financial statements (empty income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow data) makes it impossible to independently verify leverage ratios, working capital trends, free cash flow generation, or margin trajectories. This is a data limitation, not necessarily a company problem, but it constrains analytical confidence.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- Aerospace engine build rates continue accelerating through 2027-2028 as Boeing resolves production issues and Airbus sustains ramp
- Engine content per aircraft is structurally higher for new platforms, expanding ATI's addressable market regardless of unit volumes
- Defense spending tailwind provides a counter-cyclical floor (aligns with world model's 9-confirmation defense thesis)
- Strategic capex positions ATI for share gains as capacity-constrained competitors lose orders
- 48% EPS growth in 2026 could prove conservative if production rates accelerate faster than expected
- Qualification barriers protect margins and pricing power

### Bear Case
- At 48.5x forward P/E, the stock already prices in the supercycle — upside requires positive surprises, while any disappointment triggers disproportionate downside
- $100+ oil sustained for 6-12+ months slows airline fleet renewal, deferring orders that feed ATI's volume growth
- Executive Chairman selling $8.6M at $144-147 suggests informed skepticism about further near-term upside
- Post-2026 growth deceleration is consensus — if the market looks forward 12-18 months, multiple compression begins before peak earnings
- ATI is a cyclical materials business with historical drawdowns of 80%+ in prior downturns; the current valuation provides zero margin of safety against cyclicality
- Macro stagflation (GDP 0.7%, recession probability 38-42%) threatens the commercial aerospace demand underpinning the growth thesis

## Conclusion

ATI has genuine structural tailwinds: increasing engine content per aircraft, multi-year OEM qualification barriers, defense spending acceleration, and a CEO who guided confidently for 2026. The Q4 2025 earnings beat confirms operational execution.

The problem is price. At 48.5x forward earnings after a 45% three-month rally, ATI is priced for the best-case scenario of a multi-year aerospace supercycle continuing uninterrupted. The stock trades at a premium more typical of high-growth technology companies than cyclical materials businesses. Insider selling by the Executive Chairman at essentially current price levels ($144-147) provides a negative signal from someone with the most visibility into the business trajectory.

The macro environment amplifies risk: $100+ oil threatening airline economics, 38-42% recession probability, and stagflationary data across all six macro dimensions. While defense spending provides a partial offset, ATI's revenue is predominantly commercial aerospace, which is pro-cyclical.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** For existing holders, the aerospace demand backdrop supports continued ownership, but the valuation leaves no margin of safety against cycle deceleration or macro deterioration. For new positions, the risk-reward at $142 is inadequate given the cyclical nature of the business and the macro overhang. A pullback to 30-35x forward earnings (roughly $88-103 based on the implied ~$2.93 forward EPS) would offer a more attractive entry point with appropriate margin of safety for a cyclical materials company.
