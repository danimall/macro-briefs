## Executive Summary

WEC Energy Group is a leading regulated utility holding company serving customers primarily in Wisconsin and other Midwestern states. The company reported strong full-year 2025 GAAP net income of $1.6 billion with adjusted earnings of $5.27 per share, beating Q4 2025 consensus estimates with $1.42 EPS versus $1.38-$1.39 expected. The company has announced an ambitious expanded five-year capital plan tied to surging large-load demand, particularly from AI data centers, and a robust 6.7% dividend increase.

Despite these positive fundamentals, WEC's current share price of ~$114.03 reflects much of the optimism, trading at a trailing P/E of ~21.8x and a forward P/E of ~24.2x. The average analyst price target of $125.10 suggests approximately 9.7% upside, and UBS maintains a Neutral recommendation. With a beta of just 0.55, WEC serves as a quality defensive holding, but the premium valuation limits the margin of safety for new entrants.

The investment thesis centers on whether WEC's expanding capital deployment into renewable assets and data center infrastructure can drive above-average earnings growth for a utility, or whether the current valuation already prices in these tailwinds. On balance, WEC appears fairly valued — a Hold for current shareholders and a watch-list candidate for income-oriented investors awaiting a pullback.

## Company Overview

WEC Energy Group Inc. (NYSE: WEC) is a Milwaukee-based utility holding company operating through several regulated subsidiaries, including We Energies, Wisconsin Public Service, and Wisconsin Gas. The company provides electric and natural gas service to approximately 4.6 million customers across Wisconsin, Illinois, Michigan, and Minnesota. WEC operates in the Utilities sector with a market capitalization of approximately $36.2 billion.

The company's business model is primarily regulated, meaning revenues and returns are determined through rate cases with state public utility commissions. This provides earnings stability and predictability but limits upside without corresponding capital investment. WEC has been actively investing in renewable energy, grid modernization, and infrastructure to support growing demand from large commercial and industrial customers, including emerging AI data center loads.

## Risk Assessment

- **Interest Rate Sensitivity:** As a utility with significant debt financing, WEC is sensitive to rising rates. The beta of 0.55 reflects this bond-proxy characteristic
- **Regulatory Risk:** Large capital plan execution depends on favorable rate case outcomes from Wisconsin and other state commissions
- **Construction/Execution Risk:** Expanded capex plans carry execution and cost overrun risks

### Emerging Risks
- **Data Center Demand Uncertainty:** While promising, large-load demand from AI data centers is still materializing and could face delays or cancellations

### Mitigating Factors
- Regulated business model provides earnings floor
- Low beta (0.55) limits downside in market selloffs
- Strong dividend coverage and consistent growth history
- Diversified customer base across multiple states and customer classes

## Conclusion

**Rating: HOLD**

WEC Energy Group is a high-quality regulated utility executing well on a compelling growth strategy anchored by renewable energy expansion and emerging AI data center demand. The company's Q4 2025 earnings beat ($1.42 vs $1.39 expected), full-year adjusted EPS of $5.27, and 6.7% dividend increase all demonstrate strong operational performance and management confidence.

However, the stock's premium valuation (21.8x trailing P/E, 24.2x forward) already reflects much of this optimism. With the average analyst target at $125.10 implying limited upside and multiple analysts rating the stock as Neutral/Hold, the risk-reward appears balanced at current levels.

**For current shareholders:** WEC remains a core defensive holding with reliable income growth. Continue to hold and reinvest dividends.

**For prospective investors:** Wait for a pullback toward the $100-$105 range (approximately 18-20x trailing earnings) to establish a position with a more attractive margin of safety. At that level, the ~3.8-4.0% yield and growth potential would be compelling.

**Key metrics to monitor:** Data center demand pipeline developments, five-year capital plan regulatory approvals, quarterly rate base growth, and interest rate trends.
