## Executive Summary

Centene Corporation is a managed care company heavily dependent on government-sponsored healthcare programs, primarily Medicaid and ACA marketplace plans. The stock has declined approximately 39% over the trailing 12 months and 45% from its 52-week high of $66.03 to the current price of $36.15. The primary driver of this collapse is a structural enrollment decline: CEO Sarah London warned at the March 2026 Barclays conference that ACA marketplace enrollment will drop from 5.5M to 3.5M members, triggering a 14% single-day decline on March 10-11.

FY2025 was deeply unprofitable on a GAAP basis, with trailing EPS of -$13.53. Adjusted EPS came in at $2.08, representing a 71% YoY decline. Q4 2025 reported an adjusted loss of $1.19 per share, slightly better than the consensus estimate of -$1.22. While management reaffirmed 2026 EPS guidance above $1 (per Benzinga reporting), this implies a company generating roughly $500M in adjusted earnings on a ~$18B market cap — a thin margin of safety given the magnitude of enrollment headwinds. The combination of deteriorating fundamentals, high policy uncertainty, and negative earnings trajectory makes this an AVOID.

## Company Overview

Centene is the largest Medicaid managed care organization in the United States, also operating significant ACA marketplace exchange and Medicare/PDP businesses. The company serves over 25 million members across all 50 states. Revenue is predominantly government-funded through Medicaid contracts, ACA premium subsidies, and Medicare Part D. The Magellan Health acquisition (2022) expanded behavioral health capabilities but added leverage. The company's business model depends on maintaining favorable enrollment levels and managing medical cost ratios within actuarially predicted ranges.

Q4 2025 adjusted loss of $1.19/share suggests the back half of 2025 was particularly weak.

## Growth Analysis

**ACA Enrollment Cliff:** The most critical data point is CEO London's warning that ACA marketplace enrollment will decline from 5.5M to 3.5M members — a 36% reduction. This is driven by: (1) the expiration of enhanced ACA premium subsidies originally enacted under the American Rescue Plan and extended through 2025, (2) Medicaid redeterminations that initially pushed members into ACA plans are now resulting in net membership losses as eligibility verification tightens, and (3) potential policy changes under the current administration that could further erode ACA enrollment.

**Revenue Impact:** ACA marketplace revenue is Centene's second-largest segment. A 36% enrollment decline translates directly to revenue loss, with limited ability to offset through pricing given rate-setting regulatory constraints. If ACA per-member-per-month revenue averages ~$500-600, losing 2M members implies $12-14B in annualized revenue at risk.

**Medicaid:** The core Medicaid business faces its own headwinds from state-level redeterminations. Centene has lost Medicaid members since the end of the COVID-era continuous enrollment provision. While some stabilization has occurred, the net direction remains negative.

**PDP Business:** Q4 2025 revenue growth was attributed to PDP (Medicare Part D) business strength. This provides partial offset but is lower-margin and subject to CMS risk corridor adjustments.

**2026 Guidance:** Management guided to adjusted EPS "slightly above" consensus expectations for 2026, which Morningstar noted was marginally positive. However, the March 2026 ACA enrollment warning appears to post-date or update this guidance, suggesting downside risk to full-year estimates.

## Valuation Assessment

At $36.15 with trailing EPS of -$13.53, traditional P/E metrics are meaningless on a GAAP basis. Using the 2026 adjusted EPS guidance of "above $1" — call it $1.50 conservatively — the stock trades at roughly 24x forward adjusted earnings. For a managed care company facing structural enrollment decline, this is expensive. Peers like UnitedHealth (UNH), Molina (MOH), and Elevance (ELV) trade at 12-16x forward earnings with more stable growth profiles.

The $17.8B market cap against a business generating perhaps $500-750M in adjusted net income implies no margin of safety for further enrollment deterioration. Book value is likely impaired given the GAAP losses, and tangible book value is almost certainly negative given the Magellan-related goodwill.

Mizuho downgraded their price target to $41 (per the March 11 news). Morgan Stanley maintained Hold with a $38 target on February 6. These are the only concrete analyst targets available, and both are relatively close to the current price, suggesting limited upside even in a stabilization scenario.

## Competitive Landscape

Centene competes with UnitedHealth, Elevance, Molina, and Humana in government-sponsored managed care. Its competitive position has specific vulnerabilities:

**Scale advantage eroding:** Centene's scale in Medicaid was built through aggressive state contract wins and the Magellan acquisition. But scale provides limited moat when enrollment is declining involuntarily due to policy changes. Fixed administrative costs create operating deleverage as membership shrinks.

**ACA concentration risk:** Centene has the largest ACA marketplace enrollment of any insurer. This was an advantage during the enrollment expansion era (2020-2024). It is now a concentrated liability as the ACA subsidy regime potentially changes.

**State contract renewals:** Medicaid contracts are subject to periodic rebidding. Centene's GAAP losses and any deterioration in quality metrics could weaken its competitive position in future RFP processes.

**Peer comparison:** Molina (MOH) has a simpler, more focused Medicaid portfolio and has managed through redeterminations with less disruption. UNH's diversification across Optum provides earnings stability that Centene lacks.

## Risk Assessment

**Policy Risk (Critical):** ACA subsidy expiration is the single largest risk. Enhanced subsidies expire at end of 2025 (already happened) or face further cuts. The 36% enrollment decline forecast by the CEO quantifies this risk. Additional Medicaid per-capita cap proposals at the federal level could further compress revenues.

**Earnings Trajectory Risk (High):** With trailing GAAP EPS of -$13.53 and adjusted EPS of only $2.08, the path to sustainable profitability requires enrollment stabilization and medical cost ratio improvement simultaneously. Neither is guaranteed.

**Balance Sheet Risk (Moderate-High):** Significant debt from the Magellan acquisition in an environment where the Fed is discussing rate hikes (per world model) increases refinancing risk. Debt reduction efforts are positive but the pace may be insufficient if operating cash flows deteriorate.

**Medical Cost Ratio Risk (Moderate):** In a stagflationary environment with healthcare cost inflation running above general CPI, maintaining target medical cost ratios becomes harder. Centene's actuarial reserving has been questioned, with prior period development adjustments noted in the Q4 2025 earnings release.

**Regulatory/Political Risk (High):** The current administration's stance toward ACA, Medicaid expansion, and managed care profit regulation all represent material uncertainties.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- 2026 adjusted EPS guidance of "above $1" provides a floor if achieved, implying the stock trades at ~24-36x trough earnings with significant upside if enrollment stabilizes
- ACA enrollment decline may be less severe than the CEO's 3.5M forecast if subsidies are partially extended or Medicaid-to-ACA transitions slow
- Debt reduction improves balance sheet flexibility over time
- The stock has already declined 45% from highs, potentially pricing in much of the bad news
- PDP business strength provides partial revenue diversification

### Bear Case
- 36% ACA enrollment decline translates to $12-14B in at-risk revenue with no clear offset
- Trailing GAAP EPS of -$13.53 suggests structural profitability problems beyond cyclical headwinds
- No insider buying at deeply depressed levels
- Forward P/E of -2.67 reflects the market's expectation of continued losses
- Rising medical cost inflation in a stagflationary macro environment compresses margins further
- Significant debt burden in an environment where rate hikes are on the table
- Competitive position weakens as enrollment shrinks and state contracts come up for renewal
- Policy risk is asymmetric: further ACA/Medicaid cuts are more likely than expansion under current administration

## Conclusion

Centene faces a deteriorating trajectory across its key business segments. The CEO's own warning of a 36% ACA enrollment decline is a management-sourced confirmation of structural headwinds. GAAP losses of $13.53/share in 2025, even with adjustments yielding $2.08, reflect a business in transition at best and in secular decline at worst. The 45% decline from highs has brought the price down but not to a level where the risk-reward compensates for the enrollment cliff, balance sheet leverage, and policy uncertainty.

The calibration framework requires AVOID for deteriorating trajectories with scores ≤4.8. CNC scores 3.73 overall. The absence of any discretionary insider buying, the negative GAAP earnings, the CEO-sourced enrollment decline warning, and the hostile macro/policy environment all point in the same direction. This is an AVOID.
