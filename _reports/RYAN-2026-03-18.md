## Executive Summary

Ryan Specialty Holdings is a specialty insurance distribution firm that reported $3.05B in 2025 revenue (+21.3% YoY), driven by 10.1% organic growth supplemented by acquisitions. However, Q4 2025 organic growth decelerated to 6.6%, Q4 EPS of $0.45 missed the consensus estimate of $0.50, and the stock has been in persistent decline since its April 2025 high of $77.16 to ~$36 today — a 53% drawdown. The company is undertaking an "Empower" restructuring expected to generate $80M in annual savings, but this is set against a softening property insurance pricing cycle and emerging AI disruption concerns that prompted Barclays to cut its price target to $45.

The core tension: RYAN has a legitimate competitive position in specialty E&S (excess & surplus) insurance distribution with demonstrated organic growth well above industry averages, and the founder/director Patrick Ryan Jr. purchased ~$1M in shares in late February 2026. But at 77x trailing earnings on $0.47 EPS, the stock prices in continued high growth that the soft pricing environment is actively undermining. The Empower restructuring signals management recognizes margin pressure is real. Given the macro backdrop — stagflationary environment with confirmed insurance pricing softness — the risk/reward at current levels warrants a HOLD pending evidence that the restructuring delivers margin improvement and organic growth stabilizes above 8%.

## Company Overview

Ryan Specialty is one of the largest specialty insurance distributors in the US, operating primarily in the E&S market. The company acts as an intermediary between insurance carriers and retail brokers, specializing in complex and hard-to-place risks. Founded by industry veteran Patrick Ryan (who previously founded Aon), RYAN went public in 2021 and has pursued an aggressive acquisition-and-organic-growth strategy. The company operates through wholesale brokerage, binding authority, and underwriting management segments.

Key business characteristics:
- **Asset-light model**: Commission/fee-based revenue with limited capital requirements
- **E&S market exposure**: Benefits when standard insurance markets tighten and more risks flow to surplus lines
- **Acquisition engine**: The 21.3% total revenue growth vs. 10.1% organic growth implies ~11% came from M&A
- **Market cap**: ~$4.5B on 129.7M shares outstanding at $34.84 (profile data; most recent close $35.98)

The deceleration from 12.8% organic in 2024 to 10.1% in 2025, and further to 6.6% in Q4, forms a clear declining trajectory. The Q4 miss was meaningful — $0.05 on a $0.50 estimate is a 10% shortfall.

## Growth Analysis

### Organic Growth Trajectory
The organic growth deceleration (12.8% → 10.1% → 6.6% quarterly) directly correlates with the softening E&S pricing cycle. When property/casualty pricing softens, fewer risks flow to surplus lines, and commission rates face pressure. This is a cyclical headwind that management cannot control.

### Acquisition Strategy
The ~11% inorganic growth component means roughly half of RYAN's revenue growth came from M&A. This creates several concerns:
1. Acquisition integration risk during a restructuring
2. Goodwill/intangible buildup on the balance sheet (unverifiable without B/S data)
3. Sustainability questions — maintaining 10%+ inorganic growth requires increasingly larger deals

### Empower Restructuring
The $80M savings target could add ~$0.30-0.40/share to earnings once fully realized (assuming ~50% tax rate on incremental savings and ~130M diluted shares). This would represent meaningful EPS uplift from the current $0.47 base, but the timeline and execution risk are uncertain.

### AI Disruption
Barclays flagged AI disruption as a concern when cutting the price target to $45. For an insurance distribution intermediary, AI could automate portions of the risk placement process, potentially disintermediating wholesale brokers for less complex E&S risks. This is a medium-term structural risk worth monitoring, though the most complex specialty risks likely require human expertise for the foreseeable future.

The 77x P/E on $0.47 EPS is the central problem. If the Empower restructuring delivers the full $80M savings and organic growth stabilizes at 8-10%, a path to $1.00-$1.20 EPS over 2-3 years is plausible. At a 25-30x P/E (appropriate for a specialty distributor with high single-digit organic growth), that yields a $25-36 fair value range, roughly in line with current prices. At a more generous 35x (reflecting the asset-light model and historical premium), fair value reaches $35-42.

The stock at $36 is pricing in restructuring success and some growth recovery. It is no longer pricing in the high-growth premium that justified $70+. The question is whether the current price adequately discounts the soft pricing cycle and AI risk.

RYAN's competitive advantages include:

2. **Scale**: $3B+ revenue provides negotiating leverage with carriers
3. **Specialization**: Focus on complex risks where expertise matters
4. **Acquisition platform**: Proven ability to integrate specialty teams

The soft pricing cycle is an industry-wide headwind that affects all distributors, but RYAN's higher-margin specialty focus should provide relative resilience compared to standard-market intermediaries.

## Risk Assessment

### Primary Risks (Near-Term)

1. **Soft pricing cycle**: The E&S market is experiencing pricing deceleration. Q4 organic growth of 6.6% may not be the trough. If organic growth falls below 5%, the earnings multiple contracts further.

2. **Valuation compression continues**: At 77x trailing P/E, the stock remains expensive on current earnings. Any further earnings disappointment accelerates the de-rating.

3. **Restructuring execution**: The Empower program must deliver $80M in savings while maintaining revenue retention. Cost cuts during soft markets risk talent attrition.

### Secondary Risks (Medium-Term)

4. **AI disintermediation**: Barclays' concern is valid for the portion of RYAN's business that involves standardized E&S placements. Complex specialty risks are less automatable.

5. **Florida legislative risk**: The February 2026 decline was partly triggered by a Florida bill that could alter the state's insurance market — RYAN has significant Florida exposure.

6. **Acquisition integration**: The company has been acquiring aggressively; integration during restructuring creates organizational strain.

### Macro Context

Per the world model, the insurance/P&C sector is broadly overweight due to bond safe-haven failure and war-risk premium validation. However, RYAN is a distribution intermediary — it benefits from hard markets (more E&S flow) and suffers in soft markets. The current soft pricing environment is a direct headwind, partially offsetting the broader sector tailwind. RYAN is structurally different from underwriters like ACGL, RNR, and CB — it does not benefit directly from rising premiums on its own book, only from increased placement volume and commission rates.

### Why Not BUY?
Three specific concerns prevent upgrading from HOLD:
1. The 77x trailing P/E means even at $36, the stock requires substantial earnings growth that the soft pricing cycle actively undermines
2. Q4 organic growth deceleration to 6.6% shows no stabilization yet — the next 1-2 quarters need to confirm a trough
3. The Empower restructuring is announced but unproven; the $80M savings claim requires execution evidence

## Conclusion

HOLD. Ryan Specialty has a legitimate competitive franchise in specialty insurance distribution, and the 50% decline from peak has removed much of the speculative premium. However, the combination of 77x trailing P/E, decelerating organic growth, a soft pricing cycle, and unproven restructuring savings means the risk/reward is not yet compelling enough for a BUY recommendation. The stock could represent an attractive entry point if: (a) organic growth stabilizes above 7% for two consecutive quarters, (b) the Empower restructuring shows measurable margin improvement, or (c) the price declines further to ~$28-32 where the valuation begins to reflect trough earnings. Patrick Ryan Jr.'s $1M insider purchase at ~$40 suggests informed buyers see value emerging, but the timing of the insurance pricing recovery is uncertain.

Monitoring triggers for upgrade to BUY: Q1/Q2 2026 organic growth ≥7%, visible margin improvement from Empower, or price decline below $30.
