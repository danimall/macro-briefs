## Executive Summary

BioMarin is a mid-cap rare disease biotech ($11.3B market cap) generating ~$3B+ in annual revenue across enzyme replacement therapies (Naglazyme, VIMIZIM, ALDURAZYME, BRINEURA, PALYNZIQ) and the growth-stage achondroplasia drug VOXZOGO. The company guided 2026 revenue of $3.325B-$3.425B, implying ~10-12% top-line growth, anchored by VOXZOGO scaling toward ~$1B and stable enzyme franchise revenues of $2.225B-$2.275B. The Amicus Therapeutics acquisition adds further diversification in rare disease.

Q4 2025 adjusted EPS of $0.46 beat the $0.25 consensus, but GAAP results showed a $46.6M net loss driven by a $119.2M inventory write-off tied to the Roctavian gene therapy withdrawal. Roctavian's removal from the market eliminates what was once positioned as a transformational hemophilia A asset — its commercial failure signals execution risk in gene therapy commercialization. At ~32x forward earnings and 3.3x 2026 revenue, valuation is full relative to the growth profile. The 0.26 beta provides genuine macro insulation in the current stagflationary environment, but insider selling patterns (CTO, CLO, Chief R&D Officer all selling post-grant) suggest limited internal conviction about near-term upside.

## Company Overview

BioMarin develops and commercializes therapies for rare genetic diseases. Revenue is concentrated in two segments: (1) enzyme replacement therapies — a mature, durable franchise generating ~$2.2B annually with limited growth — and (2) VOXZOGO (vosoritide), a C-type natriuretic peptide analog for achondroplasia in children, on a trajectory toward $1B. The company recently agreed to acquire Amicus Therapeutics, adding Galafold (Fabry disease) and pipeline assets.

Key products:
- **Enzyme therapies** ($2.225B-$2.275B guided 2026): Naglazyme, VIMIZIM, ALDURAZYME, BRINEURA, PALYNZIQ
- **VOXZOGO** ($975M-$1.025B guided 2026): Growth driver, recently expanded into adult achondroplasia studies
- **PALYNZIQ**: Recently received FDA approval for expanded adolescent use (Feb 27, 2026)
- **Roctavian**: Gene therapy for hemophilia A — being withdrawn from market

## Financial Analysis

**Revenue & Earnings:** 2026 guidance of $3.325B-$3.425B in total revenue. Working backward from the ~$11.3B market cap and ~32x forward P/E, implied forward earnings are approximately $350M, or ~$1.81/share on ~193M diluted shares. Q4 2025 adjusted EPS of $0.46 beat consensus by $0.21, but the GAAP loss of $46.6M (vs. prior year profit) reflects the Roctavian write-off. Earnings declined 50% YoY on an adjusted basis according to reporting.

**Balance Sheet:** Detailed balance sheet data was not available in the provided financials. The company's ability to fund the Amicus acquisition and manage existing debt will be a critical factor. BioMarin has historically carried convertible notes and term loans. The Amicus deal likely adds leverage.

**Cash Flow:** Not available in provided data. Historically, BioMarin has been FCF-positive on its enzyme therapy base, with R&D and SG&A consuming significant operating cash flow. The Roctavian withdrawal should reduce ongoing manufacturing and commercialization costs, creating a modest offset.

**Profitability:** At ~$3.4B revenue and ~$350M implied net income, the net margin sits at roughly 10%. For a rare disease biotech with established commercial products, this is below peers like Vertex (VRTX, ~30%+ net margins) and Regeneron (~35%). The margin gap reflects BioMarin's higher manufacturing complexity (enzyme replacement) and pipeline investment.

## Growth Analysis

**VOXZOGO trajectory:** Growing from ~$700-800M in 2025 toward $975M-$1.025B in 2026 represents ~25-30% YoY growth. The drug treats achondroplasia, the most common form of dwarfism, affecting ~1 in 15,000-40,000 births globally. Market expansion into adult indications would meaningfully expand the addressable population. This is the primary organic growth engine.

**PALYNZIQ label expansion:** FDA approval for adolescent PKU patients (Feb 2026) extends the addressable population. PKU therapies are a smaller market (~$500M globally) but the label expansion should contribute incremental revenue.

**Amicus acquisition:** Adds Galafold (migalastat) for Fabry disease — an oral therapy competing against enzyme replacements (Replagal, Fabrazyme). Galafold generates ~$500M+ annually with high margins. The deal diversifies the rare disease portfolio and adds manufacturing synergies. Execution risk is moderate; Amicus was a known entity in rare disease.

**Roctavian withdrawal:** This is a material negative. Gene therapy for hemophilia A was positioned as a potential multi-billion dollar opportunity. Commercial uptake was severely disappointing due to durability concerns, high pricing ($2.9M per dose), and payer resistance. The $119.2M write-off covers inventory; additional impairments may follow. The withdrawal signals that BioMarin's gene therapy platform has limited near-term commercial viability, removing a major long-term growth vector.

**Pipeline:** BioMarin maintains R&D programs in gene therapy (BMN 307 for PKU) and small molecules. The Roctavian failure reduces confidence in the gene therapy platform's commercial translation.

## Valuation Assessment

At $58.51, BMRN trades at:
- **~32x forward P/E** (both trailing and forward P/E listed at 32.25x)
- **~3.3x 2026 guided revenue** ($11.3B / $3.375B midpoint)
- **~20% discount to 52-week high** ($73.18)
- **~15% above 52-week low** ($50.76)

Canaccord's $104 price target implies ~78% upside — this appears aggressive. At $104, BMRN would trade at ~57x current forward earnings. This would require either significant margin expansion or revenue acceleration beyond current guidance.

For context, VRTX trades at ~31x forward earnings with higher margins and a cleaner growth profile. Regeneron trades at ~20x. BioMarin's 32x multiple requires VOXZOGO to sustain 20%+ growth rates and margin expansion toward 15-20% net margins over the next 2-3 years. Achievable but not priced for disappointment.

The valuation is reasonable but not cheap. Downside in a recession scenario is limited by the 0.26 beta and non-discretionary nature of rare disease therapies (patients can't defer treatment). Upside requires pipeline execution and Amicus integration success.

## Competitive Landscape

BioMarin operates in rare disease niches where competition is limited by small patient populations and regulatory barriers (orphan drug exclusivity). This is the core moat.

**Enzyme therapies:** Largely uncontested in their specific indications (MPS I, II, IVA, VI; CLN2). Generic/biosimilar risk is low due to manufacturing complexity. Revenue growth is flat to low-single-digits — driven by patient identification in emerging markets rather than pricing power.

**VOXZOGO:** First-in-class for achondroplasia. Ascendis Pharma's TransCon CNP is the primary competitive threat, with pivotal data expected in coming years. If TransCon CNP demonstrates superior efficacy or dosing convenience, VOXZOGO's growth trajectory could be impaired. For now, VOXZOGO has 2+ years of commercial lead.

**Amicus/Galafold:** Competes with Sanofi's Fabrazyme and Takeda's Replagal in Fabry disease. Galafold's oral formulation is a meaningful differentiator vs. IV-infused enzyme replacements, but limited to amenable mutations (~30-50% of patients).

**Gene therapy:** With Roctavian's withdrawal, BioMarin cedes the hemophilia gene therapy market. Pfizer and others continue development.

## Risk Assessment

**Roctavian impairment risk:** Additional write-downs beyond the $119.2M inventory charge are possible if manufacturing assets or intangible assets require further impairment. The withdrawal also raises questions about BioMarin's gene therapy R&D capital allocation discipline over the past decade.

**Amicus integration:** Acquisition execution carries standard risks — retention, commercial disruption, debt service. The deal's financial terms were not fully detailed in available data, but adding an acquisition during a period of Roctavian-related P&L pressure compresses financial flexibility.

**Insider selling:** CTO Charles Guyer sold $997K on March 11, 2026. CLO George Davis sold $1.6M on February 26. Chief R&D Officer Friberg sold $382K on February 26. All sales occurred shortly after grant vesting. While post-vest selling is common, the aggregate pattern ($2.98M across three senior executives within 2 weeks) warrants monitoring. No insider purchases were observed.

**Macro sensitivity:** Low. Beta of 0.26. Rare disease therapies are non-discretionary with payer coverage. Oil prices, interest rates, and consumer spending have minimal direct impact. This is a genuine defensive characteristic in the current stagflationary environment. However, credit market tightening could increase cost of capital for the Amicus acquisition financing.

**Regulatory:** PALYNZIQ adolescent approval is a positive. Pipeline regulatory risk is standard for biotech.

**Concentration:** VOXZOGO is becoming a larger share of the growth story. If VOXZOGO growth stalls (competitor data, safety signal, reimbursement challenges), the stock re-rates significantly lower.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- VOXZOGO scales past $1B in 2026 and expands into adult indications, opening a $2B+ peak revenue opportunity
- Amicus acquisition adds ~$500M+ in Galafold revenue with high margins and limited integration risk
- Enzyme therapy franchise provides $2.2B+ of stable, recurring revenue as a floor
- Roctavian withdrawal eliminates an R&D cost drain, improving margins
- 0.26 beta provides genuine portfolio protection during macro volatility
- PALYNZIQ adolescent expansion, pipeline progress provide incremental catalysts
- Canaccord $104 target implies significant upside if growth materializes

### Bear Case
- 32x forward earnings for ~10-12% revenue growth is fully valued
- Roctavian failure raises questions about capital allocation and gene therapy platform viability
- $119.2M write-off may be followed by additional impairments
- Insider selling ($3M in 2 weeks) suggests insiders see limited near-term upside
- Amicus acquisition adds complexity and potentially leverage during uncertain credit markets
- TransCon CNP competitive data could threaten VOXZOGO's growth trajectory
- Net margins of ~10% are well below rare disease biotech peers

## Conclusion

BMRN is a **HOLD** at $58.51. The defensive characteristics (0.26 beta, non-discretionary rare disease revenue) are valuable in the current macro environment, and VOXZOGO's growth trajectory provides a credible path to $3.4B+ revenue. However, the Roctavian withdrawal removes a long-term growth vector, the valuation at 32x forward earnings leaves limited margin of safety, and insider selling patterns are not encouraging. The Amicus acquisition could prove accretive but adds execution risk during a period of existing operational complexity. Accumulation at or below $52-54 (near the 52-week low, ~28x forward earnings) would offer better risk-reward. The stock needs either (a) VOXZOGO adult indication data success, (b) evidence of meaningful margin expansion toward 15%+ net margins, or (c) a valuation compression to sub-28x to warrant upgrading to BUY.
