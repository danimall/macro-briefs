## Executive Summary

AECOM is a global infrastructure consulting firm that has declined ~30% over the past six months, trading at $91.06 versus a 52-week high of $135.52. The Q1 FY2026 earnings report (February 2026) showed an EPS beat but a revenue miss, triggering a further 12%+ decline in the stock. The company reports a record backlog driven by secular infrastructure investment trends across transportation, water, and environmental systems. At 21x forward earnings with a 1.4% dividend yield, the valuation is neither compellingly cheap nor expensive for a professional services firm with government exposure.

The macro backdrop creates a mixed setup. AECOM's revenue base is heavily tied to government infrastructure spending, which has been growing due to multi-year authorization bills (IIJA, CHIPS Act) but faces uncertainty under the current administration's fiscal priorities and potential DOGE-driven spending reviews. Rising oil prices and stagflationary conditions could slow private-sector project activity, but public infrastructure spending tends to be counter-cyclical and already-authorized. The record backlog provides 12-18 months of revenue visibility, but the revenue miss suggests conversion rates may be slowing.

## Company Overview

AECOM operates through Americas, International, and AECOM Capital segments, providing design, consulting, and management services for infrastructure projects globally. The company serves governments (federal, state, local), businesses, and organizations across transportation, water, environmental, facilities, and energy end markets. With a market cap of ~$11.8B, AECOM is one of the larger pure-play infrastructure consulting firms, competing with Jacobs, WSP Global, Quanta Services, and others.

The company underwent a strategic transformation starting in 2019, divesting its construction-at-risk business (Management Services segment sold to a consortium including Lindsay Goldberg and American Securities) to focus on the higher-margin professional services model. This shift reduced revenue volatility and improved margins, though it also reduced the revenue base.

## Growth Analysis

**Record Backlog:** The primary growth narrative. Per Zacks (March 10, 2026): "Rising investment in transportation networks, water systems and environmental infrastructure is creating a steady pipeline of projects." A record backlog provides revenue visibility over 12-24 months, assuming normal conversion rates.

**Revenue Miss:** Q1 FY2026 showed EPS above consensus but revenue below expectations. This divergence — beating on earnings while missing on revenue — typically indicates margin discipline (cost control, favorable project mix) offsetting slower top-line conversion. If the backlog is at record levels but revenue is coming in below expectations, the implication is that project starts are being delayed, possibly due to permitting, client decision-making speed, or macro-related caution.

**Secular Tailwinds:** IIJA (Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act) authorized $1.2T over 5 years, with spending still ramping through 2026-2027. This provides a structural floor under federal infrastructure demand. State and local governments are similarly increasing infrastructure budgets, particularly for water/wastewater systems facing deferred maintenance.

**Macro Headwinds:** In the current stagflationary environment (Q4 GDP 0.7%, core PCE 3.1%, NFP -92K), private-sector capital expenditure is likely to slow. AECOM's government exposure provides partial insulation, but any administration effort to claw back unspent IIJA funds or slow federal procurement would directly impair the growth trajectory. Section 301 tariff probes across 60 economies add uncertainty to international project pipelines.

## Valuation Assessment

At $91.06 and 21x forward earnings, AECOM trades at a discount to its 5-year average multiple (which has ranged from ~18x to ~28x forward, depending on the period). The 30% drawdown has compressed the valuation to the lower end of the historical range.

**Comparison framework:** Jacobs Solutions (J) typically trades at 18-22x forward earnings. WSP Global (WSP.TO) trades at 25-30x. Quanta Services (PWR) trades at 20-25x. ACM at 21x forward is roughly in-line with the peer median, which suggests fair value absent a differentiated catalyst.

The Nasdaq article from February 27 referenced a mean analyst price target of ~$129 on a $99.56 close (29.65% upside). With the stock now at $91.06, implied upside to that mean target is ~42%. However, that target likely needs downward revision given the revenue miss and macro deterioration since the estimates were set.

A reasonable current-year EPS estimate of ~$4.34 at 21x implies a fair value range of ~$87-$100 depending on multiple assumptions. The stock sits near the middle of that range.

## Competitive Landscape

AECOM's competitive position is solid. As one of the top 3 global infrastructure consulting firms by revenue, it benefits from scale advantages in bidding on large government contracts, geographic diversification, and deep technical expertise across multiple infrastructure verticals. The shift away from construction-at-risk toward professional services improved the margin profile and reduced competitive exposure to pure construction firms.

The infrastructure consulting market is consolidating. WSP's acquisition of Power Engineers and other bolt-on deals have intensified competition at the top end. AECOM's response has been organic growth plus selective acquisitions, maintaining its position without the balance sheet risk of large M&A.

Government relationships — particularly with US federal agencies (DoD, DOT, EPA, USACE) — represent a meaningful moat. These relationships are built over decades and are difficult to replicate. The classification requirements for defense-related work add an additional barrier.

## Risk Assessment

**Government Spending Risk:** AECOM's revenue is heavily dependent on government budgets. DOGE-driven spending reviews, continuing resolutions instead of full-year appropriations, and potential rescissions of IIJA funds are all risks. Probability of material impact: 20-30%.

**Revenue Conversion Risk:** The record backlog is only valuable if projects convert to revenue at historical rates. The Q1 revenue miss suggests possible slippage. If this is a trend rather than a one-quarter anomaly, earnings estimates need to come down despite the backlog.

**Macro/Cyclical Risk:** Beta of 1.07 means ACM moves roughly with the market. In a stagflation scenario with recession probability at 38-42%, ACM would likely see private-sector project delays and potential government budget pressure. The 30% drawdown already prices in some of this risk.

**Oil Price Impact:** Higher oil prices increase construction and materials costs, potentially squeezing margins on fixed-price or capped-fee contracts. As a professional services firm, AECOM has less direct materials exposure than construction firms, but indirect effects through client budget constraints are real.

**Insider Selling:** CLO sold $1.5M in December at prices 7-9% above current. No insider buying during the drawdown. Mildly negative signal.

**Key Man Risk:** CEO Troy Rudd has led the strategic transformation successfully, with 259K+ shares held. His departure would introduce execution uncertainty.

## Investment Thesis

**Bull Case:**
- Record backlog converts to revenue over 2-4 quarters, demonstrating the Q1 miss was a timing issue
- IIJA spending continues to ramp, providing multi-year revenue visibility through 2027-2028
- Professional services model delivers 12-15% EBITDA margins with steady improvement
- At 21x forward, there's meaningful upside to a 24-25x multiple if growth accelerates
- Government infrastructure spending is among the most recession-resistant budget categories
- Implied fair value: $110-$125 (25-37% upside)

**Bear Case:**
- Revenue miss in Q1 is the beginning of a deceleration trend as project starts slow
- Administration shifts infrastructure spending priorities or claws back IIJA authorizations
- Stagflation deepens, pushing private-sector clients to defer projects
- Forward EPS estimates of ~$4.34 prove too high; at $3.80-$4.00 and 18x, stock trades to $68-72
- No insider buying during 30% drawdown suggests management sees limited near-term catalyst
- Implied fair value: $68-$80 (12-25% downside)

## Conclusion

AECOM is a quality infrastructure franchise trading at a reasonable valuation after a significant drawdown. The record backlog provides genuine visibility into future revenue, and the secular tailwinds from government infrastructure investment are real and multi-year in nature. However, the Q1 revenue miss raises legitimate questions about backlog conversion timing, the macro environment is deteriorating (stagflation confirmed across all six dimensions), and the absence of insider buying during a 30% decline suggests management doesn't see an imminent inflection.

The stock is fairly valued at 21x forward earnings given these crosscurrents. The right entry point would be either: (a) a subsequent quarter demonstrating improved revenue conversion from the backlog, which would validate the growth story, or (b) a further decline to ~$80-$85 (18-19x forward), which would provide adequate margin of safety against the bear case.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** Monitor Q2 FY2026 earnings for revenue conversion improvement. Accumulate below $83 (19x forward) if the backlog thesis remains intact.
