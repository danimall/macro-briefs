## Executive Summary

AECOM is a global infrastructure consulting firm trading at $91.06 after a 29.7% decline over six months, bringing forward P/E to ~21x. The Q1 FY2026 earnings call (February 10, 2026) showed an EPS beat paired with a revenue miss, which triggered further selling. The company reports a record project backlog driven by transportation, water systems, and environmental infrastructure investment globally, providing multi-year revenue visibility.

The core tension: AECOM's asset-light professional services model benefits from secular infrastructure spending trends (IIJA, global climate adaptation, defense-adjacent work), but the current stagflationary macro regime — GDP at 0.7%, oil above $100, NFP at -92K — pressures both government fiscal capacity and private capital investment timelines. The stock has corrected meaningfully and is no longer expensive, but insufficient data granularity on margins, debt structure, and cash flow dynamics prevents a high-conviction BUY recommendation. At 21x forward earnings with a 1.07 beta in a confirmed stagflation environment, the risk-reward is roughly balanced.

## Company Overview

AECOM operates through three segments: Americas, International, and AECOM Capital. The firm provides planning, consulting, architectural and engineering design, construction management, and program management services primarily to government clients (federal, state, local) and private sector entities. Key end markets include transportation infrastructure, water/wastewater, environmental remediation, facilities, and energy. The company's transformation over the past several years has shifted toward a professional services model (divesting the construction services and management services businesses), which reduced revenue volatility and improved margins.

Market cap stands at $11.77B. The company pays a dividend yielding approximately 1.38%.

## Growth Analysis

**Record Backlog:** Multiple news sources (Zacks, March 10) confirm AECOM has a record project backlog, driven by:
- Transportation network investments (IIJA-related federal spending)
- Water systems and environmental infrastructure
- Global infrastructure demand across multiple geographies

The record backlog is the strongest single data point supporting the growth thesis. Infrastructure consulting backlogs typically convert to revenue over 18-36 months, providing visibility well into FY2027-2028. The 20% implied EPS growth (trailing to forward P/E compression) is consistent with backlog conversion plus margin expansion from the professional services transformation.

**Headwinds:**
- Q1 FY2026 revenue missed consensus despite the EPS beat, suggesting either project timing delays or pricing pressure
- Jim Cramer (March 9) specifically noted ACM "didn't have a good quarter" and recommended Quanta Services (PWR) as a preferred alternative — Cramer is often a contrarian signal, but the revenue miss is real
- The stagflation environment introduces risk to government infrastructure spending. Federal budget constraints may delay IIJA disbursements; state/local tax receipts decline in recession, constraining matching funds
- Oil above $100 raises construction input costs (asphalt, diesel, materials), which can delay project starts even when engineering/consulting budgets are funded

**Secular Tailwinds:**
- Climate adaptation infrastructure (water systems, coastal protection, environmental remediation) represents a durable multi-decade demand driver
- Defense-adjacent infrastructure consulting benefits from the current conflict environment (military base construction, logistics infrastructure)
- The professional services model means AECOM captures fees earlier in the project lifecycle, before construction cost inflation hits

## Competitive Landscape

AECOM is one of the largest infrastructure consulting firms globally. Key competitors include:

- **Jacobs Solutions (J):** Closest peer; similar professional services transformation. Both compete for government infrastructure contracts
- **Quanta Services (PWR):** More construction-focused, less comparable but competes in energy and infrastructure
- **WSP Global:** Canadian-headquartered competitor with significant global presence
- **Sterling Infrastructure (STRL):** Smaller, more US-focused

AECOM's competitive advantages include global scale (Americas + International segments), long-standing government relationships (particularly US federal), and the record backlog providing revenue visibility. The shift to professional services has improved the margin profile relative to peers still carrying construction risk.

The infrastructure consulting industry has moderate barriers to entry: government contracting requires track records, security clearances, and established relationships that take years to build. This provides an embedded advantage for incumbents. However, pricing power is constrained by the competitive bidding process typical of government contracts.

Pelican Bay Capital Management initiated a position in Q4 2025, citing value opportunity — a data point supporting the thesis that the drawdown has attracted fundamental buyers.

## Risk Assessment

**Macro Sensitivity (High):** Beta of 1.07 means the stock moves roughly in line with the market. In the current stagflation environment with 38-42% recession probability, cyclical infrastructure spending is at risk. Government budgets face competing demands from defense ($55B/month conflict run rate) and social programs.

**Revenue Miss Signal:** The Q1 FY2026 revenue miss despite an EPS beat suggests potential project delay or cancellation risk. If this pattern persists in Q2, the record backlog narrative weakens because backlog only matters if it converts.

**Oil and Input Cost Inflation:** $100+ oil raises costs for transportation, materials, and energy across AECOM's project base. While AECOM itself is asset-light (consulting fees, not construction costs), clients facing higher construction costs may delay project approvals, slowing backlog conversion.

**Federal Spending Reprioritization:** The current geopolitical environment may redirect federal spending from infrastructure toward defense. Section 301 tariff probes across 60 economies add uncertainty to cross-border infrastructure investment.

**Interest Rate Environment:** FOMC holding rates during stagflation constrains municipal bond issuance used to fund state/local infrastructure. Higher rates for longer means higher project financing costs, potentially reducing project volumes.

**Concentration Risk:** Significant government revenue exposure creates dependency on political budget cycles. Changes in administration priorities could shift infrastructure spending allocations.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- Record backlog converts at or above historical rates, delivering the ~20% EPS growth implied by forward/trailing P/E compression
- Infrastructure spending proves resilient as bipartisan IIJA disbursements continue regardless of macro conditions
- The professional services model provides margin expansion as lower-margin construction work phases out
- The 30% drawdown has priced in most near-term risks; further buying from fundamental investors (like Pelican Bay) provides support near $85-90
- Mean analyst target of ~$129 implies 40%+ upside from current levels
- Climate adaptation and environmental remediation spending accelerates independent of economic cycle

### Bear Case
- Stagflation worsens into recession (38-42% probability), federal/state infrastructure budgets face sequestration or delay
- Revenue miss in Q1 FY2026 is the beginning of a pattern, not an anomaly — backlog conversion slows materially
- Oil above $100 persists, raising project costs and delaying client approvals
- Defense spending crowds out infrastructure appropriations as conflict continues
- Further multiple compression from 21x toward 16-18x on earnings downgrades would take the stock to $70-78
- Insider selling (CLO, CFO disposing shares upon vesting) suggests management doesn't see material upside from current levels

## Conclusion

AECOM at $91 after a 30% drawdown offers a reasonable entry for patient investors with a 2+ year horizon, but the risk-reward is balanced rather than compelling. The record backlog provides genuine multi-year visibility, and the professional services transformation has improved the business model structurally. However, the Q1 revenue miss, stagflationary macro environment, and potential for government spending reprioritization toward defense create meaningful near-term headwinds.

The stock is approximately fairly valued at 21x forward earnings in the current environment. A recession that delays infrastructure spending could push the stock toward the mid-$70s; successful backlog conversion and margin expansion could return it toward $115-125 over 12-18 months. Without detailed financial statements to assess debt levels, cash flow generation, and margin trends, conviction must remain moderate.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The drawdown has improved the valuation, but initiating a position requires either (a) confirmation that Q2 revenue trends improve, demonstrating backlog conversion, or (b) a further 10-15% decline toward the $78-82 range that provides genuine margin of safety. The 1.07 beta with 38-42% recession probability means further downside is plausible.

**Why Not BUY:** Revenue miss in Q1 despite EPS beat signals potential backlog conversion delays. 21x forward P/E with 1.07 beta in stagflation offers insufficient margin of safety. Insider selling at $97-99 (above current price) by C-suite officers suggests internal assessment that shares were fairly valued at higher levels. Limited financial statement data prevents assessment of debt load and cash flow resilience.
