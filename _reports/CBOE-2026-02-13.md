## Executive Summary

Cboe Global Markets is one of the world's largest exchange holding companies, operating across options, futures, equities, FX, and digital assets. The company delivered a standout fiscal year 2025, achieving record diluted EPS of $10.42 for the full year and $2.97 for Q4 2025, representing approximately 60% year-over-year growth in Q4 diluted EPS (compared to $2.10 a year ago on an adjusted basis of $3.06 vs. consensus of $2.93). The strong results were driven by robust derivatives trading activity and continued strength across its core product lines.

Looking ahead, management has guided for mid-single-digit net revenue growth in FY2026 with disciplined expense management. The company is also exploring new growth avenues including prediction markets, which leadership described as a "logical extension" of Cboe's capabilities. With a market capitalization of approximately $28.3 billion and a beta of just 0.36, CBOE offers a compelling combination of defensive characteristics and exposure to secular growth in derivatives markets.

However, the stock currently trades at roughly 25.7x earnings, which is at the higher end of fair value for a financial exchange business growing in the mid-single digits organically. Morgan Stanley maintains an Underweight recommendation, and the average analyst price target of ~$271.66 is essentially at parity with the current price of $270.60, suggesting limited near-term upside from current levels.

## Company Overview

Cboe Global Markets Inc is a leading global exchange operator headquartered in Chicago. The company operates regulated exchanges and platforms that facilitate trading across multiple asset classes:

- **Options**: Cboe is the largest U.S. options exchange by volume, home to the proprietary VIX Index and SPX options
- **Futures**: Operates Cboe Futures Exchange (CFE) offering VIX futures and other products
- **U.S. and European Equities**: Runs multiple equities exchanges in the U.S. and Europe
- **FX**: Operates Cboe FX, an institutional spot FX trading platform
- **Digital Assets**: Has expanded into digital asset data and trading
- **Global Listings**: Operates listing venues including Cboe Australia (currently in a sale process)

The company's core competitive advantage lies in its proprietary products — particularly SPX options, VIX futures, and the VIX Index itself — which cannot be replicated by competitors, creating a durable economic moat.

## Financial Analysis

### Earnings Performance

Cboe delivered exceptional results in FY2025:

- **Full Year 2025 Diluted EPS**: $10.42 (record), representing substantial year-over-year growth
- **Q4 2025 GAAP Diluted EPS**: $2.97 (record for a quarter), up ~60% YoY
- **Q4 2025 Adjusted EPS**: $3.06, beating the Zacks Consensus Estimate of $2.93 by $0.13 (4.4% beat)
- **Q4 2024 Adjusted EPS comparison**: $2.10 per share, indicating ~46% adjusted EPS growth YoY

The Q4 beat was driven by strength in the derivatives business, which benefits from elevated market volatility and increasing demand for hedging instruments.

### Profitability & Capital Allocation

While detailed financial statements were not available in the data feed, exchange businesses typically operate with very high operating margins (40-55%), and Cboe's record EPS performance suggests margins are at or near peak levels. The company pays a dividend yielding approximately 1.07%, reflecting a conservative payout ratio that preserves capital for reinvestment and share repurchases.

### Balance Sheet

Cboe has historically maintained a conservative balance sheet appropriate for an exchange operator. The company's asset-light business model generates significant free cash flow relative to capital expenditure requirements, typical of exchange businesses.

### Low Beta Profile

Cboe's beta of 0.36 is notably low, indicating the stock has historically moved significantly less than the broader market. This is partially a function of the counter-cyclical nature of some of its products — volatility products (VIX) tend to see higher volumes during market stress, providing a natural hedge within the business.

## Growth Analysis

### Recent Growth Trajectory

The 60% YoY growth in Q4 diluted EPS and record full-year EPS of $10.42 demonstrate a strong growth trajectory. This was likely driven by:

1. **Record options volumes**: U.S. options markets have seen explosive growth, particularly in 0DTE (zero days to expiration) options, where Cboe has been a primary beneficiary via SPX options
2. **Derivatives market expansion**: Growing institutional and retail participation in options and volatility products
3. **International expansion**: Growing European derivatives and equities business

### FY2026 Guidance

Management issued guidance for FY2026 calling for:
- **Mid-single-digit net revenue growth**
- **Disciplined expense management**

This suggests organic revenue growth in the 4-6% range, which is respectable but more modest than the exceptional 2025 performance.

### New Growth Vectors

**Prediction Markets**: Cboe is positioning itself to enter the prediction market space, which management described as a "logical extension" of its capabilities. Rob Hocking, global head of derivatives, expressed excitement about financial and economic event contracts. This represents a potentially significant new addressable market as prediction markets gain regulatory clarity and mainstream acceptance.

**International Expansion**: Cboe continues to expand globally, though the sale process for Cboe Australia suggests a strategic refocusing. The Australian unit reportedly sees strong demand for its listings market and anticipates more mining IPOs.

### Growth Score Considerations

- Strong secular tailwinds in options/derivatives volumes
- New product opportunities (prediction markets, 0DTE expansion)
- Mid-single-digit revenue guidance is solid but not exceptional
- International strategy appears to be rationalizing (Australian sale process)

## Competitive Landscape

### Market Position

Cboe holds a dominant position in several key markets:

1. **SPX Options**: Cboe has an effective monopoly on S&P 500 Index options, its most important product
2. **VIX Products**: Complete ownership of the VIX franchise (index, options, futures)
3. **U.S. Options**: Largest U.S. options exchange by volume
4. **Multi-listed Options**: Strong competitive position among approximately 16 U.S. options exchanges

### Competitive Moat

Cboe's moat is among the strongest in the exchange industry:

- **Proprietary Products**: SPX and VIX products cannot be traded elsewhere — this is an irreplaceable franchise
- **Network Effects**: Liquidity begets liquidity; deep order books attract more participants
- **Regulatory Barriers**: Exchange licensing creates high barriers to entry
- **Data Revenue**: Proprietary data and indices generate high-margin, recurring revenue

### Key Competitors

- **CME Group (CME)**: Largest derivatives exchange globally; competes in futures
- **Intercontinental Exchange (ICE)**: Owns NYSE; competes across multiple asset classes
- **Nasdaq Inc (NDAQ)**: Competes in equities, options, and market data
- **MIAX/BOX**: Smaller options exchange competitors

### Industry Dynamics

The exchange industry benefits from secular growth in derivatives usage, regulatory complexity driving demand for listed products, and increasing global market participation. The 0DTE options phenomenon has been a particular tailwind for Cboe.

## Risk Assessment

### Key Risks

1. **Volume Cyclicality**: While Cboe benefits from volatility, sustained periods of very low volatility can compress trading volumes and revenue. The current elevated VIX and active options market may not persist.

2. **Regulatory Risk**: Exchange operators are heavily regulated. Changes in options market structure, position limits, or new regulations around 0DTE options could impact Cboe's business.

3. **Competitive Pressure**: Multi-listed options face ongoing price competition. New exchange entrants (e.g., MEMX) and consolidation among competitors could pressure market share and pricing.

4. **Concentration Risk**: Heavy dependence on SPX and VIX products means any regulatory action targeting these specific products (or reduced demand) would be disproportionately impactful.

5. **Technology Risk**: Exchange operations require zero-downtime technology. System outages can damage reputation and result in regulatory fines.

6. **International Expansion Uncertainty**: The sale process for Cboe Australia suggests international growth hasn't met expectations in all markets.

7. **Prediction Markets**: While an opportunity, regulatory uncertainty around prediction markets could delay or limit this growth vector.

### Mitigating Factors

- Low beta (0.36) provides portfolio diversification benefits
- Counter-cyclical elements (VIX products benefit from market stress)
- Strong regulatory moat protects existing franchise
- Diversified revenue across options, futures, equities, FX, and data
- Conservative balance sheet management

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case 🐂

1. **Secular Options Growth**: The options market continues its structural expansion, with 0DTE options driving incremental volume growth. Cboe, as the dominant options exchange with proprietary SPX products, is the primary beneficiary of this trend.

2. **Record Earnings Momentum**: FY2025's record EPS of $10.42 and 60% Q4 YoY growth demonstrate powerful operating leverage. Mid-single-digit revenue growth in 2026 with disciplined expenses could drive continued EPS expansion.

3. **Prediction Markets Optionality**: The nascent prediction markets space could become a significant new revenue stream. As a regulated exchange with existing infrastructure, Cboe is ideally positioned to capture this opportunity.

4. **Defensive Characteristics**: With a beta of 0.36, Cboe provides portfolio protection during market downturns while still participating in upside through growing derivatives volumes.

5. **Irreplaceable Franchise**: SPX and VIX products represent an unassailable competitive moat that will continue generating premium returns on capital.

### Bear Case 🐻

1. **Valuation Concern**: At ~26x earnings with mid-single-digit organic growth guidance, the stock is priced for near-perfection. Any disappointment in volumes or execution could lead to multiple compression.

2. **Peak Volumes**: The current options trading boom (particularly 0DTE) may be cyclically elevated. Mean reversion in volatility and trading activity could pressure results.

3. **Morgan Stanley Underweight**: A major sell-side firm's bearish stance suggests informed institutional investors see limited upside at current levels.

4. **Analyst Price Target at Parity**: The average price target of ~$271.66 essentially matches the current price, suggesting the market has fully priced in near-term positives.

5. **Regulatory Headwinds**: Potential SEC scrutiny of 0DTE options, market structure reform, or prediction market regulatory delays could create headwinds.

6. **International Challenges**: The Australian divestiture signals that global expansion hasn't delivered consistent returns, potentially limiting the addressable growth opportunity.
