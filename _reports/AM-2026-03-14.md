## Executive Summary

Antero Midstream Corp is a midstream energy company that has returned 271% over three years, driven by Appalachian natural gas gathering/processing volumes and a consistent dividend policy. The stock trades at $23.14, near its 52-week high of $23.35, at approximately 26.5x forward earnings with a 3.9% dividend yield. The recently closed HG Mid acquisition and $1.2B Utica asset sale to Infinity Natural Resources together reposition the asset base, and management guided to double-digit free cash flow growth in 2026.

The central tension: AM's valuation at ~26.5x forward P/E is elevated for a midstream company (peers typically trade at 10-16x), and multiple insiders sold shares in March 2026 immediately after receiving equity grants. The stock's 0.77 beta and fee-based revenue model provide defensive characteristics during the current Iran conflict and energy volatility. However, the three-year run has pulled forward substantial value, and the financial data available is insufficient to independently verify the FCF growth trajectory management has outlined.

## Company Overview

Antero Midstream operates gathering pipelines, compression stations, processing and fractionation plants, and water handling assets primarily serving Antero Resources (AR), its upstream parent, in the Appalachian Basin. The company's business model is fee-based: revenue is tied to throughput volumes rather than commodity prices directly, though indirectly it depends on AR's drilling activity, which responds to natural gas and NGL prices.

Key recent corporate actions:
- **HG Mid acquisition** closed in early February 2026, expanding the asset base and development project pipeline
- **$1.2B Utica asset sale** to Infinity Natural Resources completed February 2026, funding the acquisition and reshaping the portfolio
- **Q4 2025 earnings**: EPS of $0.28 vs. $0.26 consensus (7.7% beat), revenue up $9.53M YoY

The company has a market cap of approximately $10.9B.

## Financial Analysis

The financial statement data provided is empty, which significantly limits the depth of quantitative analysis. Working from available data points:

**Earnings:** Q4 2025 EPS of $0.28 implies annualized earnings of approximately $1.12 per share. At $23.14, this yields a trailing P/E of ~20.7x, though the reported forward and trailing P/E of 26.5x suggests quarterly variation (the $0.28 Q4 may have been above-trend). The Q3 2025 EPS miss ($0.24 vs. $0.25 consensus) indicates uneven quarterly performance.

**Dividend:** The 3.9% yield at current price translates to approximately $0.90-0.91 per share annually. For a midstream MLP-successor C-corp, this yield is moderate — below the 5-7% yields common among larger midstream peers (e.g., ET, MPLX, WMB historically yielded higher). The dividend is a meaningful component of total return for income-oriented holders.

**Balance Sheet:** Without balance sheet data, leverage ratios cannot be independently assessed. Midstream companies typically carry 3-5x net debt/EBITDA. The HG Mid acquisition and Utica divestiture likely altered the capital structure, though management funded the acquisition partially through asset sale proceeds, which suggests discipline around leverage.

**Cash Flow:** Management guided to "double-digit growth in free cash flow" for 2026 on the Q4 call. If 2025 FCF was in the range of $400-500M (estimated from market cap and typical midstream FCF yields), double-digit growth would imply $440-550M+ in 2026 FCF, or roughly $0.93-1.16 per share on ~473M diluted shares. This would represent a 4-5% FCF yield at current prices — adequate but not compelling.

## Growth Analysis

The primary growth driver is the HG Mid acquisition, which management described as "immediately increasing" the asset base and future development projects. The transaction structure — selling Utica assets for $1.2B to fund new midstream assets — is a portfolio repositioning rather than a pure expansion. The net growth impact depends on the relative cash flow contribution of acquired vs. divested assets.

Appalachian natural gas volumes provide a secular tailwind: the basin remains one of the lowest-cost natural gas production areas in the US. Antero Resources' drilling activity directly drives AM's throughput. With natural gas prices benefiting from LNG export demand growth and the current Iran conflict disrupting global energy supply chains, upstream activity should remain robust.

Growth constraints: AM's heavy dependence on a single counterparty (Antero Resources) limits diversification. The HG Mid acquisition may broaden the customer base somewhat, but the company remains fundamentally an Appalachian Basin midstream operator tied to one primary E&P company.

## Valuation Assessment

At 26.5x forward P/E, AM trades at a significant premium to midstream peers:

- Enterprise Products Partners (EPD): ~10-11x forward P/E
- MPLX: ~10-12x
- Williams Companies (WMB): ~20-22x
- Kinder Morgan (KMI): ~18-20x
- Targa Resources (TRGP): ~18-20x

AM's premium likely reflects: (1) the 271% three-year return creating momentum-driven multiple expansion, (2) the "double-digit FCF growth" guidance for 2026, and (3) the stock's C-corp structure (no K-1 tax complexity), which broadens the investor base versus MLPs.

Even accounting for above-average growth, 26.5x forward earnings is stretched for a company whose revenue is fundamentally tied to one customer's drilling program in one basin. If the guided FCF growth materializes, the forward FCF yield of ~4-5% provides modest support, but the earnings multiple would need to compress to peer levels (~15-18x) for a new buyer to earn adequate returns.

The stock is 53% above its 52-week low of $15.08, leaving limited margin of safety.

## Competitive Landscape

AM operates in a structurally favorable position as the dedicated midstream provider for Antero Resources in the Appalachian Basin. Long-term, fee-based gathering and processing contracts with minimum volume commitments provide revenue visibility. The relationship with AR functions as both competitive advantage (captive volumes) and risk (customer concentration).

Within the broader midstream sector, AM is mid-sized at $10.9B market cap. Larger diversified operators (EPD at ~$65B, WMB at ~$70B, KMI at ~$50B) benefit from geographic and customer diversification that AM lacks. AM compensates through operational focus and the structural growth of Appalachian gas production.

The HG Mid acquisition strengthens AM's gathering footprint, potentially adding third-party volumes that reduce counterparty concentration. The degree to which this diversification materializes will be a key metric to track over 2026-2027.

## Risk Assessment

**Customer Concentration:** AM's dependence on Antero Resources is the defining risk. If AR reduces drilling activity, faces financial distress, or shifts development priorities, AM's volumes decline proportionally. AR's financial health and capex plans are effectively AM's growth determinants.

**Valuation Risk:** At 26.5x forward P/E and near the 52-week high, the stock has limited cushion against negative surprises. A miss on the "double-digit FCF growth" guidance or a broader midstream multiple compression would disproportionately affect AM given its premium valuation.

**Insider Selling:** Multiple executives sold shares in March 2026. Yvette Schultz sold 25,000 shares at $22.81 ($570K), Sheri Pearce sold 14,000 shares at $22.76 ($319K), and Director Brooks Klimley sold 5,000 shares at $23.16 ($116K). These sales followed equity grant vesting, which partially mitigates the signal — but the fact that multiple insiders chose to sell rather than retain the vested shares suggests limited conviction in further upside at current levels.

**Macro Environment:** The Iran conflict has pushed oil to ~$100/bbl and gasoline up 25%. While AM's fee-based model provides insulation from commodity price swings, the broader stagflationary environment (GDP 0.7%, core PCE 3.1%) could compress equity multiples across sectors. AM's 0.77 beta suggests lower downside participation, but the elevated starting multiple creates vulnerability.

**Interest Rate Sensitivity:** Midstream companies compete with fixed income for yield-oriented capital. With Treasury yields elevated and the Fed holding rates steady amid inflation, AM's 3.9% dividend yield is less competitive than it would be in a lower-rate environment.

## Investment Thesis

**Bull Case:**
- HG Mid acquisition delivers on the double-digit FCF growth guidance, pushing 2026 FCF to $500M+
- Appalachian natural gas production grows as LNG export capacity expands, driving volume growth beyond acquisition effects
- C-corp structure attracts institutional capital that avoids MLP K-1 complexity
- 0.77 beta and fee-based model provide defensive positioning during current energy/geopolitical volatility
- Dividend grows in line with FCF, supporting total returns of 8-12% annually

**Bear Case:**
- 26.5x forward P/E compresses toward peer average of 15-18x, implying 30-40% downside even with flat earnings
- Antero Resources reduces drilling activity if natural gas prices weaken from current elevated levels
- Multiple insider sales signal near-term exhaustion of the 271% three-year rally
- Integration risk on HG Mid acquisition: development project costs exceed estimates or timeline extends
- Rising interest rates make the 3.9% dividend yield insufficiently competitive versus risk-free alternatives

## Conclusion

AM is a well-run midstream operator with a clear growth catalyst (HG Mid acquisition) and defensive characteristics (0.77 beta, fee-based revenue, consistent dividend). The problem is price. At 26.5x forward earnings and near the 52-week high, the stock has priced in the acquisition-driven growth thesis and then some. Multiple insider sales in March 2026, while partially explained by vesting schedules, do not inspire confidence in incremental upside.

For existing holders, the position is reasonable to maintain given the dividend income and defensive profile during the current stagflationary environment. For new capital, the risk-reward is poor: the upside case requires continued multiple expansion or FCF growth meaningfully exceeding guidance, while the downside case involves substantial P/E compression toward midstream peer averages.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The stock requires either a meaningful pullback (toward $17-19, representing 12-16x forward P/E) or demonstrated execution on the FCF growth trajectory over 2-3 quarters to warrant a BUY. At current levels, the 271% three-year return has extracted most of the available value.
