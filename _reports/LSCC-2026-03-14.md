## Executive Summary

Lattice Semiconductor is a low-power FPGA specialist recovering from a significant cyclical downturn that bottomed in 2024-2025. Q4 2025 results showed 24% YoY revenue growth and EPS of $0.32 (more than doubled from $0.15 a year prior), with management guiding for further sequential acceleration. The company's positioning in AI edge inference, automotive, industrial, and communications markets provides a credible multi-year growth story centered on its Nexus and Avant product families.

The core problem is valuation. At $85.77 per share and an $11.7B market cap, the stock trades at approximately 3,800x trailing earnings. Even assuming aggressive forward estimates (e.g., $0.32/quarter annualized = ~$1.28 EPS), the forward P/E would be ~67x. This prices in years of flawless execution in an industry subject to cyclical demand swings, customer inventory management, and macro sensitivity. A 1.71 beta amplifies downside risk in the current stagflationary environment. Heavy insider selling across six executives in March 2026, totaling over $3.5M, provides a concrete negative signal from those with the best visibility into near-term business conditions.

## Company Overview

Lattice Semiconductor designs and sells low-power, small form factor FPGAs (Field Programmable Gate Arrays). Unlike Xilinx (now AMD) and Altera (now Intel subsidiary, potentially re-IPO'd), Lattice targets the low-to-mid-range FPGA market where power efficiency and small physical footprint matter more than raw compute performance. Key end markets include communications/computing (~50% of revenue historically), industrial/automotive (~50%), and an expanding presence in AI edge inference applications.

The company underwent a significant transformation under former CEO Jim Anderson (2018-2023) that shifted the product portfolio upmarket and expanded margins substantially. Ford Tamer succeeded as CEO and has continued this trajectory. Lattice is fabless, outsourcing manufacturing to foundries, which limits capital intensity but creates supply chain dependencies.

## Valuation Assessment

This is where the investment case breaks down quantitatively.

**At $85.77 with ~137M shares outstanding (implied by $11.73B market cap / $85.77):**
- If annualized adjusted EPS is ~$1.28 (4x Q4's $0.32): Forward P/E ~67x
- If the growth trajectory continues and FY2026 adjusted EPS reaches $1.50-$1.80: Forward P/E 48-57x
- The 3,804x trailing P/E in the profile data, even if reflecting GAAP distortions, signals that current reported profitability is negligible relative to the market cap.

**Peer comparison:**
- AMD (which owns Xilinx, Lattice's larger FPGA competitor): trades at ~22-28x forward earnings
- Intel FPGA/Altera: undergoing restructuring, but when valued independently, has traded at lower multiples
- Microchip Technology (MCHP), another analog/embedded competitor: ~18-25x forward

Lattice has historically commanded a premium due to its higher growth rate and margin expansion story. A 40-50x forward P/E was justified during the 2021-2023 upcycle when revenue growth was 25-35% and margins were expanding 200-400 bps annually. At the current recovery stage, where growth is cyclical recovery rather than structural acceleration, and where macro conditions create significant demand uncertainty, 67x+ forward earnings is aggressive.

**DCF consideration:** Without reliable revenue and margin projections, a formal DCF is not possible. However, to justify $11.7B at a 10% discount rate, the company would need to generate roughly $700-900M in free cash flow within 5-7 years. Peak revenue was approximately $740M (2023), with FCF margins historically in the 25-30% range, implying peak FCF of ~$185-220M. Revenue would need to roughly triple from the 2023 peak to justify the current valuation on a DCF basis.

## Competitive Landscape

**Lattice's competitive position has several genuine strengths:**
1. **Niche dominance in low-power FPGAs:** Neither AMD/Xilinx nor Intel/Altera competes aggressively at the low end of the FPGA market. Lattice owns this segment.
2. **Software ecosystem:** Lattice Radiant and associated IP libraries create meaningful switching costs once customers design in Lattice FPGAs.
3. **Fabless model:** Limits capital requirements and allows flexible foundry sourcing.
4. **Expanding TAM:** The Avant family moves Lattice into mid-range FPGAs, where ASPs are 3-5x higher than the traditional Nexus/iCE40 range.

**Competitive risks:**
1. AMD/Xilinx could push downmarket with adaptive SoC products that overlap with Lattice's expanding mid-range.
2. ASIC alternatives: as AI edge inference volumes grow, customers may shift from FPGAs to custom ASICs, which are more cost-effective at scale.
3. Chinese FPGA competitors (Gowin, Pango) are gaining capability in the low-end segment, particularly for price-sensitive industrial applications.

## Risk Assessment

**Macro/cyclical risk (HIGH):** Lattice's industrial and automotive end markets are directly exposed to the current stagflationary environment. A 1.71 beta means the stock amplifies market moves in both directions. In the world model's 38-42% recession probability scenario, semiconductor demand (particularly industrial/automotive) would decline meaningfully.

**Valuation risk (VERY HIGH):** At 67x+ forward adjusted earnings, any disappointment in growth trajectory, margin expansion, or end-market demand gets punished severely. The stock already fell 14.2% in the month following a quarter that beat estimates. This price action suggests the market requires continuous positive surprises to maintain the valuation.

**Insider selling (MODERATE-HIGH):** Between March 4-11, 2026, six executives sold shares:

**Concentration risk:** Lattice derives significant revenue from a small number of distributors and end customers. Loss of a major customer or distributor relationship would have outsized impact.

**Supply chain risk:** As a fabless company, Lattice depends on foundry partners (primarily TSMC and others). The current geopolitical environment (see world model: sulphur disruption, helium scarcity, Iran-related supply chain effects) adds incremental risk to semiconductor fabrication.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- Lattice is the only pure-play low-power FPGA company, with a dominant position in a growing niche that benefits from AI edge inference, automotive ADAS, industrial automation, and 5G infrastructure.
- The Avant product family expands the addressable market by 3-5x, moving into mid-range FPGAs with higher ASPs and margins.
- Revenue is recovering at 24%+ YoY growth with sequential acceleration; management guidance is optimistic.
- If Lattice can sustain 20-25% annual revenue growth and expand operating margins to 35-40% (from mid-20s currently), the stock could grow into its valuation over 2-3 years.
- AI edge inference is a genuine secular tailwind that maps directly to Lattice's low-power, small form factor competitive advantage.

### Bear Case
- At ~67x forward adjusted earnings (and ~3,800x GAAP), the stock prices in multiple years of flawless execution with zero margin for error.
- The current "recovery" includes cyclical restocking that will normalize, making 24% growth unsustainable.
- Industrial and automotive end markets face macro headwinds from stagflation, $100+ oil, and potential recession (38-42% probability per world model).
- 1.71 beta means the stock falls ~1.7x the market in a broad selloff — and the world model assigns 40-50% probability to further equity correction.
- Broad-based C-suite insider selling in March 2026 suggests management doesn't believe the stock is undervalued at $90-95.
- Chinese FPGA competitors and potential AMD/Xilinx downmarket moves could compress margins over time.
- ASIC alternatives for high-volume AI edge inference could limit the long-term FPGA opportunity as volumes scale.

## Conclusion

Lattice Semiconductor has a defensible competitive position in low-power FPGAs, a credible growth trajectory driven by AI edge inference and Avant platform adoption, and is clearly recovering from a cyclical trough. The product roadmap is sound, and the 24% YoY revenue growth with sequential acceleration demonstrates execution.

The recommendation is HOLD because the valuation leaves no margin of safety. At 67x+ forward adjusted earnings and ~3,800x trailing GAAP P/E, the stock requires sustained 20%+ growth for multiple years to justify the current price. The macro environment (stagflation, 38-42% recession probability, $100+ oil) creates meaningful demand risk for industrial and automotive semiconductors. The 1.71 beta amplifies drawdown risk, and broad C-suite insider selling at $90-95 suggests the people with the best visibility aren't buying.

For existing holders, the secular growth story provides a reason to maintain the position. For new money, waiting for a pullback to a more reasonable entry point (40-50x forward earnings, implying $50-65 on $1.28 EPS, or $60-80 if FY2026 EPS reaches $1.50+) would substantially improve the risk-reward profile. The stock was at $34.69 as recently as 52 weeks ago; patience may be rewarded given macro conditions.

**Why not BUY (per calibration gate):** Valuation is extreme on every metric. Insider selling is broad and recent. Macro headwinds to industrial/automotive semiconductor demand are significant. The calibration data shows high-beta cyclical names with elevated valuations are precisely where BUY calls have historically produced the worst losses.

**Why not AVOID:** The business is genuinely recovering. The AI edge inference positioning is real and differentiated. The competitive moat in low-power FPGAs is durable. Revenue growth is accelerating, not decelerating. The stock is already 21% below its 52-week high, providing some buffer.
