## Executive Summary

PJT Partners is a pure-play advisory investment bank that reported record full-year and Q4 2025 results, driven by strength across Strategic Advisory, Restructuring, and Park Hill (fund placement). Q4 adjusted EPS of $2.55 beat consensus by 6%, though revenue missed expectations and the stock sold off ~7% on the day. The firm operates an asset-light model with a strong competitive position in restructuring advisory and large-cap M&A.

The stock has fallen 33% from its January 2026 high of $195.62 to $131.76 as of March 17, 2026. This decline reflects two converging forces: (1) post-earnings disappointment on the revenue miss, and (2) a rapidly deteriorating macro environment — confirmed stagflation, rising recession probability (38-42%), and tightening credit markets — that directly threatens M&A deal volumes. Insider transactions show CEO Paul Taubman converting 36,000 partnership units at $159.92 (~$5.8M) on February 5, two days after earnings. At ~19.7x forward earnings, the stock is priced for moderate growth continuation, which may prove optimistic if deal activity contracts in the current macro regime.

## Company Overview

PJT Partners (NYSE: PJT) is a global advisory-focused investment bank spun off from Blackstone in 2015. The firm operates three core business lines: Strategic Advisory (M&A, capital markets advisory, shareholder activism defense), Restructuring & Special Situations, and PJT Park Hill (private fund placement and secondary advisory). The firm has approximately 24.2 million shares outstanding and a market cap of ~$3.15 billion. It is led by founder and CEO Paul Taubman, a former Morgan Stanley investment banking co-head.

The business model is entirely fee-based with no balance sheet risk from proprietary trading or lending. Revenue is overwhelmingly derived from advisory and placement fees, making it one of the purest plays on the advisory cycle in public markets.

## Financial Analysis

**Record FY2025 Results:** The company reported record annual and quarterly results for FY2025. Key figures from the Q4 earnings release:

- Q4 GAAP net income: $53.4 million ($1.97/share GAAP, $2.55 adjusted)
- Q4 adjusted EPS of $2.55 vs. consensus estimate of $2.40 — a 6.0% beat
- Full-year GAAP compensation and benefits expense: $1.16 billion (up from $1.03 billion prior year, a 12.6% increase)
- Adjusted compensation expense: $1.15 billion vs. $1.03 billion

**Revenue dynamics:** While the company beat on earnings, revenue missed expectations. Benzinga reported revenue was up $57.88 million year-over-year in Q4, but the miss on the top line triggered the 6.9% selloff on February 3.

**Profitability:** Trailing EPS of $6.68 on the current share count implies approximately $161 million in annual GAAP earnings. The compensation ratio (the key profitability lever for advisory firms) appears to have been managed well given the firm's record results, though the $130 million YoY increase in comp expense (12.6%) outpaces what appears to be more modest revenue growth in certain quarters.

**Balance Sheet:** Detailed financial statements were not available in the data feed, but as a pure advisory firm, PJT carries minimal balance sheet risk. The business requires almost no capital — no loan book, no trading inventory, no significant fixed assets beyond office leases. This is structurally advantageous. Cash flow conversion from earnings should be high, though the partnership unit structure creates dilution dynamics that require monitoring.

**Compensation structure:** The firm's cost base is dominated by comp and benefits ($1.15-1.16 billion annually), typical for elite advisory firms where the primary asset is human capital. The ratio of comp to revenue is the key margin variable. Partner compensation through partnership units creates ongoing dilution pressure.

## Growth Analysis

**Positive trajectory through FY2025:** Record results were driven by strength across all three segments. The earnings call highlighted particular strength in Strategic Advisory and a "strong finish" in Restructuring and Park Hill.

**Restructuring tailwind:** The current macro environment — confirmed stagflation, 55-60% credit cascade probability, $98-108 oil — should be a significant positive for PJT's restructuring practice. Higher interest rates, tighter credit, and potential recession drive restructuring volumes. This provides a natural hedge against M&A weakness.

**Strategic Advisory headwinds:** The M&A cycle is the primary concern. Stagflation, geopolitical uncertainty (Iran conflict), and a potential recession (38-42% probability) historically compress M&A volumes. Large-cap M&A mandates — PJT's core — are the most sensitive to CEO confidence, board risk appetite, and financing availability. With credit markets under stress (EA's $18B junk offering as a live test this week), leveraged buyout activity faces particular pressure.

**Park Hill fund placement:** Private fund placement may face headwinds from the credit cascade risk and potential restrictions on semi-liquid fund redemptions. If Ares or KKR restrict redemptions by March 31, this could dampen LP appetite for new fund commitments, directly affecting Park Hill's pipeline.

**Net growth outlook:** The offsetting dynamics of restructuring tailwinds vs. M&A and placement headwinds make the net growth trajectory uncertain. In a mild recession, restructuring growth could offset M&A declines. In a severe downturn with frozen credit markets, even restructuring mandates may be delayed as companies attempt to survive rather than restructure.

## Valuation Assessment

**Current valuation:** At $131.76, PJT trades at approximately:
- 19.7x trailing and forward P/E (on $6.68 EPS)
- Market cap: ~$3.15 billion
- Dividend yield: ~0.77%

**Historical context:** The stock hit $195.62 on January 22, 2026, implying PJT traded at ~29x trailing EPS at the peak. The current 19.7x represents a 32% de-rating in under two months. The 52-week low of $119.76 (reached on April 7, 2025) implies 9% further downside risk to trough levels.

**Peer comparison:** Compared to other independent advisory firms (Evercore, Lazard, Moelis), 19.7x is a moderate premium that reflects PJT's restructuring counter-cyclicality and perceived quality. Evercore typically trades at 12-16x, Lazard at 10-14x, Moelis at 14-18x. PJT's premium is justified by its higher growth rate and restructuring hedge, but in a macro regime where M&A volumes may contract 20-30%, this premium is vulnerable.

**Valuation verdict:** At 19.7x, PJT is priced for earnings stability or modest growth. If FY2026 EPS declines 10-15% due to M&A contraction (partially offset by restructuring gains), the stock trades at 22-24x forward — expensive for a cyclical advisory firm in recession. A more attractive entry would be at 15-16x trailing (approximately $100-107), where restructuring growth alone could support the valuation.

## Competitive Landscape

PJT Partners occupies a strong position in independent advisory. Key competitive advantages:

1. **Restructuring franchise:** One of the top 3-5 restructuring advisory practices globally, alongside Lazard, Houlihan Lokey, and Evercore. This provides counter-cyclical revenue that bulge-bracket banks (Goldman, Morgan Stanley, JPM) cannot easily replicate due to conflicts from their lending businesses.

2. **Founder-led:** Paul Taubman's relationships and reputation, built over decades at Morgan Stanley, provide deal-sourcing advantages in large-cap advisory. This is also a key-person risk.

3. **No balance sheet risk:** Pure advisory model eliminates credit, trading, and liquidity risk that affects diversified banks. During credit crunches, PJT can advise while banks must manage their own exposure.

4. **Park Hill differentiation:** The fund placement business provides a third revenue leg that most advisory peers lack. However, this business is directly exposed to LP sentiment and fundraising cycles.

5. **Talent retention:** The partnership unit structure aligns incentives but creates dilution. Competition for advisory talent from bulge brackets offering guaranteed packages during weak markets is an ongoing risk.

## Risk Assessment

**Macro sensitivity (HIGH):** Advisory revenue is inherently cyclical. The current stagflationary regime, with 38-42% recession probability and tightening credit conditions, directly threatens M&A volumes. The Hormuz disruption and $98-108 oil create additional uncertainty that makes corporate boards reluctant to pursue transformative transactions.

**Insider selling (MODERATE-HIGH):** The insider transaction data reveals significant conversions shortly after earnings:
- CEO Paul Taubman: 36,000 partnership units at $159.92 = $5.76 million (February 5)
- Kenneth Whitney: 30,000 units at $159.92 = $4.80 million (February 5)
- David Travin (General Counsel): 8,264 units at $159.92 = $1.32 million (February 5)

Total insider monetization: ~$11.9 million within two days of earnings. These are partnership unit conversions (not open-market sales), which are partially mechanical, but the timing and magnitude suggest insiders were motivated to lock in value near $160. The stock subsequently fell to $129.

**Revenue concentration:** PJT derives a disproportionate share of revenue from a relatively small number of large mandates. Loss of one or two major engagements in any quarter can cause significant revenue volatility.

**Key-person risk:** CEO Taubman is central to the firm's brand and relationship network. His departure or reduced involvement would be a material negative event.

**Credit cascade exposure:** If the 55-60% credit cascade materializes, two effects follow: (1) LBO and leveraged M&A volumes collapse (negative for Strategic Advisory), and (2) restructuring mandates eventually increase (positive, but with a lag of 6-12 months). The net near-term impact is negative.

**Dilution:** Ongoing partnership unit conversions create structural dilution. The March 2 RSU conversions for CFO Meates (9,627 shares), Managing Partner Lee (12,944 shares), General Counsel Travin (5,052 shares), and Cornwell (18,865 shares combined) total ~46,500 shares — roughly 0.2% dilution in a single day.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- Restructuring revenue accelerates materially as the credit cycle turns, with 55-60% cascade probability driving a wave of mandates in H2 2026 and into 2027
- 33% pullback from January highs creates a reasonable entry point if M&A volumes hold up better than feared
- Pure advisory model with no balance sheet risk provides safety during credit stress
- Record FY2025 results demonstrate operating leverage and franchise strength
- Park Hill benefits if distressed fund strategies see increased fundraising

### Bear Case
- M&A volumes contract 20-30% in a stagflationary recession, overwhelming restructuring gains
- At 19.7x trailing, the stock still carries a premium multiple that assumes growth — vulnerable to further de-rating toward 14-16x in a downturn (implying ~$94-107)
- $11.9 million in insider monetization within 48 hours of earnings suggests management's private valuation assessment is below market
- Credit cascade freezes leveraged finance markets, eliminating a significant portion of Strategic Advisory pipeline
- Key-person risk and talent retention challenges during fee compression periods
- Quarterly revenue lumpiness amplified by reduced deal flow creates negative earnings surprises

## Conclusion

PJT Partners is a high-quality advisory franchise that reported record results for FY2025 and possesses a valuable restructuring practice that should benefit from the current credit cycle. However, the macro environment is hostile to the M&A business that drives the majority of revenue. At 19.7x earnings, the valuation assumes continued growth or earnings stability, which is inconsistent with the 38-42% recession probability and confirmed stagflationary regime.

The $11.9 million in insider monetization two days after earnings, combined with the subsequent 18% stock decline from those conversion prices, supports patience. A more compelling entry would be at 15-16x trailing earnings (~$100-107), where the restructuring counter-cycle alone could justify the valuation even if Strategic Advisory and Park Hill revenues decline meaningfully.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** Wait for either (1) a pullback to 15-16x trailing earnings (~$100-107), or (2) evidence that restructuring revenue growth is materializing fast enough to offset M&A weakness. The restructuring tailwind is real but has a 6-12 month lag from credit stress to mandate completion, and PJT's near-term earnings path is more likely down than up.
