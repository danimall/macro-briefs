## Executive Summary

Copart Inc is a global leader in online vehicle auctions and remarketing services, operating a technology-driven marketplace that connects sellers of salvage and clean-title vehicles with buyers worldwide. The company has delivered extraordinary long-term shareholder value, generating an average annual return of 17.68% over the past 20 years — outperforming the broader market by nearly 9% annually. A $100 investment in CPRT 10 years ago would be worth approximately $909 today, underscoring the compounding power of this business model.

Despite this impressive track record, CPRT has experienced meaningful price weakness in recent months, declining from a 52-week high of $63.85 to a current price of approximately $36.72, a drop of roughly 42%. This pullback appears driven by a combination of valuation compression and some institutional selling (Qualivian Investment Partners and Aoris International Fund both exited positions in Q3 2025). The company recently bolstered its financial flexibility by announcing a new $1+ billion credit facility aimed at supporting acquisitions, capital expenditures, share repurchases, and global expansion.

The investment case for Copart centers on its dominant competitive position in a structurally growing market, asset-light scalability, and proven management execution. The primary risk is that even after the pullback, the stock trades at a premium valuation (~23x trailing earnings) that may limit near-term returns if growth decelerates. We rate CPRT as a **Hold/Accumulate on Weakness** — a high-quality compounder that rewards patient, long-term investors.

## Company Overview

Copart Inc (NASDAQ: CPRT) is a U.S.-based company specializing in online vehicle auctions and remarketing services. The company provides vehicle sellers — primarily insurance companies, fleet operators, dealers, and individual owners — with a platform to auction vehicles to a global buyer base. Copart operates in the United States, Canada, the United Kingdom, Germany, Spain, Brazil, Ireland, Finland, the Middle East, and other international markets.

**Key Business Characteristics:**
- **Market Cap:** ~$35.5 billion (as of the data date)
- **Headquarters:** Dallas, Texas, USA
- **Website:** [copart.com](https://www.copart.com/)
- **Beta:** 1.12 (slightly above market risk)
- **Dividend:** None — Copart reinvests all earnings into growth and share repurchases
- **Sector:** Commercial Services & Supplies

Copart operates a two-sided marketplace model where it earns fees from both buyers and sellers. The company owns and leases a vast network of storage yards (over 300+ locations globally) and uses its proprietary VB3 (Virtual Bidding Third Generation) online auction platform to facilitate sales. This asset-heavy yard infrastructure combined with technology-driven auctions creates a powerful competitive moat.

## Growth Analysis

### Structural Growth Drivers

1. **Rising Vehicle Complexity & Total Loss Rates:** Modern vehicles are increasingly expensive to repair due to advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS), sensors, and electronic components. This drives higher total loss rates for insurance companies, increasing the volume of vehicles flowing through Copart's auction platform.

2. **International Expansion:** Copart has been steadily expanding its global footprint. The new $1B+ credit facility explicitly targets global expansion, suggesting management sees significant international runway (particularly in Europe and emerging markets).

3. **Non-Insurance Categories:** Copart has been growing its non-insurance volume, including vehicles from dealers, financial institutions, charities, and municipalities. This diversifies the revenue base beyond traditional insurance company sellers.

4. **Technology Platform:** The VB3 platform provides a best-in-class digital auction experience with global buyer reach. Continued investment in technology (AI, mobile, data analytics) enhances the platform's competitive advantage.

5. **Catastrophe Events (CATs):** While unpredictable, hurricanes, floods, and other natural disasters create temporary volume surges for Copart's salvage auction business.

### Growth Concerns

- The forward P/E being slightly higher than the trailing P/E (23.41x vs. 22.80x) may indicate the market anticipates a minor earnings slowdown or flat growth in the near term.
- Some institutional investors (Qualivian, Aoris) have exited positions citing valuation concerns, which could signal consensus that the easy growth phase has passed.
- Seeking Alpha analysis (Feb 2026) noted that "valuation is still too rich for a bullish stance" and warned shareholders "could be in for a tough year."

## Competitive Landscape

### Market Position

Copart is one of two dominant players in the U.S. salvage vehicle auction market, alongside IAA (now part of Ritchie Bros. following the 2023 merger). Together, these two companies control the vast majority of the U.S. insurance salvage auction market — creating an effective duopoly.

### Competitive Moat

Copart possesses one of the widest moats in the commercial services sector:

1. **Network Effects:** More buyers on the platform → higher prices for sellers → more sellers list → more buyers attracted. This flywheel is extremely difficult for new entrants to replicate.

2. **Scale & Yard Infrastructure:** Copart owns and operates 300+ storage locations. Acquiring and zoning land for vehicle storage yards is becoming increasingly difficult (NIMBY opposition, environmental regulations), creating a significant barrier to entry.

3. **Switching Costs:** Insurance companies have deep integrations with Copart's technology for claims processing and vehicle disposition. Switching to a competitor involves significant operational disruption.

4. **Technology Leadership:** The VB3 platform with global buyer reach gives Copart a meaningful advantage in achieving the highest auction prices for sellers.

5. **Global Scale:** International expansion creates an additional moat layer as Copart replicates its U.S. model in new markets before competitors can gain footholds.

### Industry Dynamics

The merger of IAA with Ritchie Bros. in 2023 has somewhat altered the competitive landscape. While Ritchie Bros. brings industrial/equipment auction expertise, the integration may create temporary distractions, potentially benefiting Copart in retaining and winning insurance company contracts.

## Risk Assessment

### Key Risks

1. **Valuation Risk (Moderate):** At ~23x earnings, any disappointment in growth could lead to further multiple compression. Institutional sellers (Qualivian, Aoris) have already flagged valuation concerns.

2. **Economic Sensitivity (Moderate):** While salvage volumes are somewhat countercyclical (more total losses in recessions due to older vehicle fleets), overall transaction volumes and buyer demand can be impacted by economic downturns.

3. **Regulatory Risk (Low-Moderate):** Zoning regulations for yard expansion, environmental regulations for vehicle storage, and potential changes in insurance industry regulations could impact operations.

4. **Catastrophe Volatility (Low-Moderate):** CAT events create lumpy revenue patterns. Years with fewer major storms may disappoint investors expecting consistent growth.

5. **Technology Disruption (Low):** While autonomous vehicles and improved safety could theoretically reduce accident rates long-term, the increasing complexity (and thus total loss rates) of modern vehicles is a more powerful near-term trend.

6. **International Execution Risk (Moderate):** Global expansion brings currency risk, regulatory complexity, and execution challenges in markets with different competitive dynamics.

7. **Concentration Risk (Moderate):** Copart derives a significant portion of revenue from a small number of large insurance company clients. Loss of a major contract could meaningfully impact results.

8. **Share Price Near 52-Week Low:** The stock is currently trading near its 52-week low ($37.41), suggesting negative momentum. The recent 7% price decline in the latest session data point reinforces this concern.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case 🐂

- **Structural industry tailwinds** (rising total loss rates, vehicle complexity) drive consistent volume growth for years to come.
- **Duopoly market structure** provides pricing power and high barriers to entry. The IAA/Ritchie Bros. integration may cause temporary competitive softness.
- **International expansion** represents a massive long-term opportunity. Copart's proven U.S. playbook can be replicated in Europe, Latin America, and the Middle East.
- **New $1B+ credit facility** provides firepower for accretive acquisitions and share buybacks.
- **The 42% pullback from 52-week highs** has created the most attractive entry point in years for a best-in-class compounder.
- **20-year track record of 17.68% annualized returns** demonstrates management's ability to compound shareholder value.

### Bear Case 🐻

- **Valuation still not cheap:** Even after the pullback, 23x earnings is a premium for a company potentially facing growth deceleration.
- **Institutional selling:** Multiple fund managers (Qualivian, Aoris) have exited positions, suggesting smart money may see limited near-term upside.
- **Growth deceleration risk:** As the company matures and the U.S. market saturates, maintaining historical growth rates becomes increasingly difficult.
- **No dividend:** Investors receive no income while waiting for capital appreciation, which may become less attractive in a higher-rate environment.
- **Macro headwinds:** Used car market dynamics, consumer spending weakness, and potential insurance industry changes could create temporary headwinds.
- **Near 52-week low with negative momentum** suggests the stock may need time to base before moving higher.

## Conclusion

**Overall Rating: 7.5/10 — HOLD / Accumulate on Weakness**

Copart is unquestionably one of the highest-quality business franchises in the commercial services sector. Its duopoly position in salvage vehicle auctions, powerful network effects, irreplaceable yard infrastructure, and technology leadership create a formidable competitive moat. The company's 20-year track record of ~18% annualized returns speaks for itself.

However, quality has its price. At approximately 23x earnings, CPRT is not cheap, even after declining 42% from its 52-week high. The forward P/E being slightly above the trailing P/E signals potential near-term earnings plateauing, and multiple institutional investors have exited citing valuation concerns. The bearish Seeking Alpha analysis warning of "a tough year" for shareholders should not be dismissed.

**Recommendation:** For long-term investors with a 3-5 year horizon, the current pullback creates a reasonable entry point for a high-quality compounder. However, we would suggest **dollar-cost averaging** rather than establishing a full position at current levels, as momentum is negative and further downside toward the $33-35 range is possible in a broader market correction. Investors with shorter time horizons may want to wait for clearer evidence of a technical bottom or earnings acceleration before committing capital.

**Target Allocation:** Core holding at 2-4% portfolio weight for growth-oriented, long-term portfolios.
