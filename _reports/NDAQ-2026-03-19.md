## Executive Summary

Nasdaq Inc. operates three segments — Capital Access Platforms, Financial Technology, and Market Services — that collectively generate revenue from exchange trading, data/analytics subscriptions, and technology licensing. The company's transformation from a pure exchange into a diversified financial infrastructure provider accelerated with the $10.5B Adenza acquisition (closed late 2023), which added regulatory technology and risk management software. At $86.02 (current price per profile) or $85.44 (latest close in price history), the stock trades at ~27.7x forward earnings, which is a premium to the broader market but a discount to the analyst consensus fair value estimate of ~$108.53 cited in recent news.

The stock has underperformed its January 2026 highs of ~$101 by roughly 15-16%, driven by the February sell-off that took shares from $97 to $77 in under two weeks. The recovery to $85-86 has been orderly but incomplete. The absence of detailed financial statements in the provided data limits the depth of balance sheet and cash flow analysis, but publicly available information and recent news indicate Nasdaq raised its mid-term revenue guidance in late February 2026, and Morgan Stanley maintained an Overweight rating on January 30, 2026. The macro environment — characterized by elevated volatility, hawkish Fed posture, and credit stress — is structurally supportive for exchange operators, though the stock's premium valuation constrains near-term upside.

## Company Overview

Nasdaq Inc. is a global financial infrastructure company operating through three segments:

1. **Capital Access Platforms** — Listings, index licensing, data products, and corporate solutions. This is the highest-margin segment and the primary driver of recurring revenue.
2. **Financial Technology** — Surveillance, regulatory technology (FinCrime solutions), and risk management software, bolstered by the Adenza acquisition.
3. **Market Services** — Exchange trading, clearing, and trade management across equities, derivatives, and fixed income.

The company has ~568M shares outstanding and a market capitalization of ~$48.9B. It pays a dividend yielding approximately 1.25%.

## Competitive Landscape

Nasdaq occupies a strong position across multiple verticals:

1. **Exchange operations:** Duopoly with NYSE (owned by ICE) in US equity listings. The Nasdaq-100 index franchise generates substantial licensing revenue with near-zero marginal cost.
2. **Data and analytics:** Competing with S&P Global, Bloomberg, and Refinitiv (LSEG), but with unique first-party data from its exchange operations.
3. **Financial Technology:** Adenza (Calypso + AxiomSL) positions Nasdaq in regulatory technology and risk management, competing with FIS, SS&C, and smaller specialists.
4. **Market surveillance:** Nasdaq's SMARTS surveillance technology is used by over 50 exchanges and regulators globally, creating a high-switching-cost recurring revenue stream.

The competitive moat is real: exchange licenses are effectively non-replicable, index franchises have strong network effects, and regulatory technology contracts are multi-year with high retention rates. Score of 8.0 reflects this durable positioning.

## Risk Assessment

**Macro risks:**

- Credit cascade risk (55-60% probability per world model) could impair capital markets activity broadly, reducing new issue volumes and data demand.
- Recession probability at 40-45% would compress trading multiples even for defensive financial infrastructure names.

**Company-specific risks:**
- **Acquisition integration:** Adenza's $10.5B price tag added significant debt. Integration synergies need to materialize within the 3-5 year mid-term guidance window.
- **Insider selling:** CEO Friedman exercised options and sold 92,840 shares at ~$90.08 on March 6 ($8.36M). While this was likely a planned exercise-and-sell (exercise price $22.22, held since pre-2020), the timing during a period of stock weakness is notable. EVP Bryan Smith also sold 6,000 shares at $87.24. One positive signal: board member Johan Torgeby purchased 12,000 shares at $79.86 ($958K) on February 17, during the sell-off trough.
- **Regulatory risk:** As a systemically important financial infrastructure provider, Nasdaq faces regulatory scrutiny on exchange fees, market data pricing, and potential antitrust concerns.

**Offsetting factor:** Elevated volatility is a direct revenue tailwind for Market Services. The current geopolitical and macro environment (oil at $119, FOMC hawkish, credit stress) is generating the kind of multi-asset volatility that benefits exchange operators. This is why the world model identifies CME, ICE, and CBOE as maximum conviction volatility beneficiaries.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- Mid-term revenue guidance raise signals management confidence in Adenza integration and organic growth trajectory.
- The ~16% discount from the 52-week high provides a better entry than the $95-101 range that prevailed in Q4 2025 / early Q1 2026.
- Analyst consensus of ~$108.53 implies 27% upside. If execution on Adenza synergies meets guidance, re-rating toward $100+ is plausible within 12 months.
- Structural volatility tailwind from current macro/geopolitical regime directly supports Market Services revenue.
- Board insider purchase ($958K at $79.86) provides a modest positive signal on value.

### Bear Case
- At 27.7x forward earnings, NDAQ trades at a meaningful premium to peers (CME ~23-25x, ICE ~22-24x) without demonstrably superior growth.
- The Adenza acquisition burden is real: $10.5B+ in acquisition costs, with integration risk still present.
- CEO's $8.36M sale on March 6 is the largest open-market disposition in the insider data, and the controller (Daly) also sold. While routine, the pattern of executive sales during a drawdown is not bullish.
- If recession materializes (40-45% probability), IPO volumes, listing fees, and data demand all contract, compressing the premium multiple.
- The trailing and forward P/E convergence at 27.65x suggests near-term earnings growth is essentially zero, making the stock a pure multiple expansion / reversion play.

## Conclusion

NDAQ is a high-quality financial infrastructure franchise trading at a fair-to-slightly-expensive multiple given the current earnings growth trajectory. The competitive position is strong (8/10), and the macro backdrop of elevated volatility is supportive. However, the 27.7x forward P/E premium over direct exchange peers, the absence of near-term earnings growth (trailing P/E = forward P/E), and meaningful acquisition-related balance sheet risk limit the attractiveness of initiating a new position at $85-86.

The stock is more interesting at $75-80 (where the February trough occurred and the board insider purchased) and less interesting above $90. At current levels, the risk/reward is balanced. The world model's exchange/volatility beneficiary thesis applies more directly to CME and ICE, which trade at lower multiples with cleaner balance sheets and more direct exposure to the volatility regime.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** The structural quality of the franchise justifies maintaining an existing position, but the valuation premium relative to peers and the flat near-term earnings trajectory do not warrant a new BUY at these levels. A pullback to $75-80 or evidence of accelerating Adenza synergies in the April 2026 earnings report would warrant upgrade consideration.
