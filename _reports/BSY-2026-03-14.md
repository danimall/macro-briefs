## Executive Summary

Bentley Systems is a vertical software provider focused on infrastructure engineering, serving architects, engineers, construction professionals, and owner-operators of infrastructure assets. The company has built a durable recurring revenue base with annual recurring revenue (ARR) growing in the low-double digits since 2022 on a constant-currency basis. Management guided 11-13% revenue growth for 2026 and is investing in AI monetization across its platform.

The investment case hinges on whether Bentley's niche dominance and ARR visibility justify a ~42x forward P/E in the current macro environment. Q4 2025 results were mixed: one source reports an EPS miss of $0.176 vs. $0.27 expectations, while another source (Benzinga) reports an EPS beat of $0.27 vs. $0.24 expectations — the discrepancy likely reflects GAAP vs. adjusted figures. Revenue grew ~$42M YoY in Q4. Piper Sandler lowered its target to $42 (from $45), while UBS raised to $43 (from $40), both roughly 10% above the current $38.19 price. The stock is down 36% from its 52-week high of $59.25, which has compressed the valuation but still leaves it rich relative to the earnings trajectory visible today.

Given the macro backdrop — stagflation confirmed, GDP at 0.7%, rising energy costs pressuring infrastructure project economics — and the stock's already-full valuation, the risk-reward is balanced. Bentley is a quality franchise trading at a fair price, which makes it a HOLD.

## Company Overview

Bentley Systems provides software for the design, construction, and operations of infrastructure assets including roads, bridges, rail, water networks, industrial plants, and power grids. The company operates globally with significant non-US revenue exposure (creating constant-currency/reported-currency divergence). Key products include MicroStation, OpenRoads, SYNCHRO, and the iTwin digital twin platform. The dual-class share structure concentrates voting power with the Bentley family — Gregory S. Bentley serves as Executive Chairman & President and holds ~7.6M Class B shares.

Market cap: ~$11.6B. Current price: $38.19. 52-week range: $30.97–$59.25. Beta: 1.12. Forward P/E: ~42x. Dividend yield: ~0.73%.

## Growth Analysis

**ARR Momentum:** Low-double-digit constant-currency ARR growth sustained since 2022 is the strongest fundamental data point. Infrastructure software benefits from multi-year project timelines that create sticky customer relationships. The 2026 guidance of 11-13% revenue growth implies acceleration or at minimum maintenance of this trajectory.

**AI Monetization:** Seeking Alpha noted that Bentley is "expanding AI monetization" — likely through its iTwin digital twin platform and design automation tools. AI in infrastructure engineering is early stage but has clear application in generative design, construction planning, and predictive maintenance. The revenue contribution is likely immaterial in 2026 but represents an optionality driver over 2-3 years.

**Services Rebound:** Piper Sandler specifically flagged expectations for a "services rebound in 2026," implying that professional services revenue (lower-margin, project-based) may have been weak in 2025 and could recover.

**Macro Risk to Growth:** Infrastructure spending is partially insulated by government funding (IIJA in the US, equivalent programs globally), but rising oil prices ($98-108 central range) increase construction input costs and can delay project starts. Constant-currency growth faces FX headwinds if the dollar strengthens further.

## Valuation Assessment

At $38.19, BSY trades at ~42x forward P/E (using adjusted EPS). For context:

- **Peer comparison:** Autodesk (ADSK) trades at ~30-35x forward earnings with higher revenue scale. Trimble (TRMB) trades at ~25-28x. Nemetschek (NEM.DE) trades at similar multiples to BSY. Bentley commands a premium to most infrastructure software peers, which requires sustained above-peer growth to justify.
- **Historical context:** The stock reached $59.25 at its 52-week high, implying the market was willing to pay ~65x+ at the peak. At $38.19 (down 36%), the valuation compression has been significant but still leaves the stock at a premium multiple.
- **Analyst targets:** Piper Sandler: $42. UBS: $43. Both imply ~10-12% upside from current levels. Zacks flagged a mean target implying ~25% upside, though this likely includes stale pre-selloff estimates.

The stock is not cheap by any conventional metric. A ~42x forward P/E for a business growing revenue 11-13% implies a PEG ratio of ~3.5x, which is stretched. Infrastructure software's recurring revenue durability provides some justification for premium multiples, but in the current macro (GDP 0.7%, core PCE 3.1%), multiple expansion is unlikely.

## Competitive Landscape

Bentley occupies a strong niche position. The infrastructure engineering software market is an oligopoly with three primary players: Bentley (roads, bridges, water, plant), Autodesk (buildings, general AEC), and Trimble (construction technology, geospatial). Each has carved out partially overlapping but distinct end-market focus areas.

**Moat characteristics:**
- High switching costs: Engineers train for years on specific platforms. Project file formats create lock-in.
- Mission-critical workflows: Design errors in bridges or water systems have safety implications, making customers reluctant to switch.
- Long project lifecycles: Infrastructure projects span 5-30+ years, and Bentley captures revenue across design, construction, and operations phases.
- Government/regulatory ties: Many public sector clients mandate specific software for compliance.

The competitive position scores well (7.5/10) because Bentley's niche focus on heavy civil and industrial infrastructure gives it defensibility that broader AEC players lack. The dual-class structure also means hostile acquisition is effectively impossible, which protects strategic continuity but limits governance accountability.

## Risk Assessment

**Macro Sensitivity (Moderate):** Infrastructure spending has government backing (IIJA provides a floor), but private-sector industrial/energy projects are cyclically sensitive. Oil above $100 increases input costs for infrastructure construction, potentially delaying project starts. Foreign currency exposure (significant non-US revenue) creates translation risk.

**Valuation Risk (High):** At 42x forward earnings, any guidance miss or macro deterioration that slows ARR growth to single digits would likely trigger significant multiple compression. The GAAP EPS miss in Q4 ($0.176 vs. $0.27) — if this reflects structural margin pressure rather than one-time items — is a yellow flag.

**Dual-Class Structure:** Gregory Bentley holds ~7.6M shares and controls the company through the dual-class structure. The only open-market sale was trivial (100 shares at $40.01 on March 10), which is immaterial. Executive compensation grants on March 6 were large (CEO received ~103K shares), typical for annual grants but dilutive to common shareholders over time.

**Acquisition Integration:** Bentley has grown through acquisitions (Seequent, Cohesive, Power Line Systems). Integration risk and goodwill impairment risk are present, though historical execution has been adequate.

**AI Disruption (Low-Medium):** Generative AI could eventually allow non-specialists to perform some engineering design tasks, but the safety-critical nature of infrastructure engineering means regulatory and liability barriers protect incumbents for the foreseeable future. Bentley is more likely to benefit from AI (via monetization) than be disrupted by it.

## Investment Thesis

**Bull Case:**
- ARR growth sustains at 11-13% through 2026-2027, driven by infrastructure spending tailwinds (IIJA, global infrastructure renewal) and AI monetization
- Services rebound in 2026 improves total revenue mix and operating leverage
- Stock re-rates toward $45-50 as growth visibility improves and macro fears abate
- Infrastructure software proves recession-resistant due to government funding floors and multi-year project commitments

**Bear Case:**
- GAAP EPS miss reflects structural margin pressure from rising SBC and acquisition costs, compressing returns on capital
- Stagflation environment (GDP 0.7%, oil $100+) delays infrastructure project starts, slowing ARR growth to single digits
- At 42x forward earnings, any growth deceleration triggers rapid multiple compression toward 25-30x (implying ~30% downside)
- Dollar strengthening erodes constant-currency growth and forces negative guidance revisions
- Dual-class structure limits governance oversight and makes excessive SBC harder for minority shareholders to resist

## Conclusion

Bentley Systems is a high-quality niche software franchise with defensible competitive positioning and visible recurring revenue growth. The 36% pullback from 52-week highs has improved the entry point, but at ~42x forward earnings the stock still requires flawless execution and macro cooperation to generate meaningful returns from here. Analyst targets of $42-$43 imply only 10-12% upside, which is inadequate compensation for the macro and execution risks at this valuation level in the current environment.

The appropriate position is HOLD. For existing holders, the quality of the franchise and ARR visibility justify maintaining exposure. For new capital, the risk-reward is insufficient: 10% upside to analyst consensus against a possible 25-30% downside if growth decelerates into multiple compression. A more attractive entry point would be $30-33, where the forward P/E would compress to ~33-35x and provide a genuine margin of safety.
