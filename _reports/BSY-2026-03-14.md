## Executive Summary

Bentley Systems is a specialized infrastructure engineering software company trading at $38.19, roughly 36% below its 52-week high of $59.25 and 23% above its 52-week low of $30.97. The company reported Q4 2025 results on February 26, 2026, missing EPS expectations materially ($0.176 reported vs. $0.27 consensus) while simultaneously guiding FY2026 revenue to $1.685-1.715B (11-13% constant-currency growth). The stock rallied ~7% on the day of the report, suggesting the market viewed the revenue guide and ARR momentum as more important than the quarterly EPS miss.

The core investment question is whether ~42x forward earnings is justified for a company growing revenue 11-13% with a sticky, recurring revenue base in infrastructure engineering. The answer depends heavily on whether Bentley can expand margins alongside that topline growth. The Q4 EPS miss raises questions about the pace of margin expansion. Meanwhile, the macro environment — stagflation with $100+ oil, 38-42% recession probability, and government spending uncertainty — creates headwinds for infrastructure project timelines, though Bentley's software-centric model provides some insulation from direct capex cuts.

The stock sits in a no-man's-land: too expensive for value investors given 42x earnings with limited margin expansion evidence, and too slow-growing (11-13%) for growth investors who can find faster-growing software names at similar multiples. Analyst targets cluster around $42-48, implying 10-26% upside from current levels, but this upside is predicated on execution and margin improvement that Q4 results did not confirm.

## Company Overview

Bentley Systems provides software for infrastructure engineering — design, construction, and operations of roads, bridges, rail, water systems, industrial plants, power utilities, and related assets. The company's products include MicroStation, OpenRoads, STAAD, and various digital twin solutions. The business model is predominantly subscription/recurring, with Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) as the key metric. Gregory Bentley serves as Executive Chairman and President, with Nicholas Cumins as CEO.

The company went public in September 2020 and has a dual-class share structure (Class A and Class B), with the Bentley family retaining significant voting control. Market capitalization is approximately $11.6B.

Bentley operates globally with significant non-US revenue exposure, making constant-currency metrics important. The company has been executing an AI monetization strategy, though specific financial contribution from AI remains early-stage.

## Growth Analysis

Bentley's growth profile is defined by several characteristics:

**Revenue Growth:** FY2026 guidance of $1.685-1.715B implies 11-13% constant-currency growth, consistent with the company's multi-year track record of low-double-digit ARR growth since 2022. This consistency is valuable — predictable growth in infrastructure software is rare.

**AI Monetization:** Seeking Alpha reported that Bentley is "expanding AI monetization" in 2026. The company's digital twin and infrastructure asset management capabilities are natural AI integration points. However, AI revenue contribution appears early-stage, and the monetization timeline is uncertain.

**End-Market Tailwinds:** Global infrastructure investment remains a secular theme — aging US infrastructure, energy transition, and urbanization in emerging markets all drive demand for engineering software. The US Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA) provides a multi-year spending backdrop, though actual project execution timelines may stretch in a higher-rate, stagflationary environment.

**Services Rebound:** Piper Sandler's note (March 2, 2026) specifically referenced expectations of a "services rebound in 2026," suggesting that the Q4 2025 shortfall may have been services-driven and is expected to normalize.

**Growth Risks:** At 11-13% topline growth, Bentley needs to demonstrate operating leverage to justify 42x earnings. If margin expansion stalls (as Q4 2025 EPS miss hints), the stock is priced for growth it may not deliver to the bottom line.

## Valuation Assessment

**P/E:** 41.6x forward earnings is a premium multiple for a company growing revenue 11-13%. For context, broader enterprise software peers growing at similar rates (10-15%) typically trade at 25-35x forward earnings unless they demonstrate accelerating growth or significant margin expansion potential.

**P/Revenue:** ~6.8x forward revenue is within the range for high-quality vertical software businesses with 80%+ recurring revenue and low churn, but sits at the upper end of that range given Bentley's growth rate.

**Implied Growth Expectations:** At 42x forward P/E with ~$0.92 implied EPS, the market is pricing in substantial earnings growth beyond the 11-13% revenue growth — meaning the stock embeds significant margin expansion expectations. The Q4 EPS miss ($0.176 vs. $0.27) creates doubt about the trajectory of that margin expansion.

**Analyst Targets:** UBS raised its target to $43 (Neutral), Piper Sandler lowered to $42. Zacks reported Wall Street sees ~25.6% upside (implying a consensus target around $47-48). The range of $42-48 suggests limited near-term upside with execution risk to the downside.

**Relative to 52-Week Range:** Trading at $38.19 vs. $59.25 high and $30.97 low, the stock is at the 36th percentile of its range. The decline from highs reflects both the Q4 miss and broader tech multiple compression.

**Verdict:** The valuation is stretched relative to growth. A 42x multiple requires both revenue growth sustaining at 11-13% and meaningful operating margin expansion. The Q4 miss introduces uncertainty about the latter.

## Competitive Landscape

Bentley occupies a defensible niche in infrastructure engineering software. Key competitors include:

- **Autodesk (ADSK):** Broader AEC (architecture, engineering, construction) platform; significantly larger. Competes in some segments but has a more horizontal product portfolio.
- **Hexagon AB:** Infrastructure and industrial measurement/software; overlaps in geospatial and plant design.
- **Trimble (TRMB):** Transportation and construction technology; adjacent markets.
- **Nemetschek Group:** European AEC software competitor.

Bentley's competitive advantages include:
1. **Deep domain specialization** in heavy infrastructure (roads, bridges, utilities, plants) vs. Autodesk's broader but shallower approach
2. **High switching costs** — engineering firms embed Bentley's tools into multi-year project workflows; switching mid-project is impractical
3. **Digital twin capabilities** position the company for lifecycle management of infrastructure assets, extending revenue beyond initial design
4. **Sticky ARR model** with low churn, providing revenue visibility

The company scores well on competitive positioning because infrastructure engineering software is a specialized market with limited viable alternatives for complex projects.

## Risk Assessment

**Macro Sensitivity:** Infrastructure projects depend on government budgets and private capital availability. In a stagflationary environment with 38-42% recession probability and $100+ oil, project timelines may extend. However, Bentley's recurring revenue model provides significant buffer — existing subscribers don't cancel because a project is delayed.

**FX Risk:** Significant international revenue means constant-currency and reported results can diverge materially. A strong dollar environment (possible given Fed holding at current rates) would compress reported growth.

**Concentration Risk:** The Bentley family's dual-class structure concentrates voting power. Gregory Bentley holds 7.6M+ shares. While alignment is generally positive, minority shareholders have limited governance influence.

**EPS Miss Pattern:** The Q4 2025 miss (35% below consensus) is concerning. If this reflects structural margin pressure rather than timing, the 42x multiple has no support. Monitoring Q1 2026 results is critical.

**AI Overpromise Risk:** The company is expanding AI positioning, but infrastructure engineering AI applications face regulatory and safety constraints that may slow monetization vs. expectations.

**Insider Activity:** Gregory Bentley's 100-share sale at $40.01 on March 10 is immaterial ($4,001). The March 6 equity grants to the C-suite (CEO received 102,946 shares total) are routine compensation. No significant insider selling signal detected.

**Section 301 / Tariff Exposure:** As a software company, direct tariff exposure is minimal. However, if tariffs delay infrastructure projects globally, Bentley's new logo acquisition and expansion revenue could slow.

## Investment Thesis

### Bear Case

- Q4 EPS miss of 35% may reflect structural cost issues (headcount, AI investment, FX) rather than temporary timing
- Stagflation environment creates headwinds for infrastructure project timelines and government budget execution
- Dual-class structure limits shareholder governance influence
- $100+ oil increases project costs for Bentley's infrastructure end-customers, potentially slowing new project starts
- AI monetization timeline is uncertain; incremental revenue likely immaterial in 2026

## Conclusion

Bentley Systems is a quality business in a defensible niche with predictable growth, but the stock's valuation assumes margin expansion that Q4 2025 results failed to confirm. At 42x forward earnings, the risk-reward is roughly balanced: the 11-13% revenue growth guide and ARR durability provide a floor, while the EPS miss and macro headwinds (stagflation, FX, infrastructure spending uncertainty) create a ceiling.

The appropriate stance is **HOLD**. The stock warrants monitoring for two triggers: (1) evidence of margin expansion in Q1 2026 results, which would validate the earnings multiple and support upgrading to BUY, or (2) further multiple compression to the low-to-mid 30s on P/E (implying a price in the low $30s), which would create a more favorable entry point. At $38.19 with analyst targets of $42-48, the upside is modest relative to the execution risk embedded in the current multiple.
