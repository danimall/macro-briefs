## Executive Summary

Azenta Inc is a life sciences services company focused on biological sample management, storage, and related genomics services. The stock has declined approximately 41% over the trailing period, falling from ~$37 in mid-March 2025 to $21.57 as of March 18, 2026, with the sharpest single-day drop occurring on February 4, 2026 (earnings day), when shares fell from ~$37 to $28.49 on 3.4M shares — roughly 4.5x average daily volume. The current market cap of ~$960M against trailing EPS of $0.53 implies a P/E of ~37.5x, which is elevated for a company that has yet to demonstrate consistent profitability or revenue acceleration.

The company completed its acquisition of UK Biocentre in early March 2026, expanding its European biorepository footprint. This is strategically coherent with Azenta's consolidation thesis in biological sample management, but it adds integration risk and capital deployment questions at a time when the stock is under significant pressure. The macro backdrop — stagflationary conditions, rising real yields, and tightening financial conditions — creates additional headwinds for a small-cap ($960M) company with thin profitability and acquisition-driven growth strategy.

## Company Overview

Azenta (formerly Brooks Automation) pivoted from semiconductor automation to life sciences services, divesting its automation business in 2021. The company now operates primarily in two areas: (1) Sample Management Solutions (biological sample storage, cold chain logistics, and repository management) and (2) Multiomics (genomics/gene synthesis services). The company serves pharmaceutical, biotech, academic, and government customers globally.

The UK Biocentre acquisition (completed March 4, 2026) adds a UK-based provider of sample management, storage, and high-throughput processing. This expands Azenta's European presence and adds government/NHS-adjacent relationships.

## Competitive Landscape

Azenta operates in biological sample management and multiomics services, competing with:

- **Fisher Scientific (Thermo Fisher)** — far larger, with integrated cold chain and biorepository services
- **Brooks Life Sciences** (former parent entity capabilities retained by competitors)
- **Precision for Medicine, ICON, and other CRO-adjacent firms** offering biospecimen services
- **Regional biobanks and academic core facilities** — fragmented competition at the lower end

Azenta's competitive position rests on its automated sample management technology and scale in ultra-low temperature storage. The UK Biocentre acquisition adds government/NHS sample processing capabilities. However, the company lacks the scale advantages of Thermo Fisher or Agilent and faces execution risk in integrating acquisitions while maintaining service quality.

The moat is moderate: switching costs exist (biological samples in storage create real lock-in), but the multiomics/genomics services business faces commoditization pressure from declining sequencing costs and competition from larger platforms.
