## Executive Summary

Westlake Corp reported deeply negative results for 2025: full-year adjusted EPS of -$0.90/share, a Q4 net loss of $544M ($4.22/share, or -$0.25 adjusted), and EBITDA that declined over 45% year-over-year. Revenue missed consensus in Q4 on both weak volumes and pricing. The company took $511M in restructuring and special charges during Q4 as part of a plan management says will produce $600M in earnings improvement for 2026.

The stock has rallied ~99% from its 52-week low of $56.33 to $112.04, pricing in substantial recovery. That recovery thesis rests on three pillars: restructuring execution, chemical cycle normalization, and building products demand recovery. All three face headwinds. Oil above $100/bbl raises ethylene feedstock costs (ethane and naphtha correlate to crude), stagflationary macro conditions with GDP at 0.7% and retail sales declining suppress downstream demand, and housing starts remain constrained by 6.25%+ mortgage rates. The market is paying for a turnaround before the turnaround has materialized.

CFO Mark Bender sold ~$1.76M worth of stock on February 27 at ~$100-101/share, one week after receiving new option and RSU grants. While option exercises often accompany sales, the net reduction in his holdings from ~62K to ~44K shares (a ~29% cut) during a period where the company is supposedly inflecting is a negative signal.

## Company Overview

Westlake Corp is a vertically integrated manufacturer of petrochemicals, polymers, and fabricated building products (pipes, fittings, siding, trim, windows). The company operates two segments: Performance and Essential Materials (PEM), which produces ethylene, polyethylene, PVC resin, and chlor-alkali chemicals; and Housing and Infrastructure Products (HIP), which fabricates PVC into building and construction products. Market cap is $13.5B. Beta of 0.80 reflects moderate cyclicality dampened by the building products segment's relative stability. Dividend yield is 2.49% ($0.53/quarter, $2.12 annualized).

## Financial Analysis

**Income Statement:** FY2025 adjusted EPS was -$0.90/share, a catastrophic decline from profitability in prior years. Q4 2025 reported a net loss of $544M ($4.22/share). Adjusting for the $511M in restructuring charges, the adjusted Q4 loss was $0.25/share, which beat the consensus estimate of -$1.46/share. Q4 EBITDA was $196M on a reported basis (net of the $511M in charges), implying ~$707M of adjusted Q4 EBITDA before items — though this figure needs verification against full financials. Full-year EBITDA declined over 45% YoY per Seeking Alpha analysis.

**Key concern:** The "beat" was driven by favorable treatment of charges, not operating improvement. Revenue missed consensus in Q4, and the company described weak volumes and prices across both segments.

**Balance Sheet:** Detailed financial statements were unavailable in the data feed, limiting direct balance sheet analysis. Westlake historically carried ~$4-5B in long-term debt with ~$1-2B in cash and equivalents. The $13.5B market cap against negative earnings implies the market is valuing recovery potential and asset base rather than current cash flows.

**Dividend:** The $0.53/quarter dividend was maintained despite the loss year, suggesting either confidence in cash flow generation from operations or unwillingness to signal weakness. At a $2.12/share annual payout and ~129M shares outstanding, the annual dividend cost is ~$274M. Sustainability depends on 2026 EBITDA recovering to at least cover capex + dividends + debt service.

## Growth Analysis

**Restructuring as Growth Catalyst:** Management outlined a $600M earnings improvement target for 2026 through cost restructuring. CEO Jean-Marc Gilson's commentary framed the $511M Q4 charge as a "reset" of cost position. The specific components are unclear from available data — whether this is headcount, plant closures, asset impairments, or a combination affects the credibility of the $600M improvement estimate.

**Cycle Recovery:** Chemical margins are driven by spread between feedstock costs (ethane, naphtha, derived from oil/gas) and product prices (polyethylene, PVC). Oil above $100/bbl raises feedstock costs. PVC demand depends on construction activity, which is suppressed by mortgage rates above 6.25%. Chlor-alkali (caustic soda) demand correlates to industrial production, which faces headwinds from stagflation. The timing of cycle recovery is uncertain and could extend well into 2027.

**Structural Headwinds:** Global polyethylene capacity additions from the Middle East and China continue to pressure pricing. The Seeking Alpha analysis from March 2 explicitly argues the 2025-2026 bounce is "way too early," suggesting the downcycle hasn't troughed.

## Valuation Assessment

At $112.04 and $13.5B market cap, trailing P/E is undefined (negative earnings). Forward P/E is unavailable from the data feed. Using the $600M earnings improvement target as a rough guide: if 2025 EBITDA was roughly $1B (implied by the >45% YoY decline from what was likely ~$2B in 2024), then $600M improvement yields ~$1.6B 2026E EBITDA, putting EV/EBITDA at roughly 9-10x assuming ~$4B net debt. That's reasonable for mid-cycle chemicals but requires the $600M improvement to fully materialize.

Price-to-book: With a 52-week range of $56.33 to $116.46, the stock at $112 is 3.8% below its 52-week high and nearly 2x its low. The rally from lows has been aggressive. The stock rose 5.8% on the Q4 earnings "beat," suggesting the market is positioned for recovery.

At current levels, the stock embeds a mid-cycle recovery scenario. Downside protection is limited if restructuring execution disappoints or if the chemical cycle extends its trough. Book value likely around $9-10B based on historical data, implying ~1.4x P/B, which is fair for a commodity chemical company but not distressed.

## Competitive Landscape

Westlake competes with Dow (DOW), LyondellBasell (LYB), Olin (OLN) in chlor-alkali, and building products companies. Its vertical integration from ethylene cracking through PVC fabrication is a structural advantage in normal times, providing margin capture across the value chain. In downcycles, this integration amplifies losses as weak pricing hits both upstream and downstream.

The HIP segment (building products) provides relative stability through residential/commercial construction demand, though this is currently suppressed. WLK's Gulf Coast manufacturing base provides cost advantages through access to cheap ethane (from associated gas production), but this advantage narrows when oil prices are high and ethane prices follow.

Westlake's founding Chao family retains significant ownership (Executive Chairman Albert Chao holds 642K+ shares worth ~$72M at current prices), aligning insider interests but also creating governance concentration risk.

## Risk Assessment

**Macro/Cycle Risk (HIGH):** Oil above $100/bbl directly raises feedstock costs. GDP at 0.7% with retail sales declining and housing starts suppressed creates a demand environment inconsistent with chemical cycle recovery. The current macro regime — stagflation with all six dimensions confirmed — is the worst possible backdrop for a cyclical commodity chemical company.

**Restructuring Execution Risk (MODERATE-HIGH):** $600M improvement targets are management projections. Chemical companies have a history of restructuring programs that deliver 50-70% of announced targets in practice. If $300-400M materializes instead of $600M, the stock's current valuation becomes difficult to justify.

**Insider Selling (MODERATE NEGATIVE):** CFO Bender sold $1.76M in stock at ~$100-101 on Feb 27, reducing his position by ~29%. This occurred one week after annual grants (which could explain partial liquidation), but the magnitude — nearly one-third of holdings — during a supposed inflection year is concerning.

**Feedstock Sensitivity:** With oil at $100+, ethane prices likely $0.25-0.35/gallon vs. $0.15-0.20 in 2020-2021. Every $0.05/gallon increase in ethane costs ~$200-300M annually for a producer of Westlake's scale.

**Housing Exposure:** HIP segment revenue depends on residential construction. At mortgage rates above 6.25%, housing starts are constrained. Section 301 tariff uncertainty adds construction cost uncertainty.

**Dividend Sustainability:** $274M annual dividend against negative earnings requires either strong operating cash flow from depreciation shielding or balance sheet funding. A dividend cut would likely trigger 15-20% downside.

## Investment Thesis

**Bull Case:** The $511M Q4 charge represents a genuine reset. If Westlake delivers 70%+ of the $600M improvement ($420M+), 2026 EBITDA reaches ~$1.4-1.5B. Chemical pricing finds a floor as inventory destocking ends. Building products benefit from any housing recovery or infrastructure spending. At $112, the stock re-rates to 8x EV/EBITDA on normalized earnings, implying $130-140. The 2.5% dividend provides income while waiting.

**Bear Case:** The chemical downcycle extends through 2026 as global PE/PVC capacity additions outpace demand in a stagflationary environment. Oil at $100+ keeps feedstock costs elevated, compressing margins even if volumes stabilize. Housing starts remain below 1.3M units. Restructuring delivers only $300M of the $600M target. 2026 EBITDA comes in at $1.0-1.1B, putting EV/EBITDA at 13-14x — expensive for trough-to-mid-cycle. Dividend cut becomes a realistic risk. Stock re-tests $75-85.

## Conclusion

Westlake's stock has nearly doubled from its 52-week low, pricing in a recovery that requires both successful restructuring execution and chemical cycle improvement. The current stagflationary macro environment — oil above $100, GDP at 0.7%, housing constrained by rates, consumer spending contracting — works against both catalysts simultaneously. The CFO selling 29% of his holdings near $100 adds a negative signal.

The $600M earnings improvement plan provides a concrete framework for monitoring execution, and the adjusted Q4 loss beating consensus by over $1/share suggests the market had overestimated near-term deterioration. At 7 analysts with mixed views, the street is divided.

**HOLD.** The stock isn't cheap enough to compensate for the macro and execution risks. At $75-85 (implying 10-11x on a realistic $1.1-1.2B 2026E EBITDA), the risk-reward improves materially. At $112, the market is paying for a turnaround in an environment that may prevent one from happening on schedule.
