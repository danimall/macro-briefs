## Executive Summary

RPM International is a $12.8B market cap specialty coatings, sealants, and building materials company trading at $99.76, down 23% from its 52-week high of $129.12. The stock declined sharply following a fiscal Q2 2026 (ended November 2025) earnings miss where both EPS and revenue came in below consensus, and adjusted EBIT declined year-over-year. Despite sell-side upgrades from JP Morgan (to Overweight) and Baird (to Outperform) citing earnings recovery potential, the fundamental backdrop is challenging: consumer segment weakness, raw material cost inflation from $100+ oil, and a stagflationary macro environment that pressures both volumes and input costs simultaneously.

The company has a solid competitive position across its four operating segments (Construction Products Group, Performance Coatings Group, Consumer Group, and Specialty Products Group), with well-known brands including Rust-Oleum, DAP, and Tremco. Its multi-year MAP 2025 restructuring initiative has delivered margin improvement, and the company maintains Dividend King status with decades of consecutive dividend increases. However, limited financial statement granularity in the available data, combined with the current macro regime of contracting consumer spending, rising energy/input costs, and 38-42% recession probability, makes the risk-reward profile unattractive for new positions.

## Company Overview

RPM International manufactures and markets specialty coatings, sealants, building materials, and related services. The company operates through four segments: Construction Products Group (waterproofing, restoration), Performance Coatings Group (high-performance industrial coatings), Consumer Group (Rust-Oleum, DAP consumer products), and Specialty Products Group (niche applications). RPM follows an acquisition-driven growth model, having completed over 100 acquisitions historically, and serves construction, industrial, and consumer end markets globally.

- Trailing P/E: 19.2x
- Forward P/E: 18.6x
- Dividend yield: 2.17%
- Beta: 1.12
- 52-week range: $95.28 – $129.12

## Financial Analysis

The available data lacks detailed income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow figures, which limits granular analysis. What we can derive from news and filings:

**Q2 FY2026 (ended November 30, 2025):** Net income of $161.2M, or $1.26/share. This missed the Zacks consensus estimate of $1.42 EPS — a ~11% miss. Net sales also missed consensus, and adjusted EBIT declined year-over-year. This is a meaningful negative inflection given RPM had been on a margin improvement trajectory through its MAP 2025 initiative.

**Implied annual earnings:** At a trailing P/E of 19.2x and a price of $99.76, trailing EPS is approximately $5.20. At a forward P/E of 18.6x, forward EPS is approximately $5.38. This implies mid-single-digit earnings growth expected, which appears optimistic given the Q2 miss trajectory.

**Dividend:** At a 2.17% yield and $99.76 price, the annual dividend is approximately $2.17/share. RPM is classified among Dividend Kings (50+ consecutive years of increases), which provides a floor of institutional support but also constrains capital allocation flexibility.

**Balance sheet:** Without detailed data, we note RPM has historically carried moderate leverage from its acquisition strategy. As a chemicals company with significant raw material exposure, rising oil prices flow directly into COGS through petrochemical-derived inputs (resins, solvents, pigments).

## Growth Analysis

RPM's growth profile is mixed:

**Positives:**
- The MAP 2025 restructuring program has driven meaningful margin expansion over the past two years through operational efficiency, plant rationalization, and procurement optimization.
- Emerging demand from data center and semiconductor facility construction could provide a structural tailwind to the Construction Products and Performance Coatings segments.
- Acquisition pipeline remains active as a growth lever.

**Negatives:**
- Consumer segment (Rust-Oleum, DAP) is under pressure from declining consumer spending. Retail sales fell 0.2% in the latest macro data, and gasoline prices are up 21%, directly competing for wallet share with discretionary home improvement spending.
- Q2 FY2026 revenue missed consensus, indicating organic growth has stalled or turned negative.
- Adjusted EBIT declining year-over-year despite MAP 2025 benefits suggests volume weakness is overwhelming margin gains — operating deleverage in action.

The Seeking Alpha coverage from January 2026 explicitly flagged "mixed growth signals" and recommended staying on the sidelines until demand clarity improves. This assessment appears correct given the macro deterioration since.

## Valuation Assessment

At 18.6x forward earnings, RPM trades below its historical premium (the stock has typically commanded 20-25x during periods of strong execution). The ~23% decline from the 52-week high has compressed the multiple to more reasonable levels.

**Relative valuation:** For a specialty chemicals company with moderate growth, 18-19x forward earnings is fair in a normal environment. In a stagflationary environment with rising input costs and declining volumes, the multiple could compress further to 15-16x, implying downside to $81-86.

**Dividend support:** The 2.17% yield is modest but the Dividend King status attracts income-oriented institutional flows. At the 52-week low of $95.28, the yield would reach approximately 2.28%.

**Analyst targets:** JP Morgan upgraded to Overweight in January 2026, and Baird upgraded to Outperform on March 4, 2026. The average one-year price target as of December 2025 was $134.04, with a range that suggests a low target around $108 and a high near $145. Given the stock is now at $99.76, the consensus target implies ~34% upside — but consensus targets were set before the full extent of the macro deterioration became clear.

The stock at $99.76 is 4.7% above its 52-week low. This proximity to support could indicate either a forming floor or the potential for a breakdown if Q3 FY2026 results (due April 8) disappoint.

## Competitive Landscape

RPM occupies a strong competitive position in specialty coatings and sealants:

**Moat characteristics:**
- Diversified brand portfolio across consumer (Rust-Oleum, DAP) and professional (Tremco, Carboline, Stonhard) channels
- Specification-driven products in construction and industrial applications create switching costs
- Decades of distribution relationships with hardware retailers, industrial distributors, and contractors
- ~100+ acquired brands create portfolio breadth that's difficult to replicate

**Competitive threats:**
- Sherwin-Williams, PPG Industries, and Axalta Coating Systems compete across multiple segments
- Private label penetration in the consumer segment during trade-down environments (current macro favors private label)
- Raw material cost inflation is industry-wide, but RPM's smaller scale relative to SHW/PPG limits procurement bargaining power

## Risk Assessment

**Macro sensitivity (HIGH):** RPM's 1.12 beta understates cyclical exposure. The company's end markets — residential construction, commercial maintenance, industrial capex — are all correlated with economic activity. In the current stagflationary regime (GDP 0.7%, NFP -92K, retail -0.2%), all three face headwinds.

**Input cost inflation (HIGH):** Specialty coatings rely on petrochemical-derived raw materials. Oil at $98-108 (central estimate) with a ceiling of $115-130 flows directly into resin, solvent, and additive costs with 1-2 quarter lags. RPM's ability to pass through costs via pricing depends on demand elasticity — harder to do when volumes are already declining.

**Consumer spending deterioration (MODERATE-HIGH):** The Consumer Group (Rust-Oleum, DAP) depends on DIY and home improvement spending, which contracts during recessions as homeowners defer discretionary projects. With recession probability at 38-42%, this segment faces meaningful risk.

**Tariff risk (MODERATE):** Section 301 probes targeting 60 economies could impact RPM's international operations and supply chain. RPM sources raw materials globally and sells in 170+ countries.

**Execution risk on Q3 FY2026 (MODERATE):** Results due April 8. Another miss after Q2's disappointment could trigger a break below the 52-week low and further multiple compression.

**Insider activity:** Three open-market sales in the past 5 months (Kinser $380K, Nance $86K, Fazzolari $40K) with no open-market purchases. Sizes are modest and consistent with routine diversification, but the absence of insider buying at 52-week lows is notable.

## Investment Thesis

### Bull Case
- MAP 2025 restructuring benefits continue to accrue, supporting margins even in a weaker demand environment
- Data center and semiconductor construction provides a structural growth vector for CPG and PCG segments
- Baird and JP Morgan upgrades reflect institutional recognition of earnings recovery potential
- Dividend King status with 2.2% yield provides downside support
- At 18.6x forward, the stock is near the lower end of its historical valuation range, creating potential for re-rating if demand stabilizes
- Spring construction season (March-June) historically drives RPM's strongest quarters

### Bear Case
- Stagflationary environment simultaneously pressures volumes (weak demand) and margins (input cost inflation) — the worst combination for a specialty manufacturer
- Q2 FY2026 miss may mark the beginning of a multi-quarter negative revision cycle
- Consumer segment (Rust-Oleum, DAP) directly exposed to retail spending contraction
- Oil at $100+ raises raw material costs with 1-2 quarter lag; margin compression accelerates through mid-2026
- Recession probability at 38-42% would drive construction and industrial maintenance spending lower
- Insider selling with no insider buying at depressed levels
- Multiple could compress to 15-16x in recession scenario, implying $81-86

## Conclusion

RPM International is a quality specialty chemicals business with genuine competitive advantages, but the timing is wrong. The company enters a challenging macro environment coming off a Q2 earnings miss, with consumer segment weakness, rising input costs, and declining volumes creating operating deleverage. The April 8 Q3 FY2026 report will be the next critical data point.

At $99.76, the stock is 23% below its 52-week high and trades at 18.6x forward earnings. This provides some valuation cushion but insufficient margin of safety given the stagflationary headwinds. The bull case requires demand recovery that the macro data does not support in the near term.

**Recommendation: HOLD.** Existing holders should maintain positions given the Dividend King yield support and MAP 2025 margin tailwinds, but new capital should wait for either: (1) a Q3 earnings beat demonstrating resilience, or (2) a further pullback to $85-90 (roughly 16x forward) providing adequate margin of safety for the bear case. The stock scores 5.9 overall, reflecting decent quality offset by elevated macro risk and near-term earnings uncertainty.

Specific reasons this is not a BUY: the macro regime (stagflation confirmed across all six dimensions) directly impairs RPM's end markets; the most recent quarter showed deteriorating fundamentals; and input cost inflation from $100+ oil has not yet fully flowed through to COGS. Specific reasons this is not an AVOID: the business has genuine competitive moats, a long track record of dividend growth, and the valuation has already compressed meaningfully from peaks.
