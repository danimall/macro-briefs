## Executive Summary

RPM International is a $12.8B market cap specialty chemicals company focused on coatings, sealants, and building materials, trading at $99.76 — down 23% from its 52-week high of $129.12. The stock sold off after Q2 FY2026 results (quarter ended November 2025) missed both earnings and revenue consensus estimates, with adjusted EBIT declining year-over-year. Consumer segment weakness and operating deleverage were the primary drivers.

Two analyst upgrades in quick succession — JP Morgan to Overweight (January 2026) and Baird to Outperform (March 2026) — indicate sell-side confidence that the pullback has created a reasonable entry point, with a consensus target around $134. However, the current macro environment is particularly hostile for RPM's business: oil at $98-108 increases raw material costs (petrochemical-derived coatings inputs), consumer spending is contracting (retail sales -0.2%, Q4 GDP 0.7%), and Section 301 probes across 60 economies add tariff uncertainty to RPM's international operations. The dividend yield of ~2.2% provides some downside support, but insufficient to compensate for the combination of earnings risk and input cost pressure.

## Company Overview

RPM International manufactures and markets specialty coatings, sealants, building materials, and related services. The company operates through four segments: Construction Products Group (CPG), Performance Coatings Group (PCG), Consumer Group, and Specialty Products Group (SPG). Key brands include Rust-Oleum, DAP, Tremco, and Carboline. RPM has historically grown through acquisitions — over 50 operating companies — and has paid consecutive annual cash dividends for roughly 50 years, earning it inclusion on "Dividend Kings" lists.

RPM's fiscal year ends May 31, so the most recent quarterly report (Q2 FY2026, ended November 30, 2025) is the freshest data point. The company reports Q3 FY2026 results on April 8, 2026.

## Growth Analysis

RPM's organic growth has been low-single-digit in recent years, supplemented by acquisitions. The Q2 FY2026 miss suggests organic growth has stalled or turned slightly negative, particularly in the Consumer segment (Rust-Oleum, DAP), which is directly exposed to DIY/renovation spending that correlates with housing activity and consumer confidence — both deteriorating.

Potential catalysts cited by bulls include demand from data center construction and semiconductor facility buildouts. These are real end-markets growing rapidly, and RPM's Construction Products and Performance Coatings segments do serve industrial/commercial construction. However, these projects represent a small portion of RPM's total revenue relative to the broader residential and commercial maintenance markets.

The Seeking Alpha analysis from January 2026 explicitly noted "unresolved demand clarity" and "consumer segment weakness and operating deleverage" as reasons for a neutral rating. This assessment appears accurate given the macro trajectory.

**Growth headwinds specific to current environment:**
- $100+ oil → higher petrochemical input costs (resins, solvents, additives are petroleum-derived) → margin pressure unless fully passed through via pricing
- Consumer spending contraction → lower DIY/renovation activity → Consumer Group volume decline
- Housing market frozen at 6.25%+ mortgage rates → reduced new construction and remodel activity
- Potential tariff exposure on imported raw materials or finished goods from Section 301 probes

## Valuation Assessment

At 18.7x forward earnings, RPM trades at a modest premium to the specialty chemicals peer group median (typically 15-18x) but at a discount to its own 5-year average forward P/E of roughly 22-24x. The stock has derated meaningfully from its $129 high.

The ~2.2% dividend yield is above RPM's historical average, reflecting the price decline. RPM's dividend track record (50+ consecutive years of increases) provides a valuation floor — income-oriented investors will step in at yields approaching 2.5-3.0%.

At $99.76 with consensus analyst targets averaging ~$134, the implied upside is ~34%. However, consensus targets typically lag fundamental deterioration, and the $134 average was set before the full stagflationary picture materialized. Adjusting for input cost headwinds and consumer weakness, fair value in the current environment is likely $105-115, implying 5-15% upside from current levels — insufficient to justify a BUY given the risk profile.

## Competitive Landscape

RPM occupies a defensible position in specialty coatings. The company has built a portfolio of recognized brands (Rust-Oleum is dominant in consumer rust-preventive coatings; Tremco is a leader in commercial waterproofing) through decades of targeted acquisitions. Barriers to entry in specialty chemicals are moderate — technical formulation knowledge, regulatory compliance, distribution relationships, and brand recognition all favor incumbents.

Key competitors include Sherwin-Williams (SHW), PPG Industries (PPG), and Axalta Coating Systems (AXTA). RPM is smaller and more niche-focused than SHW or PPG, which gives it less pricing power but also less direct competition in many submarkets.

RPM's competitive moat is real but narrow — brand loyalty in coatings is moderate (contractors develop preferences but will switch on price), and raw material costs are largely commoditized across the industry. In an inflationary environment, RPM's ability to pass through input cost increases depends on competitive dynamics that favor larger players with more market power.

## Risk Assessment

**Input Cost Risk (HIGH):** Petrochemical-derived raw materials (resins, solvents, pigments, additives) represent the majority of RPM's COGS. Oil at $98-108, with a ceiling of $115-130 if SPR depletes, directly increases these costs. RPM's historical pattern shows a 1-2 quarter lag between raw material cost increases and pricing actions, creating margin compression during transition periods.

**Consumer Demand Risk (HIGH):** The Consumer Group (Rust-Oleum, DAP) depends on DIY activity and housing renovation spending. With Q4 GDP at 0.7%, retail sales declining, gasoline up 21%, and mortgage rates at 6.25%+, this segment faces volume declines that pricing alone cannot offset.

**Tariff Risk (MODERATE):** RPM operates in 160+ countries and sources raw materials globally. Section 301 probes across 60 economies create uncertainty for both input sourcing and international sales, particularly if retaliatory tariffs affect RPM's European or Asian operations.

**Leverage Risk (MODERATE):** RPM's acquisition-driven model requires ongoing debt capacity. In a tightening credit environment (credit cascade probability 55-60% per world model), RPM's ability to pursue acquisitions may be constrained, removing a key growth lever.

**Cyclicality (MODERATE):** Beta of 1.12 confirms RPM is modestly more volatile than the market. Construction and renovation spending is cyclical, and RPM's earnings leverage amplifies volume swings.

## Conclusion

RPM International is a well-managed specialty chemicals company with genuine competitive advantages in niche coatings markets and an exceptional dividend track record. The 23% decline from highs and analyst upgrades suggest the market may be overweighting near-term weakness. However, the macro environment is specifically hostile to RPM's business model: rising petrochemical input costs, contracting consumer spending, frozen housing markets, and potential tariff disruption create a multi-vector headwind that is intensifying rather than abating.

At 18.7x forward earnings, RPM is not expensive enough to be an AVOID, and the dividend provides genuine downside support. But the risk-reward is unfavorable for a BUY when the earnings trajectory is negative, insiders are net sellers, and the macro headwinds (oil, consumer, housing, tariffs) are all moving in the wrong direction simultaneously. HOLD with a catalyst-dependent reassessment: watch Q3 FY2026 results (April 8) for evidence of pricing power absorption of input cost increases, and monitor consumer segment volume trends for stabilization.
