## Key Events & Analysis

### Bushehr Nuclear Plant Proximity Strike Creates New Escalation Vector

The projectile striking near Iran's Bushehr nuclear facility introduces a dimension absent from prior analysis: direct Russian diplomatic engagement in the conflict. Rosatom built Bushehr, Russian personnel operate portions of it, and Russia's condemnation carries implicit warning about escalation near nuclear infrastructure. The practical consequence is that Russia, which has been earning an estimated $150M/day from elevated oil prices and supplying Iran with drones, now faces a reason to reduce cooperation with the US — specifically, the sanctions waiver that briefly pushed oil below $100. If Moscow restricts oil cooperation in response, the 200-500K bpd Russian supply contribution disappears, widening the supply gap from approximately 7-9M bpd to 7.5-9.5M bpd.

This is a single event and should be treated as an early signal rather than an established thesis. But it sits at the intersection of two confirmed trends: (1) Russian economic benefit from the conflict (confirmed by $150M/day calculation in world model), and (2) geographic scope of strikes expanding to increasingly sensitive targets (Abu Dhabi → Fujairah → Dubai airport → Bushehr). The direction is toward greater risk, not less.

### EA $18B Debt Offering Is a Live Credit Market Test

Banks preparing to offload $18B in junk bonds and loans for the Electronic Arts take-private during the most volatile week since the conflict began represents either remarkable confidence or desperate timing. The offering's reception will provide actionable information about the credit cascade thesis: if it prices at reasonable spreads with strong demand, credit markets are more resilient than the 22-data-point cascade thesis suggests. If it prices poorly, requires concessions, or is pulled/downsized, it adds a 23rd data point and confirms that leveraged finance markets are functionally impaired.

This is the largest single new-money HY offering since the conflict began. Combined with $63B in bonds at fallen angel risk (separate event), Amazon absorbing €14.5B from euro credit markets, and Novartis taking $11B, the total capital demand on corporate bond markets this week exceeds $90B. The supply-demand balance in credit is genuinely strained.

### Trump's Exit Signal Versus Israel's Retaliation Cycle

Trump stating the US will leave Iran operations in the "very near future" and a senior security official publicly resigning over the war's justification are the first concrete domestic political signals of de-escalation pressure. The world model has carried abrupt exit at 5-8% probability. These events provide supporting evidence for the existing estimate but do not warrant an increase because: (1) Israel's independently confirmed 3+ week timeline continues regardless of US political signals; (2) Iran's retaliatory strikes following Larijani/Khatib killings are escalatory, not de-escalatory; (3) Hormuz disruption persists independently of US military operations — Iran controls the strait through naval assets, mines, and missile batteries that would not be removed by a US withdrawal.

The critical distinction is between US military participation and Hormuz supply disruption. Even if the US "declares victory" and exits, Hormuz remains disrupted unless Iran chooses to reopen it. There is no evidence Iran would do so while under Israeli attack. The practical implication: oil prices stay elevated regardless of the US political decision, though defense stocks would face a temporary 5-10% correction on exit announcement.

Combined with the SEC-CFTC MOU (prior brief), Indiana pension law, Australian regulatory framework, T. This moves COIN from "early BUY approaching established" to "established BUY" territory, though position sizing should reflect the asset's correlation to Bitcoin price, which remains volatile.
