## Executive Summary

The March 17 batch consolidates the stagflationary regime into what is now an industrial reality, validated by direct infrastructure attacks, allied non-cooperation, and the US government's explicit admission that it has exhausted its policy toolkit. Iran struck Abu Dhabi's Shah gas field and Fujairah port, a tanker was hit near Hormuz, UAE crude output fell more than 50%, diesel crossed $5/gallon in the US, and Iran's new Supreme Leader rejected de-escalation on the same day Israel confirmed killing Larijani. Germany, Spain, and Greece refused the Hormuz coalition. Treasury Secretary Bessent stated the US will not intervene in oil markets. The IMO chief said naval escorts cannot guarantee safe passage. Every potential circuit breaker has been tested and found insufficient: the record 400M-barrel SPR release failed to suppress prices (oil rose 3% on release day), the Russian sanctions waiver provided at most 200-500K bpd against a 7-9M bpd gap, and allied military cooperation has been refused. The SPR depletion probability at 65-75% from the prior brief requires no revision — if anything, the combination of confirmed infrastructure attacks reducing UAE production and allied refusal extending the timeline pushes this probability toward the upper end.

Running in parallel, two developments deserve emphasis for their portfolio implications. Nvidia's $1 trillion order pipeline through 2027 for Blackwell and Vera Rubin architectures is the largest single demand signal in semiconductor history and validates the AI hardware thesis completely. But it operates in a different causal universe from the macro regime: $1T in GPU orders does not lower oil prices, restore Hormuz transit, or reduce core PCE from 3.1%. The AI-to-energy rotation may pause during GTC week, but the macro driver is structurally dominant. Second, the credit cascade thesis reaches its 22nd independent data point with Blue Owl pushing Century Capital into insolvency after discovering "irregularities" — the first confirmed portfolio company failure with fraud-adjacent characteristics, raising the question of how many other private credit portfolios contain hidden problems. March 31 quarter-end is now 14 days away and represents the structural focal point for retail redemptions from semi-liquid funds.

## Key Events & Analysis

### The Policy Toolkit Is Empty — Oil Price Floor Now Market-Determined

Bessent's statement that the Treasury has "no authority" to intervene in oil markets, combined with the confirmed failure of every attempted intervention (SPR release, Russian waiver, Saudi Red Sea rerouting, selective Hormuz transit), establishes that oil prices are now entirely market-determined within a structural supply deficit. The four-step escalation since the prior brief — UAE infrastructure attacks, allied refusal of coalition, IMO declaration of escort unsustainability, and Bessent's admission — removes any remaining expectation of policy-driven price containment.

The diesel-to-CPI transmission mechanism operates with approximately 4-8 weeks lag. Diesel at $5/gallon in March means April-May CPI prints will mechanically capture the freight cost passthrough. This compounds the six supply disruption channels already validated by industrial force majeure (petrochemicals, aluminium, fertilizer, pharma, fashion, metals). The multi-channel CPI peak estimate of 3.2-5.2% continues tracking toward the upper end. Moody's explicit recession warning — the first from a major ratings agency — provides institutional weight to the 38-42% recession probability estimate.

For positioning, this solidifies three exposures: refiners (VLO, MPC, PSX) benefit from widening crack spreads as refined product scarcity (China/Thailand jet fuel export bans, $5 diesel) exceeds crude price increases; CF Industries benefits from domestic natural gas feedstock advantage during confirmed spring planting fertilizer disruption; and energy broadly maintains 50-60% retention as an SPR exhaustion hedge.

### Larijani Assassination + Iran Supreme Leader Rejection = Zero Diplomatic Off-Ramps

The Larijani killing and Iran's new Supreme Leader explicitly rejecting de-escalation on the same day constitute a complete closure of diplomatic channels for the near term. Historical precedent from targeted killings (Soleimani in 2020, various Hamas leaders) shows that decapitation strikes produce intensified responses, not capitulation. Iran's geographic escalation pattern — from Hormuz blockade to Dubai airport drone strike to Abu Dhabi Shah gas field attack to Fujairah tanker strike — demonstrates expanding willingness to target civilian economic infrastructure across the Gulf.

The defense thesis now has 12+ independent data points with zero contradictions: LMT CEO/Trump mandate, Rheinmetall 45% growth, AVAV upgrade, France warships, US Navy escort infeasibility, Indian defense outperformance, Pentagon CTO, munitions depletion, Trump ceasefire rejection, AI Czar dissent, Israel's 3-week floor, and now allied refusal of coalition (forcing US to bear greater operational burden). The multi-year restocking cycle is conflict-duration-insensitive: even a ceasefire tomorrow leaves years of Tomahawk stocks, interceptor missiles, and precision munitions to replenish.

### Nvidia's $1T Pipeline Validates Hardware, Not the Macro

Jensen Huang projecting $1T in orders through 2027 provides the longest demand visibility in semiconductor history. Combined with TSMC's 22% YoY revenue, Amazon's €14.5B bond, Meta's $135B capex plan, and the Google/Meta AI capacity leasing report, the hardware layer of AI infrastructure has unambiguous demand. The hierarchy remains: power/infrastructure (CEG, EQIX, GEV) > optical (COHR, LITE) > compute (TSM, AVGO) > memory (MU) > software (AVOID leveraged SaaS).

The Google/Meta "intelligence-as-a-utility" capacity leasing signal is a single Tier 3 data point but directionally significant. If hyperscalers shift from buying GPUs to leasing compute capacity, it changes the revenue model from hardware capex cycles to recurring utility-style revenue — benefiting cloud infrastructure providers (ORCL, AMZN AWS, GOOG) over pure hardware suppliers. This is a hypothesis to monitor, not an established thesis.

The corporate bond issuance required to fund $1T+ in AI capex (Amazon, Meta) continues crowding out leveraged borrowers, which feeds directly into the credit cascade. The 22nd data point (Blue Owl insolvency) sits alongside this structural crowding-out mechanism as dual pressure on stressed credits.

### Credit Cascade at 22 Data Points, March 31 Quarter-End in 14 Days

Blue Owl discovering "irregularities" at Century Capital and pushing it into insolvency carries implications beyond a single portfolio company failure. If a sophisticated alternative asset manager with significant due diligence resources missed material problems in one borrower, the question of how many similar issues exist across the $1.7T+ private credit market is legitimate. Private credit secondaries market emergence (CNBC, separate event) confirms investors are seeking exits, which creates visible price discovery that could trigger NAV revisions across the industry.

BNP Paribas betting European private credit withstands a US downturn (FT, Tier 2) is directionally interesting but represents geographic diversification rather than sector health. European credit may outperform US credit, but this does not reduce US-specific risks.

The March 31 quarter-end is now the critical timing variable. Retail investors in semi-liquid vehicles who want to redeem must submit requests before quarter-end. If redemption requests exceed liquidity buffers, funds must either: (a) gate redemptions, which triggers immediate confidence crisis and public reporting, or (b) force-sell assets at discounts, which creates visible marks that ripple through valuations. The APO/BX pair trade remains at maximum conviction: Athene's permanent capital cannot be gated, while Blackstone's semi-liquid vehicles face precisely this quarter-end pressure.

### China's Strategic Position Strengthens While US Allies Defect

China securing preferential Hormuz passage through its Iran alliance while simultaneously banning jet fuel exports creates a structural energy cost advantage over Western manufacturers. Chinese crude access at standard prices versus $100+ for competitors represents a 30-40% input cost differential for energy-intensive industries. This is a competitive reshoring pressure that may accelerate US manufacturing investment in energy-independent processes.

Trump delaying the Xi summit by approximately one month, combined with Bessent meeting his Chinese counterpart in Paris, suggests the administration is exploring Chinese cooperation on Hormuz as diplomatic leverage. Section 301 trade probes targeting 60 economies remain the baseline tariff pathway following the Supreme Court IEEPA ruling, but Hormuz cooperation could become a bargaining chip in US-China negotiations. EWBC's US-China banking franchise positions it to benefit from ongoing financial channel communication.
