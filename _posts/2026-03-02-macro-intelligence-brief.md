## Executive Summary

The central question posed — whether additional stealth QE signals have emerged — requires a nuanced answer supported by six pre-strike data points and one critical post-strike counter-signal. **Before the Iran strikes, the evidence for background liquidity easing was accumulating at an accelerating pace.** Mortgage rates dropped below 6% without Fed cuts (CNBC, NPR, Fox Business). Barclays documented record oversubscription in US primary credit markets. Private credit EM inflows hit a record $22.3B (Reuters). BlackRock reported bonds outperforming cash 7.3% vs 4.3% for the first time since 2020. The yield curve was steepening from falling short-end rates. Apollo noted the bond market was "starved for income" at 2% real yields. All six are consistent with excess liquidity flowing through the system via mechanisms other than announced Fed rate cuts — the TGA drawdown, RRP facility contraction, and Treasury issuance composition (concentrated in bills rather than duration) are the most plausible channels.

**The post-strike evidence cuts the other way.** CNBC reports bonds "defied the safe-haven playbook" after the Iran strikes — they did not rally despite a massive risk-off event. If the Fed or Treasury were actively scaling up covert liquidity operations, one would expect Treasuries to rally on both safe-haven demand AND implicit backstop support. The absence of a rally suggests either that oil-driven inflation fears are overwhelming safe-haven flows, or that whatever background liquidity support existed pre-strike is NOT being expanded to meet the crisis. Corporate bond issuance froze entirely (GlobalCapital). This is the opposite of what would occur under active stealth QE. **My assessment: pre-strike stealth easing was real and measurable through financial conditions. Post-strike, it has not accelerated, and may face a test if the Fed must choose between maintaining background liquidity and fighting oil-driven inflation.** The Warsh nomination hearings will bring balance sheet operations under public scrutiny, potentially closing the window for covert operations entirely.

Since Brief #33, three developments are materially new. First, Qatar LNG production was halted by Iranian drone strikes on Ras Laffan and Mesaieed, transforming this from an oil-only supply shock into a dual oil-and-gas energy crisis. Qatar supplies ~15% of Europe's LNG; this disruption compounds the oil price spike with natural gas price spikes, broadening the inflationary impulse beyond gasoline into heating, industrial production, and fertilizer costs. Second, three US jets were downed in Kuwait, indicating Iranian military capability exceeding initial market assessments and extending the expected conflict duration. Brief #33 assigned 40% to prolonged drone warfare; that should tick to 45%. Third, a Saudi refinery was specifically targeted, causing gasoil/diesel to spike more than crude oil. This refined-product shortage hits transportation and logistics directly, adding a second inflationary transmission channel beyond raw crude prices.

The portfolio from Brief #33 requires no structural changes. The 56% allocation to physical assets and war economy (NEM + LMT + RTX + XOM) is validated by every new data point. The credit thesis (APO at 10%) strengthened with corporate bond issuance freezing and dual credit cascade mechanisms confirmed. ACGL at 11% provides the 0.38 beta insulation needed for Monday's gap-down.

## Key Events & Analysis

### Stealth QE Assessment: Six Pre-Strike Signals, One Post-Strike Counter-Signal

The evidence must be graded carefully because the stealth QE question bridges market mechanics and policy intent. Hard data supporting background liquidity easing pre-strike:

**Signal 1: Mortgage rates below 6% without Fed cuts (three sources: CNBC, NPR, Newsweek).** The 30-year fixed dropped from ~6.3% in early February to mid-5% range by late February. This 30-40bp decline occurred while the Fed held rates at 3.5-3.75%. The mechanism: falling Treasury yields transmitted through the MBS market. The Fed-Treasury accord converting MBS holdings to bills (flagged in Brief #29) may be reducing MBS supply, supporting mortgage-backed securities prices and lowering mortgage rates. This is passive QE for housing by a different name.

**Signal 2: Record corporate bond oversubscription (Barclays/Reuters).** US primary credit markets at most competitive on record. Unprecedented investor demand for new issuance. This is consistent with excess reserves flowing into credit markets and compressing yields/spreads beyond what fundamentals justify.

**Signal 3: Record EM private credit inflows, $22.3B (Reuters).** Capital flowing into riskier EM credit during a period of alleged monetary tightening suggests abundant global liquidity seeking returns — a pattern typical of easy monetary conditions.

**Signal 4: Yield curve steepening from falling short-end (BlackRock/InvestmentNews).** Short-term rates falling while long rates hold is consistent with liquidity provision that suppresses front-end rates. The TGA and RRP are the channels.

**Signal 5: 10Y below 4% on hot PPI (CNBC).** MarketWatch described this as "strange." Yields falling despite inflationary data could reflect structural demand (pension rebalancing, insurance liability matching) or non-market-driven buying activity.

**Signal 6: Apollo's 2% real yield observation (Seeking Alpha).** Bond market participants earning constrained real income despite elevated nominal rates — consistent with financial repression dynamics where real yields are suppressed through liquidity management.

**Counter-Signal: Bonds failed to rally on Iran strikes (CNBC).** This is the strongest single piece of evidence against active stealth QE escalation. If background liquidity support were being ramped up, it would manifest most visibly during a crisis as the natural policy response to maintain market functioning. The bond market's failure to rally suggests oil-driven inflation expectations are dominating, and no additional covert support is materializing.

**Synthesis:** Pre-strike financial conditions eased materially through non-explicit channels. This is consistent with Treasury issuance management (bills vs. bonds), TGA drawdowns, and the Fed-Treasury MBS accord functioning as de facto easing. But the Iran shock reveals the limits of this approach: when inflation expectations spike from supply disruption, background liquidity support cannot simultaneously suppress yields and contain inflation expectations. **The stealth QE thesis is confirmed for the pre-strike period but faces an inflection point.** If the Fed/Treasury must choose between maintaining background liquidity and fighting oil inflation, they will choose fighting inflation — which means the stealth easing window may close in March.

Medicare insolvency accelerating 12 years earlier than projected (CBO/MarketWatch) adds a structural fiscal driver: entitlement shortfalls increase pressure on Treasury issuance and could eventually force the Fed to accommodate larger deficits through balance sheet operations. This is a 12-24 month driver for gold, not an immediate catalyst, but it establishes why eventual monetization pressure is structural.

### Qatar LNG Halt: Energy Crisis Broadens From Oil to Gas

Brief #33 assessed the conflict primarily as an oil supply shock. Iranian drone strikes halting Qatar's Ras Laffan and Mesaieed LNG production transforms this into a dual energy crisis. Qatar was the world's largest LNG exporter before this disruption. CNBC confirms production halt. The transmission channels:

1. **European gas prices:** Qatar supplied approximately 15% of European LNG imports. European gas futures will spike, hitting industrial production costs and household heating expenses. This is the third European energy crisis in four years (2022 Russia, 2025 winter cold snap, 2026 Qatar disruption).

2. **Asian gas prices:** Japan, South Korea, and India are major Qatar LNG importers. India specifically flagged as "especially vulnerable" due to combined oil import dependence and LNG supply disruption.

3. **Fertilizer costs:** Natural gas is the primary feedstock for ammonia-based fertilizers. LNG disruption raises fertilizer costs, which feeds through to food prices with a 3-6 month lag.

4. **Sulfur supply chain:** Shanghai Metals Market reports the Hormuz closure is severing global sulfur trade, with downstream impacts on nickel/battery supply chains.

The Saudi refinery closure adds a refined product dimension. Gasoil/diesel spiking more than crude means the transportation and logistics cost impact exceeds what crude oil prices alone would suggest. FedEx, trucking companies, and airlines face immediate cost pressure from diesel, not just jet fuel.

### Credit Market: Issuance Freeze + Dual Cascade Mechanisms

Brief #33 estimated credit cascade probability at 28-33%. New evidence supports maintaining this range with the mechanisms now more clearly defined. Corporate bond issuance froze entirely upon Iran escalation (GlobalCapital). The sequence matters: Barclays documented record oversubscription (excess liquidity chasing yield) immediately before the freeze. This whiplash from maximum liquidity to zero liquidity mirrors March 2020 dynamics.

Three simultaneous credit stress mechanisms are now active: (1) Oil shock compresses margins for energy-intensive leveraged borrowers; (2) AI displacement reduces revenue for headcount-dependent leveraged borrowers (JPMorgan $40-150B CLO risk); (3) Bond market freeze prevents refinancing for companies with near-term maturities. These mechanisms are partially overlapping but not identical — a trucking company faces oil stress, a software company faces AI stress, and both face refinancing stress.

BofA's warning on rising problem loans in bank-loan ETFs is relevant to the stealth QE question: if banking system stress materializes, the Fed's historical response has been to create liquidity facilities (BTFP in 2023, etc.) that are functionally stealth QE. Rising problem loans could be the trigger that forces background liquidity support from quiet TGA management into visible emergency facilities.

### Defense: Three US Jets Downed Extends Conflict Timeline

Brief #33 assessed prolonged drone warfare at 40%. Three US jets being downed in Kuwait is hard data indicating: (1) Iranian military capability exceeds the "limited retaliation" scenario many analysts expected; (2) the conflict has spread to Kuwait, adding a fourth country (after UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Qatar) to Iran's target list; (3) aircraft losses create domestic political pressure for sustained response, not de-escalation.

BCA Research's rotation recommendation now includes shipping tanker ETFs alongside energy and defense — the Hormuz rerouting forces tankers through the Cape of Good Hope, approximately doubling voyage distances from Gulf producers to Asian buyers. Tanker utilization rates spike mechanically from longer distances, independent of actual oil production changes.

The CSG acquisition of a 49% stake in 4iG's defense unit with HIMARS contract exposure demonstrates that European defense supply chain restructuring is accelerating in real-time during the conflict. This confirms the structural multi-year procurement cycle thesis from six independent evidence streams.

### NVDA Photonics Investment: Strategic Moat Shift

NVDA investing $4B ($2B each in Coherent and Lumentum) in photonics while simultaneously losing GPU exclusivity with top customers reveals the company's strategic direction: if the chip monopoly erodes (Meta→AMD, OpenAI→Amazon), NVDA is building a moat around the data center interconnect layer. Photonics (optical interconnects between GPU clusters) is the bandwidth bottleneck for scaling AI training beyond single-rack configurations. This is analytically important for two reasons:

First, it validates photonics as a distinct investable layer within AI infrastructure. Coherent and Lumentum receive both capital validation and demand signal from the dominant AI compute player.

Second, it confirms NVDA management recognizes the customer diversification threat and is responding by expanding their value proposition beyond chips. The question is whether this strategic shift can happen fast enough to prevent multiple compression as the GPU monopoly premium erodes. For the current portfolio, this reinforces the decision to remove NVDA (right thesis, wrong macro regime) while providing a watchlist addition (COHR, LITE) for the eventual re-entry.
