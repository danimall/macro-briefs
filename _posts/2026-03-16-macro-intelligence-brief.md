The events of March 15-16 confirm and extend every major thesis in the world model while introducing two new developments that warrant attention: Israel's explicit announcement of 3+ more weeks of war provides the first hard floor for conflict duration, and the government bond market's loss of safe-haven status during an active geopolitical crisis represents a structural regime change in cross-asset correlations.

The stagflationary regime described in the prior brief has received additional confirmation through seven channels simultaneously: (1) oil CEOs warning the administration the crisis will worsen, (2) refiners balking at premiums creating demand destruction feedback loops, (3) China/Thailand banning jet fuel exports, (4) Chinese petrochemical firms declaring force majeure, (5) Hindalco invoking force majeure on aluminium, (6) pharma air routes disrupted threatening cancer drug supply, and (7) fashion supply chains confirmed disrupted for Zara and H&M. The multi-channel CPI peak estimate of 3.2-5.2% is tracking toward the upper end as disruptions compound rather than attenuate.

The credit cascade thesis accumulated its 20th and 21st independent data points: Davidson Kempner's Tony Yoseloff (a top-tier credit hedge fund principal) warning that a "substantial portion" of PE firms are stressed or distressed, and a separate FT confirmation of billions in retail redemptions from private credit funds. Public market credit spread widening confirms the contagion pathway from private to public credit is now active. The cascade probability holds at 55-60%, with the Ares/KKR semi-liquid fund restriction trigger intact.

## Key Events & Analysis

### Israel's 3-Week Timeline Eliminates Rapid Resolution and Depletes the SPR

Israel's announcement of "at least three more weeks" of war is the single most consequential piece of new information because it comes from a primary combatant and provides a verifiable minimum duration floor. Combined with Trump's ceasefire rejection (prior brief, 9th confirmation), the rapid de-escalation scenario probability drops from 12% to approximately 5-8%. The war is now operating on a known minimum 5-6 week timeline from inception.

The SPR arithmetic becomes fatal on this timeline. The world model's 9-14 day buffer estimate placed depletion at 50-60% probability with ~10 days elapsed. Adding 3+ more weeks of maximum-rate drawdown makes depletion near-certain unless drawdown rates have been substantially lower than estimated. The "US exhausting tools" reporting from Reuters and CNBC, plus oil CEOs warning Trump the crisis will worsen, are consistent with near-depletion. I raise SPR depletion probability to 65-75% within the now-confirmed conflict window. The oil ceiling scenario of $115-130 becomes significantly more likely.

Kharg Island seizure, if pursued, would permanently remove Iran's 1.5M bpd export capacity. The fact that CENTCOM struck military targets on Kharg while sparing oil infrastructure suggests deliberate restraint — but the option's existence as a reported policy consideration means it could be exercised if Iran escalates further. This creates a convex upside tail for oil prices beyond the central $98-108 range.

### Government Bonds Failing as Safe Haven Is a Structural Break

The CNBC report that Treasuries, gilts, and bunds are selling off during an active geopolitical crisis is one of the most analytically significant developments in this cycle. In every prior geopolitical crisis since 1970, developed-market sovereign debt rallied. The failure to do so in 2026 reflects the market pricing fiscal deficits ($55B/month war cost), inflation persistence (core PCE 3.1% and rising), and political pressure on central bank independence as structurally more threatening than the geopolitical risk itself.

The implications cascade through every asset class. The 60/40 portfolio, already under pressure from seven independent sources per the world model, is now definitively broken for this cycle. Institutional allocators who need defensive assets face a void: bonds don't work, gold is struggling against the dollar, and cash earns below inflation. This void is precisely what positions BRK.B ($373B cash, confirmed buyback resumption), gold miners (despite near-term headwinds, negative real rates are the structural driver), and insurance P&C names (non-correlated revenue, investment income from higher yields) as the genuine defensive assets. The traditional safe-haven trade is structurally impaired.

For mortgage rates specifically, the bond selloff mechanically pushes 30-year fixed rates higher. The CNBC mortgage rate report confirming 7-month highs is a direct consequence. The sub-6% window that briefly opened in February is confirmed closed for the entirety of 2026 under this regime.

Davidson Kempner is not a casual commentator. This is a practitioner with direct visibility into credit portfolios saying the problem is larger than consensus estimates.

The retail redemption wave from private credit funds (separate FT report) adds a demand-side pressure that did not exist three weeks ago. This makes the next quarter-end (March 31) a structural stress point for any fund that accepted retail capital.

The combination of: (1) top-tier credit fund warning of widespread PE distress, (2) retail redemption wave, (3) public credit spread widening, (4) Amazon €14.5B crowding out leveraged borrowers, and (5) oil-driven economic deterioration weakening portfolio company cash flows makes the Ares/KKR threshold trigger more likely. ### Multi-Channel Supply Disruption Now Validated by Industrial Force Majeure

The progression from thesis to data is now complete for the supply chain disruption framework. Chinese petrochemical force majeure on PET contracts (March 9 effective date) and Hindalco's aluminium force majeure are not forecasts — they are companies legally declaring inability to fulfill obligations due to the conflict. This is Tier 1 evidence that the disruption has penetrated industrial production chains beyond energy.

Fertilizer supply disruption during spring planting (Reuters, March 13) activates the 6-12 month food price persistence mechanism described in the world model. Farmers who cannot access affordable fertilizer in March-May 2026 either: (a) plant less acreage, reducing food supply, or (b) absorb higher input costs, which pass through to food prices for the following 12 months. CF Industries' domestic natural gas feedstock advantage (~$2/MMBtu vs. $100+ oil-linked international competitors) is now a live structural advantage, not a theoretical one.

The pharma air route disruption (Reuters, temperature-sensitive cancer drugs) opens a supply chain vulnerability that has direct humanitarian implications and likely triggers regulatory intervention — emergency import authorizations, alternative routing mandates, or strategic stockpile releases for critical medications. Companies with diversified supply chains benefit; those dependent on Gulf routing face material risk.
