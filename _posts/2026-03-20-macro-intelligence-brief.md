## Executive Summary

The March 19-20 data batch confirms and extends every major thesis from the prior brief while introducing two structural escalations that warrant attention. First, Qatar's LNG capacity has been reduced by 17% for up to five years per QatarEnergy's CEO — this transforms the energy supply disruption from a cyclical conflict-duration play into a multi-year structural deficit in global LNG markets. Second, China's restriction on fuel and fertilizer exports to preserve domestic stockpiles removes the world's largest fertilizer producer from global supply at exactly the moment when Qatar LNG damage, Malaysian production halts, and spring planting emergencies are compounding. The CF Industries thesis now has 7-8 independent confirmations from entirely different source categories (supply disruption, policy response, weather, geopolitical escalation, agricultural data, Chinese export restrictions).

The macro regime has shifted from the "stagflation with active policy constraint tightening" identified 24 hours ago to what I'd characterize as "globally synchronized stagflation with no policy offset." All three major central banks (Fed, ECB, BOJ) plus the SNB are holding rates with hawkish inflation warnings. The Fed discussed hikes. The ECB called the outlook "significantly more uncertain." The BOJ flagged inflationary pressure. There is no central bank anywhere in the developed world positioned to ease. Meanwhile, the S&P 500 broke below its 200-day moving average for the first time in 214 sessions, gold is falling despite peak geopolitical crisis because real yields are rising, and Goldman Sachs explicitly warned that bonds won't hedge equity declines. The 60/40 portfolio is broken. Cash, P&C insurance, defense, fertilizer, and exchanges remain the highest-conviction positions.

One genuinely contrary data point deserves acknowledgment: weekly jobless claims fell to 205K, the lowest since January, and FedEx beat estimates while raising guidance. These data points challenge the pure recession narrative. Claims at 205K and FedEx raising guidance are inconsistent with an economy in freefall. However, they are consistent with a stagflationary environment where supply disruptions create both pricing power (FedEx) and labor hoarding (claims) even as aggregate demand weakens. The recession probability stays at 40-45% but the February -92K NFP may have been distorted by seasonal/survey factors rather than reflecting genuine employment collapse.

## Key Events & Analysis

### Qatar LNG: From Cyclical Disruption to Multi-Year Structural Deficit

QatarEnergy's CEO confirming 17% capacity loss for up to five years is the single most consequential energy development in this batch. Qatar produces approximately 77 MTPA of LNG; 17% is ~13 MTPA, or roughly 3.5% of global LNG supply removed for half a decade. This is not recoverable by OPEC spare capacity or SPR releases. LNG liquefaction trains take 4-7 years to build. The destroyed capacity simply will not exist.

The transmission mechanism to the tracked universe is straightforward. Cheniere Energy (LNG) is the largest US LNG exporter and the most direct beneficiary — its existing long-term contracts reprice upward at renewal, and spot sales capture elevated pricing. European gas prices already surged 25% on the initial strike; five-year removal of Qatar capacity means European gas prices stay structurally elevated. Natural gas producers (EQT, AR, RRC) benefit from the pull-through effect: higher LNG export prices increase demand for US domestic gas feedstock, tightening the Henry Hub market.

CF Industries benefits through two channels: (1) global fertilizer production using natural gas feedstock (much of it sourced from Qatar LNG) faces structural supply reduction, raising fertilizer prices; (2) CF's domestic US natural gas feedstock advantage widens as global gas prices diverge upward from US domestic prices. This is now the 7th or 8th independent data point for the CF thesis.

### China's Fertilizer Export Ban: The Missing Piece for Food Inflation Thesis

China restricting fuel and fertilizer exports to preserve domestic stockpiles removes the world's largest fertilizer producer from the global market during the most supply-constrained period since 2022. China accounts for approximately 30% of global phosphate and 25% of global nitrogen fertilizer exports. Removing this supply compounds: Qatar LNG damage (gas-based fertilizer production impaired for years), Malaysian production halt (prior brief), spring planting emergency (WFP warning of 45M facing acute hunger), and South Pars risk. The fertilizer supply chain is now under simultaneous pressure from 4-5 independent disruption sources.

The food inflation pipeline accelerates: fertilizer scarcity → higher input costs → reduced planting or lower yields → food price inflation in Q3-Q4 2026. This operates on a 3-6 month lag from the fertilizer disruption, meaning the CPI impact will arrive in late summer/early fall, overlapping with the diesel passthrough already underway and the European gas cost passthrough. Multi-channel inflation exceeding 5% remains at 30-35% probability.

### Credit Markets: Hawkish Fed Compounds Structural Stress

Today's data adds concrete evidence of credit market deterioration: Akzo Nobel paid 25bp new-issue concession (highest euro-denominated this year), European and Asian borrowers delayed bond issuances, Indian mutual funds dumped government bonds at record pace, and Indian corporate bond funds saw $2.5B in outflows over three months. Each data point independently confirms that fixed income markets are under stress from the war's inflationary impact.

The EA $18B junk bond offering — the single most informative test of the credit cascade thesis — faces incrementally worse conditions than 24 hours ago. The hawkish Fed, rising credit spreads, and crowding-out from Amazon's €14.5B deal all reduce the appetite for junk-rated debt. March 31 is now 11 days away. BX BCRED's 7.9% NAV redemption rate and JPMorgan's restrictions on private credit lending remain the proximate triggers for cascade risk. If Ares or KKR restrict semi-liquid fund redemptions before quarter-end, the probability rises from 55-60% toward 70%.

### Jobless Claims and FedEx: Genuine Contrary Signals to Recession Thesis

Weekly claims at 205K and FedEx beating estimates and raising guidance are hard data that conflict with the recession narrative. I won't dismiss these. Claims at 205K suggest layoffs remain historically low; if true, the February -92K NFP may have been a statistical anomaly rather than a genuine employment collapse. March NFP data will be the deciding factor. FedEx's guidance raise is harder to explain in a recessionary environment — though it may reflect pricing power from supply disruption (freight rates higher on rerouted trade) rather than volume growth.

These contrary signals don't change the overall stagflationary thesis because inflation is accelerating regardless of employment conditions. But they modestly temper the recession probability: if March claims and NFP both suggest employment stability, the recession probability would decline from 40-45% toward 35-40%. The stagflation characterization requires both rising inflation AND rising unemployment; if only one leg materializes, the policy response and market dynamics differ significantly.

### Defense: $223B Total — $200B Pentagon Supplemental + $23B Emergency Gulf Arms

Trump invoking emergency powers for $23B in Gulf arms sales, on top of the $200B Pentagon supplemental, brings total identifiable defense spending to $223B. This is 15+ independent data points for the defense thesis with concrete dollar figures. The emergency powers invocation bypasses Congressional review, ensuring near-term execution regardless of political dynamics. Saudi Arabia and UAE as buyers deepen the US commitment to regional coalition operations, reducing the probability of abrupt US exit while expanding the total addressable market for US defense primes.

### Technical Breakdown: S&P 500 Below 200-Day MA

The 200-day MA breach after 214 sessions triggers systematic selling from trend-following strategies, CTAs, and rules-based allocators. Combined with the fourth consecutive weekly decline, this creates a self-reinforcing negative momentum signal. Goldman's explicit warning that bonds won't hedge equity declines means the traditional rebalancing mechanism (sell equities, buy bonds) doesn't work. Investors are left with cash, commodities, or alternative assets. This benefits exchange operators (CME, ICE, CBOE) through elevated multi-asset volatility and trading volumes, and benefits BRK.B as the ultimate cash-rich defensive position.
