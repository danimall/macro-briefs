## Executive Summary

The stagflationary regime described in prior briefs is now accelerating on multiple fronts simultaneously, with two genuinely new developments that alter the world model. First, the US eased Russian oil sanctions — temporarily authorizing purchases of stranded cargoes at sea — which represents a material policy reversal that signals the administration's concern about energy prices is higher than Trump's public rhetoric ("US benefits from high oil") suggested. The contradiction between rhetoric and action is itself information: the administration recognizes the $100+ oil price is creating unacceptable economic damage but is unwilling to de-escalate the Iran conflict to solve it, instead reaching for the Russia sanctions lever. This shifts the world model's Russian sanctions relief probability upward from 10-15% to 15-20%, with additional easing probable if oil sustains above $100 for another 2-3 weeks.

Second, the Section 301 trade probes launched against 60 economies — including the UK, EU, and Canada — following the Supreme Court's IEEPA tariff invalidation, provide the structural explanation for the claims-vs.-NFP divergence that has puzzled this analysis for three weeks. Companies facing simultaneous uncertainty from: (a) Iran war and $100 oil, (b) potential tariffs on imports from 60+ trading partners, (c) credit market stress, and (d) a pending FOMC facing maximum ambiguity have a rational basis for freezing hiring while retaining existing workers. The -92K NFP with 213K claims is not a measurement error — it is the labor market signature of pervasive corporate uncertainty. This framing has direct implications for whether the weakness reverses: it only reverses when the uncertainty resolves, and the Section 301 probes add 6-12 months to the uncertainty timeline.

The Dow falling 700+ points to its 2026 low, oil near $100 with the largest weekly gain since the 1980s, core PCE at 3.1%, Trump claiming Iran is "about to surrender" while aides have no exit strategy, and munitions depleted by "years" worth in two weeks — this collection of data points describes an economy being squeezed from three directions (energy costs, policy uncertainty, credit stress) without an identifiable release valve. The March 18-19 FOMC faces the worst information environment of any meeting since 2022: stale CPI at 2.4% (pre-oil spike), core PCE at 3.1%, -92K NFP vs. 213K claims, $100 oil, and IEEPA tariffs struck down but Section 301 initiated. Any forward guidance will be invalidated within weeks by subsequent data.

## Key Events & Analysis

### Russian Sanctions Easing: The First Domino in Sanctions Unwind

The temporary authorization to purchase Russian oil stranded at sea is analytically important beyond its marginal supply impact (estimated 0.5-1.5M bbl/d of floating crude). The mechanism reveals the administration's implicit priority ordering: (1) prevent domestic gasoline prices from becoming politically toxic, (2) maintain Iran military operations, (3) maintain Russia sanctions regime. When (1) and (3) conflict, (3) yields. Germany's Merz immediately criticized the decision, confirming the US-European alliance strain that the world model flagged. If this precedent is extended — which I consider likely if oil remains above $100 for two more weeks — it creates a path toward broader sanctions relaxation that could ultimately bring 2-3M bbl/d of Russian supply back to global markets.

The perverse incentive structure is now even clearer: Russia earns $150M/day from elevated prices AND gets sanctions relief. Moscow's optimal strategy is to quietly support the continuation of the Iran conflict while publicly calling for peace. The emerging Russia-Iran energy coordination (both allowing selective transit for India-bound crude through the same corridor) is consistent with this incentive alignment.

For refiners specifically (VLO, MPC, PSX), access to cheaper Russian crude feedstock while gasoline remains at $3.50+/gallon creates a temporary margin windfall. This is the first time in this crisis that the refining complex has an identifiable positive catalyst.

### Section 301 as the NFP Explanation — and Why It Persists

I've been tracking the claims vs. NFP divergence for three weeks without a satisfying causal explanation. The Section 301 probes targeting 60 economies provide it. When the IEEPA tariff mechanism was available, it created acute but defined uncertainty — companies knew tariffs could come quickly but also knew they'd be legally challenged. Now, the administration has initiated investigations against virtually every significant US trading partner through a legally more defensible mechanism that takes 6-12 months to resolve. This means: companies with global supply chains (which is nearly every large employer) face unresolvable uncertainty about future input costs for at least the next two quarters. The rational response is exactly what the data shows — retain existing workers (costly to fire and rehire) but freeze new hiring until the regulatory environment clarifies.

This reframing changes the recession probability calculus. The -92K NFP is not a cyclical demand-driven employment decline that monetary policy can address. It's a policy-uncertainty-driven hiring freeze that only resolves when the uncertainty resolves. The Fed cutting rates does nothing to reduce tariff uncertainty. This argues against emergency cuts (supporting my 3-5% probability estimate) and increases the probability that the labor market weakness persists through mid-2026 regardless of monetary policy action.

### Munitions Depletion: Multi-Year Defense Thesis Now Hardware-Confirmed

The FT reporting that the US has burned through "years" of munitions including Tomahawk missiles in approximately two weeks is the most concrete validation of the defense restocking thesis to date. This is now the 8th independent data point: (1) LMT CEO/Trump production mandate, (2) Rheinmetall 45% growth, (3) KeyBanc AVAV upgrade, (4) France warships to Hormuz, (5) US Navy escort infeasibility, (6) Indian defense outperformance, (7) Pentagon CTO defense-AI comments, (8) confirmed multi-year munitions depletion. The production timelines for Tomahawk missiles (2-3 year production cycles) mean this is not a one-quarter demand pulse but a structural increase in defense procurement that persists regardless of conflict duration — the munitions must be replaced even if a ceasefire occurs tomorrow.

Combined with the $55B/month war cost run rate and Poland's veto of EU defense funding (which pushes European procurement toward bilateral US purchases), the defense sector has the highest evidentiary support and most clearly defined multi-year demand driver of any theme in this portfolio.

The accumulation rate is accelerating: we've added 5 data points in the last 48 hours (Partners Group, Deutsche Bank expansion, Carlyle restructuring, FT 2008 comparison reaching mainstream consciousness, and credit spreads widening to November highs in European HY). ### The Helium Channel: Supply Disruption Thesis Expands to Six Channels

Qatar's LNG production halt causing helium price surges is a new supply chain disruption vector not previously tracked. Helium is critical for: semiconductor manufacturing (process cooling and carrier gas), MRI equipment operation, aerospace, and fiber optic production. This is the sixth confirmed disruption channel:

1. Oil/energy (IEA — Tier 1)
2. Fertilizer/sulphur (multiple sources — Tier 1-2)
3. Pharmaceuticals (Indian pharma impact — Tier 3)
4. Semiconductors via petrochemicals (Samsung CEO — Tier 1)
5. Fashion/consumer goods (Zara, H&M — Tier 1)
6. **Helium** (Qatar LNG halt — Tier 1, NEW)

The helium channel is particularly concerning for semiconductor manufacturing because there are very few alternative sources — helium is not synthesizable and comes primarily from natural gas processing. The world's helium supply is concentrated in Qatar, the US, and Russia. With Qatar disrupted and Russian supply subject to sanctions, the US Bureau of Land Management reserve becomes a critical bottleneck. If wafer fabrication plants cannot source adequate helium, the 15-20% probability of wafer start constraints I previously estimated may need to be revised upward to 20-25%.

### Ulta Beauty: The Consumer Canary

Ulta's weak 2026 guidance explicitly citing consumer caution from "global conflicts" is the first consumer discretionary earnings report to directly attribute softness to the Iran war. This is analytically important because it shifts from inference (we estimate oil at $100 hurts consumers) to confirmation (a company with 1,400+ US stores is seeing the behavioral response in real time). The calibration document warns against Consumer Discretionary BUY calls, which average -1.54% across 11 historical calls. Ulta's guidance reinforces that discipline. The consumer discretionary AVOID list — NCLH, CCL, SBUX, ULTA, NKE, LULU — continues to accumulate confirming evidence.
